# Crème Chiboust

![Crème chiboust](resources/creme-chiboust.png)

*This crème brings together the richness of crème pâtissière with the lightness of Italian meringue, creating an exceptionally light and elegant cream.*

**Serves:** 1.3kg

**Prep Time:** 10 minutes

## Overview
Crème chiboust is the building block for the classic Saint-Honoré gateau and any pastry that wants a cream lighter than crème pâtissière but more flavoured and stable than crème Chantilly: a hot crème pâtissière with custard powder for body, flavoured with Grand Marnier, folded into a fresh Italian meringue while both are still soft so the mixture sets to a mousse-light filling that can be piped, layered or torched to a brûlée. The two components have to be made together and combined the moment both are ready, because chiboust is one of those creams with no shelf-life: the meringue starts weeping water within an hour of mixing, so you need the rest of the dessert assembled and waiting. Make a full quantity of crème pâtissière (with custard powder replacing half the flour for a cleaner finish) and a full quantity of Italian meringue. Stir 50 ml of Grand Marnier into the warm crème pâtissière first so the liqueur dissolves evenly through the custard, then whisk in a third of the Italian meringue to slacken the base; that first third is doing the work of bringing the two textures into the same world. Switch to a spatula and fold the remaining meringue through in big gentle scooping motions till the cream is uniform and smooth (you'll see the colour and texture even out). Don't be tempted to keep folding once it's homogeneous; another minute and you'll have flattened all the air you just built in. Use immediately while it's at its lightest, piping into baked choux puffs, spreading onto a Saint-Honoré, or torching the top to a caramelised brûlée crust.

## Ingredients

### For the Crème pâtissière
- 6 egg yolks
- 80 grams sugar
- 30 grams custard powder
- 350 ml milk
- ½ vanilla pod

### For the Meringue Italienne
- 80 ml water
- 360 grams sugar
- 30 grams glucose
- 6 egg whites

### For the Crème chiboust
-  50 ml Grand Marnier

## Method
1. Using the ingredients, make a quantity of Crème pâtissière and Meringue Italienne
1. Stir in the alcohol into the Crème pâtissière, and with a whisk, stir in one-third of the Meringue Italienne . 
1. Then, with a spatula, gently fold in the remaining Meringue Italienne until the mixture is completely homogeneous.
1. Do not overwork, or the cream will collapse and lose its lightness.

**Note**: You must use this cream as soon as you have mixed in the meringue italienne, so it is essential to have the rest of the dessert ready before finishing the Crème Chiboust.

## Notes
- Timing is critical; prepare all other dessert components before beginning the cream so assembly can be immediate
- Fold the meringue gently with a spatula using lifting motions; aggressive stirring deflates the mixture
- The alcohol dissolves into the crème pâtissière before meringue is added to distribute the flavor evenly
- The temperature contrast between the warm custard and cooled meringue should be minimal to prevent texture loss

## Serving
Use crème chiboust immediately as a filling for tarts, St. Honoré cakes, or mousse-based desserts. Its light texture makes it particularly suited to delicate presentations. Often torched or browned under the grill for added visual appeal.

## Storage
Best consumed immediately; do not store prepared crème chiboust as the meringue will gradually weep and separate. If necessary, refrigerate for a maximum of 1-2 hours in an airtight container, though texture degradation is expected.
