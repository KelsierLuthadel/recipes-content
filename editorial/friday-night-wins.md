It's been a week. The fridge has the basics, the kettle is on, and you'd
rather not learn anything new tonight. These five dinners trade
ambition for reliability: each one cooks in roughly the time it takes
to pour a drink and decide on a film, and every one of them has earned
its place in someone's regular rotation by being repeatedly satisfying
rather than once-impressive.

Pick whichever protein matches what's in the fridge: smash some beef
into a hot pan, slide a tray of chicken thighs under the grill, or
char something tomato-y and rich while the pasta water boils. Friday's
job is to feed you well without asking much in return.
