The classic dinner-party trap is spending the afternoon on a dessert
that the guests then politely don't eat much of because they're full
of main course. The fix is to pick a dessert that's nine parts cold
storage and one part assembly. Pour, layer, chill, slice, serve.

These five lean into that. A tiramisu that comes together in twenty
minutes and gets better overnight. A panna cotta that needs four
ingredients and a careful warming of the cream. A creme brulee whose
only flourish is a final blast of caramel. Choose by what's in the
fridge and how many hours of head-start you have.
