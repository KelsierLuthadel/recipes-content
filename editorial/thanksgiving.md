Thanksgiving is the one meal of the year where almost everyone is cooking the same thing at the same time. The structure barely varies: a roast bird as the centrepiece, a stuffing it sits next to, mashed potatoes, sweet potatoes, something with corn, a green vegetable nobody really came for, and at least two pies. The skill is not improvisation. The skill is timing, oven space, and keeping the gravy hot.

What makes a Thanksgiving table good is the harmony across all those sides, not any one dish doing the heavy lifting. A great stuffing balances out a leaner main. Maple baked beans bring sweetness that the savoury sides need. Cornbread or buttermilk biscuits soak up the gravy. And the pies, plural, are the whole point of the evening for at least half the table.

Cook on the larger end of every quantity. Leftovers are part of the contract.
