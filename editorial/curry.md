The word "curry" is an English shortcut for something that does not really fit one word. It came back with the British from the spice ports of the Indian subcontinent and grew up on the way, picking up Thai coconut pastes, Sri Lankan crab boils, Malaysian laksas, Indonesian rendangs, Japanese kare-raisu, Caribbean roti shops and South-African Cape Malay sambals on the journey. What ties them all together is method: a layered spice base bloomed in fat, an aromatic build of onion, garlic, ginger or lemongrass, a slow simmer or a fast wok-fry, and a deep complex sauce that holds the protein at the end. Almost everything else is regional argument.

A good curry is built in stages. The spice paste or powder is the soul of the dish: get it right and the rest of the cooking falls into place. The fat carries the flavour, the onions build the body, the tomato or coconut milk pulls it all together, and time does the last bit of work. Some take ten minutes and a wok; others take three hours and a slow oven. They reward attention but punish nothing more than a rushed temper of mustard seeds or a powder dumped in cold oil.

The pages below pull together the catalogue's curry universe in one place. Start with the country that has the curry tradition you grew up on, or the spice profile you like best, then branch out. The rice and bread sit beside every curry on the table; the chutneys, raitas and pickles do the same fixing-the-balance job that bread and butter does on an English plate. Build the whole meal, not just the curry, and the table sings.

## Sides

The sides are what make a curry feast. A cooling cucumber raita softens a hot vindaloo; a sour-sweet tamarind chutney lifts a creamy korma; lime pickle and mango chutney sit on the table as the sharp counterpoints that the gravy cannot provide on its own. Build at least one wet (raita or yoghurt-dip) and one sharp (pickle or chutney) per curry plate.

## Countries

Each curry tradition is its own thing. Pick a country and work through its iconic dishes; once you have the spice base, the technique transfers to dozens of variants. Recipes here are gathered by the country they originate from rather than by the protein, so you can build a whole regional meal at once.

### Indian

The deepest curry tradition in the world. North Indian dishes lean on dairy, butter and cream; South Indian on coconut, curry leaves and mustard seed; Bengali on mustard oil and fish; Goan on vinegar and chilli (the Portuguese inheritance). The British Indian Restaurant (BIR) tradition that arrived through Bangladesh in the 1960s pruned and standardised this universe into the pub-menu dishes (tikka masala, korma, vindaloo, jalfrezi) most non-Indians meet first.

### Pakistani

Pakistan's curry table sits between Indian Punjabi and Persian-Afghan. Karahi (the wok-fried tomato-and-meat dish), nihari (the overnight slow-simmer beef shank), and biryani are the pillars; the spice profile leans on garam masala, dried red chilli and a heavy hand on the ginger-garlic paste.

- [Chicken Karahi](../cuisine/pakistani/chicken-karahi.md)
- [Beef Nihari](../cuisine/pakistani/beef-nihari.md)
- [Lamb Nihari](../cuisine/pakistani/lamb-nihari.md)
- [Sindhi Biryani](../cuisine/pakistani/sindhi-biryani.md)

### Lahori

The kitchen of Punjab's old capital. Lahori cooking lives on its tandoor and its karahi: meat slow-browned with whole spices, finished with a fistful of fresh ginger, green chilli and coriander.

- [Lahori Mutton Karahi](../cuisine/lahori/lahori-mutton-karahi.md)
- [Lahori Mutton Biryani](../cuisine/lahori/rice/lahori-mutton-biryani.md)
- [Lahori Chana Pulao](../cuisine/lahori/rice/lahori-chana-pulao.md)

### Bengali

East India and Bangladesh. Mustard oil, panch phoran (Bengali five-spice), fish and the freshwater prawn (chingri). Bengali curries are looser and lighter than the dairy-thickened North-Indian gravies.

- [Chingri Malai Curry](../cuisine/bengali/chingri-malai-curry.md)
- [Chanar Dalna](../cuisine/bengali/chanar-dalna.md)

### Goan

Where Portuguese ingredients (vinegar, port, pork) folded into the Indian spice cabinet. Vinegar-spiked vindaloo, coconut-and-tamarind fish caldine, prawn balchao. The most chilli-forward Indian-coast cuisine.

- [Goan Fish Curry](../cuisine/goan/goan-fish-curry.md)
- [Goan Pork Vindaloo](../cuisine/indian/goan-pork-vindaloo.md)
- [Goan Chicken Biryani](../cuisine/goan/rice/goan-chicken-biryani.md)

### Sri Lankan

A separate tradition from Indian curry: heavier on the roasted chilli, lighter on the dairy, with curry leaves and Maldive fish flakes as the bottom note. The black-curry-powder roast is the signature step.

- [Sri Lankan Chicken Curry](../cuisine/sri-lankan/sri-lankan-chicken-curry.md)
- [Polos Curry](../cuisine/sri-lankan/polos-curry.md)

### Thai

Thai curry is paste-based rather than powder-based: red, green, yellow, massaman and panang pastes pounded in a mortar and stewed in coconut milk. Bright, aromatic and fast; ready in twenty minutes once the paste is made.

- [Thai Green Chicken Curry](../cuisine/thai/thai-green-chicken-curry.md)
- [Thai Beef Massaman](../cuisine/thai/thai-beef-massaman.md)
- [Thai Green Curry (Vegetarian)](../cuisine/thai/thai-green-curry-vegetarian.md)
- [Jungle Curry](../cuisine/thai/jungle-curry.md)

### Malaysian

Curry powders meet laksa pastes. Malaysian curries sit between Indian, Chinese and South-East Asian: the country's three big culinary inheritances on one plate.

- [Malaysian Laksa](../cuisine/malaysian/malaysian-laksa.md)
- [Fennel Shellfish Laksa](../cuisine/malaysian/fennel-shellfish-laksa.md)
- [Dhal Curry (Malaysian)](../cuisine/malaysian/dhal-curry.md)

### Indonesian

Spice-trade ground zero. Rendang is the dish that built the islands' reputation; a slow dry-curry of beef and coconut, simmered down till the sauce caramelises onto the meat.

- [Beef Rendang](../cuisine/indonesian/beef-rendang.md)

### Burmese

Lemongrass, ginger, tamarind, chickpea flour. The Burmese kitchen sits between Indian and South-East Asian, with its own coconut-noodle curry that everyone in Yangon eats for breakfast.

- [Ohn No Khao Swe](../cuisine/burmese/ohn-no-khao-swe.md)

### Afghan

Karahi-style cooking with Persian spice influence; less chilli than Punjab, more saffron and dried lime.

- [Lamb Karahi (Afghan)](../cuisine/afghanistan/lamb-karahi-afghani.md)

### Filipino

A different lineage: a coconut-and-peanut "curry" (kare-kare) that owes more to South-East Asian peanut sauces than to the Indian spice tradition, but reads as curry to anyone outside the Philippines.

- [Kare-Kare](../cuisine/filipino/kare-kare.md)
