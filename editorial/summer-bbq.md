Summer cooking moves outdoors. The oven goes off and stays off, the grill takes over, and the kitchen is reduced to a holding area for the salads and the dessert. This is the season of long marinades, charred edges, smoke that hangs in your clothes for hours afterwards, and the kind of meals that start at five and finish whenever the light goes.

The best cookout spreads are not single-cuisine. American pulled pork sits perfectly happily next to Korean bulgogi, Mexican carne asada, Jamaican jerk chicken and Cantonese char siu. What ties them together is technique: a generous rub or marinade, real heat, and enough time near the fire to build a proper crust. Build the rest of the plate around what is in season: corn, tomatoes, stone fruit, anything you can eat with your hands.

Pick two or three proteins, three or four cold sides, one big bowl of something that holds, and a pie. That is the whole formula.
