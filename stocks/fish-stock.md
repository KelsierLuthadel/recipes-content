# Fish Stock

![Fish Stock](resources/fish-stock.jpg)

*Delicate and refined, fish stock (fumet de poisson) is the essential foundation for classical French fish sauces, bisques, and fish-based preparations requiring subtle aquatic flavor.*

**Prep Time:** 15 minutes

**Cook Time:** 30 minutes

**Yield:** Approximately 2 litres

## Overview

Fish stock (fumet de poisson) is the delicate base for the entire French fish-cookery repertoire: beurre blanc, sauce vin blanc, bisques, fish soups and poaching liquors. It's the most fragile stock in the kitchen and the easiest to ruin, and the rule that keeps it on the right side is short timing: simmer for 25 minutes, no longer, because every extra minute drives the bones bitter and acrid. The other rule is to use bones from white fish only (sole, turbot, brill, plaice frames); oily fish (mackerel, salmon, sardine) carry too much fat and break the flavour. Rinse the bones repeatedly under cold water till the water runs clear, pulling out scales, dark gills and any clinging blood. Melt butter in a wide pan over low heat and sweat thinly sliced leek whites and onion gently for three or four minutes without colour, then add the bones, stir to coat, give them two or three minutes of bubble before pouring in dry white wine and letting it reduce by two-thirds to concentrate the acidity. Cover with 2.5 litres of cold water, bring just to a boil, drop straight to a bare simmer and skim the surface four or five times. Now add the bouquet garni, lemon slices and sliced mushrooms, and at the 15-minute mark drop in the crushed white peppercorns tied in muslin (loose peppercorns make the stock harsh; muslin gives the lift without the burn). Don't stir. At 25 minutes, ladle through a fine sieve into a clean bowl, plunge the bowl into an ice bath to drop the temperature fast, and decant. Pale gold and crystal clear is the look you want.

## Ingredients

### Fish Bones & Trimmings
- 1 ½ kilograms fresh fish bones and trimmings (white fish preferred: sole, turbot, brill, avoid oily fish)

### Fat Base
- 50 grams unsalted butter

### Aromatics & Vegetables
- 2 leeks (white parts only, washed thoroughly, thinly sliced, approximately 200 grams)
- 75 grams onions (thinly sliced)
- 75 grams fresh button mushrooms (thinly sliced)

### Liquid Base
- 200 millilitres dry white wine
- 2 ½ litres cold water

### Aromatics & Seasoning
- 1 [Bouquet Garni](../base-ingredients/herbs/bouquet-garni.md)
- 2 slices fresh lemon (with peel)
- 8 white peppercorns (crushed and tied in muslin)

## Method

### Stage 1 - Prepare Fish Bones
1. Rinse 1 ½ kilograms fresh fish bones thoroughly under cold running water.
1. Remove scales, blood, viscera, and dark tissue.
1. Rinse multiple times until water runs clear.
1. Drain thoroughly.

### Stage 2 - Sweat Aromatics in Butter
1. Melt 50 grams unsalted butter in a large saucepan over low heat.
1. Add 2 leeks (white parts only, thinly sliced) and 75 grams onions (thinly sliced).
1. Stir to coat vegetables in butter.
1. "Sweat" over low heat for approximately 3-4 minutes, stirring occasionally.
1. Vegetables should soften without browning.

### Stage 3 - Add Fish Bones
1. Add the rinsed fish bones and trimmings to the pan.
1. Stir gently to distribute bones among vegetables.
1. Allow the bones to bubble gently for 2-3 minutes.

### Stage 4 - Wine Reduction
1. Pour 200 millilitres dry white wine over the bones and vegetables.
1. Increase the heat to medium-high.
1. Allow the wine to bubble and reduce for approximately 2-3 minutes.
1. The volume should reduce by approximately two-thirds.

### Stage 5 - Add Water & Initial Cooking
1. Add 2 ½ litres cold water to the pan.
1. Increase heat to high and bring just to a rolling boil (approximately 5 minutes).
1. As soon as the water reaches a full boil, immediately lower the heat to very low.
1. The surface should show a bare simmer.
1. Using a large, flat spoon, skim the surface thoroughly (4-5 passes).

### Stage 6 - Add Aromatic Ingredients & Continue Simmering
1. Add 1 Bouquet garni, 2 slices fresh lemon, and 75 grams button mushrooms.
1. Stir gently to distribute.
1. Maintain a bare simmer.
1. Simmer gently, uncovered, for exactly 25 minutes.
1. At the 15-minute mark, add the muslin-wrapped 8 white peppercorns.
1. Do not stir the stock during cooking.

### Stage 7 - Final Straining
1. Place a fine-meshed sieve over a clean bowl.
1. Gently ladle the stock through the strainer.
1. Allow the liquid to flow without forcing.
1. Discard all solids.

### Stage 8 - Cool & Store
1. Allow strained stock to cool to room temperature (approximately 20-30 minutes).
1. Prepare a bowl of ice water.
1. Place the bowl with warm stock into the ice bath for approximately 15 minutes.
1. Decant into storage containers.

## Notes
- **Fresh Fish Bones Essential:** Fresh bones are critical; frozen or old bones produce unpleasant stock.
- **White Fish Preferred:** Use mild white fish (sole, turbot, brill). Avoid oily fish.
- **Cooking Duration Critical:** 25 minutes is exact; longer cooking produces bitter flavors.
- **Wine Reduction Technique:** Concentrates acidity before water addition.
- **Butter Sweating:** Used for flavor contribution; unsalted butter is essential.
- **Stock Clarity:** Should be clear and pale golden; cloudiness indicates improper skimming.

## Variations
- **Red Wine Fish Stock:** Substitute dry red wine for white wine.
- **Herb-Forward:** Add 1-2 sprigs fresh tarragon or dill.
- **Saffron Addition:** Add ¼ teaspoon saffron (soaked first) 5 minutes before end.

## Serving
- **Primary Use:** Base for classical fish sauces
- **Secondary Use:** Braising liquid for fish, poaching medium
- **Temperature:** Heat to steaming (85-90°C); avoid boiling
- **Pairing:** Delicate white fish, shellfish

## Storage
- **Refrigeration:** 2-3 days in covered container
- **Freezing:** Up to 2 months
- **Lemon Removal:** Remove lemon before storing
- **Reheating:** Thaw in refrigerator, then reheat gently
- **Smell Test:** Always verify pleasant aroma before use
