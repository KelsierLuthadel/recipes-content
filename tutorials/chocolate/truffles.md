# Truffles

![Cocoa-dusted dark chocolate truffles](resources/truffles.jpg)

*A firm ganache, scooped into balls, coated in cocoa powder or chopped nuts. The simplest finished chocolate. No tempering required for the basic version; an optional tempered-chocolate shell elevates them to professional bonbons.*

## Overview
The truffle is the easiest finished chocolate confection. The technique is: make a firm ganache (2:1 chocolate to cream); chill until set; scoop into balls; roll; coat. The whole process is a half-day project that produces 30-40 chocolates of legitimate gift-quality.

There are three increasing levels of refinement:

1. **Rolled cocoa truffle.** Ganache + cocoa coating. No tempering. The classic French "truffe au chocolat."
2. **Hand-dipped truffle.** Ganache rolled into a ball, dipped in tempered chocolate. Adds a snappy shell.
3. **Moulded bonbon.** Tempered chocolate shell formed in a polycarbonate mould, filled with ganache, sealed. The cleanest finish; covered in [Bars and Bonbons](bars-and-bonbons.md).

This lesson covers the first two. The third is the next step up.

## The Ganache

For approximately 30-40 truffles:

- 300 g dark chocolate, finely chopped (60-70% cocoa)
- 200 g double cream
- 30 g unsalted butter, cubed (optional but improves texture)
- 1-2 tbsp liqueur (optional - cognac, rum, kirsch, Grand Marnier)

### Method

1. Place chopped chocolate in a heatproof bowl.
2. Heat the cream to a simmer (90 C); pour over the chocolate. Wait 60 seconds.
3. Stir gently from the centre outwards until smooth and glossy (see [Ganache](ganache.md) for the technique).
4. Whisk in butter cubes one at a time until incorporated.
5. Stir in liqueur, if using.
6. Pour into a shallow dish, ideally about 2 cm deep. Press cling film directly onto the surface (prevents a skin forming).
7. Refrigerate 4 hours minimum, ideally overnight. The ganache must be firm enough to hold its shape.

## Forming the Truffles

1. **Set up.** Line a baking tray with greaseproof paper. Have a melon baller or small ice cream scoop (15-20 ml capacity) ready. Have a bowl of cocoa powder (or other coating, see below) at hand.
2. **Scoop.** Run the scoop under hot water briefly, dry it; press into the ganache and lift out a ball. The hot scoop helps release the ganache cleanly. Drop the scoop directly onto the tray.
3. **Speed matters.** Work fast - ganache softens the moment it leaves the fridge. Scoop the whole batch onto the tray.
4. **Refrigerate scooped balls.** 15-20 minutes - they firm back up.
5. **Hand-roll.** Wear thin food-safe gloves to prevent skin warmth melting the chocolate. Quickly roll each ball between your palms to smooth into a sphere. Do not over-handle - 2-3 seconds per truffle is enough. Return to the tray.
6. **Refrigerate 15 minutes more.**

## Coating Option 1: Cocoa Powder (Classic)

The traditional French finish. Looks rustic, tastes intensely chocolate.

1. Sift unsweetened cocoa powder into a wide shallow bowl. Use Dutch-processed (alkalised) cocoa for the deepest colour and the least bitter taste.
2. Roll each chilled truffle in the cocoa to coat fully. Tap off excess. (Or place several truffles at a time in the bowl and shake gently to coat.)
3. Lift onto a clean tray.

Optional refinements:
- Mix 1 tbsp icing sugar into the cocoa for the slightly sweet version
- Mix 1 tsp ground cinnamon or 1 tsp instant espresso powder for variations
- Roll first in cocoa, then in icing sugar for the snow-dusted look

## Coating Option 2: Tempered Chocolate Shell

A hand-dipped truffle with a thin chocolate shell. Slightly harder but produces the snap-when-bitten effect.

1. Temper 300 g of chocolate per the [Tempering](tempering.md) lesson. The chocolate should be at the working temperature (31-32 C for dark).
2. Drop a chilled truffle into the tempered chocolate. With a chocolate dipping fork (or two regular forks), turn the truffle to coat completely.
3. Lift, tap the fork gently on the bowl edge to drain excess, then drop onto a sheet of greaseproof paper.
4. Optionally: while the chocolate shell is still wet, sprinkle with chopped nuts, sea salt, gold leaf, finishing chocolate shavings.
5. Allow to set at room temperature, 10-15 minutes. Tempered chocolate sets at 18-22 C; if the room is hot, use a cool corner.

## Coating Option 3: Chopped Nuts

Toasted hazelnuts, almonds, pistachios, or walnuts, finely chopped.

1. Toast 100 g of nuts in a 180 C oven for 8-10 minutes until fragrant; cool fully.
2. Chop finely (a food processor pulses); keep some larger pieces for texture.
3. Roll each ganache ball directly in the nuts.

Hazelnuts are the classic for dark chocolate; pistachios for milk chocolate; almonds for white. Optionally combine: roll first in tempered chocolate shell, then roll in nuts before the shell sets.

## Coating Option 4: White Chocolate Sphere

Roll a dark ganache ball, dip in tempered white chocolate. The contrast is striking.

## Coating Option 5: Striped or Decorative

Roll in tempered chocolate, then drizzle a contrasting chocolate (white on dark, milk on dark, etc.) over the top using a piping bag with a tiny tip or simply a spoon waved across the truffles.

## Flavour Variations

Vary the flavour of the ganache itself:

- **Salted caramel truffle:** Add a swirl of liquid salted caramel through the ganache before it sets. The caramel pockets remain liquid inside the firm ganache.
- **Espresso truffle:** Use the espresso-infused ganache from the [Ganache](ganache.md) lesson.
- **Earl Grey truffle:** Steep loose Earl Grey in the cream first.
- **Cardamom-orange:** Steep crushed cardamom pods plus orange zest in the cream.
- **Chilli:** Steep dried chilli flakes in the cream; the heat develops in the back of the throat as you eat.
- **Mint:** Crushed fresh mint steeped in the cream; very bright.
- **Rum-and-raisin:** Soak raisins in dark rum for an hour; fold the drained raisins into the ganache after pouring; finely chop or leave whole.

## Storage

Hand-rolled cocoa-coated truffles keep 1 week refrigerated. Cold-storage truffles need to come up to room temperature 20 minutes before serving to restore their proper texture.

Tempered-shell truffles keep 2-3 weeks at room temperature in a cool dark spot, or refrigerated. The shell protects the ganache from oxygen.

Boxed for gifts, they keep up to 2 weeks at room temperature. Pack in a sturdy box with paper or plastic dividers between layers.
