# Fiery Citrus Salsa

![Fiery citrus salsa](resources/citrus-salsa.jpg)

*This unusual salsa, which combines fruit with chillies, makes a fantastic marinade for shellfish and is also delicious drizzled over barbecue-cooked meat.*

**Serves:** 4

**Prep Time:** 15 minutes

**Cook Time:** 0 minutes

## Overview
Fiery citrus salsa is the building block for a bright unusual fruit-and-chilli sauce that doubles as a marinade for prawns or scallops, a drizzle over grilled meats, or a dipping sauce for carnitas tacos: orange segments and green apple blitzed in a food processor with raw fresh red chilli, garlic and mint leaves, finished with a squeeze of lemon juice. It's not a Mexican-style chopped tomato-and-onion salsa; it's a smooth fruit-based sauce that lives in its own category, refreshing and unexpectedly complex despite the short ingredient list. Three steps in 15 minutes from cupboard to plate. Two technique points lift it. First, segment the orange properly. Slice the base off so it stands upright, cut away the peel and pith with a sharp knife, then hold the orange over a bowl to catch the juices and cut down between each membrane to release clean segments into the bowl; squeeze the remaining membrane to capture every drop of juice. Skipping this and using whole orange chunks (with membrane) gives you a bitter sauce because the pith carries the bitterness. Second, peel the apple. Green apple skin can be tough in the blender and gives a slightly tannic edge that competes with the citrus brightness; peeled apple blitzes smooth. Halve the chillies and decide on heat (deseed for milder, seeds in for fiery). Tip the orange segments and juice, peeled cored apple, chilli, garlic and mint leaves into a food processor and blitz till smooth. With the motor still running, pour in the lemon juice; the acid stops the apple oxidising and goes brown. Season with salt and pepper, taste, and serve immediately. The flavours fade within hours and the sauce doesn't keep beyond a day, so make it to order.

## Ingredients

### Fruits
- 1 orange
- 1 green apple

### Chilli & aromatics
- 2 fresh red chillies
- 1 garlic clove
- 8 fresh mint leaves

### Finishing
- 1 lemon (juice)
- salt
- pepper

## Method

### Stage 1 - Prepare orange
1. Slice the base off the orange so that it will stand firmly on a chopping board.
1. Using a sharp knife, remove the peel and pith in segments.
1. Holding the orange over a bowl to catch the juices, cut away the segments from the membrane, letting them fall into the bowl.
1. Squeeze any juice from the remaining membrane into the bowl.

### Stage 2 - Prepare apple & chilli
1. Peel, quarter and core the apple and put it into a food processor.
1. Cut the chillies in half, and if necessary discard the seeds for milder heat.

### Stage 3 - Blend
1. Add the chillies to the food processor with the orange segments and juice, garlic and the mint.
1. Process until smooth.

### Stage 4 - Finish
1. With the motor still running, pour in the lemon juice.
1. Season to taste with salt and pepper.
1. Serve immediately.

## Notes
- **Orange segments:** Cutting away membrane removes bitterness and improves texture.
- **Chilli seeds:** Remove entirely for milder result or leave for more heat.
- **Immediate service:** Flavours brighten when freshly made; does not keep well.

## Serving
Serve as a marinade for shellfish (scallops, prawns) or drizzle over barbecue-cooked meat. Also excellent as condiment for carnitas tacos or grilled fish.

## Storage
- Keeps refrigerated for 1 day in an airtight container.
- Do not freeze; texture and flavour both deteriorate significantly.
- Best served fresh; flavours fade within hours of preparation.
