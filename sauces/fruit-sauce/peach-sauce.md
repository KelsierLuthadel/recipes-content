# Peach Sauce

![Peach sauce](resources/peach-sauce.png)

*This sauce goes particularly well with roasted pigeon or young duckling.*

**Serves:** 4

**Prep Time:** 15 minutes

**Cook Time:** 45 minutes

## Overview
Peach sauce is the building block for game birds and tender duck: a glossy mahogany sauce of caramelised peaches deglazed with Cognac, red wine vinegar and Burgundy red wine, then reduced with veal stock, fennel seed and clove and mounted with cold butter at the end into silky richness. The combination of fruit, wine and game-stock reduction is the signature European pairing for roasted pigeon, young duck or pheasant; the fruit sweetness balances the gaminess of the bird, and the wine and stock build the savoury structure that lets the peach come through as more than just sweetness. Use very ripe fragrant peaches; underripe ones have no caramel sweetness to give and the sauce comes out thin. Peel by scoring around the equator, dropping into boiling water for 20 to 30 seconds, then iced water; pull off the skins, stone and cube. Melt butter in a wide pan, add caster sugar and stir till lightly caramelised, then add the peaches and cook on increased heat, stirring constantly, till they almost collapse into a syrupy purée. Splash in Cognac and let it bubble, then red wine vinegar for another minute, then 100 ml of red Burgundy with the clove and fennel seeds; reduce gently for 10 minutes, skimming. Pour in the veal stock and simmer 30 minutes till the sauce reduces and thickens enough to coat a spoon. Strain into a clean pan to remove the spices and any peach fibre, then off the heat (or on the very lowest) whisk in cold cubed butter a piece at a time to mount the sauce into a glossy silky emulsion. Season and serve straight away over pan-fried pigeon breast, duck or pheasant.

## Ingredients

### Fruit
- 2 very ripe peaches

### Aromatics & base
- 30 grams butter
- 30 grams caster sugar

### Liquid
- 20 ml cognac
- 3 tablespoons red wine vinegar
- 100 ml red wine (preferably Burgundy)
- 300 ml veal stock

### Spices & finishing
- 1 clove
- 2 teaspoons fennel seeds
- 40 grams butter (chilled and diced)
- salt
- pepper

## Method

### Stage 1 - Prepare peaches
1. To peel the peaches, lightly score around the middle, then immerse in boiling water until the skin starts to lift. 
1. Refresh in iced water, then peel, stone and cut into cubes.

### Stage 2 - Caramelize & build
1. Melt the butter in a frying pan, add the sugar and stir until lightly caramellised. 
1. Add the peaches, increase the heat and cook, stirring continuously, until almost collapsed into a purée. 
1. Add the Cognac, bubble briefly, then add the wine vinegar and bubble for 1 minute. 

### Stage 3 - Reduce sauce
1. Add the red wine and spices and cook gently for 10 minutes, skimming as necessary.
1. Pour in the veal stock and simmer for 30 minutes, or until reduced and thickened. 

### Stage 4 - Finish
1. Strain into a clean pan and whisk in the butter, a little at a time. 
1. Season and serve.

## Notes
- **Peach ripeness:** Use fragrant, ripe peaches; they should yield slightly to pressure. Overripe fruit becomes mushy.
- **Wine reduction:** This step is crucial; let the wine fully reduce to concentrate flavours and remove excessive acidity.
- **Mounting butter:** Add cold butter in small pieces to create silky sauce; whisk vigorously to emulsify.

## Serving
- Serve warm as an accompaniment to roasted pigeon, young duck, pheasant, or other game birds.

## Storage
- Best eaten immediately after preparation.
- Keeps refrigerated for 1-2 days; reheat gently over low heat, whisking constantly.
- Does not freeze well due to the butter emulsion breaking down.
