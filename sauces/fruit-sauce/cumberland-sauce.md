# Cumberland Sauce

![Cumberland sauce](resources/cumberland-sauce.png)

*This is a lovely accompaniment to cold meats and terrines. Serve it cold with galantines and ballotines, pork pies or any cold poultry and game. It tastes even better the day after it is made.*

**Serves:** 4

**Prep Time:** 15 minutes

**Cook Time:** 25 minutes

## Overview
Cumberland sauce is the building block for cold game and traditional British charcuterie plates: a glossy ruby-coloured cold sauce of redcurrant jelly, ruby port, orange juice, blanched lemon zest, shallot, Worcestershire and crushed peppercorns, served alongside galantines, ballotines, pork pies, cold roast goose, terrines and pâtés. The character is sharp and sweet at the same time, the kind of sauce designed to cut through cold fatty meats and lift them. Critically this is one of those sauces that improves overnight; the flavours marry and deepen, so always make it a day ahead if you can. Start with a vinegar reduction: chopped shallot, red wine vinegar and crushed white peppercorns bubbled hard in a small pan till reduced by two-thirds; this concentrates the acidity and builds the base sharpness. Add 100 ml of veal stock, 50 ml of ruby port, two tablespoons of redcurrant jelly, a teaspoon of Worcestershire and the juice of an orange, bring to the boil, then simmer gently for 20 minutes till the sauce takes on a glossy mahogany colour and coats a spoon. Strain through a fine sieve to remove the shallot and peppercorn bits (you want a smooth pour, not a chunky relish), cool, then chill in the fridge. Just before serving, blanch finely pared lemon zest in boiling water for a minute to remove the bitter edge, drain, cut into fine julienne and stir through; the bright lemon strands lift the rich glossy sauce visually and on the palate. Serve straight from the fridge alongside cold game terrines, pork pies and ham, or on the cheese board.

## Ingredients

### Base
- 1 shallot (medium, finely chopped)
- 4 tablespoons red wine vinegar
- 12 white peppercorns (crushed)
- 100 ml Veal stock
- 50 ml ruby port

### Finishing
- 2 tablespoons redcurrant jelly
- 1 teaspoon Worcestershire sauce
- one orange (juice)
- one lemon (finely pared, zest)
- salt

## Method

### Stage 1 - Reduce vinegar
1. Combine the shallot, wine vinegar and crushed peppercorns in a small saucepan. 
1. Place over a high heat and let bubble to reduce by two-thirds.

### Stage 2 - Build sauce
1. Add the veal stock, port, redcurrant jelly, Worcestershire sauce and orange juice. 
1. Quickly bring to the boil, then lower the heat and simmer gently for 20 minutes. 
1. Season with salt to taste.

### Stage 3 - Finish and chill
1. Pass the sauce through a fine-meshed conical sieve into a bowl. 
1. Set aside to cool, then cover and chill in the refrigerator.
1. Blanch the lemon zest in boiling water for 1 minute, then drain and cut into fine julienne. 
1. Stir into the sauce just before serving.

## Notes
- **Make ahead:** Prepare this sauce 1 day ahead; flavours develop and meld beautifully overnight.
- **Redcurrant jelly:** Essential for authentic taste; it provides both sweetness and pectin body to the sauce.
- **Lemon zest:** Blanching removes bitterness; add it just before serving to maintain brightness and texture.

## Serving
Serve chilled alongside cold cuts, terrines, pâtés, and cold roasted game. Also excellent with cheese selections on a charcuterie board.

## Storage
- Keeps refrigerated for 5-7 days in an airtight container.
- Freezes well for up to 3 months (freeze without lemon zest; add when thawed).
- Best eaten chilled, directly from the refrigerator.
