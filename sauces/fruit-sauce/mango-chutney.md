# Mango Chutney

![Mango chutney](resources/mango-chutney.jpg)

*This lightly spiced, sweet chutney provides a lovely contrast to a spicy meal and is perhaps one of the best known and loved Indian chutneys.*

**Serves:** 8 (makes 500 ml preserve)

**Prep Time:** 20 minutes

**Cook Time:** 60 minutes

## Overview
Mango chutney is the building block for the sweet fruity preserve that turns up next to almost every Indian meal in Britain: ripe mango slowly cooked down with white wine vinegar, sugar and a serious lineup of whole and ground spices (cloves, star anise, cinnamon sticks, peppercorns, nigella seeds, ginger, garlic, chilli powder) into a glossy jam-like preserve you spoon onto a poppadom, alongside a curry, or onto a cheese board next to a mature cheddar. Two ingredient choices set the quality. Use mangoes that are ripe but still firm; an overripe mango breaks down into mush during the cook and the chutney loses its texture, while an underripe one stays fibrous and acidic. And the nigella seeds (the small black onion-scented seeds) are non-negotiable for authentic flavour; they're tiny but distinctive, and a mango chutney without them tastes flat. Heat sunflower oil in a heavy pan over medium heat, drop in the grated ginger, crushed garlic, whole cloves, star anise, cinnamon sticks, peppercorns, nigella seeds and chilli powder, and stir-fry for one or two minutes till the spices release their oils and the kitchen smells deeply fragrant. Tip in the chopped mango, white wine vinegar and caster sugar, bring to the boil, then drop to low and let it bubble away for 45 minutes till the mixture turns jam-thick and a wooden spoon drawn through it leaves a clear channel on the bottom of the pan. Taste, adjust salt, then ladle hot into sterilised glass jars and seal. Store refrigerated for up to 2 months; the flavour actually improves over the first few days as the spices and mango marry, so make it ahead.

## Ingredients

### Aromatics & spices
- 1 tablespoon sunflower oil
- 1 teaspoon ginger (finely grated)
- 1 garlic clove (crushed)
- 5 cloves
- 1 star anise
- 2 cinnamon sticks
- 5 black peppercorns
- 2 tablespoons nigella seeds
- ½ teaspoon mild chilli powder

### Fruit & binding
- 800 grams ripe but firm mango flesh (chopped)
- 400 ml white wine vinegar
- 270 grams caster sugar
- sea salt

## Method

### Stage 1 - Toast spices
1. Heat the sunflower oil in a saucepan over a medium heat.
1. Add the ginger, garlic, cloves, star anise, cinnamon, peppercorns, nigella seeds and chilli powder and stir-fry for 1-2 minutes.

### Stage 2 - Cook chutney
1. Add the mango, vinegar and sugar and bring to the boil.
1. Reduce the heat to low and cook for 45 minutes, or until the mixture is jam-like.

### Stage 3 - Jar and store
1. Season with sea salt to taste and pour into hot sterilized jam jars.
1. Seal and leave to cool before storing in the refrigerator for up to 2 months.

## Notes
- **Mango selection:** Use fruit that is ripe but still firm; overripe mangoes will become too soft and lose structure.
- **Nigella seeds:** These tiny, onion-scented seeds are essential to authentic mango chutney; they add subtle complexity.
- **Spices:** Toast the whole spices briefly to release their oils; this amplifies flavour significantly.

## Serving
Serve alongside curries, with roasted game and charcuterie, or as part of a cheese board. Also excellent with pâté and cold roasted meats.

## Storage
- Keeps refrigerated for up to 2 months in sealed glass jars.
- Does not freeze well due to sugar content and texture changes.
- Best eaten after 2-3 days once flavours have developed; improves over time.
