# Aurora Sauce

![Aurora Sauce](resources/aurora-sauce.jpg)

*A variation of béchamel that goes well with poached eggs, pasta, cauliflower or grilled turkey escalope.*

**Serves:** 6

**Prep Time:** 10 minutes

**Cook Time:** 15 minutes

## Overview
Sauce Aurore is the building block for the gentle pale-pink sauce that drapes over poached eggs, cauliflower gratins, butter-tossed pasta or grilled turkey escalopes: a derived sauce built on a base of béchamel enriched with double cream, then coloured and lightly flavoured with tomato coulis, and finished with cold butter mounted at the end. The name comes from the dawn-pink colour (aurore is the French word for dawn) and the technique is the classic French derived-sauce approach: take a mother sauce (béchamel here), then build new character on top through enrichment and a flavouring agent. The tomato coulis does both jobs at once, adding the pale pink hue and the subtle fruit-savoury background without ever announcing itself as tomato. Use a freshly made béchamel for the best result; prepared sauce works in a pinch but the texture is less refined. Combine the béchamel and double cream in a saucepan and bring to the boil over slow heat, whisking constantly. Bubble gently for 5 minutes, whisking continuously so the sauce doesn't catch on the bottom. Add the tomato coulis and bring back to the boil; cook another 5 minutes stirring continuously to integrate the coulis into the béchamel evenly. Off the heat, whisk in cold cubed butter a piece at a time to mount the sauce into a glossy emulsion and to add the final layer of body. Season with salt and pepper, then pass through a fine-meshed conical sieve into a serving jug to ensure a silky pour without any small lumps from the béchamel base. Serve immediately. Use over softly poached eggs as a vegetable course, over steamed cauliflower as a quick gratin, or as a pasta sauce.

## Ingredients

### Base
- 300 ml [Béchamel Sauce](./bechamel-sauce.md)
- 100 ml double cream

### Colourant & finishing
- 120 ml [Tomato Coulis](../../coulis/vegetable/tomato-coulis.md)
- 20 grams butter (chilled and diced)
- salt
- pepper

## Method

### Stage 1 - Heat béchamel
1. Combine the béchamel sauce and double cream in a saucepan and bring to the boil over a slow heat, stirring with a whisk. 
1. Let the sauce bubble for 5 minutes, whisking continuously, then add the tomato coulis. 

### Stage 2 - Add coulis
1. Bring back to the boil and cook for another 5 minutes, stirring continuously.

### Stage 3 - Finish
1. Turn off the heat and whisk in the butter, a little at a time. 
1. Season the sauce and pass through a fine-meshed conical sieve and serve.

## Notes
- **Béchamel quality:** Use freshly made béchamel for best results; prepared sauce works but is less refined.
- **Coulis colour:** The tomato coulis gives the pale pink hue distinctive to this sauce; intensity varies by coulis quality.
- **Straining:** Final sieving creates silky texture; do not omit for superior finish.

## Serving
Serve with poached eggs, cooked cauliflower, butter pasta, grilled turkey escalope, and light white meats.

## Storage
- Keeps refrigerated for 2 days in an airtight container.
- Freezes well for up to 1 month.
- Best eaten warm; reheat gently, stirring frequently to prevent lumping.
