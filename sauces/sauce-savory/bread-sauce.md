# Bread Sauce

![Bread sauce](resources/bread-sauce.jpg)

*The perfect traditional sauce to accompany a roast dinner.*

**Serves:** 4

**Prep Time:** 5 minutes

**Cook Time:** 55 minutes

## Overview
Bread sauce is the building block of the British roast Christmas dinner, the soft pale clove-scented sauce that gets dolloped alongside roast turkey, goose, chicken, pheasant or partridge. It's deceptively simple: cubed white bread dissolved in clove-onion-infused milk, finished with double cream and seasoned with salt and white pepper. The two technique points that separate a good bread sauce from a sad one are the infusion and the cooking time. Stud a whole peeled onion with two whole cloves (this is the traditional way; loose cloves rolling around the pan are a serving hazard), drop it into the milk in a small pan with finely chopped onion that's been gently sweated in butter, then bring to a bare simmer and infuse 20 minutes till the milk takes on the warm spice and onion sweetness. Add the cubed crustless white bread and bring back to the boil, drop to low and cook gently for 30 minutes stirring occasionally; this long slow cook is what fully dissolves the bread into the sauce so it goes soft and creamy rather than lumpy. Use white bread, not wholemeal; white bread melts into the milk smoothly, while wholemeal stays grainy and never collapses properly. At the end of the cook, fish out the clove-studded onion and discard, stir in the double cream and bubble gently another 5 minutes, whisking delicately so the sauce stays smooth. Season with salt and white pepper. The sauce should sit somewhere between thick gravy and porridge in consistency; loosen with a splash of milk if it's too thick after sitting. Serve warm in a jug alongside the roasted bird.

## Ingredients

### Base & aromatics
- 20 grams butter
- 60 grams onion (chopped)
- 400 ml milk
- 1 onion (peeled)
- 2 cloves

### Bread & finishing
- 80 grams white bread (crusts removed and cut into cubes)
- 50 ml double cream
- 1 pinch salt and pepper

## Method

### Stage 1 - Infuse milk
1. Insert the cloves in to the onion. Melt the butter in a small saucepan, and add the chopped onions and sweat gently for 1 minute. 
1. Pour in the milk and add the clove-studded onion and bring to a bare simmer. 
1. Cook gently, stirring occasionally, for 20 minutes.

### Stage 2 - Add bread
1. Stir in the bread cubes and bring to the boil. 
1. Lower the heat and cook the sauce gently for 30 minutes, stirring occasionally with a wooden spoon.

### Stage 3 - Finish
1. Remove the studded onion, add the cream and let the sauce bubble gently for 5 minutes, whisking delicately. 
1. Season with salt and pepper to taste and serve.

## Notes
- **Bread selection:** Use white bread, not wholemeal; white bread dissolves smoothly while wholemeal remains grainy.
- **Cooking time:** Long, gentle cooking allows bread to fully dissolve; rushing results in a lumpy sauce.
- **Onion removal:** The clove-studded onion must be removed before serving to prevent unexpected cloves in diners' mouths.

## Serving
- Serve warm alongside roasted chicken, turkey, gamebirds, and other roasted poultry.

## Storage
- Keeps refrigerated for 2 days in an airtight container.
- Freezes well for up to 1 month.
- Best eaten warm; reheat gently over low heat, stirring frequently to prevent sticking.
