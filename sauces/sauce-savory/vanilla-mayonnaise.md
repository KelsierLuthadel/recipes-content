# Vanilla Mayonnaise

![Vanilla mayonnaise](resources/vanilla-mayonnaise.jpg)

*This is a light mayonnaise, due to the fact that it uses the egg whites as well as the yolk. It works especially well with liquorice poached salmon.*

**Serves:** Makes 400ml

**Prep Time:** 10 minutes

**Cook Time:** 0 minutes

## Overview
Vanilla mayonnaise is the building block for an unexpected delicate refinement of classic mayonnaise designed specifically for poached salmon (especially the liquorice-poached version) and other delicate fish: vanilla seeds scraped from the pod and whisked into a lighter-than-usual mayonnaise made with both whole egg and yolk rather than yolks alone, with the proportion of egg white giving the sauce its lifted floaty texture. The vanilla doesn't dominate; it sits underneath the lemony Dijon base as a faint floral warmth that softens the sharpness of the vinegar and rounds the sauce out into something more luxurious than a regular mayonnaise. Two ingredient choices set this version apart. The whole egg plus extra yolk combination lightens the mayonnaise considerably compared to a yolk-only version; the egg white adds bulk and bounce without the heaviness of straight yolks. And the vanilla seeds, scraped fresh from two split pods, not vanilla extract; the extract turns bitter when whisked into oil-based sauces, while the seeds disperse cleanly and carry a softer flavour. Combine the egg yolk, whole egg, Dijon mustard, salt and scraped vanilla seeds in a bowl and whisk thoroughly to disperse. Slowly add the groundnut oil a little at a time, whisking constantly; this initial slow addition is what builds the emulsion. Once the mayonnaise begins to thicken visibly, you can pour the rest of the oil in a steady stream while whisking. When all the oil is incorporated and the sauce is thick and glossy, stir in the white wine vinegar and check the seasoning. Use immediately; the lighter texture from the whole egg means the sauce holds its shape less well than classical mayonnaise, so it's at its best made fresh. Pair with cold or warm poached salmon, sea bream or any other delicate white fish.


## Ingredients

### Base & emulsifier
- 1 egg yolk (large)
- 1 whole large egg
- 15 grams Dijon mustard
- seeds from 2 vanilla pods

### Oil & acid
- 350 grams groundnut oil
- 20 grams white wine vinegar

### Seasoning
- 5 grams salt

## Method

### Stage 1 - Combine base ingredients
1. Combine the egg yolk, whole egg, mustard, salt and vanilla seeds in a bowl.
1. Using a hand whisk, mix the ingredients together thoroughly.

### Stage 2 - Initial emulsification
1. Slowly add in the oil, a little at a time, whisking constantly.

### Stage 3 - Steady incorporation
1. When the mayonnaise begins to emulsify, pour in the oil in a steady stream, making sure you keep whisking.

### Stage 4 - Finish with acid
1. When the mayonnaise has emulsified, stir in the vinegar.
1. Check seasoning and adjust if necessary.

## Notes
- **Whole egg advantage:** The egg white provides body and reduces richness compared to yolk-only mayonnaise.
- **Vanilla seeds:** Essential for subtle flavour; vanilla extract is too strong and leaves bitter aftertaste.
- **Oil temperature:** Cold oil emulsifies best; room temperature oil creates separated, greasy result.

## Serving
- Serve with poached salmon (especially liquorice-poached), other delicate white fish, or cold chicken. The vanilla adds sophistication without overwhelming delicate flavours.

## Storage
- Keeps refrigerated for 2 days in an airtight container.
- Does not freeze; emulsion breaks upon thawing.
- Best when made fresh; flavour and texture degrade with time.
