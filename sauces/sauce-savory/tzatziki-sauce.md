# Tzatziki Sauce

![Tzatziki sauce](resources/tzatziki-sauce.png)

*A Greek dip made from yogurt, chopped cucumber, and mint.*

**Serves:** 8

**Prep Time:** 10 minutes

**Cook Time:** 0 minutes

## Overview
Tzatziki is the building block of Greek mezze, the cool yogurt-and-cucumber sauce that turns up on every taverna table next to grilled lamb skewers, kalamari, falafel, warm pita and crudités. It's a four-ingredient assembly (Greek yogurt, finely grated cucumber, crushed garlic, lemon juice) that lifts mid-summer cooking with its sharp clean refreshing character; the cucumber gives a quiet crunch, the garlic gives bite that mellows with rest, the lemon adds brightness, and the yogurt holds the whole thing together. Two technique points matter, though this recipe is short. First, use Greek-style strained yogurt, not regular plain yogurt; the thick consistency lets the sauce cling to whatever you spoon it over, where thin yogurt watered down by the cucumber turns into a soup. If only thin yogurt is available, strain it through muslin for 30 minutes first. Second, the rest in the fridge is essential. Mix the yogurt, lemon juice and crushed garlic in a bowl, fold in the finely grated cucumber gently so the cucumber pieces stay intact rather than going to mush, then chill for at least 30 minutes (longer is better; an hour is ideal). During that rest, the raw garlic's aggressive bite mellows into a softer rounder presence, and the cucumber juices marry with the yogurt to give the sauce a unified character rather than feeling like discrete ingredients in a bowl. Decorate with crossed stalks of chives just before serving; chill till the moment of service. Serve cold alongside grilled meats, warm pita, falafel, charred aubergine or any Mediterranean or Levantine mezze plate. Keeps three days refrigerated; the garlic strengthens over time, so by day three the sauce tastes considerably more assertive than on day one.

## Ingredients

### Base
- 400 ml Greek yoghurt
- 1 clove garlic (crushed)

### Vegetables & herbs
- 8 cm cucumber (finely grated)
- 2 stalks chives

### Finishing
- 1 tablespoon lemon juice

## Method

### Stage 1 - Combine base
1. Mix together the yoghurt, lemon juice and garlic in a bowl.

### Stage 2 - Add cucumber
1. Fold in the grated cucumber gently, ensuring even distribution.

### Stage 3 - Chill
1. Refrigerate for at least 30 minutes before serving to allow flavours to meld.

### Stage 4 - Serve
1. Decorate with crossed stalks of the chives.
1. Serve chilled.

## Notes
- **Cucumber:** Grate and use immediately; excess moisture can dilute sauce.
- **Garlic:** Slightly overwhelming when raw; chilling mellows its intensity.
- **Chilling time:** Essential for flavour development; do not skip.

## Serving
Serve as dip with warm Turkish bread, crudités, grilled vegetables, or lamb dishes. Perfect accompaniment for Greek and Mediterranean meals.

## Storage
- Keeps refrigerated for 3 days in an airtight container.
- Does not freeze well; yogurt texture deteriorates upon thawing.
- Flavour improves slightly in first 24 hours as garlic mellows.
