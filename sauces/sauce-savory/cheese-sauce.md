# Cheese Sauce

![Cheese sauce](resources/cheese-sauce.png)

*A rich velvety cheese sauce that works well with macaroni or cauliflower*

**Serves:** 4 (makes 600 ml)

**Prep Time:** 5 minutes

**Cook Time:** 25 minutes

## Overview
Cheese sauce is the building block for macaroni cheese, cauliflower cheese and any pasta gratin where you want a deeply savoury silky cheese pour rather than just a flour-thickened béchamel: a reduction of dry white wine and chicken stock infused with Gruyère cheese rind, then thickened with finely grated Gruyère tossed with cornflour, finished with a knob of butter and an optional drizzle of truffle oil. This version skips the classical béchamel base entirely (no flour roux, no milk) and builds the body from the stock reduction plus cornflour-coated cheese; the result is far more cheese-forward and less starchy than a mornay-style cheese sauce. Two technique points lift the sauce. First, infuse the chicken stock with cheese rind. If you have a leftover Gruyère rind, drop it into the warm stock for 20 minutes to extract every drop of cheese flavour; this layer of savoury depth is what makes the sauce taste like proper cheese rather than just melted Gruyère. Reduce 300 ml of dry white wine over high heat to a mere 30 ml; this concentrates the wine to a sticky syrupy base. Warm the chicken stock with the cheese rind separately, infuse 20 minutes, then strain into the reduced wine. Toss the grated Gruyère with cornflour (the cornflour stops the cheese clumping and going stringy when it hits the warm liquid, and gives the sauce its smooth gloss), then stir into the warm wine-stock mixture till the cheese melts into the liquid and the sauce thickens to a silky pour. Let cool slightly, then stir in a knob of butter for added gloss. Serve immediately over hot macaroni or steamed cauliflower; a few drops of truffle oil at the table are restaurant-style.

## Ingredients

### Wine reduction
- 300 ml dry white wine
- 300 ml chicken stock

### Cheese & binder
- 120 grams gruyere cheese (grated)
- 10 grams cornflour

### Finishing
- 1 knob butter
- truffle oil (for decoration)
- cheese rind

## Method

### Stage 1 - Reduce wine
1. In a small saucepan, reduce the white wine over a high heat to 30 ml.

### Stage 2 - Infuse stock
1. Warm the chicken stock in a pan, and add the cheese rind.
1. Leave to infuse for 20 minutes.

### Stage 3 - Combine & add cheese
1. Add the warm chicken stock to the reduced wine.
1. Mix the grated cheese with the cornflour and add to the pan.
1. Stir until the cheese has been incorporated into the sauce, then leave to cool slightly.

### Stage 4 - Finish
1. Add the butter and stir until it has melted.
1. Serve with optional truffle oil drizzle for elegance.

## Notes
- **Cheese rind:** Adds subtle flavour depth; use aged Gruyère rind for best results.
- **Wine reduction:** Concentrating the wine is essential for balancing the cheese richness.
- **Cornflour:** Prevents greasiness and creates smooth emulsion; do not omit.

## Serving
- Serve with macaroni, cooked cauliflower, broccoli, and other vegetables. Also excellent over pasta gratins.

## Storage
- Keeps refrigerated for 2 days in an airtight container.
- Freezes well for up to 1 month.
- Best eaten warm; reheat gently, stirring frequently.
