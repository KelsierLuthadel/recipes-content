# Butterscotch Sauce

![Butterscotch sauce](resources/butterscotch.png)

*Serve this rich sauce with vanilla ice cream or an apple dessert such as apple Charlotte.*

**Serves:** 8

**Prep Time:** 5 minutes

**Cook Time:** 10 minutes

## Overview
Butterscotch sauce is the building block for the rich golden caramel-butter sauce that drapes over vanilla ice cream, apple Charlotte, sticky toffee pudding, warm chocolate brownies and anything that wants a deeper richer hit than plain caramel: a base of single cream, glucose-rich sirop à sorbet and caster sugar cooked together to a pale hazelnut colour, scented with fresh vanilla seeds, then finished with diced unsalted butter swirled in at the end. The character is distinct from a pure caramel sauce because the cream goes in at the start rather than being added to cooked caramel; this means the sugars caramelise gently in the cream-and-syrup mixture rather than dry-caramelising first, which gives a softer rounder more deeply buttery flavour than the sharper cleaner depth of a classic caramel. The glucose in the sirop à sorbet stops the sugar crystallising during cooking and gives the sauce its glossy smooth finish. Split the vanilla pod lengthways and scrape out the seeds with the tip of a knife. Pour the single cream into a heavy-based saucepan, add the sirop à sorbet, caster sugar and vanilla seeds. Bring slowly to the boil over medium heat stirring continuously with a small whisk so the sugar dissolves cleanly into the cream. Once it boils, let the mixture bubble gently while whisking constantly till it darkens to the colour of pale hazelnuts; watch carefully because it darkens quickly and overcooking pushes it from butterscotch into burnt-bitter territory. Stir in the diced butter a piece at a time until completely smooth, glossy and amalgamated. Serve piping hot over scoops of vanilla ice cream or warm puddings. Keeps five days refrigerated; reheat gently over low heat (never boil after the butter goes in or it splits).

## Ingredients

### Base
- 400 ml single cream
- 120 ml [Syrup for Sorbet (Sirop à Sorbet)](../../base-ingredients/syrup/sirop-a-sorbet.md) (heavy)
- 75 grams caster sugar

### Flavoring & enrichment
- 1 vanilla pod
- 60 grams unsalted butter (diced)

## Method

### Stage 1 - Prepare vanilla
1. Split the vanilla pod length-ways and scrape out the seeds with the tip of a knife.

### Stage 2 - Combine base
1. Pour the cream into a heavy-based saucepan and add the sugar syrup and sugar.
1. Add the vanilla seeds to the pan.

### Stage 3 - Cook to proper color
1. Slowly bring to the boil, stirring continuously.
1. Let the mixture bubble gently, stirring continuously with a small whisk, until it is the colour of pale hazelnuts.

### Stage 4 - Finish with butter
1. Stir in the butter, a little at a time, until completely amalgamated.
1. Serve piping hot.

## Notes
- **Sirop a sorbet:** Heavy glucose syrup prevents sugar crystallization; essential for smooth sauce.
- **Color stage:** Pale hazelnut color indicates proper caramelization; monitor carefully as it darkens quickly.
- **Butter incorporation:** Whisk thoroughly to ensure smooth, glossy final sauce.

## Serving
Serve hot with vanilla ice cream, apple Charlotte, or other warm desserts. Can be poured over warm soufflés or puddings.

## Storage
- Keeps refrigerated for 5 days in an airtight container.
- Reheat gently over low heat, stirring frequently; do not boil.
- Can be frozen for up to 1 month; thaw slowly at room temperature.
