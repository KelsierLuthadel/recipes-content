# Lemon Custard

![Lemon custard](resources/lemon-custard.png)

*Served with red fruits, this sharp lemony custard makes the perfect summer dessert. To accentuate the flavour, sprinkle on some shreds of candied lemon zest just before serving.*

**Serves:** 4

**Prep Time:** 10 minutes

**Cook Time:** 10 minutes

## Overview
Lemon custard is the building block for a tart pourable summer sauce designed to drape over fresh red fruits (raspberries, strawberries, redcurrants), poached fruit and light desserts: an egg-yolk custard built with fresh lemon juice rather than milk, with single cream and caster sugar balancing the sharp acidity, finished cool with optional candied lemon zest scattered at the top. It's the unusual cousin of crème anglaise; instead of milk, the dairy comes from cream and the structural liquid is half lemon juice, which gives the sauce its bright tart character and its slightly tighter set (the acid in the lemon juice helps thicken the custard alongside the egg yolks). Use fresh lemon juice from six lemons; bottled juice is acidic but flat-tasting, and the bright citrus oils that lift the sauce are missing. Combine the single cream, 60 g of the sugar and the lemon juice in a saucepan and bring slowly to the boil over low heat; the dairy may look slightly curdled as the acid hits the cream, but this evens out once it's whisked with the eggs. Separately, whisk the six egg yolks with the remaining 80 g of sugar till pale and ribboned. Pour the boiling cream-and-lemon mixture onto the egg yolks in a thin steady stream while whisking constantly to temper; tip back into the saucepan and cook very gently for 2 minutes, stirring continuously with a wooden spoon. Don't let it boil or the yolks scramble. Strain through a fine-meshed conical sieve into a chilled bowl, cool stirring occasionally so a skin doesn't form, then cover and refrigerate. Serve cold over fresh berries with shreds of candied lemon zest on top for visual elegance.

## Ingredients

### Liquid base
- 240 ml single cream
- 240 ml lemon juice (about 6 lemons)

### Sweetening
- 140 grams caster sugar (plus extra if the lemons are very tart)

### Custard
- 6 egg yolks

## Method

### Stage 1 - Prepare liquid
1. Combine the cream, 60 grams of sugar and the lemon juice in a saucepan.
1. Slowly bring to the boil over a low heat.

### Stage 2 - Temper egg yolks
1. Meanwhile, in a bowl, whisk the egg yolks with the remaining 80 grams of sugar to a ribbon consistency.
1. Pour the boiling cream on to the egg mixture, whisking continuously.

### Stage 3 - Cook custard
1. Pour the custard back into the saucepan.
1. Cook very gently for 2 minutes, stirring continuously with a wooden spoon or spatula; don't let it boil or it will curdle.

### Stage 4 - Strain & cool
1. Pass the custard through a fine-meshed conical sieve into a chilled bowl.
1. Leave to cool, stirring occasionally to prevent a skin from forming.
1. When the custard is cold, cover the bowl with cling film and refrigerate until ready to serve.

## Notes
- **Fresh lemon juice:** Essential; bottled lemon juice lacks fresh acidity and flavor.
- **Tart lemons:** Lemon acidity varies; taste and adjust sugar upward if needed.
- **Gentle cooking:** Prevents curdling; high heat scrambles egg yolks.

## Serving
- Serve chilled with fresh red fruits (berries, currants, raspberries). Add candied lemon zest shreds just before serving for visual elegance and enhanced flavour.

## Storage
- Keeps refrigerated for 3 days in an airtight container.
- Do not freeze; custard texture becomes granular upon thawing.
- Flavour is best within 24 hours of making.
