# Thermidor Sauce

![Thermidor sauce](resources/thermidor.png)

*This famous companion to lobster thermidor works with most crustaceans. It tastes wonderful mixed with crab meat and served au gratin.*

**Serves:** 6

**Prep Time:** 10 minutes

**Cook Time:** 30 minutes

## Overview
Sauce Thermidor is the building block for the famous Lobster Thermidor and most crustacean au-gratin dishes: a tangy mustard-warm cream sauce built from a white wine and fish stock reduction, béchamel, cream, both Dijon and English mustards, finished with cold butter mounted off the heat and a scatter of fresh tarragon. The signature is the dual mustard. Dijon brings the rounded smooth heat, English mustard powder brings the sharper bracing edge, and using both gives a layered mustard warmth that neither alone could achieve. Use them sparingly though; this is a sauce that supports the lobster rather than overpowers it. Combine finely chopped shallot, fish stock and white wine in a saucepan and reduce by two-thirds over high heat (this concentrates the wine and shallot flavours into a tight base). Pour in the béchamel, drop the heat to low and cook the sauce gently for 20 minutes, stirring every 5 minutes so it doesn't catch. Stir in the cream, bubble 5 minutes, then add both mustards and cook another 2 minutes for the mustard heat to integrate. Turn off the heat and whisk in 50 g of cold cubed butter a piece at a time to mount the sauce into a glossy emulsion. Season with a good pinch of cayenne, salt, and a tablespoon of finely chopped tarragon. Optional but excellent: a teaspoon of Cognac whisked in at the very end for extra warmth and richness. Spoon the sauce over halved cooked lobster or stir through fresh white crab meat, scatter cheese over the top and grill briefly till bubbling and gold. Serve immediately; the mounted-butter emulsion breaks within an hour, so make it just as you're ready to plate.

## Ingredients

### Wine reduction base
- 40 grams shallots (very finely chopped)
- 200 ml Fish stock
- 200 ml dry white wine

### Sauce base
- 300 ml Béchamel Sauce

### Finishing
- 100 ml double cream
- 1 teaspoon Dijon mustard
- 1 teaspoon English mustard powder
- 50 grams butter (well chilled and diced)
- 1 pinch cayenne pepper
- 1 tablespoon tarragon (finely chopped)
- salt

## Method

### Stage 1 - Reduce wine
1. Combine the shallot, fish stock and white wine in a saucepan and let bubble until the liquid has reduced by two-thirds. 

### Stage 2 - Build sauce
1. Add the béchamel and cook the sauce over a low heat for 20 minutes, stirring every 5 minutes.
1. Pour in the cream, let bubble for 5 minutes, then add both mustards and cook for another 2 minutes.

### Stage 3 - Finish
1. Turn off the heat and whisk the butter into the sauce, a piece at a time. Season with salt and a good pinch of cayenne to taste. 
1. Finally add the tarragon, if using, and serve immediately.

## Notes
- **Wine reduction:** Reduce by two-thirds to concentrate flavours; this is essential to the sauce's character.
- **Mustards:** Use both Dijon and English mustard powder for complexity; each adds different heat and flavour.
- **Cognac optional:** Add a teaspoonful of Cognac at the end of cooking for extra richness, if desired.

## Serving
- Serve immediately with lobster thermidor, crab thermidor, and other crustaceans. Also excellent mixed with crab meat and served au gratin.

## Storage
- Best eaten immediately after preparation.
- Keeps refrigerated for 1-2 days; reheat gently without boiling to prevent emulsion breaking.
- Does not freeze well due to butter emulsion and béchamel base.
