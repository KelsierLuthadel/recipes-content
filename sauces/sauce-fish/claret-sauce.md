# Claret Sauce

![Claret sauce](resources/claret.jpg)

*This characterful sauce makes a wonderful base for pink-fleshed fish, such as salmon, red mullet or tuna escalope. Pan-fry the fish at the last moment, pour the sauce on to the plate and place the fish on top.*

**Serves:** 8

**Prep Time:** 10 minutes

**Cook Time:** 35 minutes

## Overview
Claret sauce is the building block for the deep-red wine sauce that pairs with pink-fleshed fish: salmon, red mullet, tuna escalope, all of which can carry a bold sauce that would overwhelm a delicate white fish. The structure is unusual because it uses both veal stock and fish stock together, the veal giving the body and the fish giving the affinity for the protein it's being served over; on its own, a pure-veal-stock red wine sauce would be too heavy for fish, and a pure fish stock with red wine never builds proper depth. Use a full-bodied red wine (Burgundy or Bordeaux style, the more colour the better); a thin pale wine fades to almost nothing during reduction. Tip the red wine, fish stock and veal stock into a saucepan with the finely sliced shallots, sliced button mushrooms and a bouquet garni. Bring to the boil over medium heat and reduce hard till the liquid becomes noticeably syrupy and glossy and has dropped to maybe a third of its starting volume; this long reduction is what concentrates the wine and stock together into something that tastes deeper than the sum of its parts. Fish out the bouquet garni, add the cream and let the sauce bubble another minute, then strain through a fine-meshed conical sieve into a clean pan, leaving the shallot and mushroom solids behind. Off the heat (or on the lowest setting; a boil will split the emulsion), whisk in 200 g of cold cubed butter a piece at a time till the sauce turns rich glossy and lacquered. Season with salt and pepper and serve immediately under pan-fried salmon, red mullet or tuna; pool the sauce on the warm plate first, then lay the fish on top so the colours read clearly.

## Ingredients

### Liquid
- 300 ml full bodied red wine
- 200 ml Veal stock
- 300 ml Fish stock

### Aromatics & vegetables
- 50 grams shallots (finely sliced)
- 60 grams button mushrooms (finely sliced)
- 1 [Bouquet Garni](../../base-ingredients/herbs/bouquet-garni.md)

### Finishing
- 50 ml double cream
- 200 grams butter
- salt
- pepper

## Method

### Stage 1 - Combine & reduce
1. Pour the red wine and both stocks into a saucepan and add the sliced shallots, mushrooms and bouquet garni.
1. Bring to the boil over a medium heat and let bubble to reduce until slightly syrupy.

### Stage 2 - Add cream
1. Remove the bouquet garni, add the cream and let the sauce bubble for a minute or so, then strain it through a fine-meshed conical sieve into a clean saucepan.

### Stage 3 - Finish with butter
1. Whisk in the butter, a piece at a time, until the sauce is rich and glossy. 
1. Season to taste and serve hot.

## Notes
- **Wine selection:** Choose a full-bodied red (Burgundy or Bordeaux style) with good colour; light wines will fade during reduction.
- **Reduction:** The sauce should be noticeably syrupy and glossy before adding cream; this concentrates flavours.
- **Butter mounting:** Do not boil after butter is added; maintain low heat to keep emulsion smooth.

## Serving
Serve immediately with pan-fried salmon, red mullet, tuna, or other pink-fleshed fish. Plate fish atop sauce for elegant presentation.

## Storage
- Best eaten immediately after preparation.
- Keeps refrigerated for 1 day; reheat gently, whisking constantly to prevent emulsion breaking.
- Does not freeze well due to butter-based emulsion and cream content.
