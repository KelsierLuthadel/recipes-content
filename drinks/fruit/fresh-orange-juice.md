# Fresh Orange Juice

![Fresh Orange Juice](resources/fresh-orange-juice.jpg)

*Six juicing oranges, one ripe pink grapefruit, a citrus reamer and four minutes of effort: the breakfast pour that bears no resemblance to the carton.*

**Serves:** 2 generous glasses

**Prep Time:** 4 minutes

**Cook Time:** 0 minutes

## Overview
Real orange juice tastes nothing like the carton. The carton stuff has been pasteurised, deaerated, stored for months and then "flavoured back" with chemical orange essence; fresh-squeezed has the immediate sweet-and-bitter punch of the actual fruit and stays alive for maybe 20 minutes before it starts losing its lift. The right oranges are the juicing kind (Valencia, navel, or whatever's labelled "juicing oranges" at the greengrocer), heavy in the hand and slightly soft when squeezed; rolling them on the counter under your palm before juicing breaks down the inner membranes and yields a good third more juice. A small splash of pink grapefruit (or a tablespoon of blood orange juice when in season) lifts the whole thing from sweet to interesting. Pour into a chilled glass, drink within 15 minutes of pressing, and consider that you've never actually had orange juice before today.

## Ingredients

### Juicing
- 6 ripe juicing oranges (Valencia, navel; about 1.2 kg total)
- ½ ripe pink grapefruit (optional but excellent; for the lift)
- 1 small naval orange (extra, for the garnish)
- Pinch of fine salt (yes, really; sharpens the citrus)

### To serve
- Plenty of ice cubes (optional; depends if you want it cold or room-temperature)
- A wedge of orange on the glass rim

## Method

### Stage 1 - Prep the fruit
1. Take the oranges out of the fridge 30 minutes ahead if possible (room-temperature citrus juices better).
1. Roll each orange firmly under your palm on the counter for a few seconds; the membranes inside break down and the fruit gives up its juice properly.
1. Halve all 6 oranges and the grapefruit half across the equator (not pole-to-pole).

### Stage 2 - Juice
1. Use a citrus reamer, a hand juicer, or a juicing attachment to extract the juice into a measuring jug.
1. Twist and press to get as much juice and pulp as you want; pulp adds body and bitterness.
1. You're aiming for about 350 to 400 ml of orange juice plus 50 to 80 ml of grapefruit.
1. Strain through a coarse sieve if you don't want pips; leave the pulp behind only if you prefer clear juice.

### Stage 3 - Mix and serve
1. Stir in the pinch of salt; it sounds wrong but try a sip before and after, the salt sharpens the sweetness without making the juice salty.
1. Pour into chilled glasses, over ice if you want it cold.
1. Notch a slice of orange onto each rim; drink within 15 to 20 minutes of juicing.

## Notes
- **Roll before juicing.** Twice the yield from each orange, no exaggeration.
- **Half a grapefruit is the upgrade.** Without it the juice is good; with it the juice is interesting. Pink grapefruit is sweeter and more forgiving than yellow.
- **Salt sharpens, doesn't salt.** A pinch is enough; it brings out the sweetness without making the juice taste of salt.
- **Drink fresh.** Twenty minutes after pressing the juice starts losing the volatile aromatics that make it taste like fresh orange rather than orange concentrate.

## Variations
- **Blood orange juice.** Use 6 blood oranges in season (January to March in the UK); a deep red-purple juice with a faint raspberry note. Drop the grapefruit.
- **Carrot-and-orange.** Add 200 ml of fresh carrot juice (juicer required); doubles the depth and turns it sunset-orange.

## Storage
- Drink within 20 minutes for the brightest flavour.
- Refrigerate up to 24 hours in a sealed bottle; the flavour fades but stays palatable.
- Freezes in ice-cube trays for 2 months; pop a cube into sparkling water for a quick fizz.
