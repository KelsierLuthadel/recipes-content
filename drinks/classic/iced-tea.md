# Iced Tea

![Iced Tea](resources/iced-tea.jpg)

*Strong black tea brewed hot and poured over ice, sweetened just enough, finished with a wheel of lemon and a sprig of mint.*

**Serves:** 6 to 8

**Prep Time:** 5 minutes

**Cook Time:** 7 minutes

## Overview
The southern American long pour, and the drink you reach for when the sun's still up and dinner's a long way off. You brew strong black tea hot (the brewing has to happen hot or the tea reads flat and tannic), sweeten while the tea is warm so the sugar dissolves cleanly, then pour the lot over a glass full of ice to chill it in seconds. The proportions land roughly at six tea bags per litre and three to four tablespoons of sugar, scaled to taste; over-strong tea diluted with melting ice is the goal, not weak tea served cold. Lemon wheels and mint go on at the end, never in the brew. A splash of peach syrup or a bruised sprig of mint thrown in while the tea cools turns it into something more interesting; serve in a tall glass with a long spoon for the obligatory stir.

## Ingredients

### Tea base
- 6 black tea bags (English Breakfast, Assam or Ceylon; not flavoured teas)
- 750 ml boiling water (just-boiled, not standing-water)
- 3 to 4 tablespoons caster sugar (to taste; some prefer it bone-dry)
- Pinch of bicarbonate of soda (optional, softens the tannins)

### To finish
- 750 ml cold water
- Plenty of ice cubes
- 1 lemon (sliced into wheels)
- Fresh mint sprigs

## Method

### Stage 1 - Brew strong and hot
1. Put the tea bags into a heatproof jug or large teapot.
1. Pour over the just-boiled water; add the pinch of bicarb if using.
1. Steep for 4 to 5 minutes (no longer, or the tea turns bitter and tannic).
1. Lift out the tea bags; press gently against the side to release the last of the tea, but don't squeeze them dry.

### Stage 2 - Sweeten while warm
1. While the tea is still warm, stir in the sugar a tablespoon at a time, tasting as you go.
1. The tea should taste slightly sweeter than you want the final drink to be; dilution with ice and water will mellow it.

### Stage 3 - Dilute and chill
1. Pour the cold water into the jug to bring the volume up to 1.5 litres and bring the temperature down.
1. Refrigerate for 2 hours, or pour straight over plenty of ice if you can't wait.

### Stage 4 - Serve
1. Fill tall glasses with ice cubes.
1. Pour the tea over the ice.
1. Garnish with a lemon wheel and a sprig of mint; stir briefly before drinking.

## Notes
- **Hot brew, cold serve.** Cold-brewing tea works but gives a thinner result; hot brewing extracts properly then chilling preserves the flavour.
- **Watch the steep time.** Five minutes is the absolute maximum. Past that, the tannins take over and no amount of sugar covers it.
- **A pinch of bicarb softens.** Mary Randolph's 1824 trick: bicarbonate of soda neutralises some of the tea tannins and gives a smoother glass. Optional but worth trying.

## Variations
- **Sweet tea, southern style.** Double the sugar to 6 tablespoons and add it while the tea is hot so it fully dissolves. The drink that gets passed at every porch table from Atlanta to Charleston.
- **Peach iced tea.** Stir 4 tablespoons of peach syrup or muddled fresh peaches into the warm tea; let infuse 10 minutes before chilling.
- **Mint iced tea.** Add a generous handful of fresh mint to the hot tea as it brews; strain before chilling.

## Storage
- Refrigerate up to 48 hours in a sealed jug; the flavour brightens slightly on the second day.
- Don't freeze: the tea breaks and develops a metallic edge.
