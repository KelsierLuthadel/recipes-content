# Raki Shqiptare

![Raki Shqiptare](resources/raki-shqiptare.jpg)

*Albanian grape brandy: the clear village distillate of late-summer grape pomace, slow-cooked in copper stills and served chilled in small glasses as the welcoming shot at every Albanian door.*

**Serves:** 1 (40 ml)

**Prep Time:** 1 minute (for serving)

**Cook Time:** None (the distillation is a home craft, see Notes)

## Overview
Raki shqiptare is the clear grape brandy of Albanian village life, distilled in copper stills in the back of every wine-growing household from the grape pomace left after the wine harvest. The strength runs from 38% to 45% ABV in commercial bottles; village raki is often 50%+ and served by the cup. The serving ritual is fixed: a small glass, lightly chilled (not iced), set down beside a sliver of fresh white cheese and a square of cornbread, drunk in two small sips, never as a single shot. The first raki of the day is served before breakfast to guests at the door; the last raki of the evening is the toast that closes a long meal. The flavour is dry and grape-clean, with a faint kerosene edge that softens as the bottle ages. Eat the cheese, drink the raki, take the next.

## Ingredients

### Per serving
- 40 ml Albanian raki (Skrapar, Përmet or village raki preferred), lightly chilled
- 1 small slender glass (40-50 ml capacity)
- 1 sliver white cheese (gjizë, feta or kashkaval), to accompany
- 1 small square cornbread or rusks, to accompany

### For a guest welcome (the traditional setting)
- A small dish of olives
- A few walnut halves
- A small dish of dried figs or grape preserve (reçel)

## Method

### Stage 1 - Chill the raki
1. Place the bottle of raki in the fridge for 1 hour (not the freezer).
2. The raki should be cool but not icy; over-chilling masks the flavour.

### Stage 2 - Set the welcome plate
1. Slice 30 g of white cheese onto a small plate.
2. Add a small square of cornbread.
3. Set out olives, walnuts and grape preserve if guests are arriving.

### Stage 3 - Pour
1. Pour 40 ml of chilled raki into a slender small glass.
2. Leave the bottle on the table; the second pour is expected.

### Stage 4 - Serve
1. Set the glass down before the cheese.
2. Toast with "Gëzuar!" (cheers).
3. Sip once, eat the cheese, sip again.
4. Refill within the hour.

## Notes
- **Buying raki:** Skrapar and Përmet are the two best-known Albanian raki regions; look for these names on the label. Look for clear glass (cloudy raki is often watered).
- **Home distillation:** Village raki is distilled in copper stills from grape pomace fermented for 30-40 days. The first run of the still (the "head") is discarded; the middle run is the drinking raki. Distillation requires a licensed still in many countries and is not a kitchen recipe.
- **The proper temperature:** Lightly chilled, not iced. Iced raki loses its flavour.

## Variations
- **Raki rrushi (grape raki):** The standard, distilled from grape pomace.
- **Raki mani (mulberry raki):** Distilled from mulberries; a Berat speciality.
- **Raki kumbulle (plum raki):** Distilled from plums; common in the north.
- **Raki dardhe (pear raki):** Distilled from pears; the rarest, the most aromatic.
- **Raki me arra (walnut raki):** Plain raki infused with green walnuts and sugar for 40 days; a homemade digestif.

## Serving
As the welcome shot when guests arrive · before lunch with cheese and bread · at weddings and Bajram (Eid) · at the end of a long meal as a digestif · with olives and walnuts on a meze plate · on a cold winter evening for warmth.

## Storage
- Sealed bottles keep indefinitely in a cool dark place.
- Once opened, the flavour holds for 2-3 years.
- Do not freeze (loses aromatic compounds).
- Homemade infused raki (mulberry, walnut) keeps 2 years at room temperature.
