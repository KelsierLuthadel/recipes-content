# Boza

![Boza](resources/boza.jpg)

*The fermented millet drink shared across the Balkans: a thick, pale-beige, slightly sour-and-sweet pour finished with a dusting of cinnamon. In Tirana the warm-weather street drink, sold from kerbside carts and corner kiosks.*

**Serves:** 6 tall glasses (about 1.5 litres)

**Prep Time:** 30 minutes (plus 24-36 hours fermenting)

**Cook Time:** 45 minutes

## Overview
Boza is the centuries-old Balkan fermented drink that arrived in Albania with the Ottomans and never left. The base is millet flour (sometimes mixed with maize), boiled with water to a thick porridge, then thinned, sweetened, and left to ferment with a spoonful of old boza or a pinch of yeast for 24-36 hours. The result is a pale-beige drink, thick enough to coat the back of a spoon, with a sour-sweet flavour that walks the line between yoghurt and lightly alcoholic ale (1% ABV at most). In Tirana the boza-seller's cart still works the warm-weather street corners; in winter it stands inside the bakery alongside the petulla. The traditional serving is a tall glass, the boza poured cold from a jug, a generous dusting of cinnamon on top, and a small spoon for stirring the cinnamon down through the drink. Drink slow and cold.

## Ingredients

### For the boza
- 200 g millet flour
- 50 g fine maize flour (optional, gives a deeper colour)
- 1.5 litres water
- 150 g caster sugar
- 1 tbsp boza from a previous batch OR 2 g dried baker's yeast
- 1 tsp ground cinnamon, to finish

## Method

### Stage 1 - Cook the base
1. Whisk the millet flour and maize flour with 500 ml cold water in a heavy saucepan until smooth and lump-free.
2. Add the remaining 1 litre of water.
3. Place over medium heat; bring slowly to a boil, whisking constantly (the mixture thickens fast and can catch on the bottom).
4. Reduce the heat to low; simmer gently for 40-45 minutes, stirring every few minutes.
5. The mixture should reduce slightly and look glossy.

### Stage 2 - Sweeten and cool
1. Take off the heat; stir in the sugar until dissolved.
2. Pour into a large bowl; let cool to 35°C (just warm to the finger, not hot).

### Stage 3 - Ferment
1. Stir in the previous boza or the dried yeast; mix well.
2. Cover the bowl with a clean cloth.
3. Leave at warm room temperature (22-25°C) for 24-36 hours.
4. The boza will bubble gently on top and turn slightly sour; taste at 24 hours and at 36 hours.
5. The right balance is sweet-tart, not vinegar-sharp.

### Stage 4 - Strain and chill
1. Pass the boza through a fine sieve to remove any lumps.
2. Pour into a clean jug or bottles.
3. Refrigerate for at least 4 hours.

### Stage 5 - Serve
1. Stir the chilled boza (it settles in the fridge).
2. Pour into tall glasses.
3. Dust generously with ground cinnamon.
4. Serve a small spoon alongside for stirring the cinnamon in.

## Notes
- **The thickness:** Proper boza coats the back of a spoon. Too thick and it is unpourable, too thin and the body is wrong.
- **The fermentation:** Warm room temperature, not hot. Above 28°C the boza turns alcoholic too fast.
- **The taste check:** Sweet-tart is the goal. If it goes vinegar-sharp, refrigerate immediately to stop the ferment.

## Variations
- **All-millet version:** Skip the maize flour for a paler, lighter boza (the classic Albanian style).
- **With chickpea flour:** Replace 50 g of the millet with chickpea flour for a thicker body.
- **With lemon zest:** Add the zest of one lemon during cooking for a brighter flavour.
- **With vanilla:** Stir in 1 teaspoon vanilla extract after cooling.
- **Less sweet:** Reduce the sugar to 100 g for a more austere, tart drink.

## Serving
Cold from a tall glass with cinnamon · in summer from the Tirana street cart · in winter alongside hot petulla at the bakery · with a slice of cake or sweet pastry · as a Bajram (Eid) drink · at breakfast with cornbread.

## Storage
- Refrigerate up to 4 days; the boza continues to sour slowly.
- Stir before each pour (it settles).
- Do not freeze (the texture breaks).
- Save 1 tablespoon as a starter for the next batch.
