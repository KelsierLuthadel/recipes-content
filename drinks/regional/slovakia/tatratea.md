# Tatratea

![Tatratea](resources/tatratea.jpg)

*The high-proof Slovak mountain liqueur: strong herbal tea, citrus and spice infused into spirit, the warming pour of every ski-lodge and mountain hut in the High Tatras.*

**Serves:** 2 (as a neat pour)

**Prep Time:** 10 minutes (plus 24 hours infusing for the homemade version)

**Cook Time:** 5 minutes

## Overview
Tatratea is the modern Slovak mountain liqueur, created in 2003 by a distillery in Kežmarok at the foot of the High Tatras. The commercial brand has become the ski-lodge pour of the country, sold in colour-coded bottles by alcohol strength (the orange 52 percent original, up to the black 72 percent). The flavour is a strong infused black tea with citrus, mountain herbs, honey and warming spice. This recipe is for a homemade approximation: a strong black tea infusion combined with vodka or grain spirit, honey, lemon and warming herbs. It will not match the commercial product perfectly but it is the same idea, ready to pour after one night in the bottle.

## Ingredients

### Homemade Tatratea-style liqueur (makes about 700 ml)
- 4 strong black tea bags (English Breakfast or similar)
- 250 ml boiling water
- 200 g caster sugar
- 100 g clear honey
- Zest of 1 orange (in wide strips)
- Zest of 1 lemon (in wide strips)
- 2 cinnamon sticks
- 4 cloves
- 2 star anise
- 1 small piece of fresh ginger, sliced
- 1 tsp dried thyme
- 500 ml vodka or grain spirit (40 percent or above)
- 50 ml dark rum (optional, for depth)

### To serve neat
- 50 ml Tatratea liqueur per glass
- A small thick-walled glass

### Tatratea hot toddy
- 50 ml Tatratea
- 100 ml hot black tea
- 1 tsp honey
- A slice of lemon
- A cinnamon stick

## Method

### Stage 1 - Brew and infuse
1. Steep the tea bags in the boiling water for 8 minutes; squeeze out and discard the bags.
2. While still hot, stir in the sugar and honey until dissolved.
3. Add the orange and lemon zest, cinnamon, cloves, star anise, ginger and thyme.
4. Cool to room temperature.

### Stage 2 - Combine and bottle
1. Pour the cooled tea infusion into a clean 1-litre glass bottle or jar.
2. Add the vodka and dark rum if using.
3. Seal and shake; leave at room temperature 24 hours (or up to a week for a fuller flavour).

### Stage 3 - Strain
1. Strain through a fine sieve lined with muslin into a clean bottle.
2. Discard the solids.
3. Store sealed; chill before pouring.

### Stage 4 - Serve
1. **Neat:** Pour 5 cl into a small thick glass, chilled or at room temperature. Drink in two short sips.
2. **Hot toddy:** Add Tatratea, hot black tea, honey and a slice of lemon to a heat-safe glass; stir with a cinnamon stick. Drink warm.

## Notes
- **The commercial bottle.** If you can find genuine Tatratea, buy it; the homemade version is a starting point. The brand bottles are colour-coded by strength.
- **Sweetness is the signature.** Tatratea is sweet by design; do not cut the sugar or honey.
- **Drink hot at altitude.** Slovak skiers and hikers warm a glass of Tatratea over the coffee machine in a mountain hut; the heat softens the alcohol and amplifies the spice.
- **Time builds depth.** A 24-hour infusion gives you the basic; a week gives you a much deeper flavour.

## Variations
- **Herbal Tatratea.** Substitute the thyme with rosemary, sage and a bay leaf for a more savoury herbal mix.
- **Forest version.** Add a handful of dried juniper berries and 1 tsp pine needles for a piney Tatra note.
- **Bittersweet.** Add 1 tsp gentian root or 5 cm of dried angelica root for a bitter edge.
- **Coffee Tatratea.** Pour 2 cl over a black espresso for a warming nightcap.
- **Apple-cinnamon.** Substitute half the spirit with apple brandy for an autumn variant.

## Serving
Pour cold or warmed · into a small thick-walled glass · after a day's skiing or hiking · with a slice of dark fruitcake or gingerbread alongside · or as a winter nightcap.

## Storage
- The bottled liqueur keeps 6 months at room temperature, sealed.
- Refrigerate after opening for a colder pour; not strictly necessary.
- The hot-toddy version is mixed per glass; do not reheat.
