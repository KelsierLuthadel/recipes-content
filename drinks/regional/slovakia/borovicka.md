# Borovička

![Borovička](resources/borovicka.jpg)

*The Slovak juniper spirit: clear, dry, piney, distilled from juniper berries in the foothill villages and poured neat from a small thick-walled glass before lunch or after a long walk.*

**Serves:** 2 (as a neat pour)

**Prep Time:** 2 minutes

**Cook Time:** 0 minutes (the spirit itself is bought, not distilled at home)

## Overview
Borovička is Slovakia's national spirit, a clear juniper-based eau-de-vie similar in family to gin but flavoured almost entirely by juniper rather than the botanical mix that defines a London dry. Spišská borovička from the Spiš region (eastern Slovakia) is the protected name, with a softly sweet juniper note; Žltá borovička (the yellow version) is aged in oak and gentler still. The traditional pour is neat, room temperature, in a small thick glass (4 cl) before a heavy meal, or icy cold straight from the freezer at the end of a hike. This recipe gives you the proper pour, and a couple of long-drink mixers if you want to stretch it out across an afternoon.

## Ingredients

### The neat pour
- 80 ml Slovak borovička (Spišská or Žltá), at room temperature or chilled
- 2 small thick-walled shot glasses (4 cl each)

### The borovička long drink (optional)
- 50 ml borovička
- 150 ml chilled tonic water
- A juniper berry, lightly crushed
- A lemon peel twist
- Ice cubes

## Method

### Stage 1 - The neat pour
1. Take borovička from the freezer (icy cold is the country drinker's preference) or pour at room temperature.
2. Pour 4 cl into each thick-walled shot glass.
3. Drink in one go (na ex), or in two short sips with a slice of dense rye bread to chase.

### Stage 2 - The long drink (if stretching)
1. Lightly crush a juniper berry between your fingers to release the oil; drop into a tall glass.
2. Add ice; pour over the borovička and chilled tonic.
3. Twist a strip of lemon peel over the glass to release the oil; drop in.
4. Stir once.

## Notes
- **Buy Slovak.** Spišská borovička and Žltá borovička are the country's best-known marques; both are sold in any decent off-licence in Central Europe. The Czech versions are similar but distilled differently.
- **Chill is optional but traditional.** Many Slovak households keep the bottle in the freezer; the juniper note softens at low temperature.
- **Glass shape matters.** A small thick-walled shot or grappa glass is the proper vessel; do not pour into a wine glass.
- **Pair with food.** Borovička cuts through rich fatty dishes; pour before kapustnica, slovak goulash or any pork plate.

## Variations
- **Borovička hriata.** Warmed borovička with honey and a strip of orange peel, the winter mountain-hut pour.
- **Borovička with juice.** A long pour over chilled apple juice and ice, the casual summer drink.
- **Borovička and tonic.** The modern bar version (see above).
- **Borovička on rocks.** Over a single large ice cube in a rocks glass, for slow sipping.
- **Borovička aperitif.** A 2 cl pour stirred into a glass of chilled white wine, the Bratislava bar trick.

## Serving
Pour cold or room temperature · into a small thick-walled glass (4 cl) · before a heavy meal or after a hike · with a slice of rye bread to chase.

## Storage
- An opened bottle keeps indefinitely at room temperature; juniper spirits do not deteriorate.
- Store in the freezer for a colder pour; the spirit will not freeze at standard freezer temperature.
- Once mixed as a long drink, drink the same day; the lemon and tonic flatten overnight.
