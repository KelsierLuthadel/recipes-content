# Slivovica

![Slivovica](resources/slivovica-slovak.jpg)

*Slovak plum brandy: clear, fierce, fruity, distilled at the village house-still from autumn damson plums, the everyday spirit of the country wedding, funeral and Christmas morning.*

**Serves:** 2 (as a neat pour)

**Prep Time:** 2 minutes

**Cook Time:** 0 minutes (the spirit itself is distilled, not made at home)

## Overview
Slivovica is the home-distilled plum brandy that defines rural Slovak life: poured at every wedding, funeral, christening and Christmas morning across the country, sometimes drunk in shot-sized glasses for breakfast on a hard winter day. The protected Slovak version (Bošácka slivovica from the Bošáca valley near Trenčín) is distilled twice from blue damson plums fermented with their skins, giving a clear high-strength spirit (52 percent and up) with a deep plum-stone note and a long warm finish. Across the country, every village still produces an unofficial slivovica from the autumn harvest, sealed in unmarked bottles passed across kitchen tables. This recipe is for the proper pour and the warmed-honey winter variation.

## Ingredients

### The neat pour
- 80 ml Slovak slivovica (Bošácka, or any reputable distillery)
- 2 small thick-walled shot glasses (4 cl each)

### Slivovica hriata (warmed slivovica)
- 50 ml slivovica per glass
- 1 tsp clear honey
- 1 strip of orange or lemon peel
- 1 clove (optional)
- A small splash of hot water

## Method

### Stage 1 - The neat pour
1. Pour 4 cl of slivovica into each thick-walled shot glass at room temperature.
2. Hand to your guest; clink, say "na zdravie" (to your health), drink in one go or in two short sips.
3. Slivovica is poured neat in Slovakia, never with ice or mixer at the table.

### Stage 2 - Slivovica hriata
1. Warm a small thick glass mug or a heat-safe glass with hot water; tip the water out.
2. Spoon the honey into the warm glass; add the citrus peel and clove if using.
3. Add the slivovica.
4. Top with 1 tablespoon of hot (not boiling) water; stir to dissolve the honey.
5. Drink warm.

## Notes
- **Buy reputable.** Bošácka slivovica is the protected name; smaller distilleries across western Slovakia produce equally good bottles. Avoid anything labelled "plum schnapps" from the supermarket bottom shelf.
- **Room temperature for the neat pour.** Slivovica is not chilled at the table; the warmth releases the plum note.
- **Do not boil the hriata.** Boiling water burns off the alcohol and the aromatics; warm to body temperature, no more.
- **The clink.** Slovak table custom: look the other drinker in the eye as you clink glasses, then drink.

## Variations
- **Slivovica hriata with cinnamon.** Add a cinnamon stick to the warmed version for the Christmas Eve pour.
- **Slivovica and apple juice.** A long pour over chilled apple juice and ice, the casual summer take.
- **Slivovica over coffee.** A 2 cl pour stirred into a black coffee at the end of a heavy meal.
- **The plum-pit infusion.** Drop 3 cracked plum stones into a bottle of slivovica and infuse 2 weeks for an almond-marzipan note (a country trick).
- **Slivovica spritz.** Modern Bratislava bars pour slivovica with sparkling wine, a strip of plum and an ice cube.

## Serving
Pour neat into a small thick glass · room temperature · before a meal as an aperitif or after as a digestif · with rye bread to chase or a strong black coffee after.

## Storage
- An opened bottle keeps indefinitely at room temperature; plum brandies do not deteriorate.
- The honey-warmed version is made fresh per glass; do not pre-mix and reheat.
- Plum-stone infusions keep 6 months sealed; strain after 2 weeks of infusing so the bitter compounds do not build up.
