# Drinks of Bosnia

![Drinks of Bosnia](resources/hero.jpg)

Bosanska kafa is the heart of the social day: very finely ground coffee simmered in a small copper džezva, poured into a tiny porcelain fildžan with a sugar cube on the saucer and a square of rahat lokum on the side, the round taking forty minutes minimum because the talk is the point. Rakija opens every meal and seals every deal; the household slivovica (plum brandy) comes out for a guest within minutes of the door closing, raised with a quiet "živjeli" before the glass touches the lip. Salep warms a winter morning at the bazaar; the orchid-root drink came in with the Ottomans and stayed.
