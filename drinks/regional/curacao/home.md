# Curaçaoan Drinks

![Curaçaoan Drinks](resources/hero.jpg)

Curaçao gave the world one of its most recognisable liqueurs in Blue Curaçao, distilled on the island from the dried peel of the laraha orange, a bitter citrus that grows nowhere else. Alongside the bottled export, the everyday drink culture runs to lime water (awa di lamunchi) sweetened with brown sugar and served by the jug, and fresh young coconut water sipped straight from a machete-opened green coconut by the roadside. The local cooler tradition is built on whatever fruit the dry-island climate yields: tamarind, soursop, papaya, passion fruit, blended with ice and a little sugar.
