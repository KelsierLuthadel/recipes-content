# Lahori Sweet Lassi

![Lahori Sweet Lassi](resources/lahori-lassi.jpg)

*Lahore's signature yogurt drink: thick, full-fat yogurt blended with cold water, ice, sugar and a touch of crushed cardamom and rose water, served in tall metal tumblers with a dollop of cream floating on top. Sweet, creamy, soothing, the antidote to a 45°C Punjabi afternoon.*

**Serves:** 4 tall glasses

**Prep Time:** 5 minutes

**Cook Time:** 0 minutes

## Overview
Lahore is the unofficial capital of lassi. Walk down any street in the Old City in summer and you'll see clay pots churning in front of lassi vendors, and stacks of bottles of fresh full-fat yogurt being whisked into tall metal tumblers with ice and sugar. Lahori sweet lassi specifically is the sweet version (as opposed to namkeen / salted lassi), and the Lahori distinction is that it leans rich: extra cream on top, more sugar than the Indian-Punjabi version, sometimes a tiny pinch of cardamom or a few drops of rose water. The drink is served absurdly cold, in tall metal tumblers that mist with condensation, and the proper presentation has a final spoonful of malai (clotted cream) floated on top. A glass of Lahori lassi after a heavy biryani lunch is a hug. The mango version (aam lassi) is the same technique with the addition of fresh mango pulp; it's a summer-only treat when Pakistani mangoes are in season (June-August).

## Ingredients

- 500 g full-fat thick yogurt (Greek-style is the best supermarket substitute; Pakistani / Indian full-fat yogurt is even better)
- 400 ml cold water (chilled)
- 8 to 10 tablespoons caster sugar, to taste (start at 6, taste, add more)
- A small handful of ice cubes (about 100 g)
- 2 cardamom pods, lightly crushed (optional)
- 1 teaspoon rose water (optional — careful, can dominate)

### To serve
- 4 tablespoons malai (clotted cream) OR thick whipped double cream
- A small pinch of ground cardamom
- A few finely chopped pistachios (optional)
- 4 tall metal tumblers or tall glasses, chilled in the freezer for 10 minutes

## Method

### Stage 1 - Whisk the yogurt
1. Spoon the yogurt into a large jug or blender.
1. Whisk with a balloon whisk (or pulse briefly in the blender) until completely smooth, with no lumps.

### Stage 2 - Blend with water and sugar
1. Add the cold water in a steady stream while whisking. Pouring all at once gives clumps; slow and steady gives a smooth drink.
1. Add 6 tablespoons of sugar and whisk until dissolved. Taste; Lahori lassi is properly sweet (the cold ice dilutes the sweetness, so it needs to taste sweet at this stage). Add more sugar 1 tablespoon at a time.
1. If using cardamom: crack the pods open, take out the seeds, crush them lightly with the back of a knife, and whisk in.
1. If using rose water: 1 teaspoon at the maximum; rose water dominates quickly. Stir gently.

### Stage 3 - Chill and finish
1. Refrigerate the mixed lassi at least 30 minutes (preferably 1 hour) so it's deeply cold.
1. Just before serving, add the ice cubes; whisk briefly to start them melting.

### Stage 4 - Serve
1. Pour into the chilled tall tumblers.
1. Top each with 1 tablespoon of malai (or a generous dollop of whipped cream) so it floats on the surface.
1. Sprinkle a tiny pinch of ground cardamom and a few chopped pistachios on top.
1. Serve immediately, very cold.

## Notes
- **Full-fat yogurt only.** Low-fat or strained yogurt gives a thin, sour drink. Full-fat is the whole point.
- **Whisk the yogurt smooth first.** This single step makes or breaks the lassi. Lumpy yogurt = lumpy lassi.
- **Sweet by Lahori standards.** Don't underdo the sugar. The diluting effect of ice means the drink needs to taste aggressively sweet before serving for the final drink to taste properly sweet in the mouth.
- **The malai on top.** Real Lahori lassi has a tablespoon of malai (the thick cream skimmed off boiled milk) floating on top. Whipped cream is the supermarket substitute; clotted cream works at a pinch. The visual matters as much as the taste.

## Variations
- **Aam lassi (mango lassi).** Add 200 g pulp from 2 ripe Pakistani / Indian mangoes (Alphonso or Chaunsa); use 6 tablespoons of sugar (the mango sweetens too). Summer special.
- **Namkeen / salted lassi.** Skip the sugar; add 1 teaspoon salt, a pinch of cumin, and a few mint leaves. Savoury, served at lunchtime to cut richness.
- **Rose-only lassi.** Skip cardamom; use 2 teaspoons rose water and 4 drops of rose syrup. Pale pink; festival drink.
- **Saffron lassi.** Add a pinch of saffron strands steeped in 1 tablespoon hot water and stirred in. Wedding version, golden colour.

## Storage
- Best fresh. Keeps 4 hours in the fridge; longer than that and the yogurt separates and the drink loses its body.
- Don't freeze; texture is destroyed.
