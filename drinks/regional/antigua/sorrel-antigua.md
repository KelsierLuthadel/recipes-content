# Antiguan Sorrel

![Antiguan Sorrel](resources/sorrel-antigua.jpg)

*The deep-red Antiguan Christmas drink, brewed from dried hibiscus calyces with fresh ginger, orange peel, cinnamon and cloves, sweetened heavily and served ice-cold from Christmas Eve through New Year.*

**Serves:** 2 litres

**Prep Time:** 15 minutes

**Cook Time:** 10 minutes (plus 12-hour steep)

## Overview
Sorrel, called hibiscus tea elsewhere, is the colour of dark rubies and the smell of an Antiguan Christmas. Dried hibiscus calyces (the red flower bracts) are simmered briefly in water, then off the heat they steep overnight with fresh ginger, orange peel, a cinnamon stick and a few cloves. The next day the brew is strained, sweetened with brown sugar, sharpened with a squeeze of lime, and rested in the fridge until ice-cold. Served in tall glasses with plenty of ice and a slice of orange, it accompanies every Antiguan Christmas meal from the pepperpot to the black cake. A splash of Antiguan dark rum turns the household drink into the host's version. The colour alone says the holidays have arrived.

## Ingredients

- 80 g dried sorrel (hibiscus calyces)
- 2 litres water
- 50 g fresh ginger, sliced
- Peel of 1 orange (no white pith)
- 1 cinnamon stick
- 6 whole cloves
- 2 allspice berries
- 250 g dark brown sugar (adjust to taste)
- Juice of 1 lime
- Dark rum, to serve (optional)

## Method

### Stage 1 - Brew
1. Rinse the dried sorrel under cold water to remove any dust.
2. Combine the water, sliced ginger, orange peel, cinnamon stick, cloves and allspice in a large pot.
3. Bring to a boil. Add the sorrel.
4. Drop heat and simmer 8 minutes, until the water turns deep red.
5. Turn off the heat, cover, leave to steep 12 hours or overnight at room temperature.

### Stage 2 - Strain and sweeten
1. Strain through a fine sieve into a clean jug, pressing on the solids to extract all the liquid. Discard the spent sorrel.
2. While still warm, stir in the brown sugar until dissolved.
3. Add the lime juice. Taste, adjust the sugar and lime to balance.

### Stage 3 - Chill and serve
1. Refrigerate at least 4 hours until properly cold.
2. Serve in tall glasses over plenty of ice.
3. Garnish with a slice of orange or a sprig of mint.
4. Add a 30 ml shot of dark rum per glass if making the spiked version.

## Notes
- **The sorrel:** Dried hibiscus calyces are sold in Caribbean and Mexican shops as "sorrel" or "jamaica" (ha-MY-ka). Both work; Mexican jamaica is slightly milder.
- **The steep:** Overnight is the right length. Less than 6 hours gives a thinner brew; longer than 24 turns it bitter.
- **The sugar:** Antiguans like their sorrel sweet, almost cordial-like. Start with 250 g and add more after the lime goes in if needed.

## Variations
- **Rum sorrel:** A splash of dark Antiguan rum in each glass; or bottle the drink with 100 ml of rum stirred in for the adult version.
- **Stronger spice:** Add 2 cardamom pods and a star anise to the brew for a fuller spice profile.
- **Sorrel syrup:** Reduce the strained brew with sugar to a syrup; dilute with water or sparkling water by the glass.
- **Sorrel and ginger beer:** Mix half sorrel and half ginger beer for a Christmas long drink.

## Serving
Serve cold in tall glasses with ice · at every Antiguan Christmas meal · alongside fungie and pepperpot · with black cake on Christmas Day.

## Storage
- Keeps 10 days refrigerated in a sealed bottle
- The colour deepens and the flavour rounds out over the first 3 days
- Freeze any leftovers as ice cubes for cold sorrel water year-round
