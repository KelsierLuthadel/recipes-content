# Antiguan Mauby

![Antiguan Mauby](resources/mauby-antigua.jpg)

*The bitter-sweet bark tea of Antigua: mauby bark boiled with cinnamon, cloves and a bay leaf, sweetened with brown sugar and served ice-cold, the acquired-taste household drink of the islands.*

**Serves:** 2 litres

**Prep Time:** 10 minutes

**Cook Time:** 15 minutes (plus overnight rest)

## Overview
Mauby is the most divisive Antiguan drink: a strong, slightly bitter herbal infusion brewed from the bark of the mauby tree (Colubrina elliptica) along with a handful of warm spices, then sweetened and served chilled. The bitterness is the signature; outsiders find it strange the first sip, locals find anything else missing it. Mauby bark is sold in shops in dried strips, and the brewing is straightforward: boil the bark with cinnamon, cloves, anise seed and bay leaf for fifteen minutes, then off the heat let the brew rest overnight. Sweetened the next day with brown sugar to taste, served over ice in a tall glass, it is the Antiguan answer to a cold tea on a hot afternoon. Anti-inflammatory, slightly diuretic, mildly bitter.

## Ingredients

- 30 g mauby bark (dried, in strips)
- 2 litres water
- 1 cinnamon stick
- 6 whole cloves
- 1 tsp anise seed
- 2 bay leaves
- Peel of 1 orange (no pith)
- 300 g brown sugar (adjust to taste)
- A small splash of vanilla extract

## Method

### Stage 1 - Brew
1. Rinse the mauby bark briefly under cold water.
2. Combine the water, bark, cinnamon, cloves, anise seed, bay leaves and orange peel in a heavy pot.
3. Bring to a boil. Drop heat, simmer 15 minutes, until the liquid turns dark brown and the kitchen smells of warm bark.

### Stage 2 - Rest
1. Turn off the heat, cover, leave to rest overnight at room temperature.
2. The longer it sits, the more bitter and complex it gets, up to 24 hours.

### Stage 3 - Strain and sweeten
1. Strain through a fine sieve or muslin into a clean jug, pressing on the solids.
2. Stir in the brown sugar while the brew is still slightly warm, until dissolved.
3. Add the vanilla.
4. Taste; mauby should sit just on the edge of bitter. Adjust the sugar carefully, too much and the character is lost.

### Stage 4 - Chill and serve
1. Refrigerate at least 4 hours.
2. Serve over ice in tall glasses.
3. A wedge of lime on the rim is the traditional finish.

## Notes
- **The bark:** Mauby bark is widely available in Caribbean grocers and online. Use it dry, not soaked beforehand.
- **The bitter:** The bitterness is the whole point. Antiguans say a sweet mauby is no mauby at all. Start light on the sugar.
- **The rest:** Overnight at room temperature, not in the fridge. The slow steep is what builds the depth.

## Variations
- **Mauby and stout:** Mix mauby with cold Guinness or stout (half and half) for the rum-shop adult drink.
- **Spiced mauby:** Add a piece of fresh ginger and a star anise to the brew for a fuller spice profile.
- **Mauby cordial:** Boil the brew down with extra sugar to a syrup; dilute to taste with water or sparkling water.
- **Mauby float:** Pour cold mauby over a scoop of coconut ice cream as a non-alcoholic dessert drink.

## Serving
Serve ice-cold in tall glasses · over plenty of ice · with a wedge of lime · alongside saltfish cakes or pepperpot · on a hot afternoon.

## Storage
- Keeps 10 days refrigerated in a sealed bottle
- The bitter character softens over the first 3 days
- Freeze in ice cube trays for mauby ice in other drinks
