# Antiguan Drinks

![Antiguan Drinks](resources/hero.jpg)

The Antiguan drinks tradition is built around three household brews that turn up at every island moment. Ginger beer is the everyday: fresh root grated, steeped in boiling water, sweetened with brown sugar and sharpened with lime, brewed by the gallon at Christmas and served cold over ice. Sorrel is the holiday drink, deep red and spiced, made from dried hibiscus calyces simmered with ginger, orange peel, cinnamon and cloves, sweetened heavily and served from Christmas Eve through New Year. Mauby is the acquired-taste bark tea, brewed from strips of mauby tree bark with cinnamon, cloves and bay leaf, kept just on the edge of bitter and drunk ice-cold from rum-shop fridges across the island. Each of the three has an adult version: sorrel mixed with dark Antiguan rum, mauby mixed with stout, ginger beer turned into a dark and stormy. Coconut water comes straight from the husk; sea moss is sold in plastic bottles at roadside stalls; cocoa tea is the breakfast hot drink from the cocoa balls grated into hot milk and spice. The Antiguan glass is sweet, cold, perfumed with island spice, and pulled out for every meal on the table.
