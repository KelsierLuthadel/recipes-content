# Atol de Elote

![Atol de Elote](resources/atol-de-elote.jpg)

*Sweet creamy hot drink of fresh corn blitzed with milk, cinnamon, vanilla and brown sugar, thickened by the corn itself: the Honduran corn-season breakfast drink that doubles as dessert.*

**Serves:** 4

**Prep Time:** 10 minutes

**Cook Time:** 25 minutes

## Overview
Atol de elote (or atole de elote, depending on which side of the Central American border you're standing on) is the Honduran (and broader Central American) sweet corn drink: fresh corn kernels blitzed with milk and water into a creamy purée, then simmered with cinnamon, vanilla and brown sugar until the corn's natural starches thicken everything into a pourable, slightly stringy hot drink. Served in mugs at breakfast during the August-October sweet corn season, especially in the highlands. Sweet enough to be a dessert but light enough for a morning drink; closer to a thin pudding than a coffee.

## Ingredients

- 4 ears fresh sweet corn (kernels stripped; about 500 g kernels)
- 500 ml whole milk
- 500 ml water
- 80 g dark brown sugar (or panela; adjust to taste)
- 1 cinnamon stick
- 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
- ½ teaspoon fine salt

### To serve
- Ground cinnamon (for dusting)

## Method

1. Combine the corn kernels and 250 ml of the milk in a blender; blitz for 60 seconds until smooth.
1. Strain the purée through a fine sieve into a heavy saucepan, pressing the pulp to extract everything liquid. Discard the solids.
1. Add the remaining 250 ml milk, the water, sugar, cinnamon stick, vanilla and salt.
1. Bring to a gentle simmer over medium-low heat, stirring constantly.
1. Continue cooking 15 to 18 minutes, stirring often, until the atol thickens to a thin pourable custard.
1. Remove the cinnamon stick.
1. Pour into warm mugs; dust with ground cinnamon.

## Notes
- **Fresh sweet corn, not tinned.** Frozen sweet corn works (thaw first); tinned is too soft and loses the texture.
- **Strain through a fine sieve.** Pittu skipping this step gives a stringy drink. The sieve removes corn fibres.

## Storage
- Best within an hour. Refrigerated atol thickens further; rewarm with a splash of milk to loosen.
