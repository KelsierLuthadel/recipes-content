# Jallab

![Jallab](resources/jallab.jpg)

*Levantine date-and-rose syrup poured over crushed ice, topped with rose water and a scatter of pine nuts and raisins: the Ramadan iftar drink, sweet and floral and properly cold.*

**Serves:** 4

**Prep Time:** 5 minutes

**Cook Time:** 0 minutes

## Overview
Jallab is the drink that comes out of every Levantine kitchen during Ramadan and shows up on Arabian street corners year-round: a syrup made from carob molasses, date molasses and rose water, diluted with cold water, poured over a glass full of crushed ice, finished with floating pine nuts and raisins on top. Some recipes use only date syrup, others a blend; either is correct. The drink hits sweet first, floral second, then the slight bitterness of carob keeps it from going cloying. Pine nuts at the top give a small textural contrast as you sip; the raisins plump up as they sit in the cold liquid.

## Ingredients

### Per jug (4 glasses)
- 6 tablespoons jallab syrup (a blend of carob and date molasses with rose; sold at Middle Eastern groceries, or substitute 4 tablespoons date syrup + 2 tablespoons carob molasses + 1 teaspoon rose water)
- 800 ml cold water
- Plenty of crushed ice

### To serve
- 2 tablespoons pine nuts (lightly toasted)
- 2 tablespoons raisins
- 1 teaspoon rose water (for the float)

## Method

### Stage 1 - Mix
1. Pour the jallab syrup (or your substitute blend) into a jug.
1. Add the cold water; stir to combine. The drink should be a dark amber colour.

### Stage 2 - Serve
1. Fill four tall glasses with crushed ice.
1. Pour the jallab over the ice.
1. Scatter pine nuts and raisins across the top; they'll float briefly before sinking.
1. Drop a few drops of rose water on top of each glass.
1. Serve immediately.

## Notes
- **Real jallab syrup is the easiest path.** Middle Eastern groceries stock it bottled; one bottle does a dozen drinks.
- **Toast the pine nuts first.** Two minutes in a dry pan; brings out the oils and adds depth.
- **Cold and crushed.** Ice cubes melt too fast and dilute; crushed ice is the canonical serve.

## Storage
- The diluted jallab keeps in the fridge for 48 hours; add fresh pine nuts and raisins per glass.
