# Roosi (Rose Soda)

![Roosi (Rose Soda)](resources/roosi-soda.jpg)

*The Estonian children's summer drink: homemade rose-petal syrup stirred into ice-cold sparkling water, garnished with lemon and a single fresh petal.*

**Serves:** Makes 6 glasses (and about 500 ml syrup)

**Prep Time:** 20 minutes

**Steeping Time:** 24 hours

**Cook Time:** 15 minutes

## Overview
Roosi is the Estonian rose-petal soda that has lived in summer fridges and garden parties since well before commercial fizzy drinks arrived. The recipe is two parts: a deeply fragrant pink syrup made from fresh rose petals, sugar, water and lemon juice, and a glass of cold sparkling water to dilute it. The petals are steeped overnight in lemony water (which extracts the colour and perfume), then briefly cooked with sugar into a thick pink syrup. A tablespoon in a tall glass of soda turns the water pale rose; the smell is unmistakably of rose garden in July. It is the children's drink at Estonian birthday parties and the non-alcoholic option at every summer wedding.

## Ingredients

### For the syrup
- 60 g fresh fragrant rose petals (about 4-5 fully open garden roses, unsprayed)
- 500 ml water
- Juice of 2 lemons
- 400 g caster sugar

### Per glass
- 2-3 tbsp rose syrup
- 250 ml ice-cold sparkling water
- A slice of lemon
- Ice cubes
- A single fresh rose petal to garnish (optional)

## Method

### Stage 1 - Prep the petals
1. Pick the petals from the roses; gently pull off and discard the bitter white base of each petal.
2. Rinse briefly in cold water; spread on a clean tea towel to drain.

### Stage 2 - Steep
1. In a large heatproof bowl combine the petals, the water and the lemon juice.
2. Press the petals down with a spoon; the liquid will turn pale pink almost immediately as the lemon juice draws out the colour.
3. Cover and stand at room temperature for 24 hours. The water will deepen to a clear pink and smell intensely of rose.

### Stage 3 - Cook into syrup
1. Strain the rose water into a clean pan, pressing the petals firmly. Discard the petals.
2. Add the sugar; warm over low heat, stirring, until the sugar dissolves.
3. Bring to a gentle simmer for 8-10 minutes until lightly thickened (it will thicken further as it cools).
4. Cool fully, then pour into a clean bottle.

### Stage 4 - Assemble the soda
1. Fill a tall glass with ice.
2. Spoon in 2-3 tbsp of the rose syrup.
3. Top up slowly with cold sparkling water; stir gently.
4. Drop in a slice of lemon and float a fresh petal on top if you have one.

## Notes
- **Unsprayed roses only:** Use garden roses you know have not been sprayed with pesticide or systemic plant treatment. Florist roses are not safe.
- **Choose for scent:** Old-fashioned heavily-scented varieties (gallicas, damasks, rugosas) give the best syrup. Hybrid teas often have little smell, and the syrup will reflect that.
- **The lemon trick:** Lemon juice in the steep is what releases the pink colour from the petals. Without it the water stays oddly grey-brown.
- **Stronger or weaker:** Adjust the syrup ratio per glass to taste; the 2-3 tbsp range is the Estonian standard but children often want it sweeter.

## Serving
Serve in tall glasses with ice and a lemon slice as a children's summer drink, a non-alcoholic party drink, or in a wine glass with a splash of sparkling wine instead of soda for a rose-petal aperitif.

## Storage
- The syrup keeps 1 month refrigerated in a clean bottle
- Freezes 6 months in ice cube trays (one cube per glass of soda)
- The finished soda must be drunk fresh; sparkling water goes flat within an hour
