# Drinks of Belgium

![Drinks of Belgium](resources/hero.jpg)

Belgium is one of the world's great drinks countries: home to over 1,500 distinct beers across Trappist abbey breweries, witbier wheat-beer traditions, lambic spontaneous-fermentation lambics, and pilsners that founded the modern style. Six certified Trappist breweries (Westvleteren, Chimay, Westmalle, Orval, Rochefort, Achel) produce some of the most sought-after beers on earth. Witbier (Hoegaarden, Blanche de Bruges) is the cloudy unfiltered Belgian wheat beer flavoured with orange peel and coriander. Genever, the malt-based grain spirit, is Belgium's contribution to the gin family and the historical ancestor of London dry gin. Belgian café tradition is a leisurely afternoon affair: a Trappist beer with paired glassware, perhaps a side of cheese cubes (kaaskubbekes) or a slice of pâté on bread, with the conversation winding past dusk.
