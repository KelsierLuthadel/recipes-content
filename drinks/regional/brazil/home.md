# Drinks of Brazil

![Drinks of Brazil](resources/hero.jpg)

Brazil's drinks reflect the country's tropical bounty and its complex spirit traditions. The caipirinha - cachaça, fresh lime, and sugar - is Brazil's national cocktail, drunk at every beach, churrasco, and bar from Rio de Janeiro to the Amazon. Cachaça (the sugarcane distillate that's distinct from rum) is the spirit of Brazil; the country produces over 1 billion litres a year, with hundreds of small-producer alembic versions worth seeking out. The non-alcoholic side is dominated by Guaraná Antarctica, the bright-yellow caffeinated soft drink made from the Amazon's guaraná berry; the açaí bowl, where frozen açaí pulp is blended into a thick purple slush; and the various fresh-fruit "sucos" (juices) that fill every Brazilian breakfast table.
