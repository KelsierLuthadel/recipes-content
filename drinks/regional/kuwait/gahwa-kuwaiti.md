# Gahwa Kuwaiti

![Gahwa Kuwaiti](resources/gahwa-kuwaiti.jpg)

*Kuwait's welcoming cup: pale lightly-roasted Arabic coffee simmered with cardamom and a thread of saffron, poured from the long-spouted dallah into tiny finjan cups beside a saucer of dates.*

**Serves:** 4 to 6 (makes about 600 ml)

**Prep Time:** 5 minutes

**Cook Time:** 15 minutes

## Overview
Gahwa in Kuwait is the first thing you're served when you visit a home and the last thing poured before you leave; the rhythm of arriving, sitting, sipping, and the cup tilt that signals enough is the shape of every social call. The Kuwaiti pour is paler than Saudi gahwa, leaning heavier on saffron and a touch lighter on cloves, the coffee itself lightly roasted Arabica ground coarse, simmered (not espresso-pressed) and served in small handleless porcelain cups called finjan. The host pours from the dallah holding it high, fills only one-third of each cup, and refills as the cups empty. Three cups is polite; tilting the empty cup side to side signals you're done.

## Ingredients

### Per dallah (600 ml)
- 750 ml cold water
- 3 tbsp lightly roasted Arabica coffee (coarsely ground; the proper Gulf blend is pale gold-brown)
- 1 tbsp green cardamom pods, lightly crushed
- 6 to 8 strands saffron (Kuwaiti pour leans saffron-forward)
- 2 cloves (optional)
- 1 tsp rose water (optional, added off heat)

### To serve
- Small handleless cups (finjan)
- A saucer of Medjool dates
- A small bowl of dried fruit and nuts

## Method

### Stage 1 - Simmer
1. Bring the water to a boil in a small saucepan.
2. Add the ground coffee and the saffron strands.
3. Drop to low; simmer gently 10 minutes. Don't stir hard; let the grounds settle.

### Stage 2 - Cardamom and finish
1. Add the crushed cardamom and the cloves if using.
2. Simmer another 3 to 5 minutes.
3. Off the heat, stir in the rose water if using.
4. Rest 2 minutes; the grounds settle.

### Stage 3 - Serve
1. Pour through a fine sieve into a warmed dallah (or any small spouted jug).
2. Fill the small cups one-third only; this is the proper Kuwaiti pour.
3. Serve alongside dates and dried fruit. Refill as cups empty.

## Notes
- **Light roast is the right roast.** Dark espresso-style beans give the wrong colour and flavour profile. Look for "Arabic coffee" or "Gulf coffee" blends.
- **The one-third pour** is custom; filling the cup is considered impolite in Kuwaiti hospitality.
- **Tilt to refuse.** Tilting the empty finjan side to side is the polite signal that you've had enough.

## Storage
- Best within an hour of brewing; the cardamom and saffron fade after that
