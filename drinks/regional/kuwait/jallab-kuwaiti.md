# Jallab Kuwaiti

![Jallab Kuwaiti](resources/jallab-kuwaiti.jpg)

*Kuwait's Ramadan iftar pour: date syrup and rose water over crushed ice with floating pine nuts and raisins, the cold-sweet drink that breaks the fast at sunset.*

**Serves:** 4

**Prep Time:** 5 minutes

**Cook Time:** 0 minutes

## Overview
Jallab is the iftar drink across the Gulf and Levant, and in Kuwait it arrives in tall glasses the moment the sunset call to prayer ends. The drink is built on date molasses (dibs), carob molasses, rose water and cold water, poured over crushed ice and finished with a scatter of toasted pine nuts and golden raisins that float briefly before settling. The Kuwaiti pour leans heavier on date syrup than the Levantine version, with a smaller hit of carob, and the rose water is more generous. Sweet first, floral second, a slight bitterness from the carob to balance, and the cold ice and pine nuts give the textural lift.

## Ingredients

### Per jug (4 tall glasses)
- 6 tbsp date syrup (dibs)
- 2 tbsp carob molasses (or 1 tbsp extra date syrup if you can't find carob)
- 1 tbsp rose water
- 800 ml cold water
- Plenty of crushed ice

### Topping
- 2 tbsp pine nuts, toasted
- 2 tbsp golden raisins
- A few extra drops of rose water for floating

## Method

### Stage 1 - Mix
1. In a jug, whisk date syrup, carob molasses and rose water until smooth.
2. Add the cold water; stir.
3. Taste; adjust sweetness or rose water.

### Stage 2 - Serve
1. Fill four tall glasses with crushed ice.
2. Pour the jallab over the ice.
3. Scatter pine nuts and raisins on top; they'll float briefly then sink.
4. Drop two or three drops of rose water on the surface of each glass.
5. Serve immediately with a long spoon.

## Notes
- **Toast the pine nuts.** Two minutes in a dry pan, watch them closely; toasted gives depth, raw is flat.
- **Crushed ice, not cubes.** Cubes melt too slowly and the drink stays too sweet at the bottom; crushed ice loosens it as you drink.
- **Date syrup quality matters.** Look for thick dark Iraqi or Saudi dibs at a Middle Eastern grocery; supermarket date syrup is thinner and less flavourful.

## Storage
- The mixed drink keeps 2 days in the fridge
- Add fresh pine nuts and raisins per pour; they soften and sink if left in the jug
