# Drinks of Kuwait

![Drinks of Kuwait](resources/hero.jpg)

Gahwa is the welcoming and farewell cup: pale cardamom-saffron coffee poured from a long-spouted dallah for arrivals and departures. Karak chai pours from every roadside diwaniya stand, strong black tea stewed with full-cream milk, cardamom and sugar. Jallab and laban anchor the Ramadan iftar table when the fast breaks at sunset. Kuwait is alcohol-free by law, and that strict no-alcohol culture shaped a deep non-alcoholic tradition: dried-lime (loomi) infusion as digestif, hibiscus and tamarind coolers for the summer heat.
