# Mugicha

![Mugicha](resources/mugicha.jpg)

*Cold roasted-barley tea: lightly roasted whole barley grains steeped in cold water for a few hours, served chilled from a big jug as Japan's universal summer drink.*

**Serves:** makes 1.5 litres

**Prep Time:** 2 minutes

**Cook Time:** 0 minutes (cold-brewed)

## Overview
Mugicha is the Japanese summer drink. There's a jug of it in every Japanese family fridge between May and September, and it's served at every restaurant, izakaya and home meal as the default cold drink. The build is cleanly simple: roasted whole barley grains (or pre-made mugicha tea bags) cold-brewed in water for several hours. The resulting tea is pale amber, faintly nutty-toasty, completely caffeine-free (so safe for kids and pregnancy), and surprisingly refreshing in heat. Bags from any Japanese grocery (Itoen, Yamamotoyama brands) are the standard at-home product. From-scratch with whole roasted barley grains is the same idea but takes slightly longer and gives a deeper toasted character.

## Ingredients

- 30 g roasted barley grains (mugicha tea, sold loose at Japanese groceries) OR 4 mugicha tea bags
- 1.5 litres cold water

### To serve
- Plenty of ice cubes
- Tall glasses or jug for serving from

## Method

### Stage 1 - Cold-brew
1. Place the barley (or tea bags) in a large heatproof jug.
1. Pour over 1.5 litres of cold water (filtered for best flavour).
1. Refrigerate at least 4 hours (overnight is ideal).
1. The water turns deep amber.

### Stage 2 - Strain and serve
1. Strain through a fine sieve (or remove the tea bags). Discard the spent barley.
1. Serve cold from the jug, pouring into tall glasses over ice or straight from the chilled jug.

## Notes
- **Cold-brew vs hot-brew.** Cold-brewing extracts a sweeter, milder mugicha; hot-brewing (10 minutes in boiling water) gives a more intensely toasted flavour. Both are correct; cold-brew is the summer way.
- **Caffeine-free.** Genuinely caffeine-free, so safe for children and pregnancy. Common to give to Japanese kids.
- **The water-to-barley ratio.** 1 part barley to 50 parts water (by weight) is the standard. More barley gives a stronger brew, less gives a weaker one.

## Variations
- **Hot mugicha (winter version).** Same barley, steeped in boiling water for 10 minutes; served hot. Less common but exists.
- **Genmai-mugicha.** Toasted brown rice added to the barley; nuttier.

## Storage
- Refrigerate up to 3 days. After that the tea picks up fridge smells; better to brew a fresh jug.
