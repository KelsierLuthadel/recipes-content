# Drinks of North Macedonia

![Drinks of North Macedonia](resources/hero.jpg)

North Macedonia's drinks are shaped by its rich wine and spirit traditions. Macedonian rakija, the traditional Balkan fruit brandy distilled from plums, grapes, or quinces, is the daily aperitif and the closer to every meal. Macedonian wines, particularly the Vranec red (a robust indigenous grape) and the Smederevka white, are increasingly recognised internationally; the Tikveš region produces over 70% of Macedonia's wine. Skopsko and Zlaten Dab are the traditional beers, both pale lagers. Boza (the fermented grain drink) and ayran (the salted yogurt drink) round out the non-alcoholic side.
