# Cricova Sparkling

![Cricova Sparkling](resources/cricova-sparkling.jpg)

*Sparkling wine from the Cricova cellars: Moldovan méthode champenoise produced in a 120-kilometre network of limestone tunnels north of Chișinău, served cold in flute glasses for any toast, name day, or wedding.*

**Serves:** 6 (a 750 ml bottle)

**Prep Time:** 10 minutes

**Cook Time:** None

## Overview
The Cricova cellars sit fifteen kilometres north of Chișinău, a former limestone quarry from the fifteenth century turned into the world's largest underground winery. The galleries run 120 kilometres long at depths of 60 to 80 metres, the constant 12°C and 97% humidity making it perfect for long bottle-ageing. The state collection holds over 1.3 million bottles, including pre-war Tokays, French wartime treasures captured from Germany in 1945, and bottles laid down for foreign dignitaries (Yuri Gagarin famously got lost in the cellars for two days in 1966 and was awarded a wine room in his name). The sparkling wine is the country's flagship: a méthode champenoise (sticla, the traditional method, with the second fermentation in the bottle) made from blends of Chardonnay, Pinot Noir, and indigenous Fetească grapes. Served chilled in a flute, it pours pale gold with a fine persistent bead.

## Ingredients

### Per serve
- 1 bottle (750 ml) Cricova brut or extra-brut sparkling wine
- 6 flute or tulip glasses, chilled
- A clean linen napkin

### For a toast service
- A bowl of mixed nuts (almonds, walnuts)
- Slices of mature brânză de oi or hard cheese
- A few thin slices of cured pork sausage (cârnați)

### For the wedding pour (per table)
- 2 bottles brut, chilled
- A bottle of dry Fetească Neagră in reserve for the main course

## Method

### Stage 1 - Chill the bottle
1. Refrigerate the Cricova bottle for at least 3 hours (6 hours is better).
2. The target serving temperature is 6 to 8°C; colder and the aromatics close down, warmer and the fizz goes flat fast.
3. An ice bucket with half water half ice will chill a room-temperature bottle in 25 minutes if you forgot.

### Stage 2 - Chill the glasses
1. Put the flute glasses in the freezer for 10 minutes (or fill with ice water, drained at pouring).
2. Cold glass keeps the bead alive longer.

### Stage 3 - Open the bottle
1. Hold the bottle at a 45-degree angle, away from any face.
2. Remove the foil cap fully.
3. Hold a folded napkin firmly over the cork.
4. With your other hand, loosen the wire cage (turn the small loop six half-turns to release) without removing it.
5. Grip the cork through the napkin; with the other hand twist the bottle (not the cork) slowly.
6. The cork should slip out with a quiet sigh, not a loud pop (a loud pop loses pressure and aromatic).

### Stage 4 - Pour
1. Tilt each flute slightly.
2. Pour the first 30 ml down the side; let the foam settle.
3. Top up to 125 ml per glass (about two-thirds full).
4. Return the bottle to the ice bucket between pours.

### Stage 5 - Toast
1. Hold the flute by the stem, not the bowl (warm hands flatten the bubbles).
2. The Moldovan toast: Noroc! or Sănătate!
3. Eye contact, clink, sip.
4. The first sip is short; the second judges; the third enjoys.

## Notes
- **Pop softly:** a loud pop is a wine fault, not a celebration; the proper opening is a quiet hiss.
- **Cold but not freezing:** 6 to 8°C is the target; below 4°C and the wine tastes thin and the bubbles vanish.
- **Hold the stem:** body heat transfers fast through a flute bowl and flattens the wine.
- **Flute or tulip:** both work; coupes are pretty but lose the fizz quickly.
- **Brut, not sweet:** Cricova make a range from extra-brut to demi-sec; brut is the default for a savoury meal.

## Variations
- **Cricova Rosé:** the pink version from Pinot Noir, summer drinking.
- **Cricova Crisecco (semi-dry):** with dessert.
- **Cricova Lacrima Dulce:** the sweet sparkling for cake.
- **Mimosa:** half-and-half with fresh orange juice for brunch.
- **Aperol spritz substitute:** 75 ml Cricova brut over ice with 50 ml Aperol and a splash of soda for a Moldovan-Italian crossover.

## Serving
In a chilled flute, with the first toast of a wedding or name-day meal. As an aperitif before sarmale or roast pork. With a wedge of brânză de oi and a few walnuts at the start of an evening. Alongside the Cușma lui Guguță cake for a birthday.

## Storage
- Unopened: refrigerate up to 1 year; the wine is ready to drink now.
- Opened: a sparkling-wine stopper, refrigerated, 24 hours; after that the bead is gone.
- An old champagne trick: a silver spoon in the neck of the bottle reportedly slows the fizz loss; in practice a proper stopper works better.
