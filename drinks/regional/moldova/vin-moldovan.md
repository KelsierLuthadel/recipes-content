# Vin Moldovan

![Vin Moldovan](resources/vin-moldovan.jpg)

*Moldovan wine: focused on the native Fetească Neagră grape, the deep dark red of the Codru hills, served at room temperature in a stem glass alongside any Moldovan meal that involves grilled pork, sarmale, or roast lamb.*

**Serves:** 4 (a 750 ml bottle)

**Prep Time:** 5 minutes

**Cook Time:** None

## Overview
Moldova plants vines on roughly one-twentieth of its entire surface area, one of the highest vineyard-to-area ratios on earth, and the country has been a wine producer for at least three thousand years (Greek amphorae and Roman wine presses turn up regularly in archaeological digs across Bessarabia). After a long Soviet stretch when the cellars were forced into bulk-blend production for the Moscow market, the indigenous grapes are now coming back into the bottle: Fetească Neagră (the Black Maiden), the deep aromatic black grape of the central Codru hills, gives the most distinctive red, with notes of dark cherry, dried plum, and a country-herb finish that matches the heavy pork plates of the cuisine. Fetească Albă (the White Maiden) and Fetească Regală (the Royal Maiden) make crisp aromatic whites for fish, chicken, and summer afternoons.

## Ingredients

### For a Moldovan Sunday lunch (per person)
- 1 (75 ml) glass of dry Fetească Albă (white, for the first course and salads)
- 1 (175 ml) glass of dry Fetească Neagră (red, for the main course)
- 1 (50 ml) glass of țuică (plum brandy, before the meal)
- Optional: 1 glass of sparkling Cricova for a toast

### Suggested wines
- Fetească Neagră (Cricova, Purcari, or Asconi)
- Fetească Albă (Castel Mimi or Crama Mircești)
- Rara Neagră (the lighter Moldovan red, from the south)
- Saperavi (the dark Georgian grape, also widely planted)

## Method

### Stage 1 - Hold the bottle properly
1. Store Moldovan red on its side in a cool dark place at 12 to 15°C.
2. Bring out of the cellar 1 hour before serving to come up to 16 to 18°C (cellar warm, not room hot).
3. The white wine should be served at 8 to 10°C; chill 2 hours in the fridge.

### Stage 2 - Open and serve the red
1. Cut the foil cleanly below the lip of the bottle.
2. Draw the cork with a waiter's friend; smell the cork (should smell of clean wood, not damp cardboard).
3. Pour a small splash into the host's glass; taste; nod.
4. Pour 175 ml into each guest's glass (about a third full of a wine glass).
5. Hold the bottle by the body, not the neck, to keep the wine still.

### Stage 3 - Serve the white
1. Open the chilled white the same way.
2. Pour 125 ml into each glass.
3. Return the bottle to an ice bucket between pours.

### Stage 4 - Match to the plate
1. **Sarmale, tochitură, roast pork:** dry Fetească Neagră, room temperature.
2. **Zeamă, baked chicken, grilled trout:** dry Fetească Albă or Regală, cold.
3. **Plăcintă cu brânză dulce or other desserts:** an off-dry late-harvest white (Pelin or a sweet Tămâioasă).
4. **Cheese course:** the red continued; a small cube of brânză de oi with each sip.

### Stage 5 - The toast
1. The Moldovan toast at the meal's start is Noroc (luck) or Sănătate (health).
2. Eye contact, glasses clinked, sip together.

## Notes
- **Fetească Neagră is the signature:** taste it before reaching for the imported Cabernet; the country is built on this grape.
- **Cellar temperature is correct:** never serve Moldovan red at 22°C kitchen temperature; the alcohol burns.
- **The white is colder than you think:** 8°C is correct; warmer and the Fetească aromatics flatten.
- **The first taste is short:** swirl, nose, sip a small mouthful; the second sip judges.
- **Cricova underground:** the country's most-visited winery; if you can visit, do.

## Variations
- **Purcari Negru de Purcari:** the historic estate blend (Cabernet, Saperavi, Rara Neagră), the bottle Stalin reportedly favoured.
- **Cricova Cabernet Sauvignon:** the international-grape version, for Western palates.
- **Tămâioasă Românească:** sweet white, dessert wine.
- **Pelin:** wormwood-infused wine, the medieval bitter spring tonic.
- **Sparkling Cricova:** see the separate recipe.

## Serving
At any Moldovan family meal as a matter of course. Red with sarmale, tochitură, mititei. White with zeamă, river fish, summer salads. Sparkling Cricova for the toast. A small țuică shot to open the meal.

## Storage
- Unopened bottles: a cool dark cellar, indefinite.
- Opened red: a stopper, 3 days at cellar temperature.
- Opened white: a stopper, 2 days refrigerated.
