# Svařák (Czech Mulled Wine)

![Svařák](resources/svarak.jpg)

*Czech mulled wine: red wine warmed with cinnamon, cloves, citrus and a splash of rum, served by the mug from steaming pots at every Czech Christmas market. Spicier than glühwein, less sweet than vin chaud, distinctively Czech.*

**Serves:** 6 mugs

**Prep Time:** 10 minutes

**Cook Time:** 20 minutes

## Overview
Svařák (svařené víno - "boiled wine") is the Czech version of mulled wine, the steaming hot red-wine drink that defines Czech Christmas markets from late November through January. The Czech version is sharper and spicier than German glühwein - less sweetened, with more cloves, allspice and orange peel, and traditionally finished with a small slug of dark rum (sometimes called "svařák s rumem"). It's poured into ceramic mugs at outdoor market stalls in Prague, Brno, Český Krumlov and every Czech town, sipped while wandering past wooden stalls selling roasted nuts, gingerbread, glassware and Christmas decorations. The smell of svařák and roasting trdelník is the Czech Christmas memory.

## Ingredients
- 750 ml medium-bodied red wine (a Czech Frankovka or a cheaper Cabernet Sauvignon - you don't want anything fancy, but you do want something drinkable)
- 1 large orange, sliced into rounds
- 1 lemon, sliced into rounds
- 100 g caster sugar or 80 ml clear honey (adjust to taste)
- 1 cinnamon stick
- 6 whole cloves
- 4 whole allspice berries
- 1 star anise (optional)
- 2 cardamom pods, lightly crushed (optional)
- 2 tbsp dark rum (optional, but traditional)
- 1 tsp vanilla extract

### To serve
- 6 extra orange slices for garnish
- 6 extra cinnamon sticks (one per mug, optional)

## Method

### Stage 1 - Combine
1. In a heavy non-reactive saucepan (enamel-lined or stainless steel, not aluminium), combine the red wine, orange slices, lemon slices, sugar (or honey), cinnamon stick, cloves, allspice, star anise (if using) and cardamom pods (if using).
2. Stir to dissolve the sugar.

### Stage 2 - Heat gently
1. Place over medium-low heat.
2. Bring to just below a simmer - the surface should be steaming but not bubbling.
3. Don't boil - boiling drives off the alcohol and turns the wine harsh.
4. Hold at this gentle warmth 15-20 minutes.
5. The flavour deepens; the citrus oils release; the spices infuse.

### Stage 3 - Finish
1. Off the heat, stir in the rum (if using) and the vanilla.
2. Taste; adjust sweetness with more sugar or honey if needed.

### Stage 4 - Strain or serve as-is
1. Strain through a fine sieve into a warm jug, discarding the spices and used fruit.
2. Or ladle directly from the pot, leaving the spices in (each mug gets a piece of citrus and a cinnamon stick).

### Stage 5 - Serve in warm mugs
1. Pre-warm the mugs by running them under hot water; dry.
2. Pour the svařák into the mugs.
3. Garnish each with a fresh orange slice and a cinnamon stick on the rim.
4. Serve hot.

## Notes
- **Don't boil the wine:** Boiling burns off the alcohol within seconds and the wine goes thin and stewed. Keep at a gentle steaming temperature.
- **Everyday drinkable wine is the right choice:** Don't waste good wine on mulling. Any decent supermarket red works. Avoid both bottom-shelf vinegar and expensive complex wines.
- **Czech vs German style:** Glühwein leans towards sweeter and milder; svařák has more spice (especially clove and allspice) and is less sweet. The rum addition is the Czech finishing touch.

## Serving
Christmas market stalls in Prague and Brno. Christmas Eve at home before dinner. New Year's Eve from a thermos on a winter walk. Always with a piece of Christmas gingerbread or a sweet pastry alongside.

## Storage
- The infused wine keeps refrigerated 5 days; reheats well over low heat.
- Make a large batch and refrigerate; reheat per serving in a small saucepan.
- Don't store with the citrus left in for more than 1 day - the bitter pith leaches out.
