# Drinks of Laos

![Drinks of Laos](resources/hero.jpg)

Laos's drinks reflect three distinct strands of the country's history: the French-colonial coffee tradition (café Lao with sweetened condensed milk and a thick layer of crema, brewed in tin Vietnamese-style drippers and poured over ice in tall glasses), the tropical-fruit-and-palm-sugar street drinks (nam mak nao - the traditional Lao limeade with palm sugar and a pinch of salt - sold from carts at every Lao market in the hot months), and the Lao traditional herbal-tea tradition (nam khing - ginger and palm-sugar warming infusion - drunk at home from morning to evening). Beerlao is the traditional national beer; lao-lao the rice spirit (often homemade, often quite rough); the Mekong-side soft-drink stalls round out the daily picture. Sticky-rice culture extends to drinks too - sticky-rice wine and the slightly funky Lao rice-water beverage that follows the traditional sticky-rice steam.
