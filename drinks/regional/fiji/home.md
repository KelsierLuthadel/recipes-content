# Drinks of Fiji

![Drinks of Fiji](resources/hero.jpg)

Fijian drinks are built around three traditions. The first is yaqona (kava), the ceremonial pounded root mixed with cold water, served from a wooden tanoa bowl in coconut-shell cups and drunk in single rounds at the centre of every village gathering, welcome and meeting. The second is bu, the young drinking coconut chopped open with a single bush-knife stroke and drunk straight from the husk through a paper straw, sold from roadside stalls all across Viti Levu and Vanua Levu. The third is the bush tea tradition that came in with the indentured Indian labourers and married into Fijian village life: fresh lemon leaves, lemongrass and ginger steeped in hot water and sweetened with raw sugar, drunk at any hour from a tin cup. Add to these the Indo-Fijian milky tea and the local Fiji Bitter beer and you have the drinks table of a Fijian household.
