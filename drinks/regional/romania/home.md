# Romanian Drinks

![Romanian Drinks](resources/hero.jpg)

Romania drinks plum first. Țuică, the clear plum brandy distilled in copper stills behind every rural barn in late autumn, is the welcoming pour, the host's first courtesy and the first taste of any meal, served chilled in tiny glasses with a slice of smoked bacon. The pelin tradition runs alongside it, the wormwood-infused wine drunk on Trifon Day (February 1st, the vine-pruner's festival), a green-bitter spring tonic from the long medieval pharmacopoeia. The wine country is rising again: the limestone slopes around Murfatlar in Dobruja on the Black Sea, the chalk of Cotnari in Moldavia, the volcanic soils of Dealu Mare, all bottling the indigenous Fetească Neagră and Fetească Albă grapes after a long Soviet dark age. In the Saxon towns of Transylvania (Sibiu, Brașov, Mediaș) the beer halls pour pale lagers in the German manner, the brewers' guilds going back five centuries.
