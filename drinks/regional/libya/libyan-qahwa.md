# Libyan Qahwa

![Libyan Qahwa](resources/libyan-qahwa.jpg)

*The morning and evening coffee of Libya: dark, thick and perfumed with freshly crushed cardamom, served in small cups with a glass of cold water and a date alongside.*

**Serves:** 4 small cups

**Prep Time:** 5 minutes

**Cook Time:** 10 minutes

## Overview
Libyan qahwa is the country's everyday coffee, brewed in a small long-handled pot the locals call a tanaka or a kanaka, and pulled to the very edge of boiling three times to build the thick brown crema known as wajh, the face. The grind is fine, almost like flour, the beans are darkly roasted, and the seasoning is freshly crushed green cardamom (about one pod per cup) that goes in with the grounds at the start. Sugar is added to the pot, not to the cup, so every guest takes the same sweetness; the host asks before pouring whether you want it sada (plain), mazboot (medium) or hilou (sweet). The coffee is served in small handleless cups, with a glass of cold water and a single date to balance the bitterness. It marks the start of the morning, the close of dinner and the centre of any visit.

## Ingredients

- 4 tbsp finely ground dark roast Arabic coffee
- 4 green cardamom pods
- 350 ml cold water
- 1-3 tsp sugar (to taste; see Notes)
- Optional: 1 clove or a tiny pinch of saffron
- To serve: 4 small handleless cups, 4 glasses of cold water, 4 medjool dates

## Method

### Stage 1 - Crush the cardamom
1. Lightly crush the green cardamom pods with the flat of a knife until they split.
2. Tip pods and seeds into the small coffee pot.

### Stage 2 - Build the pot
1. Add the coffee, sugar, and the cold water to the pot.
2. Stir once with a small spoon to settle the grounds.

### Stage 3 - Three boils
1. Set the pot over medium-low heat.
2. Watch closely; as the foam rises to the rim, lift the pot off the heat for 5 seconds, then return it.
3. Repeat twice more; three rises in total build the thick wajh crema.
4. Do not let it boil over; the foam is the point.

### Stage 4 - Rest and pour
1. Take the pot off the heat.
2. Tap the side gently twice to settle the grounds.
3. Wait 30 seconds.
4. Pour slowly into the small cups, holding back the grounds at the spout.
5. Each cup should have a small layer of foam on top.

### Stage 5 - Serve
1. Set a glass of cold water and a medjool date beside each cup.
2. Sip slowly; bite the date between sips.

## Notes
- **Grind fine:** Arabic coffee is ground almost to a powder; espresso grind is too coarse and the coffee tastes thin.
- **Three boils, not one:** the lift-and-return is what builds the crema. One boil gives flat bitter coffee.
- **Sugar level:** Libyans usually ask before pouring. Sada (no sugar) is 0 teaspoons, mazboot (medium) is about 1 teaspoon per pot, hilou (sweet) is 2-3 teaspoons. Add at the start so it dissolves with the grounds.
- **Drink slowly:** let the grounds settle in the bottom of the cup; stop drinking when you taste sediment.

## Variations
- Add a single clove for a wintry version.
- Add a small pinch of ground ginger for an eastern Libyan touch.
- Add a tiny pinch of saffron threads at the third boil for a festive cup.
- Replace one cardamom pod with a pinch of mastic for a Gulf-style version.
- Brew without sugar and pass a dish of honeyed dates alongside.

## Serving
- First thing in the morning before breakfast · after lunch and after dinner · the moment a guest arrives, with a plate of dates · with a piece of ghraybeh or a square of basbousa · at a wedding or funeral as the welcoming drink.

## Storage
- Brew fresh; Arabic coffee does not keep.
- Ground coffee keeps 2 weeks in an airtight tin out of the light; whole beans keep 2 months.
- Cardamom pods keep 6 months whole; crush only what you need.
