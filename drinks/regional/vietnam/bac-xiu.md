# Bạc Xỉu (Vietnamese Coconut Coffee)

![Bạc Xỉu](resources/bac-xiu.jpg)

*Saigon's gentler take on iced coffee: less coffee than a cà phê sữa đá, more milk, often blended with frozen coconut milk to make a thick, slushy, faintly coconut-flavoured slushie. Modern Vietnamese cafés sometimes top it with whipped cream or shaved coconut.*

**Serves:** 2 tall glasses

**Prep Time:** 3 minutes

**Cook Time:** 5 minutes

## Overview
Bạc xỉu (the name is a Vietnamese phonetic adaptation of the Cantonese "白糖", meaning roughly "white sugar / pale") is a Saigon (Ho Chi Minh City) speciality, originating in the Chinese-Vietnamese coffee shops of the city's Cholon district. Where cà phê sữa đá leans heavily on the strong dark Vietnamese drip coffee with a slick of sweetened condensed milk underneath, bạc xỉu inverts the ratio: it's mostly sweetened condensed milk with just enough coffee to colour and flavour it, served over ice or in modern cafés blended with frozen coconut milk into a thick coffee-flecked slushie. The coconut version (bạc xỉu dừa) is particularly popular and pairs the gentle Vietnamese coffee with the tropical coconut flavour. It's the drink you order if cà phê sữa đá is too strong for you, or as a dessert-drink in the afternoon. The Saigon version is now spreading north into Hanoi and across modern Vietnamese cafés worldwide, often as a frozen coconut-coffee slushie alongside a more traditional drip cà phê.

## Ingredients

- 1 tablespoon strong Vietnamese ground coffee (Trung Nguyên brand; espresso-grind dark Robusta)
- 80 ml just-off-the-boil water
- 4 tablespoons sweetened condensed milk
- 200 ml coconut milk (the tinned kind; full-fat for richness)
- 2 tablespoons caster sugar (to taste; less if condensed milk is sweet enough)
- 4 large handfuls of ice cubes (about 500 g)
- Pinch of fine salt

### To serve
- 2 tall glasses, chilled
- Optional: 2 tablespoons shaved fresh coconut OR a swirl of whipped cream
- Optional: a small mint sprig per glass

## Method

### Stage 1 - Brew strong coffee
1. Brew a small batch of Vietnamese coffee: 1 tablespoon of grounds in a phin filter (or small French press, or 1 shot espresso) with 80 ml of just-off-the-boil water. Let it drip / steep to a strong concentrated brew (about 60 ml of finished coffee).

### Stage 2 - Mix with condensed milk
1. While the coffee is still warm, stir in the sweetened condensed milk until completely combined. The mixture turns a uniform creamy beige.
1. Let cool to room temperature, then refrigerate 10 minutes if you have time.

### Stage 3 - Build the slushie
1. Pour the cooled coffee-milk mixture into a high-powered blender.
1. Add the coconut milk, sugar, salt and ice cubes.
1. Blitz on high for 60-90 seconds until thick, slushy, and pale-tan in colour. The texture should be like a thick milkshake.

### Stage 4 - Taste and adjust
1. Taste: it should be sweet, faintly coffee-flavoured (not bitter), distinctly coconut, very cold. Adjust sugar if too coffee-sharp; add a splash of coconut milk if too thick.

### Stage 5 - Serve
1. Pour into chilled tall glasses.
1. Top with shaved coconut or a swirl of whipped cream if using; garnish with a small mint sprig.
1. Serve immediately with a wide straw or a long spoon.

## Notes
- **The milk-heavy ratio.** Bạc xỉu is mostly milk and condensed milk; the coffee is just a flavour and colour accent. If you make it strong enough to taste like proper coffee, you've made a cà phê sữa đá by mistake.
- **Coconut milk choice.** Full-fat tinned coconut milk gives the right richness; light or carton coconut milk gives a thin, sad drink. Real cream of coconut (Coco López) is too sweet for this; tinned full-fat is right.
- **Cold ingredients.** Coffee chilled, coconut milk fridge-cold, ice blended in. The drink should be deeply cold from the first sip.
- **Pinch of salt.** Salt amplifies the sweetness and rounds the coconut. Skip and the drink can feel one-note.

## Variations
- **Bạc xỉu nóng (hot version).** Just the coffee mixed with hot frothed sweetened condensed milk and a splash of warm coconut milk. Served in a mug. The winter / morning version.
- **With coconut cream layer.** Pour the blended bạc xỉu into a glass, then float 2 tablespoons of thick coconut cream on top. Visual two-tone, sweeter.
- **Without coconut.** Just coffee, condensed milk, and ice, blended. The classic non-coconut bạc xỉu, a Saigon dessert-drink before the coconut version became popular.
- **Pandan bạc xỉu.** Add a knotted pandan leaf to the coffee while brewing. Faintly grassy-floral; modern café variant.

## Storage
- Doesn't store; the blended drink separates within 30 minutes. The base (coffee mixed with condensed milk) keeps 3 days in the fridge as a syrup; blend with coconut milk and ice on demand.
