# Drinks of Canada

![Drinks of Canada](resources/hero.jpg)

Canada's drink traditions are coast-to-coast distinct: the Caesar (clamato + vodka + spicy seasonings + a celery stalk in a Worcestershire-and-celery-salt-rimmed glass) is the national cocktail, invented in Calgary in 1969 and consumed by the case at every Canadian brunch. The London Fog (Earl Grey + steamed milk + vanilla syrup) was invented in Vancouver in the 1990s and has spread across the country as the Canadian winter coffee-shop staple. Hot maple toddies and maple-laced winter cocktails surface every December from Quebec to British Columbia. Tim Hortons coffee is the national caffeine staple, drunk by the million-cups-a-day from cars on every Canadian highway. Indigenous and Métis drink traditions include Labrador tea (made from the native ledum plant) and the Yukon Sour-Toe cocktail (Dawson City, with the famous severed-toe garnish, not a recipe to attempt at home).
