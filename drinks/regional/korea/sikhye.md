# Sikhye

![Sikhye](resources/sikhye.jpg)

*Korea's other traditional punch: a sweet, lightly fermented drink made by infusing malt barley powder into warm cooked rice until enzymes break the starches into sugars, served chilled with whole soft rice grains floating in pale-yellow liquid.*

**Serves:** 8 cups

**Prep Time:** 15 minutes

**Cook Time:** 6 hours (mostly unattended fermentation)

## Overview
Sikhye is the other half of Korea's traditional drink pairing alongside sujeonggwa. Where sujeonggwa is dark, spiced and bold, sikhye is pale, sweet and subtle. It's made by warming a slurry of cooked short-grain rice and yeotgireum (powdered malt barley) at body temperature for 5 to 6 hours so the diastase enzymes in the malt convert the rice starches into maltose. The result is a clear, golden, faintly sweet liquid with soft rice grains floating on top: the rice is reserved separately during cooking, then added back at serving for the iconic floating grain effect. Served deeply chilled, it's a traditional after-meal palate cleanser and the standard drink at Korean ceremonial meals, jesa (ancestor rites), and Lunar New Year. Convenience stores in Korea sell it in cold bottles; the homemade version is gentler and more honest.

## Ingredients

- 200 g short-grain rice (Korean sushi rice or sticky rice)
- 240 ml water (for cooking the rice)
- 100 g yeotgireum (powdered malt barley, sold at Korean groceries)
- 2 litres warm water
- 150 g caster sugar
- 1 small piece of fresh ginger, sliced (optional, for warmth)

### To serve
- 2 tablespoons pine nuts
- A few jujubes (red dates), thinly sliced

## Method

### Stage 1 - Cook the rice
1. Wash the rice in cold water until the water runs clear.
1. Cook with 240 ml water in a rice cooker or saucepan until tender (about 20 minutes). The rice should be soft but the grains intact.
1. Let it cool until just warm.

### Stage 2 - Steep the malt
1. Put the yeotgireum into a large bowl. Add 2 litres of cold water and stir well.
1. Let stand 1 hour. The starch will settle to the bottom; the cloudy liquid on top is what you want.
1. Carefully pour off the clear-cloudy liquid into another bowl, leaving the gritty sediment behind. Discard the sediment.

### Stage 3 - Combine and ferment
1. Pour the strained malt liquid into a large slow-cooker pot, or a stainless-steel pot kept warm in a turned-off oven.
1. Add the cooked rice; stir gently to break up clumps.
1. Keep the mixture at body temperature (about 55 to 60°C) for 5 to 6 hours. A slow cooker on "keep warm" is the easy way; an oven preheated to 60°C then switched off works too.
1. After 4 hours, individual rice grains will start to float to the surface; this is the signal the enzymes are working.

### Stage 4 - Sweeten and chill
1. Once most of the rice grains are floating, fish them out with a sieve and reserve in a separate bowl in the fridge.
1. Strain the remaining liquid through a fine sieve into a saucepan.
1. Add the sugar (and ginger if using). Bring to the boil for 5 minutes to stop the fermentation and dissolve the sugar.
1. Remove from the heat, fish out the ginger, and cool to room temperature.
1. Refrigerate at least 4 hours.

### Stage 5 - Serve
1. Ladle the chilled liquid into small bowls or glass cups.
1. Top each with a spoonful of the reserved rice grains.
1. Scatter pine nuts and a thin slice of jujube on top.

## Notes
- **Temperature is everything.** Diastase enzymes work between 50 and 65°C. Hotter kills them; cooler is too slow. A slow cooker on "keep warm" is the most reliable home setup.
- **Cook the brew at the end.** Boiling for 5 minutes stops the fermentation, kills the enzymes, dissolves the sugar, and prevents the drink turning sour in the fridge.
- **Reserve the rice separately.** Adding it back at serving (rather than chilling everything together) keeps the rice grains floating instead of sodden.
- **Yeotgireum quality.** Use Korean malt barley powder, not malt syrup or extract. Korean groceries (or Amazon) sell it labelled "malt barley powder" or 엿기름.

## Variations
- **With ginger.** A few coins of fresh ginger added during the final sweetening boil gives a warmer winter version.
- **Pure rice version.** Skip the floating rice topping for a clearer drink; the texture stays the same.

## Storage
- The chilled drink keeps 5 days in the fridge in a sealed jug. The reserved rice keeps 3 days separately; add to each cup as you serve.
