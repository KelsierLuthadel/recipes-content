# Drinks of Angola

![Drinks of Angola](resources/hero.jpg)

Angolan drinks split between the savanna villages and the Luanda coast. Múcua juice, made from the powdery flesh of the baobab fruit soaked in water with lime and sugar, is the cooling daily drink of the interior; kissangua, a fermented maize-and-pineapple low-alcohol brew, is the traditional village welcome. The Portuguese colonial legacy runs strong on the coast: bottled Cuca beer, vinho verde and bagaço (grape distillate) shaped the bar culture of Luanda, while the wider Lusophone-Atlantic exchange brought the caipirinha tradition across from Brazil, made here with locally distilled sugarcane spirit, fresh lime and sugar.
