# Múcua Juice

![Múcua Juice](resources/mucua-juice.jpg)

*Angolan baobab-fruit juice: the dry powdery fruit pulp soaked in cold water, strained, sweetened with sugar and brightened with lime; the cooling drink of the savanna.*

**Serves:** 4

**Prep Time:** 15 minutes (plus 1 hour soaking)

**Cook Time:** None

## Overview
Múcua is the Angolan name for the fruit of the baobab tree (called the imbondeiro in the south of the country) and the juice made from it is the everyday cooling drink of the savanna interior. The fruit looks unusual: a velvety green-grey pod the size of a small melon, cracked open to reveal hard white-yellow chunks of dry pulp covering small kidney-shaped seeds. The pulp is naturally tangy (a little lemony, a little powdery) and rich in vitamin C, and traditionally it is soaked in cold water for an hour to soften, mashed through a sieve to separate the pulp from the seeds, and the resulting cloudy juice sweetened with sugar and sharpened with lime. Drunk ice-cold from a clay jug it is the proper rural mid-afternoon drink. Baobab powder (sold in African and health-food shops) is the off-the-shelf substitute and works fine.

## Ingredients

- 100 g múcua pulp (or 4 tbsp baobab powder; African and health-food shops sell it)
- 1 litre cold water
- 4-6 tbsp caster sugar (to taste)
- 1 lime, juiced
- Ice cubes
- A pinch of salt

## Method

### Stage 1 - Soak
1. If starting from chunks of dry pulp: break the pulp into small pieces in a wide bowl, cover with the cold water, and leave to soak 1 hour.
2. If starting from powder: stir into the water and let sit 15 minutes.

### Stage 2 - Mash and strain
1. Mash the soaked pulp into the water with the back of a spoon to release the flavour.
2. Strain through a fine sieve into a jug, pressing the pulp with the back of a spoon; discard the fibres and seeds.
3. The juice will look cloudy and pale cream-coloured.

### Stage 3 - Sweeten
1. Stir in the sugar to taste; start with 4 tbsp and add more if you like it sweet.
2. Add the lime juice and the pinch of salt; stir until the sugar dissolves.

### Stage 4 - Chill
1. Refrigerate at least 30 minutes.

### Stage 5 - Serve
1. Pour over ice cubes in tall glasses.
2. Stir before each pour; the pulp settles to the bottom of the jug as it sits.

## Notes
- **The pulp is dry:** Unlike a tropical fruit, baobab pulp is dry and powdery in the pod. Soaking in cold water is the only way to extract the flavour; hot water gives a slightly bitter drink.
- **Adjust the sugar:** Some Angolans like it sharp and just-sweet, others like it dessert-sweet. Start at the low end and add to taste.
- **The settling:** The pulp settles in the jug as it sits; this is normal. Stir before pouring each glass.

## Variations
**With ginger:** Add 1 tsp grated fresh ginger to the soaking water for a warming version.
**With coconut water:** Replace half the water with coconut water for a richer drink.
**With mint:** Add a few mint leaves to the jug; lifts the savanna flavour with a fresh top note.

## Serving
The cooling afternoon drink. Served in tall glasses with ice; a slice of lime on the rim. Alongside spicy stew or grilled meat for the cooling counterpoint.

## Storage
- Keeps 3 days refrigerated in a covered jug.
- Stir before serving; the pulp settles.
- Don't freeze.
