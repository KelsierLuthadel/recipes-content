# Caipirinha Angolan

![Caipirinha Angolan](resources/caipirinha-angolan.jpg)

*Angolan-style caipirinha: lime wedges muddled with sugar in a heavy glass, topped with locally distilled sugarcane spirit and crushed ice; the Lusophone-Atlantic cousin of the Brazilian original.*

**Serves:** 1

**Prep Time:** 5 minutes

**Cook Time:** None

## Overview
The caipirinha crossed the Atlantic to Angola with the Lusophone-Atlantic exchange and has settled in as the standard Friday-evening cocktail of Luanda and the coastal bars. The construction is the same as the Brazilian original (lime wedges muddled with sugar, sugarcane spirit poured over, glass filled with crushed ice) but the Angolan version is built around a locally distilled cane spirit (kaporoto, the artisan Angolan cane distillate sold along the coast, or any Brazilian cachaça as the import). Many Angolan bars are slightly more generous with the lime and a little less with the sugar than the Brazilian default, giving a sharper, drier drink. A few mint leaves crushed in with the lime is a common local touch.

## Ingredients

### Per drink
- 1 fresh lime, cut into 8 wedges, the white central pith removed
- 2 tsp caster sugar (some Angolan bars use slightly less than the Brazilian standard)
- 60 ml kaporoto (Angolan sugarcane spirit) or cachaça
- Crushed ice
- A heavy old-fashioned glass (200-250 ml)
- A wooden muddler

### Optional
- 3-4 fresh mint leaves
- An extra wedge of lime for the rim

## Method

### Stage 1 - Lime
1. Cut the lime in half lengthways.
2. Cut each half into 4 wedges (8 wedges total).
3. With a small knife, remove the white central pith running along each wedge; this is the bitter part.
4. Place the wedges in a heavy old-fashioned glass.

### Stage 2 - Muddle
1. Add the sugar (and mint leaves if using) over the lime.
2. Using a wooden muddler, press and twist 8-12 times.
3. The goal is to release the lime juice, release the essential oils from the lime skin, and partially dissolve the sugar.
4. Stop before the lime turns to mush; the wedges should still be visible.

### Stage 3 - Spirit
1. Pour the 60 ml kaporoto or cachaça over the muddled lime and sugar.
2. Stir briefly with the muddler to combine.

### Stage 4 - Ice
1. Fill the glass with crushed ice.
2. Stir briefly to chill and combine.

### Stage 5 - Serve
1. Serve immediately with a short straw.
2. Garnish with a wedge of lime on the rim if using.

## Notes
- **Kaporoto, cachaça or a clean rum:** Kaporoto is the Angolan sugarcane spirit, sold along the coast; cachaça is the Brazilian original and works perfectly; a clean white rum is the international fallback (the result is closer to a daiquiri than a caipirinha but still good).
- **Pith out:** The bitter white pith ruins the drink. A few seconds with a knife is worth it.
- **Sharper, less sweet:** Many Angolan bars run the drink slightly drier than the Brazilian default. Start with 2 tsp sugar; add more only if you find it too sharp.

## Variations
**Kaporoto de coco:** Top with a splash of fresh coconut water for a tropical Atlantic version.
**Caipirinha de múcua:** Stir a teaspoon of baobab powder into the muddled lime and sugar before adding the spirit; the cocktail-bar Luanda variant.
**Caipirinha de maracujá:** Add the pulp of one passion fruit to the muddle.

## Serving
At every Luanda bar at sunset. At a coastal restaurant alongside grilled fish. At a Friday-evening welcome on the veranda. At a wedding reception or party.

## Storage
- Drink fresh; best within 10 minutes of making.
- Spirit bottles keep indefinitely.
- Don't batch in advance; the lime turns bitter and the dilution suffers.
