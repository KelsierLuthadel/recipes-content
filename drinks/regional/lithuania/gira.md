# Gira

![Gira](resources/gira.jpg)

*The everyday Lithuanian summer drink: dark rye bread fermented with water, sugar, raisins and yeast into a faintly fizzy, mildly sour, dark-amber refresher poured cold by the glass from every roadside kiosk in July.*

**Serves:** Makes 2 litres (8 glasses)

**Prep Time:** 20 minutes (plus 2-3 days fermentation)

**Cook Time:** 30 minutes (drying the bread)

## Overview
Gira is the Lithuanian version of the wider Slavic kvass, a lightly-fermented soft drink made from toasted rye bread that has been the everyday non-alcoholic refresher of the Baltic and Slavic world for over a thousand years. The Lithuanian version leans darker and slightly sweeter than its Russian or Ukrainian neighbours, often with a handful of raisins added to encourage a deeper second fermentation and a brighter bubble. In Lithuanian summers, gira is everywhere: roadside kiosks fill plastic cups from yellow barrels, supermarkets stock it in plastic bottles by the wall, every grandmother has a jug in the cellar. The flavour is unmistakable, dark malty rye, faint yeast tang, gentle sweetness, refreshing acid. Alcohol content sits very low (well under 1.5%), so it's drunk by everyone, including children, throughout the hot months. Home-made gira is significantly better than commercial.

## Ingredients

### For the base
- 500 g dense dark rye bread (Lithuanian or Russian-style sourdough)
- 2 litres hot water
- 150 g caster sugar (or honey for a softer version)
- 7 g instant dried yeast (1 sachet)
- 80 g raisins
- Zest of 1 lemon (optional)
- 2 sprigs fresh mint (optional)

### To serve
- Ice cubes
- Mint sprigs

## Method

### Stage 1 - Toast the bread
1. Heat the oven to 180°C.
2. Slice the rye bread thinly; spread on a tray.
3. Bake 25-30 minutes until dark, very dry and faintly charred at the edges.
4. The deeper the toast, the darker the gira; do not burn.

### Stage 2 - Steep
1. Place the toasted bread in a large clean jug or pot.
2. Pour over the hot (but not boiling) water.
3. Stir; cover with a clean cloth.
4. Steep 4-6 hours at room temperature; the water turns dark.

### Stage 3 - Strain
1. Strain the dark liquid through a fine sieve lined with muslin into a clean fermentation vessel (a 3-litre glass jar or food-safe plastic bucket works well).
2. Squeeze the bread gently; do not press hard (releases bitterness).
3. Discard the spent bread.

### Stage 4 - Add sugar and yeast
1. Stir in the sugar (or honey) while the liquid is still warm; dissolve completely.
2. Let cool to body temperature (35°C).
3. Sprinkle the yeast across the surface; stir lightly.
4. Add the raisins, lemon zest and mint sprigs.

### Stage 5 - Ferment
1. Cover with a clean cloth secured with string (lets gas escape; keeps flies out).
2. Stand at warm room temperature (18-22°C) for 24-36 hours.
3. The surface foams gently; raisins float; the liquid develops a faint fizz.
4. Taste at 24 hours: when faintly tangy with a soft prickle of carbonation, it is ready.
5. Do not over-ferment, after 48 hours it grows too sour and slightly alcoholic.

### Stage 6 - Bottle and chill
1. Strain through fine muslin into clean glass bottles.
2. Cap loosely (gira keeps fermenting; tight caps risk a bottle blowing).
3. Refrigerate at least 12 hours before drinking.
4. The cold sharpens the flavour and slows further fermentation.

### Stage 7 - Serve
1. Pour cold over ice in tall glasses.
2. Garnish with a fresh mint sprig.

## Notes
- **Toast hard:** the darker the bread, the deeper the gira colour and flavour. Stop just before true burning.
- **Raisins are not optional:** they bring wild yeasts and a brighter second fermentation; the gira fizzes more with them.
- **Loose caps:** a sealed bottle of fermenting gira can explode. Always vent.
- **Taste at 24 hours:** the line between just-right and too-sour passes quickly. Catch it early.

## Variations
**Honey gira (medumi gira):** swap the sugar for honey, a softer rounder flavour.
**With horseradish:** add 1 tablespoon grated fresh horseradish at bottling; the Russian-style sharper version.
**With apple:** add 2 sliced tart apples to the fermenting jar.
**With cranberry:** add 100 g fresh cranberries for a pink tart version.
**Sweet kid version:** use 200 g sugar; the children's-table standard.

## Serving
Pour cold over ice · at every Lithuanian summer table · with cepelinai · with kepta duona at the beer bar · as a soft picnic drink · with cold beet soup šaltibarščiai · with mint or lemon · the everyday refresher of June through August.

## Storage
- Refrigerated, drink within 5 days; the flavour grows sourer.
- Vent the bottles every 24 hours to release pressure.
- Don't freeze; the bottle bursts.
- The fermentation can be slowed by chilling but not stopped fully.
