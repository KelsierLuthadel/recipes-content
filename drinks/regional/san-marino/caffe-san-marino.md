# Caffè San Marino

![Caffè San Marino](resources/caffe-san-marino.jpg)

*The San Marinese after-meal pour: a single espresso "corrected" with a teaspoon of local grappa, served in a small cup with the spoon on the side.*

**Serves:** 1

**Prep Time:** 1 minute

**Cook Time:** 2 minutes

## Overview
Caffè corretto ("corrected coffee") is the Italian habit of pouring a shot of spirit into the espresso at the end of a long meal, and San Marino keeps the tradition with its own twist: the grappa used is almost always from one of the Republic's small distillers (the most famous is Distilleria Mancino), and the proportions lean a touch heavier on the grappa than in nearby Romagna. The drink wakes you up and warms you down at the same time. Order it after lunch in the old town and the waiter brings it in a small porcelain cup with the grappa already in, no spoon visible: the choice has already been made for you.

## Ingredients

### Per cup
- 1 single espresso shot (30 ml; pulled fresh)
- 15 ml grappa (San Marinese if you can find it; any clear unaged Italian grappa works)
- 1 small sugar cube or 1/2 tsp caster sugar (optional, traditional)

### To serve
- An espresso cup
- A teaspoon

## Method

### Stage 1 - Pull the espresso
1. Warm the espresso cup with hot water; pour out.
2. Pull a single espresso shot into the cup. You want a crema-topped shot, hot.

### Stage 2 - Correct it
1. Pour the grappa into the espresso. The crema breaks; the spirit slides into the coffee.
2. Drop the sugar cube in if using; do not stir. The sugar dissolves as you sip.

### Stage 3 - Serve
1. Set the cup on a small saucer with a teaspoon. The teaspoon is for the etiquette, not for stirring (a stirred caffè corretto is heretical to a San Marinese waiter).
2. Drink in three sips, hot.

## Notes
- **Grappa choice.** A young clear grappa is the traditional pour; a barrel-aged grappa makes the drink rounder but is the modern variation. Avoid sweet liqueurs (sambuca, amaretto) and you will be drinking something else entirely.
- **The sugar question.** Sugar is optional; many older San Marinese drink theirs unsweetened on the grounds that the grappa already softens the coffee.
- **The when.** Caffè corretto belongs at the end of a meal, not the morning; the morning version is a plain espresso.

## Serving
At the end of a long lunch or dinner; small cup, slow drinker. Pair with nothing; it is the close of the meal.

## Storage
- A drink-as-you-pour proposition; nothing to store.
- A grappa bottle keeps indefinitely on the shelf; once opened the flavour starts to fade after a year or so, but it does not spoil.
