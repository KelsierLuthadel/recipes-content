# Sangiovese di San Marino

![Sangiovese di San Marino](resources/sangiovese-di-san-marino.jpg)

*The national red wine of the Republic: Sangiovese grapes from the steep mountain vineyards, dry, medium-bodied, a glass to drink with the everyday table.*

**Serves:** 1 (a 125 ml glass; a bottle pours 6)

**Prep Time:** 2 minutes

**Cook Time:** 0 minutes

## Overview
San Marino sits inside the broader Sangiovese country of Romagna and the Marches, and its own version of the grape is bottled under the Consorzio Vini Tipici di San Marino. The wine is dry, medium-bodied, with a cherry-and-rosemary nose and a soft tannin: a Sangiovese a degree lighter than its Tuscan cousin, made for the table rather than the cellar. This is not a recipe in the cooking sense; it is a serving recipe, the gestures that make the wine taste the way it is meant to. Pour into a wide-bowled glass, serve slightly below room temperature, drink alongside pasta e ceci, faggotti, coniglio in porchetta, or a slice of cheese.

## Ingredients

### Per glass
- 125 ml Sangiovese di San Marino (or any young Sangiovese: a young Chianti, a Romagna Sangiovese DOC, a Sangiovese di Romagna)

### To serve
- A medium-sized red-wine glass (around 350 to 500 ml capacity)
- Cool storage and a clean palate

## Method

### Stage 1 - Temperature
1. Pull the bottle from a cool cellar or rest it in the fridge for 20 minutes before pouring; Sangiovese is at its best at around 16 to 18°C, slightly below room temperature.
2. If the bottle is at warm room temperature, the wine will taste flabby and the alcohol will dominate.

### Stage 2 - Pour
1. Open the bottle. With a young Sangiovese you do not need to decant.
2. Pour 125 ml (about a third of the way up the glass) so there is room to swirl.
3. Hold the glass by the stem, never the bowl, so your hand does not warm the wine.

### Stage 3 - Drink
1. Swirl gently. The wine should look bright ruby, almost translucent at the edges.
2. Smell first: cherry, dried herbs, a hint of earth.
3. Sip alongside food; this is a wine that needs a plate, not a couch.

## Notes
- **Light or aged.** Young Sangiovese di San Marino is the everyday table version. The Brugneto della Serenissima is a longer-aged single-vineyard expression with more body; pour it for an occasion.
- **Pair direction.** Tomato sauces, pork, rabbit, mushroom, hard sheep cheese; awkward against fish, fresh salads, and anything heavy on chilli.
- **Closing the bottle.** Recork and keep in the fridge; finish within 2 days for the best flavour.

## Serving
Two glasses with dinner; the Republic is small enough that wine and food cross the same kitchen most evenings.

## Storage
- Unopened bottles keep years on their side at cellar temperature.
- An opened bottle keeps 2 to 3 days recorked in the fridge.
