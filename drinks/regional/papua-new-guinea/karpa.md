# Karpa

![Karpa](resources/karpa.jpg)

*Fresh sap of the sago palm, tapped at dawn from the cut crown of the tree and drunk the same morning while it is still sweet and faintly milky, the village wake-up drink of the lowlands.*

**Serves:** 4 (small cups)

**Prep Time:** 5 minutes

**Cook Time:** None

## Overview
Karpa is the fresh sap of the sago palm, collected before sunrise from a cut made into the flowering stalk at the top of the tree. The sap drips overnight into a bamboo tube; at dawn the cup is brought down, the contents poured into a gourd, and the household drinks it sweet and slightly milky before the heat of the day starts the natural fermentation. The flavour is gentle and faintly sweet, almost like coconut water with a vegetal palm note, and the texture is thin and lightly creamy. Left longer than a morning, the sap ferments quickly into a country-strength toddy; in the strict Christian village tradition it is the fresh sap that is drunk, not the fermented one. The recipe below is a respectful home approximation using coconut water, palm sugar and a touch of fresh coconut, since true karpa cannot be made off the islands.

## Ingredients
- 600 ml young coconut water (fresh from a young drinking coconut, or sold chilled in cartons)
- 100 ml fresh coconut water from a mature coconut (or extra young coconut water)
- 1 tbsp palm sugar, grated (or coconut sugar)
- A pinch of salt
- 1 tbsp finely grated young coconut flesh (optional, for the milky note)

## Method

### Stage 1 - Warm and dissolve
1. Pour the coconut water into a small pan.
2. Warm very gently to no more than blood-warm (do not boil).
3. Stir in the palm sugar and salt; whisk until dissolved.

### Stage 2 - Lift the colour
1. Stir in the grated coconut flesh; the liquid turns faintly milky.

### Stage 3 - Serve
1. Pour into small cups or a halved young coconut.
2. Drink straight away while still warm-cool from the pan.

## Notes
- **True karpa:** The real thing is the morning sap of a tapped sago palm; nothing made off the islands quite captures it. Coconut water with palm sugar gets close in flavour and is what migrants in town often substitute.
- **Do not boil:** Boiling drives off the delicate fresh note. Keep the warming gentle.
- **Drink the same day:** Even the substitute is best the same morning; flavour fades by the afternoon.

## Variations
- **Iced karpa:** Cool the prepared drink in the fridge and serve over ice for a hot-day version.
- **Pineapple karpa:** Stir in 30 ml fresh pineapple juice for a sharper note.
- **Lime karpa:** A squeeze of lime brightens the drink and balances the sweetness.
- **Coconut milk karpa:** Replace 100 ml of the coconut water with coconut milk for a richer creamier cup.

## Serving
Drink at room temperature in the morning, before food · pour from a gourd or jug into small cups · best from a halved young drinking coconut · alongside a roast kaukau breakfast or a wedge of grilled sago pancake.

## Storage
- Best drunk the day it is made; fermentation starts within hours at room temperature.
- Refrigerate up to 2 days; the freshness fades quickly.
- Do not freeze.
