# Papua New Guinean Drinks

![Papua New Guinean Drinks](resources/hero.jpg)

The morning karpa, the fresh sweet sap tapped from the sago palm at dawn before it ferments in the heat. The Highlands cup of pit-pit ginger and lemongrass tea, drunk hot in the cool mountain air. Fresh tropical juices from the village trees: pawpaw, pineapple, soursop, all squeezed and drunk the same day. The Christian-temperance traditions of the missions kept alcohol modest, and the village drink remains the fresh-pressed juice, the tea and the coconut water from a freshly-felled nut.
