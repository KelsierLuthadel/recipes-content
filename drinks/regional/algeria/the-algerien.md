# Thé Algérien

![Thé Algérien](resources/the-algerien.jpg)

*Algerian mint tea: gunpowder green tea brewed dark with a generous bunch of spearmint and sugar, poured three times to develop the foam and the proverb.*

**Serves:** 4

**Prep Time:** 5 minutes

**Cook Time:** 15 minutes

## Overview
Thé algérien is the everyday tea of the country and the centrepiece of every social visit. Like its Moroccan cousin it is built on green gunpowder tea, fresh spearmint and sugar, but the Algerian version is brewed darker and stronger, simmered gently on the stove rather than steeped quickly, and traditionally poured three times from the same pot, each pour stronger and sweeter than the last. The proverb runs: "the first glass is bitter as life, the second strong as love, the third sweet as death." A guest is expected to drink all three. The pour is from a height, theatrical, with a long-spouted metal pot called a berrad raised at least a forearm above the small glass to oxygenate the tea and put a foam head on the surface. The right glasses are small, heatproof, often gilded or painted in colour. Mint sprigs go in each glass after pouring.

## Ingredients

- 2 tbsp green gunpowder tea
- 1 litre boiling water (for the three pours; about 300 ml each glass-round)
- 1 large bunch fresh spearmint (about 40 g; not peppermint)
- 6 to 8 tbsp sugar
- A small handful pine nuts (optional, a Saharan touch for the third pour)

## Method

### Stage 1 - Wash the tea
1. Tip the gunpowder tea into a metal teapot or saucepan.
1. Pour over 100 ml boiling water; swirl 5 seconds; pour off and discard. This removes the first bitterness.

### Stage 2 - First brew
1. Add a third of the mint sprigs (stalks and all) and 3 tbsp sugar to the pot.
1. Pour over 350 ml boiling water.
1. Set on a low heat; bring to a very gentle simmer; hold for 3 to 4 minutes.

### Stage 3 - The mix-pour
1. Pour a small amount of tea into a glass, then pour it back into the pot. Repeat twice. This mixes the sugar through and helps the foam build.
1. Taste; adjust sugar to your liking. Algerian tea is properly sweet.

### Stage 4 - The first round (bitter as life)
1. Hold the pot at least 30 cm above the first glass; pour in a steady stream until the glass is two-thirds full and topped with white foam.
1. Repeat for each glass.
1. Slide a sprig of mint into each.
1. Serve hot.

### Stage 5 - The second round (strong as love)
1. Top up the pot with another 300 ml boiling water and another third of the mint.
1. Simmer gently 4 minutes.
1. Add 2 tbsp more sugar if needed; mix-pour as before; pour from height.

### Stage 6 - The third round (sweet as death)
1. Top up with the last 300 ml boiling water, the rest of the mint and 2 tbsp more sugar (a pinch of pine nuts in the pot is a Saharan touch).
1. Simmer 4 minutes.
1. Mix-pour; pour from height; serve. By now the tea is dark and properly sweet.

## Notes
- **Gunpowder is the right tea.** The tight pellets stand up to the long brew; loose green tea goes bitter halfway through the second pour.
- **Spearmint not peppermint.** Peppermint is too sharp; the rounded-leaf bright green spearmint is the right herb.
- **The pour matters.** The height is not theatre for its own sake; it cools the tea slightly, aerates it and builds the foam that is the hallmark of a proper Algerian pour. Practise over a sink first.

## Variations
- **Saharan style.** Brew the third pour with extra sugar and pine nuts; the desert nomads' version, syrupy and warming.
- **With orange-blossom water.** A few drops in the third pour for a Friday or Mawlid touch; not everyday.

## Storage
- Drink immediately; brewed tea does not store.
- The same leaves can sit in the pot through the three rounds; do not try a fourth (it goes bitter).
- Dry tea and mint store at room temperature in sealed jars; mint dries well for winter use.
