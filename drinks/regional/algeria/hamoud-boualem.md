# Hamoud Boualem

![Hamoud Boualem](resources/hamoud-boualem.jpg)

*A homemade take on Algeria's national lemon soda, in production in Algiers since 1878 and the bottle on every Friday lunch table; bright lemon, light fizz, a hint of orange-blossom.*

**Serves:** 4 tall glasses

**Prep Time:** 10 minutes (plus 1 hour chilling)

**Cook Time:** 5 minutes

## Overview
Hamoud Boualem is the national soft drink of Algeria, first produced in Algiers in 1878 by a Kabyle apothecary of the same name and still in production by the same family company today. The yellow bottle with the white label is found on every Algerian table and out-sells every American import in the country; the recipe has not changed in nearly 150 years and the company has resisted every offer of acquisition. The factory recipe is closely guarded but the bones are simple: lemon juice, a touch of orange-blossom water, sugar and carbonated water. This is a home version that gets very close: a quick lemon-and-orange-blossom syrup stirred into cold sparkling water and poured over ice. Serve with the Friday couscous, with a heavy lunch or as a Ramadan refreshment alongside the chorba.

## Ingredients

### Lemon-and-orange-blossom syrup
- 200 g sugar
- 200 ml water
- Juice of 3 lemons (about 120 ml)
- Zest of 1 lemon, in long strips
- 1 tbsp orange-blossom water
- A tiny pinch of citric acid (optional, sharpens the lemon edge)

### To serve
- 1 litre sparkling water, well chilled
- Ice cubes
- A few thin slices of lemon
- A small sprig of fresh mint (optional, a modern touch)

## Method

### Stage 1 - Make the syrup
1. Combine the sugar, water and lemon zest strips in a small pan.
1. Bring to a simmer over medium heat; stir until the sugar dissolves; cook 3 minutes more.
1. Off the heat, fish out the zest strips and discard.
1. Stir in the lemon juice, orange-blossom water and citric acid (if using).
1. Cool to room temperature; chill at least 1 hour (the syrup must be cold to keep the fizz when mixed).

### Stage 2 - Build the drink
1. Fill 4 tall glasses with ice.
1. Spoon 3 to 4 tablespoons of the chilled syrup into each glass.
1. Top up gently with cold sparkling water.
1. Stir very briefly with a long spoon (over-stirring kills the fizz).

### Stage 3 - Serve
1. Slide a thin slice of lemon down the side of each glass.
1. Add a small sprig of mint if using.
1. Serve immediately while properly cold and fizzy.

## Notes
- **The orange-blossom water is the signature.** Without it the drink is just a lemonade; with it, you have the unmistakable Algerian floral lift that makes Hamoud what it is.
- **Cold-on-cold for fizz.** Both syrup and water must be properly chilled. Warm syrup into cold soda kills the bubbles in seconds.
- **Adjust to taste.** The factory recipe is slightly sweet by western standards; start with 3 tbsp syrup per glass and add more if needed.

## Variations
- **Hamoud-style limonetta.** Add 1 tbsp lime juice to the syrup for a sharper edge.
- **Boualem mocktail.** Stir a spoonful of the chilled syrup into a glass of cold mint tea over ice for a Ramadan refresher.

## Storage
- Syrup keeps 3 weeks refrigerated in a sealed bottle
- Build the drink to order; pre-mixed loses fizz within an hour
- The undiluted syrup freezes 2 months; thaw in the fridge before using
