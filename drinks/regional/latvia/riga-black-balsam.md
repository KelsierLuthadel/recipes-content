# Riga Black Balsam

![Riga Black Balsam](resources/riga-black-balsam.jpg)

*Latvia's national digestif: a 45% ABV black herbal bitter built on 24 herbs, roots, oils and botanicals (yarrow, valerian, oak bark, wormwood, gentian, ginger, linden, sweet flag) macerated in grain spirit, sweetened with honey, aged in oak. Drunk neat from a small ceramic flask, or stirred with blackcurrant juice, hot coffee or sparkling wine.*

**Serves:** Suggested servings per cocktail listed below

**Prep Time:** 1 to 2 minutes per drink

**Cook Time:** 0

## Overview
Riga Black Balsam (Rīgas Melnais balzams) is the country's most famous drink, a black herbal bitter dating to a recipe written down in 1752 by Riga pharmacist Abraham Kunze, sold as a medicinal tonic and tied to Latvian identity ever since. The current formula is held closely by the producer (Latvijas balzams) and uses 24 botanicals: yarrow, valerian, oak bark, wormwood, gentian, ginger, linden flower, sweet flag, juniper, oranges, peppermint, plus several proprietary herbs and oils. The bitters mature in oak barrels and are bottled at 45% ABV in heavy ceramic flasks coloured matte black, the shape recognisable in any duty-free across Europe. The drink is too intense to serve as a long pour: neat in a small glass after a heavy meal is the proper Latvian way, or mixed sparingly with sweet partners (blackcurrant juice, coffee, sparkling wine, cola, hot tea) to bring out the herb notes without overwhelming the palate. The recipes below are the standard Latvian bar combinations; they are not original cocktails but the served-everywhere variants you will meet in Riga.

## Ingredients

### Neat
- 40 ml Riga Black Balsam, chilled to about 8°C
- A small ceramic shot glass

### Black Balsam with blackcurrant (the classic)
- 40 ml Riga Black Balsam
- 120 ml chilled blackcurrant juice (unsweetened or lightly sweetened)
- Ice
- A small piece of orange peel (optional)

### Hot coffee with Black Balsam
- 30 ml Riga Black Balsam
- 150 ml hot strong black coffee
- 1 teaspoon brown sugar (to taste)
- A splash of cream (optional)
- A strip of orange peel

### Black Balsam and sparkling wine
- 30 ml Riga Black Balsam
- 150 ml dry sparkling wine (Cava, Prosecco, or Latvian Stari sparkling)
- A blackberry or blackcurrant on a skewer

### Black Balsam with hot tea
- 30 ml Riga Black Balsam
- 200 ml hot strong black tea
- 1 teaspoon honey
- A slice of lemon

## Method

### Stage 1 - Neat
1. Pour 40 ml of chilled Black Balsam into a small ceramic or heavy glass shot.
2. Sip slowly; do not slam (the herbs need to register).

### Stage 2 - Black Balsam with blackcurrant
1. Fill a tumbler with ice.
2. Pour in the Black Balsam.
3. Top with cold blackcurrant juice; stir gently.
4. Twist a piece of orange peel over the top, drop it in.

### Stage 3 - Hot coffee with Black Balsam
1. Brew a strong black coffee.
2. Stir in the brown sugar.
3. Pour the Black Balsam into a heatproof mug; top with hot coffee.
4. Float a spoonful of cream if you like; garnish with orange peel.

### Stage 4 - Black Balsam and sparkling wine
1. Pour the Black Balsam into a chilled coupe or flute.
2. Top slowly with cold sparkling wine; do not stir.
3. Drop the blackberry skewer in.

### Stage 5 - Black Balsam with hot tea
1. Brew a strong black tea.
2. Stir in the honey.
3. Pour into a heavy mug; add the Black Balsam.
4. Garnish with a lemon slice; drink while hot.

## Notes
- **Cold neat or cold mixed.** The bitter is most balanced at fridge temperature; room-temperature pours read fierce.
- **Sweet partners are right.** The 24 herbs lean hard towards bitter; pair with sweet (blackcurrant, honey, sparkling wine) or hot-rich (coffee, tea) to draw out the herb top notes.
- **45% ABV.** A 40 ml pour is enough; double pours hit hard fast.

## Variations
- **Cherry Black Balsam:** A sweeter, lower-strength version (30% ABV) sold by the same producer with macerated cherries; mix the same way but it tolerates longer drinks.
- **Hot chocolate float:** Pour Black Balsam over hot chocolate with a whipped cream top.
- **Cola version:** Black Balsam and cola is a popular Latvian student-bar pour; long, sweet, very accessible.

## Serving
Serve as a digestif after a heavy meal of pelēkie zirņi or karbonāde. A small ceramic shot at the end of the night in any Riga bar.

## Storage
- Sealed bottles last indefinitely.
- Once opened, keeps months in a cool dark cupboard with the cap on.
- Chill the bottle before serving for the best balance.
