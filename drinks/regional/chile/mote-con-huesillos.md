# Mote con Huesillos

![Mote con Huesillos](resources/mote-con-huesillos.jpg)

*Chile's drink-and-snack hybrid: cooked husked wheat (mote) floating in sweet caramel-cinnamon syrup with a whole dried peach (huesillo), served in a tall glass with a long spoon for the wheat at the bottom.*

**Serves:** 4

**Prep Time:** 10 minutes (plus overnight soaking)

**Cook Time:** 90 minutes

## Overview
Mote con huesillos is the Chilean summer institution and confuses every tourist: is it a drink or is it a dessert? It's both. Pelted-and-husked wheat (mote, traditionally mote pelado) cooks soft, drained, and waits at the bottom of a tall glass; whole dried peaches (huesillos) plump up overnight then simmer in water with sugar, cinnamon and a touch of orange peel to produce a deep amber syrup; the syrup is ladled over the wheat and one whole peach goes in on top. You drink the syrup and chase the wheat with the long spoon. Sold from kiosks on every Santiago street corner from December through March; the phrase "más chileno que mote con huesillos" (more Chilean than mote con huesillos) is the national way of saying something is quintessentially Chilean.

## Ingredients

### Huesillos and syrup
- 8 dried peach halves (huesillos; from a Latin American grocer)
- 1.5 litres cold water (for soaking + cooking)
- 200 g dark muscovado or panela sugar
- 2 cinnamon sticks
- Wide strip of orange peel
- 4 cloves (optional)

### Mote (cooked husked wheat)
- 200 g pelted wheat (mote pelado; or substitute pearled barley)
- Generous pinch of salt

### To serve
- 4 tall glasses
- 4 long spoons

## Method

### Stage 1 - Soak and cook the huesillos
1. Soak the dried peach halves in 1 litre of cold water for 12 hours (overnight).
1. Tip the peaches and soaking water into a saucepan with the sugar, cinnamon sticks, orange peel and cloves (if using).
1. Bring to a boil; reduce to a simmer and cook covered for 45 to 60 minutes until the peaches are plump and tender and the liquid has reduced into a deep amber syrup.
1. Cool, then refrigerate at least 4 hours.

### Stage 2 - Cook the mote
1. Bring a saucepan of salted water to a boil; add the pelted wheat and simmer 40 to 60 minutes until tender but still chewy.
1. Drain and refrigerate.

### Stage 3 - Assemble
1. Place 3 tablespoons of cooked mote in the bottom of each tall glass.
1. Pour over the peach syrup; add 2 huesillos per glass (or 1 if they're big).
1. Serve cold with a long spoon.

## Notes
- **Pelted wheat (mote pelado) is the canonical grain.** Pearled barley works as a substitute; freekeh-style cracked wheat doesn't quite hit the same texture.
- **Soak the dried peaches the night before.** Skipping this step gives leathery huesillos that won't plump properly.
- **Eat the syrup first, the peach second, the mote last with a spoon.** The Chilean drinking order; ignore at your own peril.

## Storage
- The syrup and mote keep separately in the fridge for 5 days; assemble per glass at serving.
