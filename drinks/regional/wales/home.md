# Welsh Drinks

![Welsh Drinks](resources/hero.jpg)

Welsh drinking has always sat in a quiet tension between pub and chapel: the chapels pulled long and hard for temperance through the nineteenth century, and the pubs kept the pumps flowing anyway. The result is a country with deep cask-ale tradition (Brains SA out of Cardiff is the everyday south-Wales pint, brewed since 1882) and a small but serious craft revival, both in cwrw (the Welsh word for ale, used across the new generation of small brewers from Anglesey to Carmarthen) and in spirits. Penderyn Distillery, founded in 2000 in a small village in the Brecon Beacons, ended a century without commercial Welsh whisky and made single-malt Welsh whisky a thing again. Add Welsh gin, Welsh mead and Welsh cider to the mix, and Wales has quietly rebuilt a full drinks shelf around its old pub-and-chapel debate.
