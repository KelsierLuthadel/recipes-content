# Akasan

![Akasan](resources/akasan.jpg)

*Haitian hot cornmeal drink: fine cornmeal simmered with milk, cinnamon, star anise, vanilla and evaporated milk into a thick spiced porridge-drink, served in mugs at breakfast on cold mornings.*

**Serves:** 4

**Prep Time:** 5 minutes

**Cook Time:** 20 minutes

## Overview
Akasan (also called akasen or chanm-chanm) is the Haitian morning drink: very fine white cornmeal whisked into water and simmered with cinnamon, star anise, vanilla, salt and sugar until it thickens to the consistency of a thin custard, then finished with evaporated milk and nutmeg. The texture is somewhere between a hot chocolate and a thin porridge - drinkable from a mug but rich and filling. The cornmeal must be fine ("akasan flour" at Caribbean groceries, ground finer than American polenta); coarser cornmeal gives a gritty drink. Served at breakfast across Haiti, especially on cold mountain mornings; also the comfort drink children get when they're not well.

## Ingredients

- 80 g fine white cornmeal (akasan flour; OR finely ground polenta blitzed further in a spice grinder)
- 200 ml cold water (to slurry the cornmeal)
- 800 ml water
- 1 cinnamon stick
- 2 star anise
- 1 pandan leaf (5 cm) OR a strip of orange peel (for the aromatic note)
- 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
- ½ teaspoon fine salt
- 6 tablespoons caster sugar (or to taste)
- 200 ml evaporated milk (or sweetened condensed milk for richer)
- ¼ teaspoon ground nutmeg
- ¼ teaspoon ground cinnamon (for finishing)

### To serve
- Mugs (warmed)
- An extra dusting of cinnamon and nutmeg on top

## Method

1. In a small bowl, whisk the fine cornmeal with the 200 ml cold water until you have a smooth slurry with no lumps.
1. In a saucepan, bring the 800 ml water to a boil with the cinnamon stick, star anise, pandan and vanilla.
1. Once boiling, reduce to a simmer and slowly pour the cornmeal slurry in while whisking constantly to prevent clumps.
1. Add the salt and sugar.
1. Cook over low heat 12 to 15 minutes, whisking every couple of minutes, until thickened to a thin custard consistency. The cornmeal should be fully cooked - no raw cornmeal taste.
1. Stir in the evaporated milk; cook another 2 minutes.
1. Remove the cinnamon stick, star anise and pandan.
1. Pour into warm mugs; dust with nutmeg and cinnamon.

## Notes
- **Fine cornmeal is essential.** Akasan flour from a Caribbean grocer is the right grind. Standard polenta is too coarse and gives a gritty drink. Blitz polenta in a spice grinder for 60 seconds as a last-resort substitute.
- **Whisk hard when adding the slurry.** Lumps form in the first 10 seconds and are impossible to remove later. Constant whisking for the first minute is non-negotiable.

## Storage
- Best within an hour. Refrigerated akasan sets solid; rewarm gently with a splash of milk to loosen.
