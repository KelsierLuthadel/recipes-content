# Sazón

![Sazón](resources/sazon.jpg)

*A homemade stand-in for Goya Sazón seasoning, the orange-tinted Latin-American spice blend recipes from Puerto Rico, Cuba, the Dominican Republic and beyond reach for as a one-spoon shortcut. This UK pantry build matches the flavour with cumin, coriander, paprika, garlic and oregano, and annatto (or turmeric) for the signature colour.*

**Prep Time:** 5 minutes

**Yield:** Approximately 35 grams (10-12 portions)

## Overview
Sazón is the orange-coloured Latin-American seasoning blend that gives Puerto Rican arroz con pollo, Cuban frijoles negros, Dominican pollo guisado and a stack of other dishes their warm-savoury base. The classic supermarket-aisle version is Goya Sazón, a sachet of cumin, coriander, garlic, oregano, salt, MSG and annatto (achiote) that's poured into a pot to season meat, rice and beans in one spoonful. It's hard to find in the UK outside specialist Latin-American grocers, so this homemade build lets a British pantry produce the same flavour with the spices already on the shelf. The annatto - a brick-red seed from the achiote tree - is what gives Sazón its trademark orange colour; ground annatto turns up at Latin grocers and online. Turmeric is a passable colour stand-in if you can't find annatto, though the flavour leans different (warmer and earthier rather than peppery-floral). Use as a one-for-one swap: 1 teaspoon of this blend replaces 1 packet of Goya Sazón (each packet is about 1 ½ teaspoons).

## Ingredients

- 1 tablespoon ground coriander
- 1 tablespoon ground cumin
- 1 tablespoon paprika (sweet or smoked)
- 1 tablespoon garlic granules
- 2 teaspoons dried oregano
- 2 teaspoons fine sea salt
- 1 teaspoon ground annatto (or 1 teaspoon ground turmeric, for colour)
- ½ teaspoon ground black pepper

## Method

1. Measure all ingredients into a small bowl.
1. Mix thoroughly until the colour is uniform and the texture even.
1. Transfer to an airtight jar.
1. Label with the date and store in a cool dark cupboard.

## Notes
- **Annatto vs turmeric.** Annatto (achiote) is the authentic orange. Available online and at Latin grocers; lasts a year ground. Turmeric gives a close visual match but tastes earthier; both work.
- **MSG.** The Goya original carries MSG for umami depth. Add ¼ teaspoon if you want the exact savoury hit; the build works fine without it.
- **Use as a 1:1 swap.** 1 teaspoon of this blend = 1 packet of Goya Sazón (the sachets are about 1 ½ teaspoons each). Recipes calling for "1 tablespoon Sazón" need 1 tablespoon of this.
- **The orange colour does work.** Tints rice yellow-gold and gives stews their warm Latin-American glow; if you skip the annatto / turmeric entirely the spice mix still works flavour-wise but loses the visual signature.

## Serving
- **Use in:** Latin-American rice dishes, bean stews, marinades for chicken and pork, soups
- **Amount:** 1 teaspoon per portion of rice, 1-2 teaspoons per pot of stew

## Storage
- Airtight jar in a cool dark cupboard; best within 3 months while the volatile aromatics are still vivid.
- Keeps 6 months at usable strength.
