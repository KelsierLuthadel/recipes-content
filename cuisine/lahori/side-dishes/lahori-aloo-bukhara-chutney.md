# Lahori Aloo Bukhara Chutney

*Dried-plum chutney: tart, sweet and ginger-warmed, made with aloo bukhara (dried sour plums) cooked down with jaggery and warming spices. The traditional Lahori companion to chapli kebabs and pakoras.*

**Serves:** 6-8 (as an accompaniment); makes about 300 ml

**Prep Time:** 10 minutes (plus 30 minutes soak)

**Cook Time:** 35 minutes

## Overview
Dried sour plums (aloo bukhara) are soaked in hot water to soften, then simmered with their soaking water, palm jaggery, ginger, salt and a small mix of spices (cumin, ginger, fennel, chilli, black salt). The plums break down during cooking; once they've collapsed, the chutney is mashed lightly to keep some texture and cooked further until it thickens to a glossy, dark-purple paste. Stores well; improves over a week.

## Ingredients
- 200 g dried aloo bukhara (sour plums; or 150 g pitted prunes + 2 tablespoons of lemon juice as a less-tart substitute)
- 350 ml hot water (for soaking)
- 100 g palm jaggery (or dark brown sugar)
- 25 g fresh ginger (finely grated)
- 1 teaspoon ground cumin (lightly toasted)
- ½ teaspoon ground fennel
- ½ teaspoon ground black pepper
- ½ teaspoon Kashmiri chilli powder
- ½ teaspoon black salt (kala namak)
- 1 teaspoon salt (to taste)
- ¼ teaspoon ground cinnamon
- ¼ teaspoon ground cardamom
- 2 tablespoons lemon juice (only if using sweeter prunes; skip for sour plums)

### Optional
- 1 tablespoon raisins
- 1 tablespoon chopped dates

## Method

### Stage 1 - Soak the plums
1. Tip the dried aloo bukhara into a bowl.
1. Cover with 350 ml of hot water.
1. Soak for 30 minutes until plump.

### Stage 2 - Remove the stones (if needed)
1. If your dried plums have stones, lift each one out of the soaking water and squeeze the stone out (it usually pops out cleanly).
1. Return the flesh to the soaking water.

### Stage 3 - Cook the chutney
1. Tip the soaked plums and their soaking water into a saucepan.
1. Add the palm jaggery, ginger, all the dried spices, salts and the lemon juice (if using).
1. Place over medium-low heat.
1. Stir until the jaggery has dissolved.

### Stage 4 - Simmer
1. Cook for 20-25 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the plums have broken down completely.
1. Press the plums against the side of the pot with the back of a spoon to mash them lightly (keep some texture; a fully smooth chutney loses character).

### Stage 5 - Reduce
1. Continue cooking for another 8-10 minutes, until the chutney has reduced to a thick, glossy paste that coats the back of a spoon (it will thicken further as it cools).
1. Add the raisins and dates (if using) in the last 5 minutes.

### Stage 6 - Taste and cool
1. Taste and adjust salt, jaggery and chilli.
1. Pull from the heat.
1. Cool to room temperature; the chutney sets to a thick spreadable consistency.

### Stage 7 - Serve
1. Transfer to a jar.
1. Serve cold or at room temperature with chapli kebabs, pakoras, samosas or as part of a chaat plate.

## Notes
- **Sour plums vs prunes:** Aloo bukhara are properly sour. Prunes (the European dried plum) are sweeter; the chutney will need extra lemon juice to balance. Try a South Asian grocer for the real thing.
- **Don't mash to a smooth puree:** Some chunks of plum flesh are what make this a chutney rather than a paste. Aim for half-mashed.
- **The kala namak is the Lahori signature:** Adds a faint sulphurous depth that distinguishes the chutney from a generic plum sauce.

## Storage
- Refrigerate up to 4 weeks in a clean jar.
- The chutney improves dramatically over the first week as the flavours settle.
