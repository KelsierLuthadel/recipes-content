# Lahori Kulfi

*Lahore's frozen-milk dessert: full-fat milk reduced for an hour to a thick condensed cream, sweetened, flavoured with cardamom and pistachio, frozen hard in conical moulds. Denser than ice cream, with a slight chewiness.*

**Serves:** 8 (small conical moulds; or 6 ramekins)

**Prep Time:** 15 minutes

**Cook Time:** 1 hour (mostly hands-off reducing)

## Overview
A litre and a half of full-fat milk is brought to a boil and reduced over medium heat for about an hour, stirring every few minutes, until it has shrunk to a third of its volume and gone the colour of pale toffee. Sugar, cardamom, ground pistachios and a touch of saffron go in once the reduction is complete. The mixture is poured into small conical moulds (or paper cups) and frozen hard. No churning; no ice cream maker; the dense texture comes from the long reduction.

## Ingredients
- 1.5 litres full-fat milk (whole milk; semi-skimmed gives a thin kulfi)
- 80 g caster sugar (adjust to taste; the milk concentrates so sweet is built in)
- 2 tablespoons cornflour (mixed with 4 tablespoons cold milk; for body and to prevent ice crystals)
- 50 g ground pistachios (or almonds)
- ½ teaspoon ground cardamom
- ¼ teaspoon saffron threads
- 2 tablespoons sweetened condensed milk (modern shortcut; gives extra body)
- A pinch of salt
- ½ teaspoon rose water or kewra water (optional)

### To finish
- 30 g pistachios (slivered, for the moulds and the top)
- 1-2 sheets of silver leaf (vark; optional)

### Equipment
- 8 kulfi moulds (conical aluminium moulds with lids) or 6 small ramekins or paper cups
- 8 wooden ice-cream sticks (if using paper cups)

## Method

### Stage 1 - Reduce the milk
1. Pour the milk into a wide, heavy-bottomed pan.
1. Add a pinch of salt.
1. Bring to a boil over medium-high heat, stirring once or twice.
1. Reduce to medium heat and cook uncovered for about 50-55 minutes, stirring every 4-5 minutes and scraping the bottom of the pan, until the milk has reduced to a third of its original volume (about 500 ml).
1. The milk will go from white to pale ivory to faint pale toffee; the consistency is similar to double cream.

### Stage 2 - Bloom the saffron
1. While the milk reduces, crumble the saffron into 1 tablespoon of warm milk; rest for 10 minutes.

### Stage 3 - Add sugar and flavourings
1. Once the milk has reduced, stir in the sugar.
1. Cook for 2 minutes until dissolved.
1. Stir the cornflour mixture into a smooth slurry and pour into the reduced milk while whisking.
1. Cook for 3-4 minutes, stirring constantly, until the mixture thickens.

### Stage 4 - Pistachio and cardamom
1. Stir in the ground pistachios, cardamom, bloomed saffron, condensed milk and rose water (if using).
1. Cook for 1 more minute.
1. Pull from the heat.

### Stage 5 - Cool
1. Cool the mixture to room temperature.
1. Place a piece of greaseproof paper directly on the surface (prevents a skin forming).

### Stage 6 - Mould
1. Scatter a few slivered pistachios into the bottom of each kulfi mould (or ramekin).
1. Pour the cooled mixture in, leaving 5 mm at the top.
1. Tap each mould to release any air bubbles.
1. Cover with the lid (or, for paper cups, cover with foil and pierce a wooden stick through the centre once they've half-frozen).

### Stage 7 - Freeze
1. Freeze for at least 8 hours, ideally overnight.

### Stage 8 - Serve
1. To unmould: dip each kulfi briefly (5-10 seconds) in warm water.
1. Twist the stick gently and slide the kulfi out onto a plate.
1. Scatter more pistachios on top.
1. Lay silver leaf across (if using).
1. Serve immediately.

## Notes
- **Long reduction is non-negotiable:** A kulfi made with un-reduced milk is just sweetened ice. The hour-long reduction is the dish.
- **Don't churn:** Kulfi is a no-churn dessert. The density comes from the reduction, not whipping.
- **Cornflour stabiliser:** Prevents large ice crystals from forming during the slow freeze. Without it, the texture goes grainy by day three.

## Storage
- Keep frozen up to 2 months in a sealed container; protect from freezer odours.
- Once unmoulded, eat within 5 minutes.
