# Rødkål (Norwegian Christmas Red Cabbage)

![Rødkål](resources/rodkal.jpg)

*Norway's Christmas red cabbage: shredded red cabbage slow-braised in vinegar, sugar, apple and spices for an hour, until silky, sweet-sour and a deep wine-red. The non-negotiable accompaniment to roast pork ribs and pinnekjøtt.*

**Serves:** 6-8

**Prep Time:** 15 minutes

**Cook Time:** 1 hour

## Overview
Rødkål - "red cabbage" - is the Norwegian (and Danish, and Swedish - all of Scandinavia claims it) Christmas-table side dish: shredded red cabbage slow-braised with apple, vinegar, brown sugar and warm spices into a glossy, sweet-sour mass that complements rich roast pork and dark game. The dish needs at least an hour of slow cooking for the cabbage to soften into silken strands and the flavours to meld; many Norwegian families make it a day or two ahead because it improves on standing. The colour - a deep velvety wine-red - is what gives the dish its festive look on the Christmas plate, alongside white potato, brown gravy and golden roast meat.

## Ingredients
- 1 large red cabbage (about 1.2 kg), outer leaves removed, cored and finely shredded
- 1 large onion, finely diced
- 2 dessert apples (Cox, Braeburn), peeled, cored, grated
- 50 g unsalted butter (or duck/goose fat for a richer flavour)
- 150 ml red wine vinegar
- 150 ml red wine (or apple juice for non-alcoholic)
- 100 g soft brown sugar
- 2 tbsp redcurrant jelly
- 4 whole cloves
- 1 cinnamon stick
- 4 juniper berries (lightly crushed)
- 1 bay leaf
- 1 tsp fine sea salt
- A grind of black pepper

## Method

### Stage 1 - Prep the cabbage
1. Quarter the red cabbage; remove the white core.
2. Finely shred with a sharp knife or mandoline into thin strips (3-4 mm).
3. Tip into a large bowl.

### Stage 2 - Soften the onion
1. Melt the butter in a large heavy pot over medium heat.
2. Add the diced onion; cook 5 minutes until soft.

### Stage 3 - Build the braise
1. Add the shredded cabbage and the grated apple to the pot.
2. Stir to coat in the butter.
3. Add the vinegar, wine, brown sugar, redcurrant jelly, cloves, cinnamon stick, juniper berries, bay leaf, salt and pepper.
4. Stir well; the cabbage starts to turn an intense purple-red as the acid hits it.

### Stage 4 - Slow braise
1. Bring to a gentle simmer.
2. Reduce heat to very low; cover with a lid.
3. Cook 50-60 minutes, stirring every 15 minutes, until the cabbage is tender, silky and a deep wine colour.
4. The liquid should be syrupy; if it's too watery, uncover for the last 10 minutes.

### Stage 5 - Adjust and rest
1. Taste; the balance should be sweet-sour with a sweet edge. Add more sugar or vinegar as needed.
2. Discard the cinnamon stick, bay leaf and (if you can find them) the juniper berries and whole cloves.
3. Rest at least 30 minutes before serving - flavour develops.
4. Even better if made a day ahead.

### Stage 6 - Serve
1. Spoon onto plates alongside roast pork, duck, goose, venison or any rich meat.
2. A small spoonful is enough - it's punchy.

## Notes
- **Slow and low:** The hour of slow cooking is what gives the silky texture. Rushed rødkål is crunchy and one-note.
- **The vinegar and sugar balance:** Adjust to taste at the end - the dish should be sweet-sour, with neither dominating. Norwegian taste leans slightly sweet; Danish slightly more sour.
- **Make ahead:** This dish is one of the few that genuinely improves overnight. The acids continue to mellow; the spices distribute fully.

## Serving
Serve at Christmas dinner alongside pinnekjøtt (dried lamb ribs - the Norwegian Christmas main), roast pork (ribbe), or any rich winter game.

## Storage
- Refrigerates 1 week in a sealed jar.
- Freezes 3 months; thaw in the fridge before reheating.
- The flavour peaks 24-48 hours after making; many Norwegian households cook a big batch in early December and use through Christmas week.
