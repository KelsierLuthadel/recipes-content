# Fiskesuppe (Norwegian Creamy Fish Soup)

![Fiskesuppe](resources/fiskesuppe.jpg)

*Bergen's coastal classic: a creamy soup of cod and salmon with carrots, leeks and dill, finished with a swirl of cream. Comforting on a cold evening, light enough to drink before dinner. The fjord-side staple.*

**Serves:** 4 as a starter or light main

**Prep Time:** 15 minutes

**Cook Time:** 30 minutes

## Overview
Fiskesuppe is the Norwegian creamy fish soup of the western coast, particularly associated with Bergen, where the harbour fish market still sells the cod and salmon that go into the pot. The soup is built on a delicate fish stock (or shortcut with fish bouillon) with sweated leeks, carrots and a pinch of saffron for the warm golden colour, then enriched with cream and finished with chunks of cod and salmon poached gently in the broth at the very end. Fresh dill and chives go in last. The flavour profile is more delicate than chowder, more substantial than consommé, with the sweetness of the cream supporting the clean taste of the fish. Served in deep bowls with crusty bread and butter.

## Ingredients

### Stock and base
- 1 tbsp unsalted butter
- 1 tbsp olive oil
- 1 medium onion, finely diced
- 2 medium carrots, finely diced
- 1 small leek, white part only, finely sliced
- 2 sticks celery, finely diced (optional)
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 800 ml fish stock (homemade, or 2 tbsp fish stock concentrate dissolved in 800 ml water)
- 200 ml dry white wine
- A pinch of saffron threads (about 0.2 g)
- 1 bay leaf
- 1 small bunch of fresh dill stems (tie with kitchen string; reserve fronds for finishing)

### Cream and fish
- 300 ml double cream
- 300 g cod fillet, skinned, cut into 3 cm chunks
- 300 g salmon fillet, skinned, cut into 3 cm chunks
- 200 g raw peeled prawns or shelled mussels (optional)

### To finish
- A small handful of fresh dill fronds, finely chopped
- A small handful of fresh chives, finely chopped
- Juice of half a lemon
- Salt and freshly ground white pepper
- Optional: 1 tbsp vermouth or aquavit

### To serve
- Crusty bread or rye bread, sliced
- Salted butter

## Method

### Stage 1 - Sweat the vegetables
1. Heat the butter and oil in a large heavy pot over medium-low heat.
2. Add the onion, carrot, leek and celery (if using).
3. Sweat gently 8-10 minutes until the vegetables are tender but not coloured.
4. Add the garlic; cook 1 minute.

### Stage 2 - Stock and wine
1. Pour in the fish stock and white wine.
2. Add the saffron, bay leaf and the bundle of dill stems.
3. Bring to a gentle simmer.
4. Cook 12-15 minutes, partially covered, to build the flavour and slightly reduce.

### Stage 3 - Cream
1. Lower the heat to very low (the soup should barely tremble - boiling causes the cream to separate).
2. Pour in the cream; stir gently.
3. Taste; add salt to balance.
4. Simmer (don't boil) 3 minutes.

### Stage 4 - Add the fish
1. Slip the chunks of cod and salmon into the soup.
2. Add the prawns or mussels if using.
3. Don't stir vigorously - the fish breaks up.
4. Poach gently 3-4 minutes; the fish turns opaque and just firm to the touch.

### Stage 5 - Final seasoning
1. Off the heat, stir in the lemon juice and the optional vermouth/aquavit.
2. Discard the bay leaf and the dill stem bundle.
3. Taste; adjust salt and white pepper.

### Stage 6 - Serve
1. Ladle into deep warm bowls.
2. Scatter the fresh chopped dill and chives over.
3. Bring crusty bread and butter to the table.
4. A wedge of lemon on the side for each diner to squeeze as they like.

## Notes
- **Don't boil the cream:** Cream separates if it boils, breaking the silky texture. Keep the heat low once the cream is in.
- **Cod and salmon together:** The two fish balance: cod for body and a clean taste, salmon for richness and colour. Substitute with any combination of firm white fish + an oily fish.
- **Add the fish at the very end:** Fish overcooks in seconds. The chunks finish poaching in the residual heat of the soup; they should be barely firm, not flaking.

## Serving
- Serve as a starter to a multi-course meal, or as a one-bowl supper with bread. A glass of dry Riesling, dry Norwegian apple cider, or sparkling water with lemon.

## Storage
- Best fresh; the fish toughens on reheating.
- The base (without the fish) refrigerates 3 days and freezes 2 months - add fresh fish each time you reheat.
- Don't microwave the assembled soup; the cream curdles. Reheat gently in a saucepan.
