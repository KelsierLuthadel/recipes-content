# Curtido

![Curtido](resources/curtido.jpg)

*The Salvadoran table relish that rides shotgun on every pupusa: lightly fermented cabbage and carrot in a pickle of cider vinegar, oregano and chilli flakes, sharp, bright and faintly fizzy.*

**Serves:** 6 (about 1 kg curtido)

**Prep Time:** 15 minutes

**Cook Time:** 0 minutes (plus 24-72 hours fermenting)

## Overview
Curtido is the Salvadoran answer to sauerkraut and Korean kimchi, a lightly fermented cabbage relish that sits in every pupusería in a clay or glass jar on the counter. The base is finely shredded cabbage and grated carrot, dressed with cider vinegar, oregano, salt and a hint of chilli, then left to bubble quietly at room temperature for a day or two before going into the fridge. It is properly fermented (not just pickled), and a good curtido is faintly fizzy on the tongue. It cuts the masa-and-cheese richness of pupusas perfectly, and the same jar lives on the table for yuca con chicharrón and tamales pisques. It keeps for weeks, getting better all the time.

## Ingredients

- 1 small white cabbage (about 800 g), cored, finely shredded
- 2 large carrots (about 300 g), peeled, coarsely grated
- 1 small onion, halved and finely sliced
- 2 tsp fine sea salt
- 2 tsp dried oregano (Mexican oregano preferred)
- 1/2-1 tsp dried chilli flakes (to taste)
- 250 ml apple cider vinegar
- 250 ml hot water (just off the boil)
- 1 tbsp panela or light brown sugar

## Method

### Stage 1 - Shred
1. Halve the cabbage, cut out the core and shred finely with a heavy knife (or a mandolin set thin). Aim for noodle-like strands.
2. Grate the carrots on the large holes of a box grater.
3. Slice the onion across the grain into thin half-moons.

### Stage 2 - Salt and bruise
1. Tip the cabbage, carrot and onion into a large bowl. Scatter over the salt.
2. Massage with clean hands for 2-3 minutes, pressing and squeezing the vegetables. They will weep liquid and reduce in volume.
3. Leave to sit for 15 minutes; more liquid will pool in the bowl.

### Stage 3 - Dress
1. In a jug, stir together the oregano, chilli flakes, vinegar, hot water and panela. Stir until the sugar dissolves.
2. Pour over the salted vegetables. Toss thoroughly.

### Stage 4 - Pack
1. Tip the lot into a large clean glass jar (or two smaller ones).
2. Press the vegetables down so the liquid rises to cover them; pack tightly.
3. Top with a small folded leaf of cabbage to keep stray strands submerged.
4. Cover loosely with the lid or a piece of cloth and a rubber band, so gases can escape.

### Stage 5 - Ferment
1. Leave at room temperature, out of direct sun, for 24-72 hours.
2. After 24 hours, taste; the cabbage will be crunchy and sharp. After 48 hours, the flavour is more rounded and a slight fizz appears. After 72 hours, it is at its most assertive.
3. Once it reaches a flavour you like, screw the lid on and move to the fridge. Fermentation slows dramatically in the cold.

## Notes
- **Cider vinegar, not white vinegar:** cider vinegar has the fruit note that defines curtido. White vinegar is too sharp; rice vinegar is too gentle.
- **Mexican oregano:** the right oregano. Mediterranean oregano works but the flavour is more grassy than citrusy.
- **Massage matters:** without the salt rub, the vegetables stay firm and the brine never reaches them. Two minutes of squeezing is enough.
- **Don't seal tight during fermentation:** active fermentation produces CO2. A tight lid risks the jar pinging.
- **Slow fermentation in winter:** below 18 C the ferment is sluggish; leave for 3-4 days.

## Variations
- **Curtido sin fermentar:** the quick-pickle version, ready to eat in two hours; less depth but useful in a hurry.
- **With beetroot:** add a single small grated raw beetroot for a striking pink curtido.
- **With jalapeño:** add 2 sliced jalapeños for a hotter relish.
- **De repollo morado:** use red cabbage; gives a deep purple jar.

## Serving
- Heaped on top of pupusas, never on the side · with yuca con chicharrón · as a filling for breakfast tortillas · in a small bowl alongside grilled meat · at any Salvadoran table the year round.

## Storage
- Keeps a month refrigerated, getting softer and more developed.
- The brine clouds and bubbles a little; this is the live ferment and is right.
- If a white film forms on top, skim it; if any pink, green or fuzzy spots appear, discard.
- Drain off and reuse the brine to start the next batch.
