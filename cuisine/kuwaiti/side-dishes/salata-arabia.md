# Salata Arabia

![Salata Arabia](resources/salata-arabia.jpg)

*Kuwait's chopped salad: cucumber, tomato, parsley and onion in a fine even dice, dressed with lemon and olive oil, the bright fresh hit beside heavy rice and stew.*

**Serves:** 4

**Prep Time:** 15 minutes

**Cook Time:** 0 minutes

## Overview
Salata arabia is the universal Arab chopped salad, cousin to Lebanese fattoush and Israeli salad but in the Gulf style: a clean, sharp, herb-heavy plate without bread crumbs or sumac, just the four core vegetables in a fine dice, plenty of parsley and mint, lemon and good olive oil. The technique that makes the difference is the dice: each piece should be the same small size so a spoonful catches a bit of everything at once. It goes beside machboos, alongside grilled fish, on every iftar table and in every Friday lunch.

## Ingredients

- 2 small cucumbers, in 0.5 cm dice
- 4 ripe tomatoes, in 0.5 cm dice
- 1 small red onion, finely chopped
- 1 green pepper, in 0.5 cm dice (optional)
- A large handful fresh parsley, finely chopped
- A small handful fresh mint, finely chopped

### Dressing
- Juice of 1.5 lemons
- 4 tbsp olive oil
- 1/2 tsp salt
- 1/4 tsp ground black pepper

## Method

### Stage 1 - Dice
1. Cut everything (cucumber, tomato, onion, pepper) to the same small even size, around half a centimetre. The uniformity is what makes the salad sing.

### Stage 2 - Combine and dress
1. Combine the diced vegetables in a wide bowl.
2. Whisk lemon, oil, salt and pepper in a small bowl.
3. Pour over the salad; toss gently.
4. Add parsley and mint; toss once more.
5. Taste; adjust salt and lemon.

## Notes
- **The dice is the work.** Sloppy chunks make a messy salad; uniform small cubes make a great one.
- **Dress at the table.** The salt draws water from the tomato within minutes; dress as late as you can.
- **No vinegar.** Lemon only. Vinegar reads Levantine, not Gulf.

## Serving
At room temperature in a wide shallow bowl, beside any spiced rice, kebab or grilled fish.

## Storage
- Eats best within 1 hour of dressing
- Undressed mixture keeps 4 hours in the fridge
