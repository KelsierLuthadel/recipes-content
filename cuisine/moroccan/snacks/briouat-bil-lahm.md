# Briouat Bil Lahm (Meat Briouats)

![Briouat bil Lahm](resources/briouat-bil-lahm.jpg)

*Morocco's iftar starter: warka triangles wrapped around slow-cooked spiced lamb with onion, herbs, ras-el-hanout and preserved lemon. Deep-fried.*

**Serves:** Makes 18 triangles

**Prep Time:** 30 minutes

**Cook Time:** 25 minutes

## Overview
Morocco's iftar starter and the small triangle pastry that turns up at the start of any celebration meal: warka triangles wrapped around slow-cooked spiced lamb mince with onion, herbs, ras-el-hanout and a sliver of preserved lemon for the salty-floral note that defines the dish. Cooking the filling fully dry is the catch; wet filling leaks during frying and the briouats burst. You soften finely diced onion in olive oil over medium heat for 10 to 12 minutes till translucent but not browned, add lamb mince and brown for five minutes breaking it up, then bloom garlic, ras-el-hanout, cumin, cinnamon, ginger, salt and pepper for a minute. Pour in stock and simmer eight to 10 minutes uncovered till dry-fragrant with no pooling liquid. Off the heat stir in parsley, coriander, a lightly beaten egg (off the heat so it binds without scrambling into ribbons) and finely diced preserved lemon peel (the peel only; the flesh is too soft and bitter). Cool fully. Stack warka or filo sheets, cut into 8 cm wide strips, cover with a damp tea towel. Brush each strip with olive oil, place a heaped teaspoon of filling at one corner, fold flag-style up the strip into a triangle, seal the final flap with egg-wash. Deep-fry at 170°C for three minutes turning till deep gold. Serve hot with lemon wedges and mint tea, optionally with a drizzle of honey.

## Ingredients

### Filling
- 2 tablespoons olive oil
- 1 onion (large, very finely diced)
- 400 g lamb mince (or beef-lamb mix)
- 4 garlic cloves (minced)
- 1 ½ teaspoons ras-el-hanout
- 1 teaspoon ground cumin
- ½ teaspoon ground cinnamon
- ¼ teaspoon ground ginger
- 1 teaspoon salt
- ½ teaspoon ground black pepper
- 100 ml stock (or water)
- 30 g flat-leaf parsley (chopped)
- 20 g fresh coriander (chopped)
- 1 egg (large, lightly beaten)
- ¼ preserved lemon (peel only, finely diced)

### Pastry
- 18 sheets warka (or filo pastry, cut to 8 × 25 cm strips)
- 2 tablespoons olive oil (for light brushing)
- 1 egg (beaten, for sealing)

### Frying
- 600 ml neutral oil

### To serve
- Lemon wedges
- Mint tea

## Method

### Stage 1 - Cook the filling
1. Heat 2 tablespoons olive oil in a wide pan over medium heat.
1. Add the diced onion; cook 10-12 minutes, stirring, until soft and translucent but not browned.
1. Add the mince; brown 5 minutes, breaking up.
1. Stir in the garlic, ras-el-hanout, cumin, cinnamon, ginger, salt and pepper.
1. Cook 1 minute till fragrant.
1. Pour in the stock; simmer 8-10 minutes uncovered until dry-fragrant (no pooling liquid).
1. Off heat; stir in the parsley, coriander, lightly beaten egg and finely diced preserved lemon peel.
1. Cool to room temperature.

### Stage 2 - Cut strips
1. Stack warka or filo sheets; cut into strips 8 cm wide.
1. Cover with a damp tea towel.

### Stage 3 - Shape
1. Take one strip; brush lightly with olive oil.
1. Place 1 heaped teaspoon of filling at the bottom-left corner.
1. Fold flag-style into a triangle: diagonal fold over the filling, then straight up, then diagonal, then straight up.
1. Walk up the strip until used.
1. Seal the final flap with egg-wash.
1. Repeat for the rest.

### Stage 4 - Fry
1. Heat oil to 170°C.
1. Lower 4-5 briouats; fry 3 minutes, turning, till deep gold.
1. Lift onto a wire rack.

### Stage 5 - Serve
1. Pile on a platter.
1. Serve hot with lemon wedges and mint tea.
1. Some versions get a drizzle of honey; this works particularly well with the warm spices.

## Notes
- **Cook the filling fully dry:** wet filling leaks during frying and the briouats burst. The stock should fully evaporate.
- **Egg in the cool filling, not the hot:** stirred in off the heat, the egg binds without scrambling. Adding hot would cook into ribbons.
- **Preserved lemon peel only:** the flesh is too soft and bitter for filling; the peel gives the salty-floral note that defines the dish.
- **Bake variant:** for a lighter version, brush with melted butter and bake at 200°C for 12-15 minutes. The filo browns and crisps. Not as Moroccan-iconic but acceptable.

## Storage
- Best fresh from the fryer.
- Refrigerate 2 days; reheat in a 180°C oven 7 minutes.
- Freeze unfried, 2 months; fry from frozen + 1 minute.
