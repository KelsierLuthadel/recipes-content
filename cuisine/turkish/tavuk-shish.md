# Tavuk Şiş

![Tavuk Sis](resources/tavuk-shish.jpg)

*Turkey's chicken kebabs: cubes of chicken thigh marinated in yogurt, garlic, lemon and Levantine spices, threaded onto flat skewers with onion and pepper, grilled hard over charcoal.*

**Serves:** 4-6 (10 skewers)

**Prep Time:** 25 minutes (plus 6 hours marinating)

**Cook Time:** 12 minutes

## Overview
Tavuk şiş is Turkey's chicken kebab counterpart to kuzu şiş (the lamb version), and the lighter of the two. The cut is essential: boneless skinless chicken thigh, never breast: thigh stays juicy on the grill; breast goes dry in minutes. Cubes 3 cm across (smaller overcook; larger don't cook evenly) marinate at least six hours and ideally twenty-four in a paste of plain yogurt, olive oil, crushed garlic, lemon juice, tomato paste, Turkish red pepper paste, Levantine seven-spice (baharat), Aleppo pepper, cumin, paprika and salt: the yogurt's enzymes tenderise the chicken while the spices penetrate. Threaded onto flat metal skewers alternating with chunks of red onion and bell pepper, grilled over hot charcoal for ten to twelve minutes till the outside chars and the inside stays juicy. Plated on a wooden platter with warm flatbread, yogurt-garlic sauce, sumac onions, grilled tomato and lemon wedges: or stuffed into a pita-style wrap with salad for the döner-style version.

## Ingredients

### Chicken and marinade
- 1 kg boneless skinless chicken thigh (cut into 3 cm cubes)

### Marinade
- 250 g plain yogurt (thick Greek-style)
- 4 tablespoons olive oil
- 8 garlic cloves (crushed)
- Juice of 2 lemons
- 2 tablespoons tomato paste
- 2 tablespoons Turkish red pepper paste (biber salçası)
- 1 tablespoon Levantine [baharat](../../base-ingredients/spices/baharat.md)
- 2 teaspoons Aleppo pepper
- 2 teaspoons ground cumin
- 2 teaspoons ground coriander
- 1 teaspoon ground paprika
- 1 teaspoon ground sumac
- 1 teaspoon dried oregano
- 2 teaspoons fine sea salt
- 1 teaspoon ground black pepper

### Vegetables for skewers
- 2 large red onions (cut into 2 cm wedges)
- 2 large red bell peppers (cut into 3 cm pieces)
- 2 large green bell peppers (cut into 3 cm pieces)
- 10 small cherry tomatoes (or 5 small tomatoes, halved)

### To serve
- Warm pide bread or lavash
- Yogurt-garlic sauce
- Sumac onions (1 sliced red onion + 1 tablespoon sumac + 1 tablespoon olive oil + pinch salt)
- Grilled tomatoes and chillies
- Bulgur or rice pilav
- Fresh parsley
- Lemon wedges

## Method

### Stage 1 - Marinate the chicken (6+ hours ahead)
1. Combine all marinade ingredients in a wide bowl; whisk to a smooth paste.
2. Add the chicken cubes; toss to coat thoroughly.
3. Cover and refrigerate 6-24 hours.

### Stage 2 - Bring to room temperature and thread
1. Take the marinated chicken out of the fridge 30 minutes before cooking.
2. Lift the cubes from the marinade.
3. Thread onto flat metal skewers (10 skewers total), alternating with onion wedges, red pepper, green pepper and cherry tomatoes.
4. Pack the chicken cubes close together (with vegetables between, but the chicken touching).

### Stage 3 - Prepare the grill
1. Light a charcoal grill; let burn down to glowing embers.
2. Or heat a heavy ridged grill pan over high heat till smoking.

### Stage 4 - Grill
1. Place skewers on the hot grill.
2. Cook 3 minutes per side, turning a quarter-turn each time (4 turns total).
3. Total cooking time: 10-12 minutes.
4. The chicken should be properly charred outside and just cooked through (165°F / 74°C internal); not pink, not dry.

### Stage 5 - Rest
1. Lift onto a warm platter; let rest 3 minutes.

### Stage 6 - Serve
1. Arrange skewers on a wooden platter.
2. Surround with warm flatbread, yogurt sauce, sumac onions, grilled tomatoes, pilav, parsley and lemon wedges.
3. Diners build their own bites: flatbread + chicken + yogurt + onions, fold up and eat.

## Notes
- **Chicken thigh, not breast:** chicken breast goes dry on the grill in minutes. Boneless skinless thigh stays juicy. Don't substitute.
- **6 hours marinade minimum:** the yogurt needs time to tenderise. Skipping the marinade gives bland tough chicken.
- **Flat metal skewers:** prevent the chicken from spinning around. Wooden skewers work but soak for 30 minutes first.
- **Pack the cubes close together:** chicken touching each other on the skewer gives proper juicy results. Spread-out cubes dry out.
- **Don't overcook:** chicken thigh has more fat tolerance than breast, but still goes dry past 12 minutes. Stop when the surface is properly charred and the inside just cooked.

## Variations
- **Tavuk şiş with cumin-yogurt marinade only:** simplify to yogurt + cumin + garlic + lemon + salt; less complex but quicker. Good for weeknights.
- **Spicy tavuk şiş:** double the Aleppo pepper and add 2 chopped fresh chillies to the marinade. Properly fierce version.
- **Chicken kebab wraps (tavuk dürüm):** strip cooked chicken from the skewers; wrap in lavash with yogurt, salad and chips. The Turkish street-food version.
- **Tavuk pirzola:** instead of cubes on skewers, use chicken thighs whole, flattened slightly, marinated and grilled. Different presentation, same flavour.

## Serving
- On a wooden platter, with all the Turkish kebab sides arranged around. Cold ayran (the traditional drink), rakı, or Türk kahvesi after. As a family meal, weekend barbecue, or restaurant dinner.

## Storage
- Best eaten fresh off the grill.
- Cooked chicken keeps refrigerated 3 days; reheat in a hot pan briefly or use cold in salads.
- Marinated raw chicken keeps refrigerated 48 hours; freezes 3 months in the marinade.
- Don't freeze cooked chicken; the texture suffers.
- Day-old chicken makes excellent shawarma-style wraps with yogurt and salad.
