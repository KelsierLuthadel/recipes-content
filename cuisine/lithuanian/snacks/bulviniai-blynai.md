# Bulviniai Blynai

![Bulviniai Blynai](resources/bulviniai-blynai.jpg)

*Lithuanian potato pancakes: finely grated raw potato bound with egg and onion, pan-fried in oil until lacy and gold-crisp, stacked high with cold sour cream and crispy bacon bits.*

**Serves:** 4

**Prep Time:** 15 minutes

**Cook Time:** 25 minutes

## Overview
Bulviniai blynai are the everyday Lithuanian potato pancake, the dish every grandmother makes, the dish every café has on the menu, the dish that hits the table whenever a quick comforting plate is needed. They differ from their German latke cousins in being thinner, lacier and faster-cooked, with no flour to weigh them down, just grated potato, onion, egg and seasoning, hot oil, and the cook's eye for the right gold colour. The trick is in the grating (fine, not coarse) and the speed (work the moment the potato is on the grater, before it browns). Stacked four high on a warm plate, crowned with a generous dollop of cold sour cream and a scatter of crisp bacon bits, they are the Lithuanian lunch most home cooks turn to first when something quick and good is called for.

## Ingredients

### For the pancakes
- 1 kg starchy potatoes (Maris Piper or similar), peeled
- 1 small onion, finely grated
- 2 large eggs
- 1.5 tsp salt
- 1/2 tsp black pepper
- 2 tbsp plain flour (for binding, optional)
- 4 tbsp sunflower oil, for frying

### For serving
- 300 ml sour cream
- 150 g smoked streaky bacon, diced and fried crisp
- 2 tbsp chopped chives or dill

## Method

### Stage 1 - Grate the potatoes
1. Grate the peeled potatoes finely on the small side of a box grater (or in a food processor with a fine grating disc).
2. Working fast (raw potato browns on contact with air), tip into a colander.
3. Press lightly with a spoon to drain some liquid; don't dry completely.

### Stage 2 - Mix the batter
1. Combine the drained grated potato, grated onion, eggs, salt, pepper and flour (if using) in a large bowl.
2. Mix well; the batter should hold loosely on a spoon, not run off freely.

### Stage 3 - Fry
1. Heat 2 tablespoons of oil in a wide pan over medium-high heat.
2. Drop heaped tablespoons of batter into the pan; flatten each into a thin disc 8-10 cm wide.
3. Fry 3-4 pancakes at a time, 2-3 minutes per side until deep gold and lacy at the edges.
4. Press down lightly with a spatula in the first minute to thin out.
5. Lift onto kitchen paper to drain briefly.
6. Add more oil between batches as needed; keep the pan hot.

### Stage 4 - Fry the bacon
1. While the pancakes fry, render the diced bacon in a small pan over medium heat 6-7 minutes until crisp.
2. Drain on kitchen paper.

### Stage 5 - Serve
1. Stack the pancakes on a warm plate (about 4 per portion).
2. Spoon over cold sour cream.
3. Scatter the crisp bacon bits over.
4. Finish with chopped chives or dill.

## Notes
- **Grate fine:** coarse grating gives a chewy uneven pancake. Fine grating gives the lacy classic texture.
- **Don't oversalt the batter:** the bacon brings salt; taste before piling more on.
- **Hot pan, thin disc:** the gold-and-crisp edge is the goal. Thick lumpy pancakes go stodgy.
- **Eat warm:** they stiffen as they cool. The right time is straight from the pan.

## Variations
**With apple sauce:** swap sour cream for cold apple sauce, the sweet-savoury version.
**With smoked salmon:** top with smoked salmon, sour cream and dill, a Baltic-coast plate.
**Cheese-stuffed:** put a small piece of curd cheese in the centre of each before frying.
**Vegetarian (no bacon):** scatter fried onion or chopped chives, with the sour cream.
**Mushroom-sauce version:** serve smothered in a creamy wild-mushroom sauce instead of sour cream.

## Serving
Serve hot stacked on a warm plate · with cold sour cream and bacon · with apple sauce · with smoked salmon · at a weekend breakfast · with a glass of kefir · as a quick weeknight dinner.

## Storage
- Best fresh from the pan.
- Cooked pancakes keep 2 days refrigerated; recrisp in a hot dry pan.
- Freeze cooked pancakes in layers between parchment for 2 months; reheat in a 200°C oven.
- Uncooked batter does not keep, it browns and turns watery.
