# Bsisa

![Bsisa](resources/bsisa.jpg)

*The traditional Libyan energy snack: toasted barley and chickpea flour kneaded with olive oil and date syrup into dark rich balls, eaten at dawn before work and carried into the fields wrapped in cloth.*

**Serves:** 12 small balls

**Prep Time:** 10 minutes

**Cook Time:** 20 minutes (toasting)

## Overview
Bsisa is one of the oldest snacks in North Africa, a toasted-flour preparation that travels back through Libyan, Tunisian and Maghrebi village kitchens for centuries. The base is a blend of barley and chickpea flour, slowly toasted in a dry pan until the kitchen fills with a nutty smell, then bound with olive oil, date syrup and a pinch of warming spice and pressed into walnut-sized balls. The texture is dense and slightly sandy on the bite, then sweet and oily as it warms in the mouth. Libyans traditionally eat bsisa in the early morning before a day of physical work, or on the first of the year, or to break a fast; children get it as an after-school energy snack. The toasting is everything: take the flour past pale and into properly dark, and the snack tastes of caramel and hazelnut rather than raw flour.

## Ingredients

- 150 g barley flour
- 100 g chickpea flour (gram flour)
- 2 tbsp sesame seeds
- 1 tbsp fennel seeds
- 1/2 tsp ground cinnamon
- 1/4 tsp ground cardamom
- Pinch of fine salt
- 80 ml extra virgin olive oil
- 80 ml date syrup (or honey)
- 40 ml warm water, as needed

## Method

### Stage 1 - Toast the flour
1. Set a wide dry pan over medium-low heat.
2. Tip in the barley and chickpea flours together.
3. Stir constantly with a wooden spoon for 12-15 minutes until the colour turns from pale beige to deep golden brown and the kitchen smells of toasted nuts.
4. Do not let it scorch; pull the pan off the heat for 30 seconds if it threatens to darken too fast.
5. Tip onto a wide plate to cool.

### Stage 2 - Toast the seeds
1. In the same pan, toast the sesame seeds for 2 minutes until pale gold.
2. Add the fennel seeds for the last 30 seconds.
3. Tip onto the same plate as the flour to cool.
4. When cool, grind half the fennel and half the sesame in a mortar; leave the rest whole.

### Stage 3 - Mix the dough
1. Sift the cooled toasted flour into a wide bowl.
2. Stir in the ground and whole seeds, cinnamon, cardamom and salt.
3. Pour the olive oil and date syrup over the dry mix.
4. Work with a spoon and then your fingers for 3-4 minutes until it forms a stiff dark paste.
5. If it feels too dry to hold together, add the warm water a tablespoon at a time.

### Stage 4 - Shape
1. Pinch off pieces about the size of a walnut (around 30 g each).
2. Roll each into a tight ball between your palms.
3. Press a sesame seed or a small piece of date into the top for decoration.
4. Lay out on a plate and rest 30 minutes for the flavours to settle.

## Notes
- **Toast properly:** undertoasted flour tastes raw and chalky; the colour should be deep golden, almost the colour of milk chocolate.
- **Date syrup over honey:** date syrup is the traditional Libyan binder and carries a deeper flavour; honey works but is sweeter and lighter.
- **Texture check:** the dough should hold a ball when squeezed; if it crumbles, add olive oil rather than water for a richer bite.
- **Resting:** the balls firm up after an hour at room temperature.

## Variations
- Stir in 50 g chopped pitted dates for a chewier centre.
- Add 50 g ground almonds for a richer nuttier ball.
- Roll the finished balls in toasted sesame seeds to coat.
- Press into a tray instead of rolling, then cut into squares.
- Stir in a pinch of ground ginger and clove for a winter version.

## Serving
At breakfast with strong tea or coffee · as an after-school snack for children · wrapped in cloth for fieldwork or a long walk · at the table to break a fast · alongside dates and milk for a traditional first meal of the year.

## Storage
- Keep in an airtight tin at room temperature for 2 weeks.
- Refrigerate up to 1 month; bring back to room temperature before eating.
- Do not freeze (the oil separates).
