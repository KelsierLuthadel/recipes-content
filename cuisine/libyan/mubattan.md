# Mubattan

![Mubattan](resources/mubattan.jpg)

*Libyan stuffed potatoes: sliced rounds sandwiched around spiced lamb mince, dipped in beaten egg, shallow-fried golden and finished in a tomato sauce.*

**Serves:** 4

**Prep Time:** 30 minutes

**Cook Time:** 50 minutes

## Overview
Mubattan turns up at the Friday family lunch and the iftar table during Ramadan. Thinly sliced potato rounds are paired up and stuffed with a spiced lamb mince; each parcel is dipped in beaten egg, then shallow-fried to a deep gold; the finished parcels are simmered briefly in a tomato sauce that softens the potato through to the centre. The bite reads as crisp shell, soft potato, savoury mince, slightly tangy tomato. Eaten with bread to mop up the sauce.

## Ingredients

### Stuffing
- 250 g lamb mince
- 1 small onion, finely chopped
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 2 tbsp finely chopped parsley
- 1 tsp cumin
- 1 tsp paprika
- 1/2 tsp cinnamon
- Salt and pepper

### Potatoes + fry
- 4 medium potatoes (Maris Piper or similar)
- 2 large eggs, beaten
- 4 tbsp olive oil for frying
- Plain flour for dusting

### Sauce
- 3 tbsp olive oil
- 1 small onion, finely chopped
- 2 cloves garlic, sliced
- 2 tbsp tomato paste
- 400 g tinned chopped tomatoes
- 1 tsp sweet paprika
- 1/2 tsp cumin
- 1 tsp bisbas or [harissa](../../base-ingredients/sauces/harissa.md)
- 400 ml water
- Salt and pepper

## Method

### Stage 1 - Mix the stuffing
1. Combine mince, onion, garlic, parsley, spices, salt and pepper in a bowl. Mix with hands until even.

### Stage 2 - Prepare the potato pairs
1. Peel the potatoes. Slice into rounds 5 mm thick.
2. Working in pairs of similar-sized rounds, place a teaspoon of stuffing on one round, top with the matching round, press to seal. Dust the rims lightly with flour to help the seal.

### Stage 3 - Fry
1. Heat the olive oil in a wide pan over medium heat.
2. Dip each potato sandwich in beaten egg, letting excess drip off.
3. Fry 4-5 minutes per side until deep golden. Drain on paper.

### Stage 4 - Make the sauce
1. In a wide pot, heat olive oil. Soften onion 5 minutes; add garlic for 1 minute.
2. Add tomato paste; cook 2 minutes.
3. Add tomatoes, paprika, cumin, bisbas. Cook 5 minutes until darkened.
4. Pour in the water, salt and pepper. Bring to a simmer.

### Stage 5 - Simmer in sauce
1. Lay the fried mubattan in the sauce in a single layer (use a wide pan).
2. Cover and simmer on low 25-30 minutes until the potatoes are tender to a knife and have soaked up colour from the sauce.
3. Taste sauce; adjust salt and chilli.

## Notes
- **Slicing thickness:** 5 mm rounds hold together well; thinner falls apart, thicker stays raw at the centre.
- **The egg coat:** Don't skip - it forms the bond that prevents the mince leaking out during frying.
- **Sauce-to-potato ratio:** The sauce should come about halfway up the parcels, not submerge them. Too much liquid and the egg coat dissolves; too little and the potato stays underdone.

## Serving
- Serve hot from the pan with crusty bread, a wedge of lemon and a small bowl of harissa. A side of Libyan tomato-cucumber salad cuts the richness.

## Storage
- Refrigerate 2 days; reheat gently in the sauce, covered, on low.
- Freezing is not recommended - the potato texture deteriorates.
