# Königsberger Klopse (Meatballs in White Caper-Cream Sauce)

![Königsberger Klopse](resources/konigsberger-klopse.jpg)

*East Prussian elegance: pale meatballs of veal and pork (anchovy-bound for depth) gently poached in stock, then served in a creamy white béchamel-style sauce brightened with capers, lemon and a touch of white wine. The dish from Königsberg (now Kaliningrad) that emigrated to West Germany with the post-war refugees; the traditional white meatball-and-sauce dish; one of the most refined dishes in the German canon.*

**Serves:** 4-6

**Prep Time:** 30 minutes

**Cook Time:** 30 minutes

## Overview
Königsberger Klopse (literally "meatballs from Königsberg"; the city is now Kaliningrad in Russia) is one of Germany's most refined classical dishes, a holdover from the cuisine of East Prussia, brought westward by the millions of German refugees who fled the region after World War Two. The construction has two distinct elements: pale meatballs made from finely minced veal and pork (with rusks/breadcrumbs as binder, an anchovy or two for deep umami without fishiness, finely chopped onion, parsley, an egg, and gentle seasoning), shaped into smooth balls and poached gently in a flavoured stock (not boiled, the meatballs would toughen); and a creamy white sauce (a classic béchamel enriched with the poaching stock, lifted with capers and lemon juice, and finished with cream and egg yolk). The dish is served with white rice or boiled potatoes. The pale colour palette is traditional: white sauce, white meatballs, white rice, all in a deep white bowl.

## Ingredients

### Meatballs
- 400 g minced veal
- 300 g minced pork (about 20% fat)
- 1 medium onion (very finely diced)
- 4 anchovy fillets (or 2 teaspoons anchovy paste)
- 4 tablespoons finely chopped fresh parsley
- 80 g stale white bread (crusts off; soaked in 100 ml milk for 5 minutes; then squeeze out excess milk)
- 1 large egg (lightly beaten)
- 1 tablespoon Dijon mustard
- 1 teaspoon fine sea salt
- 1 teaspoon coarsely ground white pepper
- Zest of 1 lemon

### Poaching stock
- 1.5 litres chicken or vegetable stock
- 1 onion (halved)
- 2 bay leaves
- 6 black peppercorns
- 4 whole cloves
- 4 allspice berries
- 1 small carrot
- A small thyme sprig

### Sauce
- 60 g unsalted butter
- 60 g plain flour
- 800 ml of the meatball poaching stock (strained)
- 200 ml double cream
- 2 large egg yolks
- 4 tablespoons capers in brine (drained; or salt-packed, well rinsed)
- 4 tablespoons fresh lemon juice
- 2 tablespoons white wine vinegar
- 100 ml dry white wine
- 1 teaspoon caster sugar
- 2 tablespoons finely chopped fresh parsley
- 1 teaspoon fine sea salt
- ½ teaspoon coarsely ground white pepper

### To serve
- 400 g cooked white rice OR 800 g boiled new potatoes
- A small bowl of extra capers (for those who want more)
- Lemon wedges
- A green salad alongside (modern; not traditional)
- A glass of dry German Riesling

## Method

### Stage 1 - Make the meatball mixture
1. In a large bowl, combine the minced veal, minced pork, diced onion, anchovy fillets (finely chopped or paste), parsley, soaked bread (squeezed dry), egg, mustard, salt, white pepper, and lemon zest.
2. Mix thoroughly with your hands till evenly combined and slightly sticky.
3. Don't overwork (toughens the meatballs).
4. Cover; refrigerate 20 minutes (firms up; easier to shape).

### Stage 2 - Make the poaching stock
1. In a wide deep pan, combine the stock, halved onion, bay leaves, peppercorns, cloves, allspice, carrot, and thyme.
2. Bring to a simmer (don't boil hard).
3. Hold at gentle simmer 10 minutes.

### Stage 3 - Shape the meatballs
1. Wet your hands lightly.
2. Form the meatball mixture into 24 walnut-sized balls (about 35 g each).
3. Roll smooth between your palms.
4. Place on a tray.

### Stage 4 - Poach the meatballs
1. Reduce the heat under the stock to LOW (you want a gentle quiver, about 80°C).
2. Lower the meatballs into the stock carefully (don't crowd; if necessary, poach in two batches).
3. Poach 15-18 minutes till the meatballs are fully cooked (internal temperature 70°C).
4. Don't boil; gentle quiver only.
5. Lift the meatballs out carefully with a slotted spoon; place in a warm bowl.
6. Strain the poaching stock through a fine sieve; reserve 800 ml.

### Stage 5 - Make the sauce (béchamel)
1. In a clean saucepan, melt the butter over medium heat.
2. Add the flour; whisk to make a roux; cook 2 minutes (don't brown).
3. Slowly whisk in the reserved poaching stock.
4. Whisk constantly; bring to a gentle simmer.
5. Cook 5-7 minutes till the sauce thickens to coat the back of a spoon thickly.

### Stage 6 - Lift the sauce
1. Add the white wine; cook 2 minutes (alcohol burns off).
2. Stir in the capers, lemon juice, vinegar, sugar, parsley, salt, and pepper.
3. Taste; adjust acid and salt.

### Stage 7 - Enrich with cream and yolks
1. In a small bowl, whisk the cream and egg yolks together.
2. Whisk a ladle of the hot sauce into the cream-yolk mix to temper (don't dump cold yolks in; they'd scramble).
3. Slowly pour the tempered cream-yolk back into the sauce, whisking constantly.
4. Heat gently 1-2 minutes WITHOUT boiling (boiling will scramble the yolks).
5. The sauce should now be glossy, creamy, and silky.

### Stage 8 - Combine and warm
1. Return the meatballs to the sauce gently.
2. Warm through 2-3 minutes over low heat (don't boil).
3. Taste again; adjust seasoning.

### Stage 9 - Serve
1. Spoon a portion of rice or boiled potatoes onto each warm plate.
2. Ladle 4-5 meatballs and a generous amount of sauce alongside.
3. Sprinkle extra chopped parsley over.
4. Add a wedge of lemon.
5. Optional: a small dish of extra capers.
6. Pair with a chilled glass of dry German Riesling.

## Notes
- **Anchovies are the secret:** the finished dish doesn't taste fishy; the anchovies add deep umami. Don't skip.
- **Gentle poaching, never boiling:** boiling toughens the meatballs. 80°C is the target.
- **Don't boil the sauce after adding egg yolks:** the yolks scramble. Heat gently after enriching.
- **Capers are non-negotiable:** they give the dish its character. Use capers in brine (or salt-packed, well rinsed).
- **The white-on-white look is traditional:** don't garnish with paprika or anything coloured. Pale meatballs in pale sauce in white bowls.

## Variations
- **Spreewald Klopse:** the Saxon-region variant uses dill pickles in the sauce instead of capers, more vinegary.
- **Klopse with mushroom (modern):** add 200 g sliced mushrooms to the sauce, modern variant.
- **Beetroot-juice-coloured (Berlin):** add a tablespoon of beet juice to the sauce for a pink tint, modernist twist.
- **Vegan Klopse:** use a mixture of finely chopped mushrooms + cooked lentils + breadcrumbs to replace the meat; oat cream instead of dairy.
- **With sour cream finish:** add 3 tablespoons sour cream to the sauce, Sile sian variant.
- **With dill and lemon (Northern German):** add 2 tablespoons fresh dill to the sauce, coastal variant.

## Storage
- Meatballs + sauce refrigerate 3 days; reheat gently in a saucepan (don't boil, the egg-yolk-enriched sauce splits if reboiled).
- Don't freeze (the sauce splits on defrosting).
- The poached meatballs alone can be frozen 1 month; remake the sauce fresh.
- Leftover meatballs in their sauce make excellent next-day open-faced sandwiches.
