# Cala (Aruban Black-Eyed Pea Fritters)

![Cala](resources/cala.jpg)

*Small golden fritters of soaked-and-blended black-eyed peas with onion, garlic and aji pepper, deep-fried until the outside is crisp and the inside is light and savoury.*

**Serves:** 6 (makes about 24 fritters)

**Prep Time:** 20 minutes plus overnight soak

**Cook Time:** 15 minutes

## Overview
Cala is the Aruban black-eyed pea fritter, the West African inheritance that travelled across the Atlantic and settled into the kitchens of the ABC islands as a side, a snack and a Lenten meatless meal. The dried peas soak overnight, then the loose skins are rubbed off and the cream-coloured inside is blended with onion, garlic, salt and a small piece of aji pepper into a sticky pale batter. Spooned into hot oil, the spoonfuls puff and brown into golden nuggets with a soft savoury inside. Aruban cala is plainer than the spiced Brazilian acaraje of Salvador (no shrimp paste, no palm oil) but shares the same African-Atlantic root. Served beside stewed beans and rice at a Friday lunch, or stacked beside sopi as a side, or eaten on their own with pepper sauce as a snack, cala is the dish every Aruban grandmother makes a batch of when family is visiting from Curacao or the Netherlands.

## Ingredients

- 300 g dried black-eyed peas
- 1 medium onion, roughly chopped
- 3 cloves garlic
- 1 small aji or Madame Jeanette pepper, deseeded (or 1 tsp pepper sauce)
- 1 tsp salt
- 1/2 tsp black pepper
- 1/2 tsp baking powder
- 2 tbsp finely chopped spring onion
- A small handful of fresh coriander or parsley, chopped
- 1 litre sunflower oil for deep-frying

## Method

### Stage 1 - Soak and skin the peas
1. Cover the dried peas with plenty of cold water; soak overnight (8 hours minimum).
2. Drain the soaked peas into a colander.
3. Rub the peas vigorously between your palms in the colander under cold running water; the loose papery skins will float off.
4. Skim the floating skins from the water; repeat until most skins are gone (a few left behind are fine).
5. Drain very well.

### Stage 2 - Blend the batter
1. Place the skinned peas, onion, garlic, aji pepper, salt and pepper in a food processor.
2. Pulse, scraping down, until you have a thick coarse paste with a little texture.
3. Add 2-3 tbsp cold water only if the blender refuses to turn; the batter should stay stiff.
4. Transfer to a bowl; stir in the baking powder, spring onion and coriander.
5. Beat the batter with a wooden spoon for 1 minute; this lightens the texture.

### Stage 3 - Fry
1. Heat the oil in a deep pot to 170 C.
2. Drop heaped teaspoons of batter into the oil, working in batches of 6-8.
3. Fry 3-4 minutes, turning, until deep golden and crisp.
4. Lift onto a kitchen-paper-lined tray; salt while hot.
5. Let the oil come back to temperature between batches.

## Notes
- **Skin the peas thoroughly:** the skins are bitter and make the cala muddy. The rubbing-in-water step is non-negotiable.
- **Keep the batter stiff:** a loose batter spreads in the oil and gives flat fritters. The mix should hold its shape on a spoon.
- **Beat the batter to lighten:** the air whisked in is what gives the soft fluffy inside.
- **170 C oil:** too hot burns the outside before the inside cooks; too low soaks up oil.
- **Eat warm:** cala go from crisp to chewy within an hour. Fry just before serving.

## Variations
**Cala cu pisca:** add 100 g finely chopped salt cod to the batter for the Lenten fishy version.
**Spicy cala:** double the aji pepper for a hotter fritter.
**Cala with herbs:** add a tablespoon of finely chopped fresh thyme.
**Mini cala:** drop teaspoons of batter for cocktail-bite-sized fritters.
**Oven cala:** spoon onto an oiled tray and bake at 200 C for 18 minutes (texture is less crisp but lighter).

## Serving
At a Friday lunch · alongside sopi di pisca · with stewed black beans and rice · as a side at Sunday lunch · as a snack with pepper sauce · piled on a paper-lined platter for a family gathering · with a wedge of lime.

## Storage
- Eat warm the same day for the best texture.
- The skinned blended batter refrigerates 1 day; stir well and re-beat before frying.
- Fried cala refrigerate 2 days; reheat at 200 C for 6 minutes to re-crisp.
- Freeze fried cala 1 month; reheat from frozen at 200 C for 10 minutes.
- The dried peas keep indefinitely in a sealed jar.
