# Aruban

*The kitchen of the southern Dutch Caribbean. Papiamento-speaking, Spanish-Dutch-African-indigenous syntheses, with keshi yena (stuffed cheese) as the showpiece and stoba (the slow-stewed one-pot) as the everyday plate.*

## A short history

Aruban cooking is the cuisine of one of the ABC islands (Aruba, Bonaire, Curaçao) — the southern Dutch Caribbean, off the coast of Venezuela. The Dutch colonised the island in 1636; the Spanish had been there before; the indigenous Caquetio Arawak population predates both. The South American mainland's proximity (Aruba is just 25km from Venezuela) layered on Venezuelan-South American influence.

The signature dish — keshi yena (literally "filled cheese") — is a remarkable colonial-era invention. Slaves working in Dutch households scraped out a hollowed Edam or Gouda cheese rind, stuffed it with spiced meat (often the leftover chicken or beef from the masters' table), and baked it. The dish became a national symbol of how the people made magic from castoffs.

Spanish, Dutch and Papiamento are spoken across the island (Papiamento is a creole language combining Portuguese, Spanish, Dutch, English and African languages — the cuisine reflects the same hybridity).

## Regional variations

Aruba is small (180 km²) and the cuisine is broadly consistent. Sub-traditions are by community:

- **Oranjestad and the south**: codified mainstream. The cosmopolitan capital, tourist-influenced.
- **San Nicolas (south-east)**: more African-Caribbean, the more traditional shelf.
- **Noord (north)**: more rural, traditional family cooking.

## Signature ingredients

- **Cheese (Edam, Gouda)**: in keshi yena.
- **Goat meat**: in stoba di cabritu.
- **Pumpkin**: in pumpkin pancakes (pan bati di pampuna), in stews.
- **Pisca (fish)**: in sopi di pisca.
- **Olives and capers**: in keshi yena filling.
- **Hot pepper**: Madame Janette is the local variety.
- **Coriander, basil, oregano**: the herb base.

## Learn it in four recipes

1. **[Keshi Yena (Aruban)](/recipes/cuisine/aruban/keshi-yena-aruban)**: hollowed Edam cheese filled with spiced chicken or beef, raisins, olives and capers, baked till the cheese melts around the filling. The national dish.
2. **[Stoba di Cabritu](/recipes/cuisine/aruban/stoba-di-cabritu)**: goat slow-stewed with onion, tomato, garlic and hot pepper. Served with funchi (cornmeal mash).
3. **[Karni Stoba (Aruban)](/recipes/cuisine/aruban/karni-stoba-aruban)**: beef stew with raisins, olives and capers — the daily home cooking.
4. **[Sopi di Pisca](/recipes/cuisine/aruban/sopi-di-pisca)**: fish soup with potato, sweet potato, plantain and Madame Janette pepper.
5. **[Pasthechi (Aruba)](/recipes/cuisine/aruban/pasthechi-aruba)**: half-moon fried pastries filled with cheese, ground beef or chicken. The Aruban empanada.

## Where to go next

Pair everything with Balashi beer (the national lager) or with ponche crema (the Venezuelan-influenced eggnog-style liqueur). End with pan bati (sweet cornmeal pancake) or with quesillo (the dense flan-style custard).
