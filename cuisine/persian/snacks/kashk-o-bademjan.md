# Kashk O Bademjan

![Kashk o Bademjan](resources/kashk-o-bademjan.jpg)

*Persia's defining aubergine dip: roasted aubergines mashed with garlic and dried mint, swirled with kashk and topped with crispy onions and walnuts.*

**Serves:** 4 (as a starter / snack)

**Prep Time:** 25 minutes

**Cook Time:** 50 minutes

## Overview
Kashk o bademjan is the Persian appetiser that turns up on every mezze plate from Tehran to Esfahan, a smoky aubergine dip drizzled with kashk and crowned with crispy fried onions, walnuts and a sizzle of dried-mint oil. Char the aubergines first till the skin is blackened and the flesh has collapsed completely (open flame over a gas burner gives the best smoky depth; oven roast at 220°C for forty minutes is the simpler home option), then steam them under cling film for ten minutes before peeling and chopping coarsely. Meanwhile fry sliced onions hard in sunflower oil for ten or twelve minutes till deep dark brown and crisp (don't stop at gold, push the colour till the edges are nearly burnt), and reserve. Soften diced onion in olive oil for the base, bloom turmeric and saffron-water, then add the chopped aubergine with a splash of water and mash to a thick spoonable dip over ten minutes of simmering. Sizzle crushed garlic in olive oil till just gold, off the heat with two generous spoons of dried mint that turns deep green on contact: fold two-thirds into the aubergine, reserve the rest. Spread the warm dip into a wide shallow bowl, drizzle kashk across in lines (thin with warm water if it's a thick paste), scatter the dark crispy onions and chopped walnuts, drizzle the reserved mint oil and finish with extra dried mint and a dust of Aleppo pepper. Eat warm or at room temperature with warm sangak or lavash for scooping, every bite catching some aubergine, kashk, fried onion, mint oil and walnut together.

## Ingredients

### Aubergine base
- 3 aubergines (large, about 800 g total)
- 4 tablespoons olive oil OR sunflower oil
- 2 onions (medium, finely diced)
- 4 garlic cloves (crushed)
- 1 teaspoon ground turmeric
- 1 large pinch saffron threads (soaked in 2 tablespoons hot water)
- 1 teaspoon salt (to taste)
- ½ teaspoon black pepper
- 100 ml water

### Mint-and-garlic oil
- 3 tablespoons olive oil
- 4 garlic cloves (sliced thin)
- 2 tablespoons dried mint

### Crispy fried onions
- 1 onion (large, sliced VERY thin)
- 3 tablespoons sunflower oil (for frying)

### To plate
- 150 g kashk (Persian fermented whey, sold liquid OR thick paste at Iranian / Middle Eastern shops; if paste, thin with 3 tablespoons warm water to a pourable consistency)
- 30 g walnut halves (toasted, chopped to a rubble)
- 1 teaspoon extra dried mint
- 1 teaspoon Aleppo pepper

### To serve
- Sangak, lavash, or pita bread (warm)

## Method

### Stage 1 - Roast aubergines
1. **Best (open flame)**: place whole aubergines directly over a gas burner; turn every 2 minutes for about 12-15 minutes until the skin is completely charred and the flesh has collapsed.
1. **Oven**: heat to 220°C; pierce aubergines a few times; roast on a tray 40 minutes until completely soft.
1. **Broiler**: 5 minutes per side under a hot grill until charred.
1. Place in a bowl; cover with cling film 10 minutes (steams the skin off).
1. Peel under cold running water; squeeze out excess liquid; chop coarsely.

### Stage 2 - Crispy onions (do early; need cooling time)
1. Heat 3 tablespoons sunflower oil over medium heat.
1. Fry the thinly sliced onion 10-12 minutes until DEEP brown and crisp (not just gold - push the colour).
1. Lift onto kitchen paper. Reserve.

### Stage 3 - Aubergine base
1. In a wide pan, heat 4 tablespoons olive oil over medium.
1. Sauté diced onion 8 minutes until soft and just gold.
1. Add crushed garlic; cook 1 minute.
1. Stir in turmeric, saffron-water, salt and pepper; cook 30 seconds.
1. Add the chopped roasted aubergine; mash gently with a wooden spoon to a rough purée.
1. Add water; simmer 10 minutes - the mixture thickens to a thick spoonable dip.
1. Taste; adjust salt.

### Stage 4 - Mint and garlic oil
1. In a small pan, heat 3 tablespoons olive oil over medium-low.
1. Add the sliced garlic; cook 60 seconds until just gold.
1. Off heat; immediately stir in dried mint (it should sizzle and turn deep green).

### Stage 5 - Combine
1. Pour two-thirds of the mint-and-garlic oil into the aubergine pan; stir.
1. Reserve the other third for topping.

### Stage 6 - Plate
1. Spread the warm aubergine mixture in a wide shallow bowl.
1. Drizzle the kashk in lines across the top (if too thick, thin with warm water to a pourable consistency).
1. Scatter crispy fried onions.
1. Drizzle the reserved mint-and-garlic oil.
1. Scatter chopped walnuts.
1. Sprinkle extra dried mint and Aleppo pepper.

### Stage 7 - Serve
1. Eat warm or at room temperature with warm sangak / lavash / pita for scooping.
1. Each scoop should include some of every layer: the aubergine, kashk, fried onion, mint oil, walnut.

## Notes
- **Char the aubergine:** The smoky note from charring the skin (open flame is best) is essential. Oven-baked aubergine gives a softer, milder dip; flame-charred gives the smoky kashk-o-bademjan character.
- **Kashk is the heart:** Without kashk this is just smoky baba ganoush. Kashk gives the salty-tangy backbone. If you can't find it: thick Greek yogurt + 1 tablespoon lemon + ½ teaspoon salt is a passable substitute.
- **Deep-fried onions: deep brown:** Pale fried onions don't have the right flavour or crunch. Push to deep brown, almost burnt at the edges.

## Storage
- Refrigerate 4 days; bring to room temperature before serving, OR warm gently. Re-top with fresh fried onions and mint oil if the toppings have been mixed in.
- The aubergine base alone freezes 2 months; the toppings are fresh additions.
