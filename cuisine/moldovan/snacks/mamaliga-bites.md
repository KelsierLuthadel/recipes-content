# Mămăligă Bites

![Mămăligă Bites](resources/mamaliga-bites.jpg)

*Fried polenta balls with sheep cheese and dill: warm mămăligă rolled into walnut-sized rounds with a melting brânză centre, pan-fried golden, and finished with a shower of chopped dill. The party canapé built from a fresh pot of polenta.*

**Serves:** 6 (about 24 balls)

**Prep Time:** 25 minutes (plus 30 minutes cooling)

**Cook Time:** 25 minutes

## Overview
This is the party canapé that every Moldovan kitchen builds from a half-stiff pot of mămăligă: cooled enough to handle, scooped into walnut-sized rounds, each stuffed with a small cube of brânză de oi that turns molten in the pan, then pan-fried in butter and oil till the outside crisps to a deep gold. A shower of crumbled brânză and freshly chopped dill on top finishes the bite. The balls are eaten with the fingers as a stand-up snack with a glass of cold beer, or with a fork as a quick supper alongside a fried egg and sour cream. The cheese hidden inside is the surprise: a soft warm pocket that bursts on the first bite.

## Ingredients

### For the polenta
- 1 L water
- 1.5 tsp salt
- 300 g coarse yellow cornmeal
- 30 g butter

### For the stuffing
- 150 g brânză de oi or feta, cut into 24 small cubes (about 6 g each)

### For frying
- 4 tbsp sunflower oil
- 30 g butter

### For finishing
- 100 g brânză de oi or feta, finely crumbled
- 3 tbsp chopped fresh dill
- 100 g sour cream (smântână), for dipping
- Black pepper

## Method

### Stage 1 - Cook the polenta
1. Bring the water and salt to a hard boil.
2. Rain in the cornmeal in a steady stream, whisking constantly.
3. Drop to a low heat; swap to a wooden spoon.
4. Cook 25 to 30 minutes, stirring, until very thick and pulling cleanly from the pot.
5. Stir in the butter.

### Stage 2 - Cool
1. Tip the cooked polenta onto a buttered tray; spread to about 3 cm thick.
2. Cool 30 minutes at room temperature, uncovered, until firm enough to handle but still warm and pliable (it must be cool enough not to burn your hands; warm enough to mould without cracking).

### Stage 3 - Shape into balls
1. Wet your hands with cold water (stops the polenta sticking).
2. Scoop a heaped tablespoon of polenta into your palm.
3. Flatten it gently into a disc; sit a brânză cube in the centre.
4. Fold the polenta around the cheese; roll between your palms into a smooth ball about 4 cm wide.
5. Sit on a tray lined with parchment.
6. Repeat for 24 balls; re-wet your hands between batches if the polenta starts sticking.
7. Chill the shaped balls 15 minutes in the fridge to firm up before frying.

### Stage 4 - Fry
1. Heat half the oil and butter in a wide non-stick pan over medium heat.
2. Lay half the balls in the pan; do not crowd.
3. Fry 6 to 8 minutes total, turning every minute or so with two spoons, until deep gold all over (the round shape needs more turns than flat squares to get even colour).
4. Lift onto a rack to drain (paper towel traps steam and softens the crust).
5. Wipe the pan; repeat with the rest.

### Stage 5 - Finish
1. Arrange the hot balls on a warm serving plate.
2. Scatter generously with the crumbled cheese (the residual heat softens it).
3. Shower with chopped dill.
4. Grind on black pepper.
5. Serve sour cream in a small bowl alongside for dipping.

## Notes
- **Mould while warm:** if the polenta cools too far it cracks and crumbles when you try to shape it. Catch it at the 30-minute mark, still warm to the touch.
- **Wet hands:** essential for shaping. Dry hands tear the polenta surface; wet hands give smooth round balls.
- **Medium heat, longer fry:** balls cook longer than flat slices because the heat has to reach the cheese centre. Medium (not medium-high) prevents the outside scorching before the inside heats through.
- **Turn often:** two spoons or chopsticks help; every minute or so for the first 5 minutes builds an all-over crust.
- **Cheese cube small enough to seal:** 6 g (a 1 cm cube) is the right size. Larger cubes blow out the side as the cheese melts.

## Variations
- **Cu jumări:** with pork crackling crumbled on top instead of (or alongside) the finishing cheese.
- **Cu ouă de prepeliță:** with a small quail's egg fried alongside, the brunch version.
- **Cu mujdei:** with a small dab of garlic sauce drizzled over each ball.
- **Sweet version:** skip the cheese centre; dust the cooked balls with icing sugar and serve with sour cream and cherry jam.
- **Deep-fried:** drop the chilled balls into 170°C oil for 3 to 4 minutes till deep golden; faster and more uniform than pan-frying.
- **Baked:** brush the chilled balls with oil and bake at 220°C for 18 minutes; lighter, less crisp, fewer calories.

## Serving
- On a warm platter as a stand-up canapé with a glass of cold local Chișinău lager. As a quick supper alongside a fried egg and a green salad. With sour cream and pickled gherkins on the side.

## Storage
- Best fresh from the pan; the crust softens within 30 minutes.
- The shaped raw balls keep 2 days refrigerated on a tray; fry fresh on the day.
- Do not freeze cooked; the texture goes grainy.
- Raw shaped balls freeze 1 month on a tray then bagged; fry from frozen by lowering the heat slightly and extending the time by 3 minutes.
