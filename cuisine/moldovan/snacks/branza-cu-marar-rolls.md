# Brânză cu Mărar Rolls

![Brânză cu Mărar Rolls](resources/branza-cu-marar-rolls.jpg)

*Moldovan cheese-and-dill phyllo rolls: thin pastry rolled around a filling of brânză de oi and chopped fresh dill, brushed with butter, baked crisp and gold. The market-stall snack and dinner-party canapé.*

**Serves:** 8 (about 20 rolls)

**Prep Time:** 30 minutes

**Cook Time:** 22 minutes

## Overview
These are the Moldovan answer to the Greek tiropita: thin filo pastry wrapped tightly around a fistful of sharp sheep cheese, beaten egg, and a green pile of chopped fresh dill, the rolls brushed with melted butter and baked until shatter-crisp. The Moldovan signature is the dill (mărar), which goes in generously enough to look green through the filling, and the use of brânză de oi (sharp salty sheep cheese) rather than the milder Greek feta. The rolls are pencil-shaped and palm-long, eaten with the fingers as a snack on a wedding buffet or pushed onto a plate next to a glass of cold Fetească Albă at the start of a Sunday lunch. Fast to assemble, faster to disappear.

## Ingredients

### For the filling
- 400 g brânză de oi or feta, crumbled
- 200 g brânză dulce de vaci or ricotta (sit in a fine sieve over a bowl for 1 hour to drain off excess whey before using)
- 2 eggs
- 1 large bunch fresh dill, finely chopped (about 6 tbsp)
- 2 spring onions, finely sliced (optional)
- Black pepper to taste
- (no salt needed; the cheese carries it)

### For the rolls
- 1 packet (270 g) ready-rolled filo pastry, thawed
- 80 g unsalted butter, melted
- 2 tbsp sesame seeds

## Method

### Stage 1 - Make the filling
1. Mash the two cheeses together in a bowl with a fork.
2. Beat in the eggs.
3. Stir in the chopped dill, spring onions if using, and a good grind of black pepper.
4. The filling should be moist but not runny.

### Stage 2 - Cut the pastry
1. Unroll the filo on a board.
2. Cut the whole stack in half lengthwise to make two long stacks.
3. Cover the cut pastry with a barely-damp tea towel to keep it from drying.

### Stage 3 - Roll
1. Take a single sheet; lay it flat in front of you, long side facing.
2. Brush lightly with melted butter.
3. Lay a second sheet on top; brush with butter.
4. Spoon a thick line of filling along the long edge nearest you (about 1 tbsp per roll).
5. Fold the long edge over the filling.
6. Fold the short ends in (to seal).
7. Roll up tight away from you into a long cigar.
8. Place on a baking sheet lined with parchment, seam-side down.
9. Brush the top with butter.
10. Repeat with the rest of the pastry and filling; you should get about 20 rolls.

### Stage 4 - Bake
1. Heat the oven to 200°C (fan 180°C).
2. Sprinkle the buttered rolls with sesame seeds.
3. Bake 18 to 22 minutes until the rolls are deep gold and crisp.

### Stage 5 - Cool briefly
1. Lift onto a rack; cool 5 minutes.
2. Serve warm; the cheese is still hot inside.

## Notes
- **Filo dries fast:** keep all unused sheets under a damp towel; dry filo cracks.
- **Two sheets:** doubling makes the rolls hold their shape; a single sheet tears.
- **Filling moisture:** runny filling soaks through; drain the soft cheese first.
- **Brush lightly:** too much butter pools and the pastry goes greasy; a light brush is enough.
- **Eat warm:** the crisp is best within 30 minutes of baking; cold rolls go soft.

## Variations
- **Cu spanac:** with finely chopped wilted spinach folded into the cheese.
- **Cu ceapă verde:** with extra spring onion and no spring herbs.
- **Cu telemea afumată:** with smoked Romanian cheese for a smokier note.
- **Bigger triangles:** folded into samosa-shape triangles instead of cigars.
- **Frozen and baked from frozen:** assembled rolls freeze raw for 2 months; bake from frozen at 200°C for 25 minutes.

## Serving
Warm from the oven on a wooden board with a small bowl of sour cream and a wedge of lemon. At a wedding buffet next to other canapés. With a glass of cold Fetească Albă or sparkling Cricova. As a market-stall snack in a paper bag.

## Storage
- Best the day they are made.
- Refrigerate up to 2 days in a sealed container; re-crisp in a 180°C oven for 6 minutes.
- Freeze cooked, wrapped tight: 1 month; reheat from frozen at 180°C for 12 minutes.
