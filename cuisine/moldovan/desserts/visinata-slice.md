# Vișinata Slice

![Vișinata Slice](resources/visinata-slice.jpg)

*The Moldovan adult layer cake: a light vanilla sponge soaked in sour-cherry liqueur (vișinata), layered with whipped cream and the macerated cherries from the jar, finished with shaved dark chocolate.*

**Serves:** 12

**Prep Time:** 45 minutes

**Cook Time:** 30 minutes (plus 4 hours chilling)

## Overview
Vișinata is the house-made sour-cherry liqueur of every Moldovan cellar, the dark red sweet-strong drink set up in autumn in a glass demijohn with the season's vișine, sugar, and a measure of grape spirit. By Christmas the cherries are syrupy and the spirit has gone red. This slice is what the household does with the cherries pulled from the jar after the liqueur is decanted: a soft vanilla sponge is split into thin layers, drenched with the liqueur until it sings, layered with whipped cream, and packed with the syrupy cherries. The cake is chilled overnight for the layers to settle, then finished with shaved dark chocolate. The slice cuts to show the cherries against the cream and the pale liqueur-soaked crumb. Eat in the afternoon with strong coffee or after dinner with a small glass of the same liqueur.

## Ingredients

### For the sponge
- 6 eggs (separated)
- 180 g caster sugar
- 180 g plain flour
- 1 tsp baking powder
- 1 tsp vanilla extract
- A pinch of salt

### For the soak
- 150 ml vișinata (sour-cherry liqueur), or substitute kirsch plus 2 tbsp cherry syrup
- 50 ml cold water

### For the cream and filling
- 600 ml double cream (cold)
- 60 g icing sugar
- 1 tsp vanilla extract
- 300 g preserved sour cherries (from the vișinata jar, drained, or jarred sour cherries)

### To finish
- 80 g dark chocolate (70% cocoa)
- 12 whole preserved cherries (for decoration)

## Method

### Stage 1 - Make the sponge
1. Heat the oven to 170°C (fan 150°C).
2. Line a 30 x 20 cm baking tin with parchment.
3. Whip the egg whites with a pinch of salt to soft peaks.
4. Gradually beat in half the sugar; whip to stiff glossy peaks.
5. In a separate bowl, beat the yolks with the rest of the sugar and the vanilla until pale and thick.
6. Sift the flour and baking powder.
7. Fold the yolks into the whites; fold in the flour in three additions, keeping the air.
8. Spread evenly in the tin; bake 22 to 25 minutes until a skewer comes out clean.
9. Cool in the tin 10 minutes; turn out onto a rack; cool completely.

### Stage 2 - Slice and soak
1. With a long serrated knife, slice the sponge horizontally into 3 thin layers.
2. Mix the vișinata with the water.
3. Brush each layer generously with the soak (about 70 ml per layer); the sponge should be wet but not falling apart.

### Stage 3 - Whip the cream
1. Whip the cold double cream with the icing sugar and vanilla to soft-firm peaks.
2. Stop before it goes stiff.

### Stage 4 - Build
1. Lay the bottom sponge layer on a serving plate or in a lined deep tray.
2. Spread a third of the whipped cream over.
3. Scatter half the drained cherries in an even layer.
4. Lay the second sponge layer on top.
5. Spread another third of cream; scatter the rest of the cherries.
6. Lay the top sponge layer.
7. Cover the top with the last of the cream in a smooth blanket.

### Stage 5 - Chill
1. Refrigerate the cake uncovered for 4 hours, ideally overnight.
2. The layers will settle and the soak will distribute.

### Stage 6 - Finish
1. Shave the dark chocolate with a vegetable peeler.
2. Scatter the shavings over the top in a thick layer.
3. Set 12 whole cherries in a row down the centre.
4. Slice into 12 rectangles with a hot dry knife (dip in hot water, wipe, slice).

## Notes
- **The soak is generous:** the sponge should drink the liqueur deeply, not just be brushed; a dry sponge ruins the slice.
- **Cold cream cold bowl:** stiff cream needs a cold bowl and cold cream; lukewarm cream will not whip.
- **Slice with a hot knife:** dipping in hot water between slices keeps the layers clean.
- **Substitute liqueur:** kirsch with a spoon of cherry syrup approximates vișinata; pure kirsch alone is too dry.
- **Make ahead:** the cake is better the day after building.

## Variations
- **Cu rom:** with dark rum in place of vișinata, the heavier version.
- **Cu coniac:** with brandy in the soak, drier and stronger.
- **With sour cream layers:** half the whipped cream replaced with smântână, the tangier Moldovan version.
- **In a glass trifle bowl:** built in layers in a glass bowl, the modern dinner-party version.
- **With walnuts:** chopped walnuts scattered between the layers.

## Serving
Sliced cold, with a small glass of the same vișinata liqueur on the side. With strong black coffee or Moldovan black tea with lemon. As the closing course of a Sunday dinner.

## Storage
- Refrigerate covered up to 4 days; the cake improves for the first two.
- Do not freeze; the cream separates and the sponge goes spongy.
- Bring out of the fridge 15 minutes before serving for the best texture.
