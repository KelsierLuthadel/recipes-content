# Salată de varză Moldovan

![Salată de varză Moldovan](resources/salata-de-varza-moldovan.jpg)

*The Moldovan cabbage salad: a quick-cured slaw of finely sliced white cabbage, grated carrot, and chopped dill, dressed with sunflower oil and white-wine vinegar, salted hard and squeezed by hand to soften.*

**Serves:** 6 as a side

**Prep Time:** 20 minutes (plus 30 minutes curing)

**Cook Time:** None

## Overview
Salată de varză is the cool sharp slaw that sits next to every Moldovan stew, sarmale, or mămăligă plate. The cabbage is sliced as thin as the cook can manage (the wedge balanced under one hand, the knife flat against the board, the slices coming off in long pale shreds), salted hard, and squeezed between the palms until it weeps and softens. A grated carrot goes in for colour, a fistful of chopped fresh dill for the green Moldovan herb signature, and the dressing is the simplest pair: sunflower oil for the slip and white-wine vinegar for the cut. The salad is left to sit half an hour for the salt to do its work, drained of the brine, and dressed at the last minute. Eat from the same bowl as the rest of the table, fork and elbow.

## Ingredients

- 1 small white cabbage (about 800 g), outer leaves removed
- 1 large carrot, peeled
- 1 small bunch fresh dill, finely chopped (about 3 tbsp)
- 1 small onion, very finely sliced (optional)
- 2 tsp fine sea salt (for curing)
- 4 tbsp sunflower oil
- 3 tbsp white-wine vinegar
- 1 tsp caster sugar
- Black pepper to taste

## Method

### Stage 1 - Slice the cabbage
1. Halve the cabbage; cut out the hard core.
2. Lay each half cut-side down.
3. Slice as thin as you can manage, in long fine shreds.
4. Tip into a large bowl.

### Stage 2 - Salt and squeeze
1. Sprinkle the 2 tsp of salt over the cabbage.
2. With both hands, scrunch and squeeze the cabbage hard for 2 minutes until it goes soft and lets out liquid.
3. Leave to sit 30 minutes; the cabbage will give up more water and soften further.

### Stage 3 - Add the rest
1. Coarsely grate the carrot on the large holes of a box grater; add to the bowl.
2. Add the chopped dill and the sliced onion if using.

### Stage 4 - Drain and dress
1. Tip the cabbage into a colander; press lightly to drain the brine (do not rinse).
2. Return to the bowl.
3. In a small jug, whisk the oil, vinegar, sugar, and a grind of black pepper.
4. Pour over the cabbage; toss thoroughly with the hands.
5. Taste; adjust salt and vinegar.

### Stage 5 - Serve
1. Mound in a serving bowl.
2. Scatter a final pinch of dill on top.
3. Serve at room temperature or chilled.

## Notes
- **The slice:** thinner is better. A mandoline is fine if a sharp knife is unsteady.
- **Salt-and-squeeze:** the step that turns raw cabbage from squeaky to silky; do not skip.
- **Sunflower oil:** the proper Moldovan choice; olive oil is too pushy for this dish.
- **White-wine vinegar:** sharper than cider, milder than white spirit vinegar.
- **Dill is the herb:** parsley is wrong; dill is the Moldovan green.

## Variations
- **Cu mărar și usturoi:** with finely chopped raw garlic added at the dressing stage.
- **Cu hrean:** with a tablespoon of grated fresh horseradish for sharper heat.
- **Murătură de iarnă:** the long-cure version, fermented in a jar for a week.
- **Cu morcov ras mult:** higher carrot ratio (200 g) for sweeter slaw.
- **Cu castravete murat:** with chopped pickled gherkin folded through.

## Serving
Next to sarmale, tochitură, or grilled meats. A bowl on the table at any Moldovan Sunday lunch. With a slice of brânză de oi and country bread for a light supper. With a glass of cold Fetească Albă.

## Storage
- Refrigerate up to 3 days; the cabbage softens further but remains crisp.
- Do not freeze; the structure collapses.
- Drain off any liquid that gathers in the bowl before re-serving.
