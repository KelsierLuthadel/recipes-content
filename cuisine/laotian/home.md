# Laotian Cuisine

![Laotian Cuisine](resources/hero.jpg)

Laos sits between Thailand, Vietnam and China, but the table is its own. Sticky rice (khao niao) is the staple, eaten by hand-rolling into small balls and dipping into a generous spread of small plates. Laap (the national dish of finely minced meat or fish dressed with toasted ground rice, fresh herbs, fish sauce, lime and chilli) and tam mak hung (the bracingly sour green papaya salad pounded in a clay mortar with garlic, chilli, lime, fish sauce and palm sugar) lead every meal. Or lam (the deep Luang Prabang herb-and-aubergine stew thickened with a bitter wood called sakhan) and sai oua (the lemongrass-and-galangal Lao sausage) anchor the main-course tradition. Jeow (chilli pastes for dipping) and pickled greens sit on every table. Lao coffee with sweetened condensed milk runs the morning; nam mak nao (palm-sugar limeade) cuts the heat at lunch.
