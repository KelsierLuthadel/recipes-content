# Oildown

![Oildown](resources/oildown.jpg)

*Grenada's national dish: a great one-pot of breadfruit, salt meat, callaloo, dumplings and coconut milk simmered until the liquid is gone and the breadfruit has drunk every last drop of golden coconut oil.*

**Serves:** 6 to 8

**Prep Time:** 30 minutes (plus overnight salt-meat soak)

**Cook Time:** 1 hour 30 minutes

## Overview
Oildown is the national dish of Grenada and the one pot every Grenadian cook learns from a grandmother. The name describes the technique: a layered pot of breadfruit, salt meat, callaloo (dasheen leaves), dumplings, turmeric and scotch bonnet is covered in fresh coconut milk and simmered slowly until the liquid renders down to a glossy yellow oil at the bottom of the pot. The breadfruit absorbs the coconut, the salt pork perfumes everything, and the callaloo wilts down into the gravy. It is a communal dish, made in a heavy iron pot over a wood fire at beach limes and Saturday yard gatherings. The colour is golden from turmeric and coconut, the flavour is rich and smoky, and the texture is somewhere between a stew and a steamed rice. Eat straight from the pot with a spoon.

## Ingredients

- 500 g salt beef or salt pork
- 1 large breadfruit (about 1.5 kg), peeled, cored, cut into 8 wedges
- 300 g callaloo (dasheen) leaves, washed and chopped, or spinach as a substitute
- 800 ml fresh coconut milk (or 2 tins full-fat)
- 1 large onion, sliced
- 4 spring onions, chopped
- 4 garlic cloves, crushed
- 1 tbsp fresh thyme leaves
- 2 tsp ground turmeric
- 1 scotch bonnet pepper, whole (not pierced)
- 1 small bunch chadon beni (or coriander), chopped

### For the dumplings
- 250 g plain flour
- 0.5 tsp salt
- 150 ml water

## Method

### Stage 1 - Soak the salt meat
1. Soak the salt beef or pork overnight in cold water, changing the water twice.
2. Drain, cut into 3 cm pieces.
3. Cover with fresh water in a pot and boil 20 minutes to draw out more salt.
4. Drain again.

### Stage 2 - Build the dumplings
1. Mix the flour and salt; add water gradually to make a firm dough.
2. Knead 2 minutes; rest 10 minutes.
3. Pinch off walnut-sized pieces; roll into small logs about 5 cm long.

### Stage 3 - Layer the pot
1. Place the salt meat in the bottom of a wide heavy pot.
2. Scatter the onion, spring onion, garlic, thyme and turmeric over the meat.
3. Lay the breadfruit wedges on top of the seasonings.
4. Tuck the dumplings between the breadfruit.
5. Pile the callaloo over the top.
6. Sit the whole scotch bonnet on the very top (do not pierce it).
7. Pour the coconut milk down the side of the pot until it just covers the breadfruit.

### Stage 4 - Cook down
1. Cover with a tight lid; bring to a hard boil.
2. Drop to a low simmer and cook 1 hour without lifting the lid.
3. After 1 hour, check: the coconut milk should have reduced to a glossy yellow oil at the bottom and the breadfruit should be soft to a knife.
4. If still too wet, uncover and simmer 10 more minutes.

### Stage 5 - Finish
1. Lift out and discard the scotch bonnet (or chop it into the pot if you want heat).
2. Scatter the chadon beni over the top.
3. Bring the pot to the table; serve straight from it.

## Notes
- **The scotch bonnet must stay whole:** the unpierced pepper releases aroma only; a burst pepper makes the pot inedible.
- **No stirring:** lifting the lid breaks the steam seal; the layers cook on their own.
- **Salt meat substitute:** smoked pork hock or smoked turkey wings work if salt meat is hard to find.
- **The right pot:** a heavy iron or enamelled pot holds the heat low and even; a thin pot scorches the bottom layer.

## Variations
**Beach lime oildown:** doubled in a 20-litre iron pot over a wood fire on the sand.
**Chicken oildown:** brined chicken thighs instead of salt meat for a lighter version.
**Pigtail oildown:** add 200 g salt pigtail with the salt beef for an extra smoky layer.
**Crab oildown:** add 4 whole blue crabs on top in the final 20 minutes.
**Vegetarian oildown:** skip the salt meat, double the dumplings, season heavily with thyme and smoked paprika.

## Serving
Eat from the pot at a beach gathering · Saturday yard lime · with a bottle of hot pepper sauce on the side · with a cold Carib or Stag beer · with slices of avocado on the side.

## Storage
- Keeps 3 days refrigerated.
- Reheat gently with a splash of coconut milk to loosen.
- Improves overnight as the breadfruit re-absorbs the flavour.
