# San Marinese

*The kitchen of the world's oldest republic and Europe's third-smallest country. Italian by ingredient, San Marinese by recipe — pasta-and-pork dishes with stuffed pastas like the swallow's-nest baked pasta that you won't quite find anywhere else in Italy.*

## A short history

San Marino — a tiny enclave entirely surrounded by Italy, 61 km² with about 34,000 residents — has been an independent republic since (legendarily) 301 CE, making it the oldest sovereign state in the world. The cuisine is essentially Romagnola Italian (the cuisine of the surrounding Emilia-Romagna region) with its own twist on a handful of signature dishes.

The country's traditional kitchen reflects its inland-hill geography: pasta, pork (cured and fresh), wild herbs, mushrooms from the local forests, the produce of nearby Adriatic Italian markets. Cappelletti in brodo (stuffed pasta in chicken broth — eaten at Christmas), nidi di rondine (the "swallow's nests" — pasta rolls stuffed with cheese and ham, baked in cream sauce), and stracotto (slow-braised beef in wine) are the dishes you can call distinctly San Marinese.

The country exports more cured meats and pasta than most regions of comparable size — Mortadella di San Marino is a PDO product.

## Regional variations

San Marino is too small to have regional sub-traditions, but there are differences by elevation: the higher villages (Castello di Montegiardino, Borgo Maggiore) lean more rustic; the capital (San Marino City) more refined-restaurant.

## Signature ingredients

- **Pasta**: fresh hand-rolled, especially cappelletti and tortellini.
- **Pork**: cured (mortadella di San Marino, salame), fresh (slow-roasted).
- **Mushrooms**: porcini and other wild varieties from the local Apennine forests.
- **Olive oil and white wine**: Mediterranean staples.
- **Pecorino**: sheep's milk cheese.
- **Parsley and rosemary**: the herb pair.
- **Tomato**: in sauces, especially the slow-cooked ragù.

## Learn it in five recipes

1. **[Nidi di Rondine](/recipes/cuisine/san-marinese/nidi-di-rondine)**: "swallow's nests" — pasta sheets rolled with ham, cheese and béchamel, cut into rings, baked in cream sauce. The signature San Marinese dish.
2. **[Cappelletti in Brodo](/recipes/cuisine/san-marinese/cappelletti-in-brodo)**: small stuffed pasta "little hats" in clear chicken broth. The Christmas Eve plate.
3. **[Coniglio in Porchetta](/recipes/cuisine/san-marinese/coniglio-in-porchetta)**: rabbit stuffed with herbs, garlic and fennel, roasted in the porchetta (suckling pig) style.
4. **[Stracotto San Marinese](/recipes/cuisine/san-marinese/stracotto-sanmarinese)**: beef slow-braised in red wine for hours till the meat shreds and the gravy turns deep brown.
5. **[Pasta e Ceci San Marinese](/recipes/cuisine/san-marinese/pasta-e-ceci-sanmarinese)**: short pasta with chickpeas, rosemary and olive oil in a thick rustic soup-stew.

## Where to go next

Pair everything with San Marinese wine — the country produces small quantities of Sangiovese and Biancale (the local white). End with torta tre monti (a layered chocolate-and-wafer cake named for the country's three towers). The local Mistrà (an aniseed spirit) makes a proper digestif.
