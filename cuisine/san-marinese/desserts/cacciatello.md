# Cacciatello

![Cacciatello](resources/cacciatello.jpg)

*The San Marinese caramel-topped egg pudding: a baked custard halfway between crème caramel and panna cotta, set in the oven and turned out cold.*

**Serves:** 6

**Prep Time:** 20 minutes

**Cook Time:** 50 minutes (plus chilling overnight)

## Overview
Cacciatello belongs to the family of central-Italian baked custards (latte alla portoghese, crema rovesciata) but the San Marinese version is distinct: a lightly higher proportion of egg yolks, a hint of lemon zest, and the caramel a touch darker than the Roman crema caramella. It is baked in a bain-marie, chilled overnight in its tin, then turned out so the caramel flows down over the wobbling custard. The texture is silky, not jelly; the flavour is gentle, the caramel does the talking. Serve in slices at the end of a long lunch with a small glass of vin santo or a coffee.

## Ingredients

### For the caramel
- 180 g caster sugar
- 60 ml water

### For the custard
- 700 ml whole milk
- 100 ml double cream
- 1 wide strip lemon zest
- 1 vanilla pod, split, or 1 tsp vanilla bean paste
- 6 large eggs plus 3 yolks
- 130 g caster sugar

## Method

### Stage 1 - Make the caramel
1. Combine the 180 g sugar and 60 ml water in a small heavy pan over medium heat. Swirl gently until the sugar dissolves; do not stir.
2. Bring to a boil and cook without stirring until the syrup turns a deep amber, around 8 minutes. Watch closely at the end; it goes from gold to burnt fast.
3. Pour into a 22 cm round cake tin or a loaf tin straight away, tipping the tin to coat the base and a little of the sides. Set aside; the caramel will harden.

### Stage 2 - Infuse the milk
1. Heat the milk, cream, lemon zest and vanilla pod in a small pan until just steaming. Take off the heat and let the flavours infuse for 15 minutes.
2. Fish out the lemon zest and the vanilla pod (scrape any remaining seeds into the milk).

### Stage 3 - Make the custard
1. Heat the oven to 150°C (130°C fan). Boil a kettle for the bain-marie.
2. Whisk the eggs, yolks and 130 g sugar in a bowl until just combined; you do not want a froth.
3. Pour the warm milk in slowly, whisking gently the whole time.
4. Strain the custard through a fine sieve into a jug.

### Stage 4 - Bake
1. Pour the custard over the set caramel in the tin.
2. Sit the tin in a deep roasting tray and pour boiling water around it to come halfway up the sides.
3. Bake for 45 to 55 minutes, until the custard is just set with a slight wobble in the middle when nudged.
4. Lift the tin out of the water, let it cool, then refrigerate at least 6 hours, ideally overnight.

### Stage 5 - Turn out
1. Run a thin knife around the inside of the tin to loosen.
2. Sit a serving plate over the tin and invert; lift the tin away. The caramel should pool over and around the custard.
3. Slice into wedges and spoon the caramel sauce over each plate.

## Notes
- **Caramel colour.** A deep amber gives the right bittersweet edge; too pale and the dessert is one-note sweet; too dark and it turns acrid.
- **No frothing.** Whisk the eggs gently; air bubbles bake into a foamy crust on top.
- **Overnight set.** Cacciatello needs to be properly cold to slice cleanly; do not rush this step.

## Serving
Slices on small chilled plates with a spoonful of the caramel sauce. A glass of vin santo or moscato; an espresso after.

## Storage
- Keeps 3 days refrigerated, wrapped or covered.
- Does not freeze well; the custard becomes grainy on thawing.
