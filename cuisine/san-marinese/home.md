# San Marinese Cuisine

![San Marinese Cuisine](resources/hero.jpg)

The world's oldest republic sits on a triple-peaked mountain inside the surrounding Emilia-Romagna countryside, and its kitchen reflects that doubled inheritance: piadina and pecorino as the everyday mountain-table foundation, Sangiovese as the wine, and a foraging tradition for wild greens and herbs that flavour the rabbit and the chickpea soups. Faggotti, nidi di rondine and bustrengo carry the Romagnolo pasta and grain craft up the slope; coniglio in porchetta and erbe spontanee belong to the rural farmsteads on the western flank. The republic has one unmistakable national dessert, the Torta Tre Monti, three peaks of wafer and hazelnut chocolate that mirror Monte Titano itself.
