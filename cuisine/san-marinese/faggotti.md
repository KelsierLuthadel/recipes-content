# Faggotti

![Faggotti](resources/faggotti.jpg)

*Small San Marinese filled pasta: thin egg-pasta squares folded around a ricotta-and-chard filling, boiled briefly, then tossed in butter foamed with sage.*

**Serves:** 4

**Prep Time:** 1 hour (plus 30 minutes resting the dough)

**Cook Time:** 5 minutes

## Overview
Faggotti are San Marino's house-made stuffed pasta, somewhere between an open ravioli and a tortelli. The pasta is rolled almost translucent on a wooden board, then cut into squares and folded around a filling of fresh ricotta, blanched Swiss chard and a generous grating of pecorino. The shape is loose, hand-pinched: a small bundle ("faggotto") rather than a sealed pillow. They are dressed simply, with butter melted to a hazelnut foam and a handful of sage leaves; the filling and the pasta do the work.

## Ingredients

### For the pasta
- 300 g 00 flour, plus extra for dusting
- 3 large eggs (or 2 eggs and 2 yolks for a richer dough)
- 1 pinch salt

### For the filling
- 300 g fresh ricotta (well-drained)
- 250 g Swiss chard leaves (about 400 g on the stalk), stalks removed
- 60 g aged pecorino, finely grated
- 1 small egg yolk
- A grating of nutmeg
- Salt and black pepper

### To finish
- 100 g unsalted butter
- 12 sage leaves
- 40 g aged pecorino, finely grated, to serve

## Method

### Stage 1 - Make the pasta
1. Tip the flour onto a board, make a well in the centre. Crack the eggs into the well, add a pinch of salt.
2. Beat the eggs with a fork, pulling in flour from the inside walls of the well until you have a shaggy dough.
3. Knead for 8 to 10 minutes until smooth and elastic; the dough should feel firm, like a yoga mat.
4. Wrap in cling film and rest at room temperature for 30 minutes.

### Stage 2 - Make the filling
1. Wash the chard leaves and blanch in salted boiling water for 2 minutes. Drain, refresh under cold water, then squeeze dry very thoroughly (this is the difference between a tight filling and a soggy one).
2. Chop the chard finely.
3. Mix the chard with the ricotta, pecorino, egg yolk, nutmeg, a good pinch of salt and a grind of black pepper. Taste and adjust.

### Stage 3 - Roll and fill
1. Cut the dough into four pieces. Work one piece at a time, keeping the others wrapped.
2. Roll each piece on a lightly floured surface (or through a pasta machine), going thinner each pass, until you can see the shadow of your hand through it (setting 7 of 9 on a machine).
3. Cut the rolled sheet into 7 cm squares.
4. Place a heaped teaspoon of filling on each square. Bring the four corners up to meet in the middle and pinch to seal, leaving the seams slightly open like a small bundle.
5. Set on a floured tray. Repeat with the remaining dough; you should end up with 32 to 36 faggotti.

### Stage 4 - Cook and dress
1. Bring a wide pan of well-salted water to a gentle boil.
2. Melt the butter in a wide frying pan over medium heat until it starts to foam and turn pale gold. Drop in the sage leaves; they will crisp within a minute. Take off the heat.
3. Drop the faggotti into the boiling water in two batches. They are ready when they float and the pasta seam tests cooked, around 3 minutes.
4. Lift out with a slotted spoon straight into the butter pan; toss gently with a splash of pasta water to emulsify the sauce.

### Stage 5 - Serve
1. Divide between warm plates. Spoon the foamed butter and sage leaves over the top. Shower with grated pecorino and plenty of black pepper.

## Notes
- **Drain the ricotta.** Wet ricotta makes a wet filling that splits the seams; sit it in a sieve for 30 minutes first.
- **Squeeze the chard.** Squeeze in handfuls until no more water comes out; you want it almost dry.
- **Pasta thickness.** Thin but not paper: you should be able to lift a faggotto without it tearing.

## Serving
A glass of Biancale or a young Sangiovese, a green salad with mountain rocket, and no second course.

## Storage
- Uncooked faggotti freeze well on a tray, then transferred to a bag for 1 month; cook from frozen, adding 1 minute.
- Cooked faggotti do not keep well; eat the same day.
