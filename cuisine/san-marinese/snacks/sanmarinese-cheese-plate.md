# San Marinese Cheese Plate

![San Marinese Cheese Plate](resources/sanmarinese-cheese-plate.jpg)

*A board of three San Marinese cheeses: aged pecorino, pit-aged Fossa, and fresh squacquerone, with chestnut honey, walnuts and crackers.*

**Serves:** 4 as a snack; 2 as a light lunch

**Prep Time:** 10 minutes

**Cook Time:** 0 minutes

## Overview
San Marino sits in pecorino country and the cheese counter at any of the alimentari around Borgo Maggiore tells the same story: aged pecorino on the slate, a wedge of Pecorino di Fossa (literally "pit cheese", aged underground in tufa pits cut into the rock at Sogliano, just outside the Republic), and a soft tub of squacquerone for spreading. The board is the after-walk pre-dinner pause: three cheeses in order of intensity, chestnut honey, a heap of walnuts, slices of pear, a few wafers of piadina toasted crisp. A glass of Sangiovese in hand and an hour to lose.

## Ingredients

### The cheese (about 80 g per person, total)
- 120 g aged pecorino (12 to 18 months old)
- 100 g Pecorino di Fossa (or any pit-aged or cave-aged sheep cheese)
- 120 g squacquerone or stracchino

### To accompany
- 4 tbsp chestnut honey (or any dark robust honey)
- A handful of shelled walnuts
- 1 ripe pear, sliced
- A small bunch of grapes
- A few dried figs, halved
- 8 thin wedges of piadina sanmarinese, toasted crisp (or thin sourdough crackers)

### To finish
- A grind of black pepper, optional
- A drizzle of best olive oil over the squacquerone

## Method

### Stage 1 - Take the cheese out
1. Take all three cheeses out of the fridge 30 minutes before serving; cheese eaten cold is cheese eaten flat.
2. Unwrap and let breathe on a chopping board.

### Stage 2 - Toast the piadina
1. If using a piadina rather than crackers, cut into thin wedges and toast on a dry pan for 30 seconds per side until crisp.

### Stage 3 - Build the board
1. Find a wooden board or a wide flat plate.
2. Arrange the three cheeses left to right in increasing intensity: squacquerone (soft, mild), young pecorino (firm, salty), Fossa (intense, funky).
3. Cut the pecorino into thick triangular wedges; leave the squacquerone in a small bowl with a spoon; cut the Fossa into smaller pieces (a little goes a long way).
4. Tuck the walnuts, pear, grapes and figs around the cheeses.
5. Set a small bowl of chestnut honey alongside the Fossa; pit cheese and dark honey are made for each other.
6. Drizzle a little olive oil over the squacquerone and grind some black pepper over.
7. Lay the toasted piadina wedges or crackers on the side.

### Stage 4 - Serve
1. Serve at room temperature. Provide small knives, a honey spoon and lots of napkins.

## Notes
- **The Fossa.** Pecorino di Fossa is aged for three months underground in tufa pits at Sogliano sul Rubicone, just over the border in Emilia-Romagna. It develops a powerful, slightly tangy, almost truffly flavour. Any pit-aged or cave-aged sheep cheese substitutes.
- **Chestnut honey.** Dark, slightly bitter and herbal; it is the foil to the salty intensity of the aged cheeses. Acacia or wildflower honey works if you cannot find chestnut.
- **Order matters.** Start mild (squacquerone), move to firm (pecorino), finish strong (Fossa). Reverse the order and the milder cheeses taste of nothing.

## Serving
Pre-dinner with a glass of Sangiovese or a young Albana. Or as a lunch on its own with a green salad.

## Storage
- Cheeses keep in their wrappers in the fridge: pecorino and Fossa 2 weeks, squacquerone 4 days.
- Once cut, wrap pecorino loosely in waxed paper rather than cling film.
- Leftover walnuts, dried fruit and crackers store fine in airtight jars.
