# Erbe Spontanee

![Erbe Spontanee](resources/erbe-spontanee.jpg)

*The San Marinese wild-greens salad: dandelion, sorrel and wild rocket gathered from the slopes of Monte Titano, dressed simply with olive oil, lemon and salt.*

**Serves:** 4

**Prep Time:** 15 minutes

**Cook Time:** 0 minutes

## Overview
Foraging for erbe spontanee ("wild greens") is a spring ritual in the Republic, and the resulting salad is the most honest dish on any San Marinese table. The greens vary with the season and the slope: young dandelion leaves for bitterness, wood sorrel for sharpness, wild rocket for pepper, a few sprigs of cicoria selvatica (wild chicory) and tender shoots of borage. The salad is dressed with a salty-sweet olive-oil-and-lemon dressing, a scatter of toasted breadcrumbs, sometimes a soft-boiled egg perched on top. It is a salad for someone who likes their greens lively, not docile.

## Ingredients

- 200 g mixed young wild greens (any combination of dandelion, sorrel, wild rocket, chicory, borage, watercress, lamb's lettuce)
- 1 small handful young mint or wild fennel fronds
- 60 ml extra virgin olive oil (a peppery one)
- Juice of half a lemon
- 1 tsp white wine vinegar
- 1/2 tsp honey
- 1 small garlic clove, peeled and bashed flat
- Salt and black pepper

### To finish
- 30 g coarse breadcrumbs, fried gold in a tablespoon of olive oil
- 2 soft-boiled eggs (optional), peeled and halved
- A few shavings of aged pecorino

## Method

### Stage 1 - Wash the greens
1. Pick over the greens, removing any tough stems, yellowed leaves or stray grit.
2. Wash in two changes of cold water; spin dry well. Wet greens slide off the dressing.

### Stage 2 - Make the dressing
1. In a small jar, combine the olive oil, lemon juice, vinegar, honey, the bashed garlic clove, half a teaspoon of salt and a grind of black pepper.
2. Shake hard and let stand 5 minutes so the garlic flavours the oil. Fish out the garlic before dressing.

### Stage 3 - Toast the crumbs
1. Heat a tablespoon of olive oil in a small pan. Add the breadcrumbs and fry, stirring, until golden and crisp, about 3 minutes. Drain on kitchen paper. Salt lightly.

### Stage 4 - Dress and finish
1. Pile the greens loosely into a wide shallow bowl.
2. Shake the dressing again, drizzle over the leaves and toss very gently with your fingertips so the leaves are just coated.
3. Scatter the warm breadcrumbs over the top.
4. Lay the halved soft-boiled eggs in if using, top with a few shavings of pecorino, and a last grind of black pepper.

## Notes
- **Pick young.** Old dandelion leaves are aggressively bitter; pick the small inner ones, no longer than your thumb.
- **Sort by bitterness.** Dandelion and chicory carry the bite; soften the salad by pairing them with sorrel (sour, soft) and lamb's lettuce (mild).
- **Foraging caution.** Pick from places you know to be free of dogs, sprays and roadside dust. If foraging is not realistic, a good greengrocer's wild leaves do the job.

## Serving
A starter or side at any San Marinese meal. Particularly good before or alongside the heavier roasted dishes (coniglio in porchetta, nidi di rondine).

## Storage
- Best eaten immediately once dressed.
- Washed undressed greens keep 2 days in a sealed bag with a piece of kitchen paper inside to absorb moisture.
