# Bajan Cuisine (Barbados)

![Bajan Cuisine](resources/hero.jpg)

Barbados (Bajan, locally) sits at the crossroads of West African, British and Caribbean influences. The national dish - cou-cou and flying fish - pairs a buttery cornmeal-and-okra polenta-like mush with steamed flying fish in a herb-and-tomato gravy, eaten every Friday at homes and rum-shops across the island. Pudding and souse (steamed pickled pork and a sweet potato or blood pudding) is the traditional Saturday lunch. Bajan macaroni pie (baked mac-and-cheese with bonnet pepper and mustard powder), Bajan fried chicken (marinated in green herb seasoning), and stew chicken in browning sauce make up the everyday Bajan plate. Cassava pone, coconut bread, sweet potato pudding and conkies (steamed cornmeal pumpkin pudding wrapped in banana leaves) define the sweet side. Bajan rum punch is the famous Caribbean cocktail; mauby (the slightly bitter bark-tea infusion) and sorrel (the hibiscus-and-ginger Christmas drink) are the traditional non-alcoholic counterparts. Cou-cou Fridays, Souse Saturdays, Sunday lunch with rice and peas - the Bajan food calendar is identity-defining.
