# Bajan Fried Chicken

![Bajan Fried Chicken](resources/bajan-fried-chicken.jpg)

*Barbados's beloved alternative to American fried chicken: bone-in chicken pieces marinated overnight in a generous bath of Bajan green seasoning + lime + mustard + Scotch bonnet + paprika + soy, dredged in a seasoned flour-and-cornstarch coat, deep-fried till deep golden and crisp. The marinade is what makes it Bajan rather than southern American: brighter, herbier, more aromatic from the green seasoning. Sold from rum-shops and roadside stalls; piled at every Bajan beach picnic and Saturday-afternoon family meal. Eaten with Bajan pepper sauce, a wedge of lime, and a cold Banks lager.*

**Serves:** 6 (about 8 pieces)

**Prep Time:** 25 minutes (plus overnight marinating)

**Cook Time:** 30 minutes

## Overview
Bajan fried chicken is the Caribbean answer to southern American fried chicken. Three things make it identity-specifically Bajan. First, the marinade: bone-in chicken pieces marinated overnight in a paste of Bajan green seasoning + fresh lime juice + prepared yellow mustard + Bajan pepper sauce + soy sauce + Scotch bonnet + paprika + salt + black pepper. The overnight rest is essential - the green seasoning permeates the chicken right to the bone, ensuring every bite is flavoured. American fried chicken uses a buttermilk-and-spice marinade; Bajan uses the green herbal paste. Second, the dredge: a blend of plain flour + cornstarch (50/50) + paprika + dry mustard powder + garlic powder + onion powder + salt + black pepper. The cornstarch is the secret to extra-crisp fried chicken - it gives a lighter, shatteringly crisp crust compared to flour alone. Third, the double-dredge: the marinated chicken is dipped in the dredge, then in a quick beaten-egg-and-buttermilk slurry, then back into the dredge - the double coat is what gives the canonical extra-thick, extra-crisp Bajan crust. Deep-fried at 175°C for 12-15 minutes till deep golden and cooked through (165°C / 75°C internal). Served with a wedge of lime, Bajan pepper sauce on the side, and the canonical Bajan accompaniments - macaroni pie, rice and peas, fried plantain. Three details: OVERNIGHT MARINATE (the green-seasoning paste permeates the chicken; minimum 4 hours but overnight is dramatically better), DOUBLE-DREDGE (marinade > dredge > egg-buttermilk slurry > dredge again; this is the canonical extra-crisp Bajan coat), and 175°C OIL (lower and the crust soaks fat; higher and the outside burns before the inside cooks).

## Ingredients

### The chicken and marinade
- 1 whole chicken (1.6-1.8 kg), cut into 8 pieces (2 thighs, 2 drumsticks, 2 wings, 2 breasts halved)
- 4 tablespoons Bajan green seasoning (see [Cou-cou and flying fish](cou-cou-and-flying-fish.md))
- 3 tablespoons fresh lime juice
- 2 tablespoons prepared yellow mustard (French's or a Caribbean equivalent)
- 2 tablespoons Bajan pepper sauce (or 1 small finely chopped Scotch bonnet + 1 tablespoon hot sauce)
- 1 tablespoon dark soy sauce
- 1 tablespoon paprika
- 2 cloves garlic, finely grated
- 1 tablespoon fresh ginger, finely grated
- 2 teaspoons salt
- 1 teaspoon black pepper

### The seasoned dredge
- 200 g plain flour
- 200 g cornstarch (cornflour in UK)
- 2 tablespoons paprika
- 1 tablespoon dry mustard powder
- 1 tablespoon garlic powder
- 1 tablespoon onion powder
- 1 tablespoon salt
- 1 tablespoon black pepper
- 1 teaspoon dried thyme
- 1 teaspoon cayenne pepper (or to taste)

### The egg-buttermilk slurry
- 2 large eggs, lightly beaten
- 200 ml buttermilk (or 200 ml whole milk + 1 tablespoon lemon juice; let stand 5 minutes)
- 1 tablespoon prepared yellow mustard

### For frying
- 2 litres sunflower or groundnut oil (or peanut oil for the canonical fried-chicken flavour)

### To serve
- Lime wedges
- Bajan pepper sauce
- Macaroni pie or rice and peas
- A few slices of fried sweet plantain
- A cold Banks lager

## Method

### Stage 1 - Marinate the chicken (overnight)
1. In a large bowl, whisk together all the marinade ingredients - it forms a thick paste.
2. Add the chicken pieces; massage the marinade into every piece, getting under the skin.
3. Cover with cling film.
4. Refrigerate overnight (minimum 4 hours; overnight is dramatically better).

### Stage 2 - Set up the dredging station
1. In a wide shallow dish, whisk together all the dredge ingredients.
2. In a second wide shallow dish, whisk together the eggs, buttermilk and mustard for the slurry.
3. Take the marinated chicken from the fridge; let stand 20 minutes at room temperature (cold chicken seizes when it hits the hot oil).

### Stage 3 - Double-dredge
1. Working one piece at a time:
2. First dredge: lift a chicken piece from the marinade; let excess drip off; roll in the seasoned flour-and-cornstarch dredge; press firmly to coat.
3. Then the slurry: dip the dredged chicken into the egg-buttermilk slurry; let excess drip off.
4. Second dredge: roll back into the seasoned flour-cornstarch dredge; press very firmly.
5. The chicken should have a thick, slightly rough, lumpy coat.
6. Place on a tray as you go.
7. Let the dredged chicken rest 10 minutes on the tray (the coating sets and adheres better).

### Stage 4 - Heat the oil
1. Heat the oil to 175°C in a deep heavy pot (use a thermometer to verify).
2. Don't go above 180°C - too hot and the outside burns before the inside cooks; below 165°C and the chicken soaks fat.

### Stage 5 - Fry
1. Fry the chicken in 2 batches (don't overcrowd; overcrowding drops the temperature):
2. Lower 4 pieces gently into the hot oil.
3. Fry 12-15 minutes total, turning every 4-5 minutes, till the chicken is deep golden brown all over and internally reads 75°C (use a probe thermometer; or cut into a thick part - juices should run clear).
4. The thigh and drumstick pieces take 14-15 minutes; breast pieces 11-12 minutes.
5. Lift out with tongs; drain on a wire rack over a tray.
6. Don't drain on kitchen paper - it traps steam and the bottom goes soggy.

### Stage 6 - Repeat with the second batch
1. Let the oil come back to 175°C between batches.
2. Fry the remaining chicken pieces.

### Stage 7 - Serve immediately
1. Pile the hot Bajan fried chicken on a warm platter.
2. Add lime wedges, a small dish of Bajan pepper sauce.
3. Serve with macaroni pie, rice and peas, fried plantain alongside.
4. Cold Banks lager.

## Notes
- **Overnight marinate:** the green-seasoning paste needs time to permeate. Minimum 4 hours; overnight is dramatically better.
- **Double-dredge is the canonical extra-crisp coat:** dredge > slurry > dredge again. Without this, you've made single-coat fried chicken.
- **Cornstarch in the dredge:** 50/50 flour and cornstarch gives the shatteringly-crisp coat. Pure flour gives a bread-like coat.
- **175°C is the magic temperature:** lower and the crust soaks fat; higher and the outside burns.
- **Don't drain on kitchen paper:** wire rack only. Paper traps steam and the bottom goes soggy.
- **Eat hot:** Bajan fried chicken is at its peak for 30 minutes. After an hour the crust has softened.

## Variations
**Spicy Bajan fried chicken:** double the Scotch bonnet and Bajan pepper sauce in the marinade.
**Bajan wings:** make the same recipe with just chicken wings (drummettes and flats); cooks in 8-10 minutes - the bar-snack variant.
**Curry-spiced Bajan fried chicken:** add 2 tablespoons of Caribbean curry powder to the marinade - the Indo-Caribbean fusion.
**Air-fryer Bajan chicken:** marinate; dredge; spray with oil; air-fry at 200°C for 18-22 minutes (turning halfway) - less crisp but acceptable.
**Oven-baked Bajan chicken:** dredge as normal; bake on a wire rack at 200°C for 35-40 minutes - the lighter variant.
**Bajan fried chicken sandwich:** pile a piece of fried chicken in a Bajan salt-bread bun with shredded lettuce, sliced tomato, and Bajan pepper sauce - the cutter sandwich variant.
**Pressure-cooker Bajan fried chicken:** pressure-cook the marinated chicken first 8 minutes; then dredge and fry only 4-5 minutes - the KFC-style technique.

## Storage
- Best within 30 minutes of frying.
- Refrigerates 3 days; reheat on a wire rack in a 200°C oven for 12 minutes to refresh the crisp.
- Don't microwave - the crust goes soggy.
- Freezes 2 months (fried); defrost in the fridge overnight; reheat in a hot oven for 15 minutes.
- The marinade refrigerates 5 days (without the chicken).
- The dredge mixture refrigerates indefinitely in a sealed jar; useful for fast subsequent batches.
- Leftover fried chicken pulled off the bone and piled into a fresh roll with lettuce and mayo is the Bajan day-after sandwich.
