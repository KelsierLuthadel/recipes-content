# Stuffat tal-Fenek (Maltese Rabbit Stew)

![Stuffat tal-Fenek](resources/stuffat-tal-fenek.jpg)

*Malta's national dish: rabbit slow-braised in red wine, tomato, bay, garlic and a touch of cocoa, served over spaghetti as a first course and the meat as the main. The dish that defines the traditional Maltese fenkata (rabbit feast); a celebration food eaten at family gatherings, village feasts, and Sunday lunches.*

**Serves:** 6

**Prep Time:** 20 minutes (plus overnight marinade)

**Cook Time:** 2 hours

## Overview
Stuffat tal-fenek is Malta's most iconic dish and the central event of the Maltese fenkata, a traditional rabbit-feast meal eaten at family gatherings and village festas. The construction: rabbit (preferably wild) is jointed and marinated overnight in red wine, bay leaves, garlic and herbs; the next day, the marinated meat is browned in olive oil and slow-braised in tomato paste, more red wine, beef stock, garlic, bay, a small piece of dark chocolate or cocoa (the Maltese signature, adds depth, not sweetness), and a pinch of cinnamon. After 90 minutes the rabbit is fork-tender and the sauce thick and mahogany. The traditional Maltese serving: the rich sauce is tossed first with spaghetti as a primo, then the rabbit meat is served as the secondo with roasted potatoes.

## Ingredients

### For 6 servings
- 1 whole rabbit (about 1.5 kg, jointed into 8 pieces)
- 500 ml dry red wine (for marinade)
- 4 bay leaves
- 1 tablespoon black peppercorns
- 6 garlic cloves (smashed)
- 1 small bunch fresh thyme
- 4 tablespoons olive oil
- 2 large onions (chopped)
- 6 garlic cloves (chopped; in addition to marinade)
- 1 large carrot (diced)
- 3 tablespoons tomato paste
- 400 g tinned chopped tomatoes
- 500 ml beef stock
- 1 small piece (20 g) dark chocolate (70%) OR 1 tablespoon cocoa powder
- 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
- 2 teaspoons fine sea salt
- 1 teaspoon coarsely ground black pepper

### To serve
- 500 g dried spaghetti
- 800 g roasted potatoes
- A green salad
- A bottle of Maltese or Sicilian red wine

## Method

### Stage 1 - Marinate (overnight)
1. In a large bowl, combine the rabbit pieces with red wine, bay leaves, peppercorns, smashed garlic, and thyme.
2. Cover; refrigerate overnight (8-12 hours).
3. Drain the rabbit; reserve the marinade liquid (strain).

### Stage 2 - Brown
1. Pat the rabbit pieces dry; season with salt and pepper.
2. Heat olive oil in a heavy Dutch oven over medium-high heat.
3. Brown the rabbit pieces in batches (6-8 minutes per batch).
4. Set aside.

### Stage 3 - Aromatics
1. In the same pot, sweat the chopped onions, garlic, and carrot 10 minutes till soft.
2. Add the tomato paste; cook 2 minutes.

### Stage 4 - Braise
1. Pour in the reserved wine marinade; reduce 5 minutes.
2. Add the chopped tomatoes and beef stock.
3. Stir in the chocolate (or cocoa) and cinnamon.
4. Return the rabbit to the pot.
5. Bring to a simmer; cover; reduce heat to LOW.
6. Braise 90 minutes till the meat is fork-tender.

### Stage 5 - Finish
1. Lift out the rabbit pieces; reduce the sauce uncovered 10 minutes if needed.
2. Taste; adjust salt.

### Stage 6 - Serve the traditional Maltese way
1. **First course (pasta):** toss half the sauce with cooked spaghetti; serve immediately.
2. **Second course (meat):** serve the rabbit pieces with the remaining sauce, alongside roasted potatoes and salad.
3. Pair with Maltese or Sicilian red wine.

## Notes
- **Overnight marinade:** non-negotiable for proper flavour.
- **The chocolate/cocoa:** the Maltese signature. Doesn't make it sweet; adds depth.
- **Sauce first, then meat:** the traditional Maltese two-course service.
- **Wild rabbit if you can find it:** stronger flavour than farmed.

## Variations
**Fenkata with garlic and wine only:** simpler; no tomato or chocolate, the older Maltese recipe.
**With olives:** add 100 g black olives in the last 30 minutes.
**Stuffat tal-fenek bil-mardquf (with bone marrow):** richer with the addition of bone marrow.
**Chicken stuffat:** swap rabbit for chicken thighs, fenkata-style chicken stew.
**Pressure cooker stuffat:** brown and pressure-cook 30 minutes high pressure.

## Serving
At a Maltese fenkata (rabbit feast): the traditional setting · at a Mġarr or Bahar ic-Caghaq village festa · at a Maltese Sunday lunch · at a Maltese wedding luncheon · at home as a celebration weekend meal.

## Storage
- Refrigerates 3 days; flavour improves overnight.
- Freezes 3 months.
- Reheat gently; the sauce thickens overnight, loosen with a splash of stock.
