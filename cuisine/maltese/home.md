# Maltese Cuisine

![Maltese Cuisine](resources/hero.jpg)

Malta's cuisine is the meeting point of Sicily, North Africa, and the Levant. The national dish, stuffat tal-fenek (rabbit stew slow-cooked in red wine, tomato and bay): anchors a tradition that uses what the islands provide: tuna and lampuki (dorado) from the Mediterranean; capers from the rocky cliffs; soft sheep's-milk ġbejniet cheese; sourdough Maltese bread (ħobż tal-Malti); and the traditional Maltese pastizzi (savoury phyllo pastry triangles, eaten everywhere from breakfast to midnight). Honey, dates, and almonds shape the desserts; Kinnie (the bitter-orange-and-herb soft drink) and Cisk lager wash everything down.
