# Coconut Crab in Lime

![Coconut Crab in Lime](resources/coconut-crab-in-lime.jpg)

*Whole coconut crab cracked open and dressed warm with lime, fresh coconut cream and chilli, picked straight from the shell with the fingers. The prize catch of the outer islands, served the day it is caught.*

**Serves:** 2 (one large crab per pair)

**Prep Time:** 15 minutes

**Cook Time:** 25 minutes

## Overview
The coconut crab (Birgus latro) is the giant land crab of the Pacific islands, a slow-grown creature that climbs palm trees and feeds on fallen coconuts; its meat is sweet, dense and faintly coconut-flavoured from its diet. On the outer PNG islands it is the ceremonial catch, boiled or steamed whole, then cracked open at the table and dressed with lime juice, fresh coconut cream and a little chilli. The recipe below uses regular mud crab or large blue swimmer crab as the realistic substitute for cooks outside the Pacific (coconut crabs are protected in many places and should not be sourced casually). The dressing and the eating are the same, fingers and shell, plenty of lime, the whole crab on a board between two people.

## Ingredients
- 1 large mud crab or 2 medium blue swimmer crabs (1.5-2 kg total, live or freshly killed)
- 2 litres water for cooking
- 2 tbsp sea salt
- 1 thumb of ginger, sliced
- 4 garlic cloves, smashed

### For the dressing
- 200 ml thick coconut cream
- Juice of 3 limes
- 1 small red chilli, finely chopped
- 1 spring onion, finely sliced
- 1 tbsp finely chopped fresh coriander
- 1/2 tsp salt
- A grind of black pepper

### To serve
- 2 limes, cut into wedges
- Extra coconut cream in a small bowl

## Method

### Stage 1 - Despatch and clean the crab
1. If the crab is live, chill it 30 minutes in the freezer to anaesthetise, then despatch quickly with a heavy blade through the underside between the eyes. This is the humane standard.
2. Rinse the crab under cold water; scrub the shell of any debris.

### Stage 2 - Boil
1. Bring the water to a rolling boil in a large pot with the salt, ginger and garlic.
2. Lower the crab into the pot; cover.
3. Boil 12 minutes for a 1.5 kg crab, 18 minutes for a 2 kg one. The shell turns bright orange-red.
4. Lift out and let drain and cool 5 minutes.

### Stage 3 - Crack
1. Twist off the claws and legs.
2. Lift off the top shell.
3. Remove the feathery gills and the stomach sac; rinse the body briefly.
4. Cut the body in half lengthways with a heavy knife.
5. Crack the claws and the larger leg segments with the back of a heavy knife or a small mallet, leaving the meat in the shell.

### Stage 4 - Dress
1. Whisk the coconut cream, lime juice, chilli, spring onion, coriander, salt and pepper in a bowl.
2. Arrange the cracked crab pieces on a wide board or platter.
3. Spoon the dressing generously over the cracked surfaces so it pools into the shell.

### Stage 5 - Serve
1. Bring the board straight to the table, while the crab is still warm.
2. Eat with the fingers; pick the meat out of the shell with a small fork or a chopstick.
3. Lime wedges and extra coconut cream alongside for dipping.

## Notes
- **Real coconut crab:** Birgus latro is protected or restricted in most of its range; do not buy it casually. Mud crab and blue swimmer are the right substitutes off the islands.
- **The dressing:** Should pour, not sit thick. If your coconut cream is very stiff, slacken it with a spoon of warm water.
- **Eat the day it is cooked:** Crab meat dries and toughens overnight; this dish is a same-day dish.

## Variations
- **Steamed instead of boiled:** Steam the crab over banana leaves for 18-22 minutes for a slightly cleaner flavour.
- **Grilled finish:** After boiling, brush the cracked pieces with coconut oil and finish 3 minutes on a hot grill for a charred edge.
- **Chilli-heavy:** Double the chilli for an island-hot version.
- **Lobster substitute:** A whole boiled lobster, split and dressed the same way, is the next-best thing.

## Serving
Bring the whole cracked crab to the table on a wooden board · plenty of lime wedges and finger bowls · steamed kaukau or rice to soak up the dressing · cold beer or fresh coconut water to drink.

## Storage
- Eat the day it is cooked. Picked crab meat refrigerated 24 hours can be folded into a salad the next day.
- Do not freeze cooked crab; the texture collapses on thaw.
