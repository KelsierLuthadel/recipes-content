# Coconut Pancakes PNG-Style

![Coconut Pancakes PNG-Style](resources/coconut-pancakes-png.jpg)

*Thin coconut-batter pancakes folded around grated young coconut and drizzled with dark palm sugar syrup, the islands' Sunday afternoon sweet.*

**Serves:** 4 (8 pancakes)

**Prep Time:** 15 minutes (plus 30 min rest)

**Cook Time:** 25 minutes

## Overview
PNG coconut pancakes are the Sunday-afternoon sweet of the coastal towns, a treat that came in with the Christian missions and stayed in the home repertoire. The batter is thin and coconut-rich, the pancakes themselves are cooked one at a time in a wide pan to a pale golden, and the filling is fresh grated coconut tossed with a little palm sugar so it stays slightly crunchy. Each pancake is folded around its filling and laid on the plate; a dark glossy palm sugar syrup is poured over the lot. The flavour is gentle, coconut-on-coconut, with the smoky depth of palm sugar holding it together. Eat warm with a fork while the filling is still slightly crisp.

## Ingredients

### The batter
- 200 g plain flour
- 400 ml coconut milk
- 100 ml water
- 2 eggs
- 1 tbsp palm sugar (or soft brown sugar), grated
- 1/4 tsp salt
- Coconut oil for cooking

### The coconut filling
- 200 g fresh grated coconut (or unsweetened desiccated coconut, soaked 10 minutes in 60 ml warm coconut milk)
- 60 g palm sugar, grated
- A pinch of salt

### The palm sugar syrup
- 120 g palm sugar, grated
- 100 ml water
- A pinch of salt
- 1 pandan leaf, knotted (optional)

## Method

### Stage 1 - Make the batter
1. Whisk the flour, salt and palm sugar in a bowl.
2. Make a well; add the eggs and half the coconut milk.
3. Whisk to a smooth lumpless paste; gradually whisk in the remaining coconut milk and the water until the batter is the consistency of single cream.
4. Rest 30 minutes at room temperature.

### Stage 2 - Make the syrup
1. In a small pan, combine the palm sugar, water, salt and pandan leaf.
2. Bring to a simmer over medium heat, stirring until the sugar dissolves.
3. Simmer 4-5 minutes until syrupy enough to coat the back of a spoon.
4. Lift out the pandan leaf; keep warm.

### Stage 3 - Make the filling
1. Toss the grated coconut with the palm sugar and salt.
2. The mixture should be moist and just sweet, not wet or syrupy.

### Stage 4 - Cook the pancakes
1. Heat a 20 cm non-stick pan over medium heat; rub with a film of coconut oil.
2. Pour in about 60 ml of batter; tilt the pan to spread thin.
3. Cook 1 minute; the surface dries and small bubbles set.
4. Flip; cook 30 seconds more.
5. Lift onto a plate.
6. Repeat with the remaining batter, oiling the pan between pancakes; you should get 8.

### Stage 5 - Fill and finish
1. Lay a pancake on a board.
2. Spoon 2 tbsp coconut filling in a line across the centre.
3. Fold one side over the filling; fold the other side over to make a parcel.
4. Repeat with the rest.
5. Arrange on a serving plate; drizzle the warm palm sugar syrup over the top.

## Notes
- **The batter rest:** Skipping the 30-minute rest gives lumpy pancakes; the flour needs the time to hydrate.
- **Fresh vs desiccated coconut:** Fresh grated coconut is much better; desiccated soaked in warm coconut milk for 10 minutes is the realistic substitute.
- **The syrup:** Should be syrupy not sticky; if it cools too thick, slacken with a spoon of warm water.

## Variations
- **Banana coconut pancakes:** Add a slice of ripe banana to the filling line before folding.
- **Lime zest:** Grate the zest of 1 lime into the filling for a citrus lift.
- **Pandan batter:** Whisk 1/4 tsp pandan extract into the batter for a green-fragrant pancake.
- **Crepe-thin:** For a more delicate version, thin the batter with another 50 ml of coconut milk and cook in a slightly larger pan.

## Serving
Serve warm on a plate, two pancakes per person, with the palm sugar syrup pooled around them · a wedge of lime alongside · a strong coffee or a cup of pit-pit ginger tea · eat within an hour of cooking for the best texture.

## Storage
- Pancakes refrigerate 2 days in a stack between sheets of greaseproof paper; warm in a low oven before filling.
- The filling refrigerates 2 days but is best fresh.
- The syrup refrigerates 1 week; reheat gently to loosen.
- Do not freeze.
