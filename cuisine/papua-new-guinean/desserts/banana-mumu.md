# Banana Mumu

![Banana Mumu](resources/banana-mumu.jpg)

*Whole ripe bananas wrapped in banana leaves and pit-baked in the mumu until the flesh turns soft, gold and jammy, the easy sweet end to a Papua New Guinean earth-oven feast.*

**Serves:** 6

**Prep Time:** 10 minutes

**Cook Time:** 35 minutes

## Overview
Banana mumu is the sweet that comes out of the pit after the pork and the kaukau, often tucked in among the meats for the last half hour of the cook so it shares the smoky heat. Whole ripe bananas (or large cooking bananas, halved) are wrapped in banana leaves with a drizzle of coconut cream and a scrape of palm sugar, and baked until the flesh inside the skin turns soft, gold and almost jam-like, while the leaf wrap holds in the steam. Open the parcels at the table; the smell is caramel and tropical and warm. Eat hot with a spoon, straight from the leaf, with a puddle of cold coconut cream over the top.

## Ingredients
- 6 large ripe bananas (the spotty yellow ones), or 3 large cooking bananas halved
- 6 large banana leaves (one per portion), softened over a flame
- 60 g palm sugar, grated (or soft brown sugar)
- 200 ml thick coconut cream
- 1/2 tsp salt
- 1 lime, cut into wedges

### To serve
- 200 ml extra thick coconut cream
- A pinch of salt

## Method

### Stage 1 - Heat the oven
1. Heat oven to 180 C.
2. Line a baking tray with foil.

### Stage 2 - Prep the leaves and bananas
1. Pass each banana leaf briefly over a gas flame, 5 seconds per side, until pliable.
2. Peel the bananas; if using cooking bananas, halve them lengthways.

### Stage 3 - Wrap
1. Lay a banana leaf flat. Place a banana in the centre.
2. Scatter 1 tsp grated palm sugar over the banana.
3. Drizzle over 2 tbsp coconut cream and a tiny pinch of salt.
4. Fold the leaf into a tight parcel, tucking the ends underneath.
5. Repeat with the remaining bananas.

### Stage 4 - Bake
1. Place the parcels seam-side down on the tray.
2. Bake 30-35 minutes. The leaves blacken slightly at the edges, the bananas inside go soft and golden.

### Stage 5 - Serve
1. Lift the parcels onto plates.
2. Open each one at the table; the steam carries the caramel-banana aroma.
3. Stir a pinch of salt into the extra coconut cream.
4. Drizzle the cold salted coconut cream over the hot banana.
5. Squeeze a lime wedge over before eating.

## Notes
- **The banana:** The ripeness matters; greenish bananas stay starchy. Look for plenty of brown spots on the skin.
- **Banana-leaf wrap:** The leaf contributes a green tea-like aroma and holds the steam in; foil is a workable last resort but loses the perfume.
- **Real mumu version:** In the actual pit, banana parcels are tucked among the meats for the last 30 minutes of the cook and pick up a faint smokiness.

## Variations
- **Chocolate banana mumu:** Tuck a few squares of dark chocolate alongside the banana before wrapping.
- **Coconut-and-lime:** Skip the palm sugar; rely on the natural sweetness of the banana and finish with a heavy squeeze of lime.
- **Spiced:** Add a pinch of grated nutmeg or a small piece of cinnamon stick to each parcel.
- **Barbecue version:** Cook the wrapped parcels on the cooler side of a barbecue, lid down, for 20 minutes.

## Serving
Open the parcels at the table; the leaf-burst-of-steam is part of the dish · drizzle cold salted coconut cream over the hot banana · eat with a spoon · a wedge of lime alongside · a strong coffee at the end of the meal.

## Storage
- Eat the day they are cooked; the texture is best straight from the oven.
- Refrigerate leftover parcels 2 days; reheat in a hot oven 8 minutes still in the leaves.
- Do not freeze; the banana turns mushy.
