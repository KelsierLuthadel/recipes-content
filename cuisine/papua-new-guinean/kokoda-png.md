# Kokoda PNG-Style

![Kokoda PNG-Style](resources/kokoda-png.jpg)

*Papua New Guinean lime-cured fish in coconut milk: fresh reef fish bathed in lime juice, then dressed with coconut cream, chilli, tomato and spring onion. Lighter and less dairy-rich than the Fijian version, the everyday coastal lunch.*

**Serves:** 4 as a starter, 2 as a main

**Prep Time:** 20 minutes (plus 30-40 min cure)

**Cook Time:** None

## Overview
PNG kokoda is the coastal cousin of the better-known Fijian dish, and it differs in small but real ways. The cure is shorter and brighter, the dressing is thinner and uses coconut milk rather than thick coconut cream, the chilli is more present, and the dish is finished with a heavier hand of spring onion and a squeeze of lime at the table. The fish, ideally a firm reef fish caught the same morning, comes out tender and opaque, the dressing pours rather than coats. Served cold in a halved coconut shell or a small bowl. A light bright dish for a hot day on the coast.

## Ingredients
- 500 g firm white reef fish (snapper, mahi-mahi, trevally), skinless and boneless
- Juice of 5 limes (about 150 ml)
- 1 tsp salt
- 250 ml coconut milk (not the thick cream)
- 1 small red onion, finely diced
- 2 ripe tomatoes, deseeded and diced 5 mm
- 1 small red chilli, finely chopped
- 2 spring onions, finely sliced
- 1 tbsp finely chopped fresh coriander
- 1 lime, cut into wedges, to serve
- Black pepper

## Method

### Stage 1 - Cube and cure
1. Cut the fish into 1 cm cubes; small enough to cure through, large enough to keep a bite.
2. Combine the fish, lime juice and 1/2 tsp salt in a glass bowl.
3. Refrigerate 30-40 minutes. The fish turns opaque white and firms.

### Stage 2 - Drain
1. Drain the fish through a sieve, pressing gently to remove excess lime juice.
2. The dish should not be soupy with cure liquid.

### Stage 3 - Dress
1. Return the drained fish to a clean bowl.
2. Pour over the coconut milk.
3. Add the red onion, tomato, chilli, spring onion and coriander.
4. Toss gently to coat. Add the remaining salt and a grind of pepper.
5. Taste; add a squeeze more lime if it needs brightening.

### Stage 4 - Rest and serve
1. Rest 10 minutes in the fridge so the flavours marry.
2. Serve cold in halved coconut shells or small bowls with lime wedges alongside.

## Notes
- **PNG vs Fijian:** PNG kokoda uses coconut milk rather than thick cream, giving a thinner and brighter dressing. The chilli is also more pronounced.
- **Fish quality:** The lime cures, it does not cook through; the fish must be sashimi-fresh. Freeze first to -20 C for 24 hours if you are uncertain about the source.
- **Cure time:** 30-40 minutes is the right window. Over an hour and the fish turns rubbery.

## Variations
- **Smoked fish kokoda:** Use lightly smoked fish in place of raw; skip the cure and just dress.
- **Coconut shell service:** A halved young drinking coconut, with the water tipped out, is the traditional vessel.
- **Extra coconut:** Some cooks finish with a spoonful of thick coconut cream on top for richness.
- **Pawpaw addition:** Diced unripe pawpaw (green papaya) added with the tomato is an island variation.

## Serving
Eat cold as a starter or light lunch · serve in coconut shells or small bowls with lime wedges and extra chilli alongside · bread or crisp cassava chips as a vehicle · cold beer or coconut water to drink.

## Storage
- Eat within 4 hours of mixing. After that the coconut milk separates and the fish toughens.
- Do not freeze.
- If you are prepping ahead, hold the cured drained fish and the dressing separately and combine just before serving.
