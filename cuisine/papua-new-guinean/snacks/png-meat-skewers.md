# PNG Meat Skewers

![PNG Meat Skewers](resources/png-meat-skewers.jpg)

*Cubes of pork or chicken marinated in coconut cream, ginger, garlic and chilli, threaded onto thin coconut-leaf skewers and grilled fast over hot coals, the everyday market street food of Port Moresby and Lae.*

**Serves:** 4 (8 skewers)

**Prep Time:** 20 minutes (plus 2 hour marinade)

**Cook Time:** 12 minutes

## Overview
PNG meat skewers are the everyday market and roadside snack of the coastal towns, the dish you buy from a charcoal-fired stand to eat from the hand while you wait for a bus. Cubes of pork (or chicken, or fish) are marinated for a couple of hours in coconut cream with ginger, garlic, lime and chilli, then threaded onto fine coconut-frond skewers and grilled fast over hot coals until the outside is charred and the inside is still juicy. The smell on a hot afternoon, when half a dozen stalls are working at once, is one of the most particular smells of urban PNG. At home, soaked bamboo skewers and a hot grill or barbecue get you to the same place.

## Ingredients
- 800 g pork shoulder (or chicken thigh, boned), in 2.5 cm cubes
- 8 bamboo skewers (25 cm), soaked 30 minutes in cold water

### The marinade
- 200 ml thick coconut cream
- Juice of 2 limes
- 2 tbsp soy sauce
- 1 thumb of ginger, finely grated
- 4 garlic cloves, finely grated
- 1 small red chilli, finely chopped
- 1 tsp ground turmeric
- 1 tsp salt
- 1 tbsp grated palm sugar (or soft brown sugar)

### To serve
- 1 lime, cut into wedges
- 1 small red onion, finely sliced
- Coconut chutney or sweet chilli sauce

## Method

### Stage 1 - Marinade
1. In a wide bowl, whisk the coconut cream, lime juice, soy sauce, ginger, garlic, chilli, turmeric, salt and palm sugar.
2. Add the pork cubes; turn to coat thoroughly.
3. Cover; refrigerate 2 hours (or up to 6 hours).

### Stage 2 - Skewer
1. Lift the pork out of the marinade, letting the excess drip off; reserve the marinade.
2. Thread 4-5 cubes onto each soaked skewer, leaving a small gap between cubes for even cooking.

### Stage 3 - Grill
1. Heat a barbecue, ridged grill pan or hot griddle to medium-high.
2. Lay the skewers on the heat; cook 3 minutes.
3. Turn; brush with reserved marinade.
4. Cook another 3 minutes; turn again and brush.
5. Repeat once more, for a total of 10-12 minutes; the meat should be charred outside and just cooked through.

### Stage 4 - Rest and serve
1. Rest the skewers 2 minutes on a board.
2. Pile onto a plate with lime wedges, sliced red onion and a small bowl of chutney or sweet chilli alongside.
3. Eat hot from the skewer with the fingers.

## Notes
- **The marinade brush:** Brushing the reserved marinade onto the skewers while they grill builds layers of coconut-and-lime flavour; do not pour the raw marinade over the cooked meat at the end.
- **The cubes:** 2.5 cm is right for a 10-minute cook; smaller cubes dry out, larger ones leave the centre raw.
- **The skewer soak:** Soaked bamboo skewers do not burn through during the cook; dry ones char and snap.

## Variations
- **Chicken version:** Substitute boneless chicken thigh; reduce the cook to 8-9 minutes total.
- **Fish version:** Substitute firm white fish (mahi-mahi or tuna); reduce the cook to 6 minutes total and handle gently.
- **Peanut sauce:** Serve with a satay-style peanut and coconut sauce on the side, a port-town variation.
- **Hot pepper:** Add 1 finely chopped small chilli pepper to the marinade for a serious heat.

## Serving
Eat hot from the skewer with the fingers · lime wedges and raw red onion alongside · with coconut rice or a wedge of grilled sago pancake · the market drinking pairing is a cold beer or a fresh young coconut.

## Storage
- Marinated raw skewers refrigerate up to a day; do not marinate longer than 6 hours or the lime turns the meat texture grainy.
- Cooked skewers refrigerate 3 days; reheat under a hot grill 3 minutes turning once.
- Freezing the marinated raw meat is fine for 1 month; thaw fully in the fridge before grilling.
