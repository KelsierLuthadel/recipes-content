# Grilled Sago Pancake (Toamas)

![Grilled Sago Pancake](resources/grilled-sago-pancake.jpg)

*Sago flour and grated coconut pressed into a thick flat cake and grilled in a hot pan until the outside is crackly gold, the lowland village breakfast snack.*

**Serves:** 4 (4 pancakes)

**Prep Time:** 10 minutes

**Cook Time:** 15 minutes

## Overview
Toamas (sometimes called sago pancake or sago flatbread) is the lowland and Sepik breakfast snack, a thick flat cake of sago flour and fresh grated coconut that is pressed into a hot dry pan and grilled until the outside crackles gold and the inside stays soft and faintly chewy. The flavour is clean and nutty, the texture is the point: crisp shell, soft warm centre, a faint sweetness from the coconut. In the villages it is cooked on a hot flat stone over the fire; at home a heavy cast-iron pan or a flat griddle gives the same result. Eat hot with the fingers, plain or with a spread of coconut cream and a scattering of palm sugar.

## Ingredients
- 300 g sago flour (or cassava starch / tapioca flour)
- 200 g fresh grated coconut (or 150 g unsweetened desiccated coconut, soaked 10 minutes in 100 ml warm coconut milk)
- 1 tsp salt
- 100-150 ml warm water (just enough to bring it together)

### To serve (optional)
- 100 ml thick coconut cream
- 2 tbsp palm sugar, grated

## Method

### Stage 1 - Mix the dough
1. In a wide bowl, combine the sago flour, grated coconut and salt.
2. Add 100 ml warm water; mix with a wooden spoon.
3. Add another 30-50 ml water as needed to bring it together; the mixture should be moist enough to clump in your hand but not wet.

### Stage 2 - Shape
1. Divide the mixture into 4 portions.
2. Shape each into a flat round about 12 cm across and 1.5 cm thick, pressing firmly so it holds together.

### Stage 3 - Grill
1. Heat a heavy dry pan (cast iron is ideal) over medium heat for 3 minutes.
2. Lay a pancake in the pan; press firmly with a spatula to flatten slightly.
3. Cook 5-6 minutes until the underside is gold and crisp and lifts away cleanly.
4. Flip; cook 5-6 minutes on the second side.
5. Press once or twice with the spatula during the cook to keep it flat.
6. Lift onto a board; repeat with the remaining pancakes.

### Stage 4 - Serve
1. Cut each pancake into wedges.
2. Eat hot, plain, with the fingers.
3. Optional: spread a spoon of coconut cream over each wedge and scatter a little grated palm sugar.

## Notes
- **The bind:** Sago flour and fresh coconut bind themselves with just enough water and pressure; no egg or flour is needed. Too much water and the pancake stays gummy.
- **The pan:** A dry pan is right; oil makes the cake soggy and not crisp. Heavy cast iron holds the heat best.
- **Press firmly:** A spatula-press at the start of the cook helps the pancake hold together and crisp evenly.

## Variations
- **Sweet toamas:** Mix 2 tbsp grated palm sugar into the dough for a sweet morning version.
- **Banana toamas:** Press a slice of ripe banana into the centre of each pancake before cooking.
- **Smoked fish toamas:** Press a tablespoon of flaked smoked fish into the centre for a savoury fill.
- **Coconut oil grilled:** A thin film of coconut oil in the pan gives a richer crust if you prefer it less dry.

## Serving
Eat hot from the pan, cut into wedges · plain as a breakfast snack with a cup of ginger tea · with coconut cream and palm sugar as a sweet · alongside aigir and smoked fish as a starch.

## Storage
- Eat the day they are cooked for the best crisp shell.
- Refrigerate up to 2 days; reheat in a hot dry pan 2 minutes per side to recrisp.
- Do not freeze; the texture turns gummy on thaw.
