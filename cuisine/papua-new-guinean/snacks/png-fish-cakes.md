# PNG Fish Cakes with Kaukau

![PNG Fish Cakes with Kaukau](resources/png-fish-cakes.jpg)

*Smoked fish patties bound with mashed kaukau and spring onion, shallow-fried until the outsides are gold and crisp, the everyday market snack of the PNG coast.*

**Serves:** 4 (8 cakes)

**Prep Time:** 25 minutes

**Cook Time:** 15 minutes

## Overview
PNG fish cakes are the kind of food you buy at a coastal market for the bus ride home: round patties of mashed sweet potato and flaked smoked fish, held together with a beaten egg, bound with a little flour, fried until the outsides go gold and crisp. The kaukau sweetness lifts the salt-and-smoke of the fish, the spring onion and chilli sharpen the lot, and a squeeze of lime at eating gives the brightness. They are the leftover dish of the islands, a way to make a half-fish and a couple of cold sweet potatoes into a real snack for four people. Eat hot from the pan with the fingers.

## Ingredients
- 400 g kaukau (sweet potato), peeled
- 250 g smoked fish (smoked mackerel, smoked trout, smoked tilapia), skin and bones removed
- 2 spring onions, finely sliced
- 1 small red chilli, finely chopped
- 1 thumb of ginger, finely grated
- 1 garlic clove, finely grated
- 2 tbsp finely chopped fresh coriander
- Zest of 1 lime
- 1 egg, beaten
- 3 tbsp plain flour, plus extra for dusting
- 1 tsp salt
- A grind of black pepper
- 4 tbsp coconut oil (or vegetable oil), for frying

### To serve
- 1 lime, cut into wedges
- Coconut chutney or sweet chilli sauce

## Method

### Stage 1 - Cook and mash the kaukau
1. Cut the kaukau into 3 cm chunks.
2. Boil in salted water 12 minutes until tender; drain well.
3. Mash to a coarse paste; spread on a wide plate to cool and dry 10 minutes.

### Stage 2 - Flake the fish
1. Flake the smoked fish with a fork into a wide bowl; check for any small bones.

### Stage 3 - Mix
1. Add the cooled mashed kaukau to the fish.
2. Stir in the spring onion, chilli, ginger, garlic, coriander, lime zest, beaten egg, flour, salt and pepper.
3. Mix to a coarse paste; the mixture should be firm enough to shape but still moist.

### Stage 4 - Shape
1. Divide the mixture into 8 portions, about 80 g each.
2. Shape each into a round patty about 7 cm across and 1.5 cm thick.
3. Dust each side lightly with flour.

### Stage 5 - Fry
1. Heat half the oil in a wide pan over medium heat.
2. Lay 4 cakes in the pan; do not crowd.
3. Fry 3-4 minutes per side until deep gold and crisp.
4. Lift onto a paper-lined plate.
5. Wipe the pan, add the remaining oil, and fry the second batch.

### Stage 6 - Serve
1. Pile the hot cakes on a plate with lime wedges and a small bowl of coconut chutney or sweet chilli sauce.
2. Eat hot.

## Notes
- **The dry mash:** Letting the mashed kaukau cool and dry on a plate 10 minutes is the secret to a cake that holds together. Wet mash gives soggy cakes.
- **The fish:** Smoked mackerel is the easiest stand-in; the role is salt, smoke and flake. Tinned tuna in oil (drained well) works as a last resort but loses the smoke note.
- **The shape:** Smaller cakes (about 60 g each) crisp through better; larger ones can stay soft in the middle.

## Variations
- **Chilli-heavy:** Double the chilli for a market-style hot version.
- **Tinned fish:** Use drained tinned salmon or tuna; add 1/2 tsp smoked paprika to mimic the smoke.
- **Baked version:** Brush the shaped cakes with coconut oil and bake at 200 C for 20 minutes, flipping once.
- **Curried:** Add 1 tsp curry powder to the mix for an Indo-influenced version.

## Serving
Pile hot on a plate with lime wedges · with coconut chutney or sweet chilli alongside · eat with the fingers · serve as a starter, a snack, or a light lunch with a salad.

## Storage
- Shape the cakes ahead and refrigerate up to a day before frying.
- Cooked cakes refrigerate 3 days; reheat in a hot oven 8 minutes to recrisp.
- Freeze raw shaped cakes between greaseproof paper up to 2 months; fry from frozen, adding 2 minutes per side.
