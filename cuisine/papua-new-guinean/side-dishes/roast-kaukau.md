# Roast Kaukau

![Roast Kaukau](resources/roast-kaukau.jpg)

*Whole sweet potatoes roasted in the embers of the cooking fire until the skins blister black and the insides go orange and steaming sweet, the Highlands' everyday morning food.*

**Serves:** 4 as a side, 2 as a meal

**Prep Time:** 5 minutes

**Cook Time:** 50 minutes

## Overview
Kaukau is the Highlands word for sweet potato, and it is the calorie base of the Papua New Guinean Highlands the way potato is for a north-European farm. Most days it is roasted whole, in the ashes of the breakfast fire or under a layer of hot coals, until the skin chars black and the flesh inside turns vivid orange and falls to a sweet steamed mash. Eat hot with the fingers, splitting the blackened skin open and scooping the inside out. A pinch of salt is the only seasoning needed. At home the oven version below gets you most of the way to the embered flavour; a barbecue with hot coals gets you all the way.

## Ingredients
- 4 medium kaukau (orange sweet potatoes), about 300 g each
- A little coconut oil for rubbing
- Sea salt
- Butter or thick coconut cream to serve

## Method

### Stage 1 - Heat the oven
1. Heat oven to 220 C.
2. Line a baking tray with foil.

### Stage 2 - Prep the kaukau
1. Wash and dry the sweet potatoes; do not peel.
2. Rub each with a light film of coconut oil.
3. Prick each two or three times with a fork.

### Stage 3 - Roast
1. Place the kaukau on the tray.
2. Roast 45-50 minutes; the skin should blister, darken and split, and a knife should slide easily into the centre.
3. The biggest tubers may need 5-10 minutes more.

### Stage 4 - Rest and serve
1. Rest 5 minutes on the tray.
2. Split each kaukau open lengthways with a knife.
3. Scatter sea salt over the orange flesh; add a knob of butter or a spoon of coconut cream.
4. Eat hot with the fingers, scooping the flesh out of the skin.

## Notes
- **Charcoal version:** A real Highlands kaukau is buried in the embers of a wood fire for 40 minutes; on a barbecue, push the coals to one side and bury the kaukau in the hot ash on the other.
- **The skin:** The blackened skin holds the flavour and the steam; do not peel before roasting.
- **The sweetness:** Orange-fleshed kaukau is the sweet one; white-fleshed varieties are starchier and need 5 minutes more.

## Variations
- **Coconut-glazed:** After the kaukau is split, drizzle thick coconut cream into the centre and return to the oven 3 minutes.
- **Chilli salt:** Mix 1 tsp salt with 1/2 tsp chilli flakes for a sprinkle that lifts the sweetness.
- **Roast with the mumu:** When you build the mumu, tuck whole kaukau in among the starches; they cook in the same time.
- **Embered finish:** Roast in the oven 40 minutes, then finish 5 minutes directly on the coals of a barbecue for the smoky note.

## Serving
Eat hot, split open with the fingers · alongside the mumu and aibika · as a Highlands breakfast on its own with a knob of butter · with smoked fish and a squeeze of lime for a heartier plate.

## Storage
- Refrigerate up to 4 days in a covered container.
- Reheat in a hot oven 10 minutes, or split and griddle the halves cut-side down to crisp.
- Mash the leftovers with coconut cream for a quick side the next day.
