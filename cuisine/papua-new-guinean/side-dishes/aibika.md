# Aibika

![Aibika](resources/aibika.jpg)

*Papua New Guinean island spinach simmered in coconut cream: the broad fuzzy leaves of the aibika plant wilted gently in coconut, salt and a little chilli, the everyday green of the Melanesian table.*

**Serves:** 4 as a side

**Prep Time:** 10 minutes

**Cook Time:** 15 minutes

## Overview
Aibika (Abelmoschus manihot, also called island cabbage or pele) is the most important leafy green of Papua New Guinea: a hibiscus relative with broad fuzzy leaves that grow on a shrubby plant in every village garden. The leaves are mucilaginous when cooked, slightly slippery in the way okra is, with a clean spinach-like flavour. The standard treatment is the simplest: wilt the leaves gently in coconut cream with salt and a little chilli, and serve alongside the mumu, fish or rice. It is the green that goes with everything. Off the islands, spinach, silverbeet or kale are the realistic substitutes; you lose the silkiness, but the soothing coconut-and-leaf flavour translates.

## Ingredients
- 500 g aibika leaves (or spinach, silverbeet or kale as substitute)
- 250 ml thick coconut cream
- 1 small onion, finely sliced
- 2 garlic cloves, finely sliced
- 1 small red chilli, finely chopped (optional)
- 1 tbsp coconut oil
- 1 tsp salt
- A grind of black pepper

## Method

### Stage 1 - Prep the leaves
1. Wash the aibika leaves thoroughly; strip from any thick stems.
2. Tear larger leaves in half; leave smaller leaves whole.

### Stage 2 - Soften the aromatics
1. Heat the coconut oil in a wide pan over medium heat.
2. Add the onion; cook 4 minutes until soft and translucent.
3. Add the garlic and chilli; cook 1 minute more.

### Stage 3 - Wilt
1. Add the leaves in two batches, stirring as they collapse into the pan.
2. Once all the leaves are in, add the salt.
3. Pour in the coconut cream; bring to a gentle simmer.
4. Cover and cook 8 minutes over low heat. The leaves should be soft and silky, the coconut cream slightly thickened.

### Stage 4 - Finish
1. Uncover; cook 2 minutes more to reduce the sauce slightly.
2. Taste and adjust salt.
3. Grind over black pepper.

## Notes
- **Substitute greens:** Spinach wilts faster (4 minutes is enough); silverbeet needs 8-10 minutes; kale needs the full time and a splash of water.
- **The mucilage:** Real aibika is faintly slippery, like okra; this is desirable and helps the coconut cream coat the leaves.
- **Coconut cream:** Use thick coconut cream, not coconut milk; the dish should be lightly creamy, not soupy.

## Variations
- **Anchovy aibika:** Add a tin of drained anchovy fillets with the garlic for a fishy-savoury depth.
- **Smoked fish aibika:** Stir in 100 g flaked smoked fish at the end for a more substantial dish.
- **Curry aibika:** A teaspoon of curry powder with the onion takes it toward an Indo-influenced version.
- **Coconut-poached:** Cook the leaves in coconut milk only (no oil sauté) for a paler, gentler version.

## Serving
Serve warm alongside the mumu · with grilled fish and rice · with smoked fish and saksak · as a side to coconut crab. Eat with a spoon to catch the coconut sauce.

## Storage
- Refrigerate up to 2 days in a covered container.
- Reheat gently in a covered pan with a splash of water or coconut milk.
- Does not freeze well; the leaves go limp on thaw.
