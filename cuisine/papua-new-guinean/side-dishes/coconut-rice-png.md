# Coconut Rice PNG-Style

![Coconut Rice PNG-Style](resources/coconut-rice-png.jpg)

*White rice cooked in coconut milk with a knotted pandan leaf and a little salt: the fragrant island starch that goes alongside the mumu, the fish and the curries on every Papua New Guinean table.*

**Serves:** 4 as a side

**Prep Time:** 5 minutes

**Cook Time:** 20 minutes (plus 10 min rest)

## Overview
Coconut rice is the everyday starch beyond the kaukau and the sago, the dish that came with the Asian influences of the twentieth century and stayed. Long-grain white rice is rinsed clean, simmered in a mixture of coconut milk and water with a knotted pandan leaf and a little salt, then steamed off the heat until each grain is separate and fragrant. The PNG version is leaner with the coconut milk than some Southeast Asian versions, the texture fluffy rather than sticky, the pandan note clear but not overpowering. It carries the meat juices from the mumu, soaks up the coconut sauce from the aibika and the kokoda dressing, and feeds a crowd cheaply.

## Ingredients
- 400 g long-grain white rice (jasmine or basmati)
- 400 ml coconut milk
- 300 ml water
- 1 pandan leaf, tied in a knot (or substitute 1/2 tsp pandan extract)
- 1 tsp salt
- 1 tbsp coconut oil (optional, for richness)

## Method

### Stage 1 - Rinse the rice
1. Place the rice in a bowl; cover with cold water.
2. Swirl with your fingers, drain and repeat 3 times until the water runs almost clear.
3. Drain well.

### Stage 2 - Combine and bring up
1. Tip the rinsed rice into a medium pot with a tight-fitting lid.
2. Add the coconut milk, water, knotted pandan, salt and coconut oil.
3. Bring to a boil over medium-high heat, stirring once to keep the rice from sticking.

### Stage 3 - Simmer covered
1. As soon as it boils, drop the heat to low.
2. Cover with the lid.
3. Cook undisturbed 12-14 minutes. The liquid should be absorbed and small craters should appear on the surface.

### Stage 4 - Rest
1. Take the pot off the heat, keeping the lid on.
2. Rest 10 minutes; the steam finishes the cook and the grains separate.

### Stage 5 - Fluff
1. Lift out the pandan leaf and discard.
2. Fork the rice gently to fluff; each grain should be separate and glossy with coconut.

## Notes
- **The rinse:** Skipping the rinse leaves the rice gluey rather than fluffy; the cloudy starch on the grain matters.
- **The pandan:** A knotted fresh pandan leaf is the real thing; an Asian shop will have them frozen. Extract works in a pinch.
- **The rest:** Lifting the lid before the 10-minute rest releases the steam early; the grains will not separate cleanly. Be patient.

## Variations
- **Richer:** Use 500 ml coconut milk and 200 ml water for a creamier rice.
- **Ginger coconut rice:** Add 1 thumb of sliced ginger with the pandan; lift out at the end.
- **Lemongrass:** A bruised lemongrass stalk in the pot adds a citrus note.
- **Brown rice version:** Use brown jasmine rice and increase the water by 200 ml and the cook to 35 minutes.

## Serving
Serve warm in a wide bowl, fluffed lightly · alongside the mumu, kokoda or fish curry · with a spoon of aibika on the side · finishes any tropical meal that needs a clean fragrant starch.

## Storage
- Refrigerate up to 3 days in a covered container.
- Reheat with a splash of water in a covered pan over low heat, or microwave covered.
- Freezes 1 month; spread thin on a tray to freeze and reheat directly from frozen.
