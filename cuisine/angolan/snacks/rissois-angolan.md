# Rissóis Angolan

![Rissóis Angolan](resources/rissois-angolan.jpg)

*Portuguese-Angolan half-moon pastries: a hot-water dough filled with a thick prawn or beef béchamel, sealed, breadcrumbed and fried gold.*

**Serves:** Makes about 24 rissóis

**Prep Time:** 1 hour

**Cook Time:** 25 minutes

## Overview
Rissóis are the small fried half-moon pastries of the Lusophone-Atlantic table, eaten in Lisbon, Luanda, Rio and Maputo at every party, drinks reception, snack stall and family lunch. The Angolan ones run on the same base as the Portuguese original but lean a little more on shrimp from the Atlantic coast and on a pinch of chilli in the filling. The dough is unusual: water, butter and salt are brought to a boil, the flour is dumped in all at once and beaten over the heat until a smooth shiny ball forms, then the dough is rolled thin while still warm and stamped into circles. A spoon of cold thick béchamel-bound prawn (or beef) goes into each circle, the edge is sealed, the rissol is breadcrumbed and fried gold. Eat hot.

## Ingredients

### Dough
- 500 ml water
- 50 g butter
- 1 tsp salt
- 400 g plain flour, sifted

### Prawn filling
- 30 g butter
- 1 small onion, very finely chopped
- 1 garlic clove, very finely chopped
- 2 tbsp plain flour
- 250 ml whole milk
- 300 g cooked prawns, finely chopped
- 2 tbsp tomato paste
- A small bunch of flat-leaf parsley, chopped
- A pinch of chilli flakes
- A pinch of black pepper
- Salt to taste

### Coating and frying
- 2 eggs, beaten
- 200 g fine dried breadcrumbs
- 1 litre vegetable oil for deep frying
- Lemon wedges, to serve

## Method

### Stage 1 - The filling (make first, must be cold)
1. Melt the butter in a small pan over medium heat.
2. Cook the onion 6 minutes until soft.
3. Add the garlic; cook 1 minute.
4. Stir in the flour; cook 1 minute to lose the raw taste.
5. Add the milk in three additions, whisking after each, until you have a very thick béchamel.
6. Stir in the chopped prawns, tomato paste, parsley, chilli flakes, pepper and salt.
7. Cook 2 minutes; the filling should hold its shape on the spoon.
8. Spread on a plate to cool quickly; refrigerate at least 30 minutes (must be cold before shaping).

### Stage 2 - The dough
1. Bring the water, butter and salt to a hard boil in a heavy pan.
2. Take off the heat; dump in all the flour at once.
3. Beat hard with a wooden spoon until you have a smooth ball that pulls cleanly from the sides of the pan.
4. Return to low heat for 1 minute to cook out the flour.
5. Tip onto a board; knead briefly with a spatula or your hands (the dough is hot) until smooth.

### Stage 3 - Shape
1. Roll the warm dough on a lightly floured surface as thin as a pound coin (about 2 mm).
2. Stamp out 10 cm circles with a glass or cutter.
3. Spoon a heaped teaspoon of cold filling on one half of each circle.
4. Brush the edge of the circle with a little water.
5. Fold the dough over the filling into a half-moon; press the edges firmly with your fingers or a fork to seal.

### Stage 4 - Coat
1. Dip each rissol in beaten egg.
2. Roll in fine breadcrumbs to coat all over.

### Stage 5 - Fry
1. Heat the oil to 175°C.
2. Fry in batches of 4-5, turning once, 2-3 minutes total, until deep gold.
3. Drain on kitchen paper.

### Stage 6 - Serve
1. Pile on a plate while hot.
2. Serve with lemon wedges.

## Notes
- **Cold filling, warm dough:** Hot filling melts the dough as you shape and leaks through the seal. Refrigerate the filling at least 30 minutes; work the dough while still warm and pliable.
- **Seal firmly:** A loose seal opens in the oil and the filling escapes. Press the edges with your fingertip and crimp with a fork.
- **Thick béchamel:** A loose béchamel filling slumps in the rissol and leaks; aim for a stiff paste that holds a peak.

## Serving
A party plate, a snack with a beer. A small bowl of pickled chillies on the side. The beef version (mince browned with onion, paprika, tomato paste and a stiff béchamel) is interchangeable with the prawn.

## Storage
- Best fresh and hot.
- Uncooked breadcrumbed rissóis freeze 2 months; fry from frozen, 5 minutes at 175°C.
- Cooked rissóis re-crisp in a hot oven (200°C, 8 minutes).
