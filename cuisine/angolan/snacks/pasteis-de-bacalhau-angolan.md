# Pastéis de Bacalhau Angolan

![Pastéis de Bacalhau Angolan](resources/pasteis-de-bacalhau-angolan.jpg)

*Portuguese-Angolan salt-cod fritters: shredded soaked bacalhau mixed with mashed potato, onion, parsley and egg, shaped into quenelles and deep-fried until gold.*

**Serves:** Makes about 20 fritters

**Prep Time:** 30 minutes (plus 24 hours soaking)

**Cook Time:** 25 minutes

## Overview
Pastéis de bacalhau (also called bolinhos de bacalhau) is the shared snack of the Lusophone-Atlantic world, eaten in Lisbon, Luanda, Rio and Maputo, but the Angolan version has its own seasoning, lifted slightly with a pinch of chilli and finished with extra parsley. Salt cod is soaked for at least 24 hours with several water changes to pull the salt out, then poached briefly, drained, picked over and shredded fine. The fish is folded into mashed floury potato with sautéed onion, parsley, egg and a crack of black pepper, then shaped into quenelles between two spoons and deep-fried until deep gold and crisp. Eat hot or warm with a wedge of lemon and a beer.

## Ingredients

- 400 g salt cod (bacalhau; thick centre-cut piece)
- 500 g floury potatoes (such as Maris Piper or King Edward), peeled
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- 1 large onion, very finely chopped
- 3 garlic cloves, very finely chopped
- A small bunch of flat-leaf parsley, chopped
- 2 eggs (large)
- 1 tsp black pepper
- A pinch of chilli flakes
- A grating of nutmeg
- Salt, only if needed (taste first)
- 1 litre vegetable oil for deep frying
- Lemon wedges, to serve

## Method

### Stage 1 - Soak the cod
1. Place the salt cod skin-side up in a wide bowl; cover generously with cold water.
2. Refrigerate 24 hours, changing the water 3-4 times.
3. Taste a small piece on the second day; it should be mildly salty but not aggressive.

### Stage 2 - Poach the cod
1. Drain the cod; place in a wide pan and cover with fresh cold water.
2. Bring slowly to a bare simmer (do not boil); cook 10 minutes.
3. Lift out; cool briefly.
4. Remove the skin and bones; flake the fish, then shred with your fingers to fine fibres.

### Stage 3 - Mashed potato
1. Cut the potatoes into chunks; boil in unsalted water 15-18 minutes until tender.
2. Drain well; return to the hot pan for 1 minute to dry off.
3. Mash smoothly; cool to warm.

### Stage 4 - Onion base
1. Heat the olive oil in a small pan over medium heat.
2. Cook the onion 8 minutes until soft and pale gold.
3. Add the garlic; cook 1 minute.
4. Let cool slightly.

### Stage 5 - Combine
1. In a wide bowl, mix the mashed potato, shredded cod, onion-and-garlic mixture, parsley, eggs, pepper, chilli flakes and nutmeg.
2. Taste; the cod is salty enough usually but adjust if it tastes flat.
3. The mixture should be soft enough to shape between two spoons but firm enough to hold; if very soft, refrigerate 30 minutes.

### Stage 6 - Shape and fry
1. Heat the vegetable oil in a deep pan to 175°C (a cube of bread should brown in 50 seconds).
2. Use two soup spoons to shape the mixture into rugby-ball quenelles; slide each one off into the hot oil.
3. Fry in batches of 4-5 (don't crowd the pan), turning once, 3-4 minutes total, until deep gold and crisp.
4. Lift onto kitchen paper.

### Stage 7 - Serve
1. Pile on a plate while hot; serve with lemon wedges.

## Notes
- **Soak properly:** Twenty-four hours and three water changes is the minimum. Skip it and the fritters are inedibly salty.
- **Dry mash:** Watery mash gives soggy fritters that fall apart in the oil. Drain the potato thoroughly and dry it in the hot pan for a minute before mashing.
- **Shape between two spoons:** This is the recognisable Lusophone quenelle shape, not balls. A scoop with one spoon, a smooth-over with the second, and a slide into the oil.

## Serving
As a snack or starter, hot from the pan. A wedge of lemon, a cold beer (Cuca or any pale lager), a small bowl of olives alongside.

## Storage
- Best fresh and hot.
- Keep 1 day refrigerated; reheat in a hot oven (200°C) for 8 minutes to re-crisp.
- Uncooked mixture keeps 1 day refrigerated; fry to order.
- Cooked fritters freeze 1 month; reheat from frozen in a hot oven.
