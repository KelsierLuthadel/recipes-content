# Bhuna Khichuri

![Bhuna Khichuri](resources/bhuna-khichuri.jpg)

*Bangladeshi rainy-day comfort: rice and red lentils slow-fried with whole spices, onion, ginger and ghee until rich and golden, eaten with beef or fried fish on the side.*

**Serves:** 6

**Prep Time:** 20 minutes

**Cook Time:** 50 minutes

## Overview
Khichuri across South Asia ranges from baby-food soft to dry-grained pilaf-like; the Bangladeshi bhuna khichuri sits at the dry-grained end, the rice and lentils properly fried in ghee with whole spices before any water joins the pot. It is the monsoon dish: a rainy afternoon, the windows open to the smell of wet earth, a thick khichuri in the pot, beef bhuna or fried hilsa on the side, a wedge of lime, a glass of borhani. The whole-spice base (cinnamon, cardamom, clove, bay) reads regal; the ginger and onion give it body; the ghee makes it rich. Done right, every grain of rice is golden and visible, the lentils are tender and intact, and the dish lands somewhere between a pilaf and a stew.

## Ingredients

- 300 g basmati rice
- 200 g red lentils (masoor dal)
- 100 g moong dal (split yellow mung), optional but traditional
- 100 ml ghee
- 2 tbsp mustard oil
- 2 medium onions, finely sliced
- 4 cm fresh ginger, finely grated
- 6 garlic cloves, finely grated
- 1 cinnamon stick (5 cm)
- 6 green cardamom pods, lightly crushed
- 4 cloves
- 2 bay leaves
- 1 tsp cumin seeds
- 1 tsp turmeric powder
- 1 tsp chilli powder
- 1 tsp ground coriander
- 2 tsp salt, plus more to taste
- 4 green chillies, slit
- 1.2 litres hot water
- 1 tsp [garam masala](../../base-ingredients/curry-powder/garam-masala.md)
- A small handful of fresh coriander, chopped

## Method

### Stage 1 - Prep
1. Rinse the rice in cold water until it runs clear; soak 20 minutes; drain.
2. Pick over the lentils for stones; rinse until the water runs clear; drain.
3. If using moong dal, dry-roast it in a heavy pan over medium heat for 4 minutes, stirring, until it smells nutty and turns a shade darker. Tip out.

### Stage 2 - The whole-spice ghee
1. Heat the ghee and mustard oil in a heavy-based pot over medium heat.
2. Drop in the cinnamon, cardamom, cloves, bay and cumin seeds; sizzle 30 seconds until aromatic.
3. Add the sliced onions; fry for 10 minutes until deep golden brown.

### Stage 3 - Bhuna the rice and lentils
1. Stir in the ginger and garlic; cook 1 minute.
2. Add the turmeric, chilli powder, coriander and 1 tsp of the salt; fry 30 seconds.
3. Tip in the drained rice, masoor dal and toasted moong dal.
4. Stir over medium heat for 4 to 5 minutes, coating every grain in the spiced ghee. The mixture should smell strongly nutty and look glossy.

### Stage 4 - Cook
1. Pour in the hot water; add the slit green chillies and remaining salt.
2. Bring to a hard boil for 2 minutes, stirring once.
3. Reduce the heat to the lowest setting; cover with a tight lid.
4. Cook for 18 to 20 minutes until the water is absorbed and the rice and dal are tender.
5. Off the heat, stir in the garam masala very gently with a fork (do not mash the grains).
6. Cover again; rest 10 minutes.

### Stage 5 - Serve
1. Fluff gently with a fork.
2. Scatter with chopped coriander.

## Notes
- **The toast step on the moong dal.** This is the difference between bhuna khichuri and ordinary khichuri; the nutty toasted moong adds depth.
- **Hot water, not cold.** Cold water shocks the rice and makes the grains uneven.
- **Do not stir during cooking.** Once the pot is sealed, leave it alone for 18 minutes; stirring breaks the grains.
- **Ghee plus mustard oil.** Pure ghee can read too rich; the mustard oil brings savoury sharpness.
- **One-pot but not casual.** The whole-spice ghee, the bhuna of the grains and the rest after cooking are all required for the dish to come out properly textured.

## Variations
- **With beef:** add 500 g cubed beef shoulder (pre-cooked separately for 90 minutes until tender) to Stage 4 along with the water; this makes a beef khichuri.
- **With chicken:** add 4 bone-in chicken thighs in Stage 4; cook 25 minutes instead of 18.
- **Vegetable bhuna khichuri:** add diced potato, carrot and cauliflower in Stage 3 with the spices.
- **Sticky version (nayem khichuri):** double the water and cook 30 minutes for a porridgey texture; the sickbed comfort version.
- **With egg:** top each portion with a soft-boiled egg, halved.

## Serving
- Beef bhuna, fried hilsa, omelette or aloo bhorta · slit chillies · wedges of lime · a small bowl of yogurt · pickled lime

## Storage
- Refrigerate up to 3 days; texture firms but reheats fine
- Freezes 2 months in portioned containers
- Reheat with 2 tbsp water in a covered pan over low heat for 8 minutes; do not microwave hard or the grains turn gluey
