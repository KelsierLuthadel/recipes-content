# Begun Bhaja

![Begun Bhaja](resources/begun-bhaja.jpg)

*Bengali fried aubergine slices: thick rounds dusted with turmeric, chilli powder and salt, shallow-fried in mustard oil until crisp at the edges and silky in the middle.*

**Serves:** 4

**Prep Time:** 10 minutes (plus 30 minutes salting)

**Cook Time:** 15 minutes

## Overview
Begun bhaja is the Bangladeshi answer to "what's the side dish": thick aubergine rounds, salted and rested to draw out the bitter water, then dusted with turmeric, chilli powder and a pinch of sugar, and shallow-fried in mustard oil until the edges go crisp and the centre stays creamy. It sits next to dal-bhat at breakfast in Bengali homes, alongside khichuri on monsoon afternoons, and as the proper opening to a long lunch. The technique is unfussy and reads almost like a fritter without the batter: the only coating is the spice dusting, the only fat is the mustard oil. Done right, the aubergine slices arrive at the table glossy with oil, edges almost burnt-dark, the middles yielding under a fork.

## Ingredients

- 2 large aubergines (about 700 g total)
- 1.5 tsp fine salt, for salting the slices
- 2 tsp turmeric powder
- 1 tsp chilli powder
- 1 tsp ground cumin (optional but traditional)
- 1 tsp caster sugar (optional, for a Bengali sweet-savoury finish)
- ½ tsp fine salt, for the dusting
- 100 ml mustard oil (more as needed)

## Method

### Stage 1 - Salt the slices
1. Slice the aubergines into rounds 1.5 cm thick (don't go too thin or they collapse, not too thick or the centre stays raw).
2. Spread on a tray; sprinkle generously with the 1.5 tsp salt on both sides.
3. Rest 30 minutes; the slices will weep brown water and soften slightly.

### Stage 2 - Pat and dust
1. Pat each slice dry firmly with kitchen paper, brushing off excess salt.
2. Mix the turmeric, chilli powder, cumin, sugar and the ½ tsp salt in a small bowl.
3. Dust both sides of each aubergine round with the spice mix; the slices will go bright yellow.

### Stage 3 - Fry
1. Heat the mustard oil in a wide pan until it shimmers and just begins to smoke; cool 30 seconds (this kills the raw pungency).
2. Add the aubergine slices in a single layer; do not crowd the pan (work in batches).
3. Fry over medium-high heat for 3 to 4 minutes per side, until the edges crisp and turn deep brown and the centres soften.
4. Lift onto kitchen paper to blot excess oil; serve hot.

## Notes
- **Salt and rest is the cheat code.** Without the 30-minute salt, the aubergine soaks up double the oil and turns soggy. With it, the slices hold their shape and the centre cooks through.
- **Mustard oil, properly heated.** Smoke once, then cool slightly, then fry; this is the Bengali oil discipline.
- **Thick slices only.** Thin slices cook through too fast and lose the centre softness; 1.5 cm is the sweet spot.
- **One layer at a time.** Stacking or crowding the pan drops the oil temperature; the aubergine then steams instead of frying.
- **Eat warm.** Begun bhaja reheats imperfectly; the crisp edges go soft.

## Variations
- **Posto begun bhaja:** mix 1 tbsp poppy seeds into the dusting spice for a Bengali twist.
- **Begun bhaja with kalo jeera:** sprinkle 1 tsp nigella seeds into the oil before adding the slices for an aromatic top note.
- **Batter-fried (beguni):** dip the dusted slices in a thin gram-flour batter before frying for the Kolkata street version.
- **Coconut-spiked:** scatter 2 tbsp freshly grated coconut over the slices in the last minute of frying.
- **Without sugar:** plain Bangladeshi begun bhaja often skips the sugar; the Bengali sweet edge is more pronounced across the border.

## Serving
A pile of warm slices next to plain rice and dal · a spoon of plain yogurt on top · slit chilli · a wedge of lime

## Storage
- Eats best the moment it leaves the pan
- Refrigerate up to 24 hours; reheats in a dry pan over medium heat (avoid microwave)
- Does not freeze
