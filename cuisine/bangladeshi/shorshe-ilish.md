# Shorshe Ilish

![Shorshe Ilish](resources/shorshe-ilish.jpg)

*Bengali celebration dish: hilsa steaks bathed in a fierce yellow and black mustard-seed paste, with green chillies and mustard oil, the showpiece of the Padma river table.*

**Serves:** 4

**Prep Time:** 20 minutes

**Cook Time:** 15 minutes

## Overview
If hilsa fish curry is the everyday hilsa dish, shorshe ilish is the celebration version: the same fish, but here drenched in a fresh-ground paste of yellow and black mustard seeds bloomed in mustard oil, sharpened with green chillies and turmeric. It is the dish that gets cooked when the first ilish of the monsoon arrives, when there's a guest, when a daughter comes home. The mustard paste is freshly pounded so the heat reads cleanly, not dusty; the cooking is brief so the fish stays tender. There's no onion, no tomato, no curry-house gravy: just mustard, oil, fish, chilli and turmeric. The smell of the mustard oil tempering in the pan is recognisable across half of Dhaka from any open window.

## Ingredients

- 600 g hilsa (ilish) steaks (4 thick cross-cut pieces, bone-in)
- 1 tsp turmeric powder, plus a pinch for dusting
- 1 tsp fine salt, plus more to taste
- 5 tbsp mustard oil

### Mustard paste
- 3 tbsp yellow mustard seeds
- 2 tbsp black mustard seeds
- 4 green chillies (2 in the paste, 2 slit for the pan)
- ½ tsp salt
- 4 tbsp warm water

### To finish
- 1 tbsp mustard oil, raw, for drizzling
- A few extra slit chillies (optional)

## Method

### Stage 1 - Prep the fish
1. Pat the hilsa steaks dry; rub with a pinch of turmeric and a pinch of salt.
2. Let sit 10 minutes.

### Stage 2 - Make the mustard paste
1. Soak both kinds of mustard seeds in just-warm water for 15 minutes (this softens them and tames the bitterness).
2. Drain; grind in a small blender or pestle and mortar with 2 green chillies, salt and the 4 tbsp warm water. The paste should be fine, pale yellow, with a sharp clean nose-tingle.
3. Taste; if it reads bitter, work in 1 more teaspoon of warm water and a pinch of salt.

### Stage 3 - Cook the dish
1. Heat 4 tbsp mustard oil in a wide pan until it shimmers and just starts to smoke; cool 30 seconds.
2. Slip the hilsa steaks in; fry 90 seconds per side just to set the surface; lift out.
3. To the pan add the remaining tablespoon of oil and the mustard paste; cook over medium heat for 2 minutes, stirring, until the raw smell mellows.
4. Stir in the turmeric and salt; add 200 ml warm water; bring to a gentle simmer.
5. Slide the fish steaks back in; tuck the 2 slit chillies around them.
6. Simmer very gently for 6 to 8 minutes, spooning the sauce over the fish; the sauce should reduce to a thick yellow coating.
7. Off the heat, drizzle with the raw tablespoon of mustard oil.
8. Rest 5 minutes before serving.

## Notes
- **Yellow plus black mustard.** Yellow seeds give mellow body, black seeds give the sharp heat. Use both; one alone is incomplete.
- **Soak first.** Raw mustard seeds taste bitter when ground straight; the 15-minute warm-water soak is the trick that home cooks know and chefs sometimes skip.
- **No overcooking.** The whole thing on the heat is barely 10 minutes; hilsa cooks fast, and the mustard sauce loses its sharpness if it boils hard.
- **Mustard oil twice.** Smoke once for cooking, drizzle raw for the proper Bengali finish.
- **The bones flavour the sauce.** Bone-in cross-cut steaks are non-negotiable; fillets do not work.

## Variations
- **Shorshe ilish with kalo jeera:** add 1 tsp nigella seeds to the oil before tipping in the mustard paste; the Old Dhaka version.
- **With coconut:** stir in 4 tbsp freshly grated coconut to the paste in Stage 2 for a Bangladesh-Chittagong twist.
- **Sorshe-narkel:** double-down: mustard paste plus coconut paste, both in equal parts; lavish and rich.
- **With aubergine:** add a fried aubergine round on top of each steak as it simmers.
- **Bhapa shorshe ilish:** instead of pan-cooking, smear the paste over the raw fish and steam in a banana leaf packet for 15 minutes; even gentler.

## Serving
- Plain steamed basmati or any non-fragrant white rice · plain yogurt on the side · slit green chillies · a wedge of lime

## Storage
- Refrigerate up to 24 hours; the mustard heat softens but holds
- Does not freeze; the texture of the fish breaks down
- Reheat very gently in a covered pan with 2 tbsp water; never microwave hard
