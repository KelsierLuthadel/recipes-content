# Bangladeshi Cuisine

![Bangladeshi Cuisine](resources/hero.jpg)

Bengal-region cooking with its own deep identity, distinct from the cuisine of Indian-Bengal across the border. Rice and fish are the spine of the table, especially hilsa (ilish) pulled from the Padma river and treated as the national fish. Mustard oil sharpens the savoury palette, alongside nigella seeds (kalo jeera) and the five-seed temper panch phoron (cumin, fennel, fenugreek, mustard, nigella). Bangladeshis love a sweet edge in savoury dishes, a touch of jaggery in a dal or a curry. Dhaka holds the post-Mughal biryani tradition, kacchi biryani slow-cooked sealed under dough. And then there's the universe of Bengali sweets: mishti doi, rasmalai, pitha, sandesh, rasgulla, sweet milk in every form.
