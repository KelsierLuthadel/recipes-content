# Bestia Chiki

![Bestia Chiki](resources/bestia-chiki.jpg)

*Small whole reef fish, scored, salted, then fried twice in hot oil until the skin shatters and the flesh stays moist, served with a wedge of lime and a slick of pika chilli sauce.*

**Serves:** 4

**Prep Time:** 15 minutes

**Cook Time:** 20 minutes

## Overview
Bestia chiki means "little beast" in Papiamento, the affectionate name for small whole reef fish (snapper, grunt, parrotfish, goatfish) fried whole by Curaçaoan fishermen's families on the day of the catch. The trick is the double fry: a first dip at moderate heat cooks the fish through, a second blast at high heat shatters the skin into a glassy crackle while leaving the flesh juicy. The fish goes straight from the oil onto kitchen paper, then onto a plate with a wedge of lime, a spoon of fiery pika sauce (the island's pickled chilli condiment) and a mound of fried funchi or rice. Eat it with your fingers, pulling off the crisp tail and fins first, working the flesh away from the spine.

## Ingredients

- 4 small whole reef fish (red snapper, grunt or seabream, around 300 g each, gutted and scaled)
- 2 limes (juice of 1 for marinating; 1 cut in wedges for serving)
- 2 tsp fine sea salt
- 1 tsp ground black pepper
- 1 tsp sweet paprika
- ½ tsp ground cumin
- 2 cloves garlic, crushed
- 60 g plain flour, for dusting
- 1 litre vegetable oil, for deep-frying

### To serve
- Pika sauce (Curaçaoan pickled chilli relish) or a hot sauce of choice
- Lime wedges

## Method

### Stage 1 - Score and season
1. Rinse the fish under cold water; pat dry inside and out.
2. With a sharp knife, score each fish three times on each side, cutting through the skin into the flesh on a diagonal.
3. Rub all over (and into the cuts and cavity) with the lime juice, salt, pepper, paprika, cumin and crushed garlic. Rest 15 minutes; pat off the surface moisture so the fish doesn't spit in the oil.

### Stage 2 - First fry
1. Heat the oil in a deep heavy pan to 170°C.
2. Dust each fish lightly in flour; shake off excess.
3. Slide in one or two fish at a time (don't crowd); fry 5 minutes per side until the skin is pale gold and the flesh inside the score marks looks opaque.
4. Lift onto a wire rack over a tray; rest 5 minutes.

### Stage 3 - Second fry
1. Bring the oil up to 190°C.
2. Return the fish, one or two at a time; fry 2 minutes per side until the skin blisters and turns deep golden brown.
3. Lift onto kitchen paper to drain; sprinkle a pinch of salt.
4. Plate immediately while the skin still crackles.

## Notes
- **Score deep enough:** Cuts should go through skin into flesh, not just nick the surface. This lets the seasoning penetrate and helps the heat get to the centre.
- **Two fries, two temperatures:** A single long fry overcooks the flesh by the time the skin crisps. The two-stage method is the whole point.
- **Dry the fish before frying:** Water on the skin makes the oil spit and stops the skin from blistering. Pat hard with kitchen paper.
- **Oil temperature matters:** Below 170°C and the fish absorbs oil; above 200°C and the skin burns before the inside is done. A thermometer is worth the investment.
- **Eat with your hands:** Trying to fork off a tiny reef fish is fiddly. Pull the head off, lift the top fillet from the spine, flip and repeat.

## Variations
- **Bestia chiki ku bakijou:** Made with whole salted cod instead, soaked overnight first.
- **Picante version:** Add 1 tsp cayenne to the spice rub for a deeper kick.
- **Grilled bestia chiki:** Same score-and-rub treatment, grilled over wood charcoal for 4 minutes per side.
- **Bestia chiki ku kokada:** Some cooks dust the fish in coconut flour as well as wheat flour for a sweeter crust.
- **Whole prawn version:** Apply the same method to head-on tiger prawns, 90 seconds each side at the second fry.

## Serving
Heap of fried funchi or white rice · a spoon of pika sauce · lime wedges · cold beer or awa di lamunchi · a salad of tomato, cucumber and onion in lime.

## Storage
- Eat immediately; the skin loses its crackle within an hour
- Leftovers refrigerate 2 days, but reheat at 200°C on a wire rack for 6 minutes to re-crisp the skin
- Spice rub keeps in a jar 6 months
