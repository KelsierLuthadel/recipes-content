# Funchi cu Yambo

![Funchi cu Yambo](resources/funchi-cu-yambo.jpg)

*Cornmeal mush served with an okra and salted-meat stew, the West African strand of Curaçao's table, ladled over a soft mound of funchi and eaten with a spoon.*

**Serves:** 4

**Prep Time:** 15 minutes

**Cook Time:** 50 minutes

## Overview
Funchi cu yambo is the dish that anchors the African side of Curaçao's cooking. Funchi (a stiff cornmeal porridge close to Italian polenta or Caribbean fungi) is whipped thick in a pot, turned out as a smooth mound, then drowned in yambo, a thickened stew of sliced okra with onion, tomato and salted pork or shrimp. The okra does the thickening: when sliced and slowly stewed, it releases its natural mucilage that gives yambo its characteristic silky body. The contrast between the firm pale funchi and the dark slippery green stew is the whole point. This is everyday food, the kind of plate that turns up at lunch counters and grandmothers' kitchens, not fancy restaurants. The salted meat lifts the okra, the okra lifts the meal.

## Ingredients

### For the yambo stew
- 400 g fresh okra, topped, tailed, sliced into 1 cm rounds
- 200 g salted pork or smoked pork belly, diced 2 cm (or 200 g raw prawns if preferred)
- 1 onion, finely chopped
- 1 green pepper, diced
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 tomato, diced
- 2 tbsp tomato puree
- 1 madame jeanette chilli, whole and pierced
- 1 tbsp fresh thyme leaves
- 1 bay leaf
- ½ tsp ground allspice
- 500 ml water or fish stock
- 1 tbsp vegetable oil
- Salt and black pepper

### For the funchi
- 250 g fine yellow cornmeal (polenta)
- 750 ml water
- 1 tsp salt
- 2 tbsp butter

## Method

### Stage 1 - Start the yambo
1. If using salted pork: soak in cold water for 20 minutes, drain, rinse.
2. Heat the oil in a wide pot over medium heat. Add the pork; render and brown for 8 minutes.
3. Add onion and green pepper; cook 6 minutes until soft.
4. Stir in garlic, allspice; 30 seconds.
5. Add tomato, tomato puree; cook 2 minutes.
6. Pour in the water or stock; tuck in thyme, bay and the whole chilli. Simmer 15 minutes.

### Stage 2 - Add the okra
1. Drop in the sliced okra; stir gently.
2. Simmer 15-20 minutes, uncovered, until the okra has softened and the stew has thickened to a glossy slick.
3. If using prawns: add at this stage and cook 3 minutes until just pink.
4. Taste for salt; the pork carries plenty, so check before adding more.

### Stage 3 - Make the funchi
1. Bring water and salt to a boil in a heavy pot.
2. Drop the heat to medium-low. Pour the cornmeal in a slow steady stream while whisking hard; whisk for 1 minute to break any lumps.
3. Switch to a wooden spoon. Stir constantly for 8-10 minutes; the funchi will thicken, then pull away from the sides of the pot.
4. Beat in the butter.
5. Turn out into a buttered bowl, pat into a dome, invert onto a warm plate.

### Stage 4 - Serve
1. Cut the funchi into wedges or set the whole mound on a platter.
2. Spoon the yambo over and around. Fish out the bay leaf and chilli before serving.

## Notes
- **Don't over-trim the okra:** Topping and tailing is enough; cutting too deep releases too much slime upfront. The slow stew is what builds the silky texture, not over-cutting.
- **Constant stirring on the funchi:** If you stop, lumps form and the bottom catches. Ten unbroken minutes with a wooden spoon is the whole technique.
- **Salt the pork carefully:** If you skipped the soak, the stew will be too salty. Taste before adding any salt to the okra.
- **Madame jeanette pierced not chopped:** Perfume without burn. Chopping makes the whole pot too hot for daily eating.
- **Fresh okra only:** Frozen okra slimes too aggressively and turns watery; fresh holds its bite.

## Variations
- **Yambo di piska:** Made with salted cod (bakijou) soaked overnight, broken into the stew.
- **Yambo di kabaron:** Prawn-only version, lighter, common in the fishing villages.
- **Yambo with crab:** A few blue crab pieces folded in at the end.
- **Vegetarian yambo:** Skip the meat; use vegetable stock; add a tin of black-eyed peas with the okra.
- **Tutu finish:** Stir 100 g cooked black-eyed peas into the finished funchi for a heavier mash (closer to tutu).

## Serving
Spoon yambo over the funchi · a piece of avocado on the side · a wedge of lime · cold beer or lime water.

## Storage
- Yambo refrigerates 3 days; thickens further on cooling, loosen with water to reheat
- Funchi sets firm in the fridge; slice and pan-fry the leftovers in oil for next day
- Reheat yambo gently over low heat; high heat splits the okra
