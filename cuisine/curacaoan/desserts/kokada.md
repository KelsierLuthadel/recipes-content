# Kokada

![Kokada](resources/kokada.jpg)

*Curaçao's coconut-sugar truffles: fresh grated coconut cooked down with brown sugar and a slug of vanilla until the mixture thickens to a stiff fudge, then rolled into rough balls and set.*

**Serves:** Makes around 24 small truffles

**Prep Time:** 15 minutes

**Cook Time:** 25 minutes

## Overview
Kokada is the Curaçao street-stall sweet, sold by the bag at school gates, on roadsides, at the entrance to every old-town market. Fresh grated coconut (or rehydrated desiccated coconut when fresh isn't possible) is simmered with brown sugar and a little water until the syrup boils, thickens and starts to grain at the edges; vanilla, a pinch of salt and sometimes a knob of butter go in at the end. The mass is dropped by the spoonful onto greaseproof paper, rolled into rough balls between damp palms, and left to set firm. The colour ranges from pale gold (light brown sugar) to deep mahogany (dark muscovado); the texture sits between a coconut macaroon and a fudge, sticky and chewy, with the coconut still recognisable in every bite.

## Ingredients

- 300 g fresh grated coconut (from 1 large mature coconut), or 250 g unsweetened desiccated coconut soaked in 100 ml warm milk for 15 minutes
- 300 g soft dark brown sugar (or palm sugar / panela for a deeper flavour)
- 150 ml water
- 1 tsp vanilla extract
- ¼ tsp ground cinnamon (optional)
- A pinch of salt
- 20 g unsalted butter

## Method

### Stage 1 - Crack and grate the coconut (if using fresh)
1. Crack the coconut open; drain the water (drink it).
2. Lever the white flesh away from the shell with a knife; trim the brown skin.
3. Grate finely on a box grater or pulse in a food processor to a fine flake.

### Stage 2 - Cook the syrup
1. Combine the brown sugar and water in a heavy-bottomed wide pan.
2. Set over medium heat; stir until the sugar dissolves.
3. Bring to a low boil; let it bubble for 4-5 minutes until the syrup thickens slightly and forms a soft thread when a drop is lifted on a spoon.

### Stage 3 - Add the coconut
1. Tip in the grated coconut, vanilla, cinnamon (if using) and salt.
2. Stir constantly with a wooden spoon over medium-low heat.
3. The mixture will thicken steadily; after 10-12 minutes it should pull away from the bottom of the pan and start to look glossy with a faint sandy graining.
4. Stir in the butter; cook 1 minute more. The mixture is ready when a spoonful dropped onto a cool plate holds its shape and doesn't run.

### Stage 4 - Shape
1. Lay a sheet of greaseproof paper on the counter.
2. Working quickly while the mixture is warm but not hot, scoop heaped teaspoons and drop onto the paper.
3. Dampen your palms with cold water; roll each scoop into a 2.5 cm ball.
4. Set the balls back onto the paper; leave at room temperature for 30 minutes to firm.

## Notes
- **Fresh coconut is best:** The flavour and texture is brighter and chewier than desiccated. If you must use desiccated, soak it in warm milk first; bone-dry desiccated gives a gritty kokada.
- **Watch the sugar:** Brown sugar can burn at the edges of a hot pan. Stir constantly once the coconut is in, and keep the heat at medium-low.
- **Damp hands for rolling:** Dry hands and the mixture sticks like glue. Keep a bowl of cold water by the chopping board.
- **The set test:** Drop a spoonful onto a cold plate; if it firms up and holds shape in 30 seconds, the mass is ready. If it spreads, cook 2 more minutes.
- **Palm sugar version:** Replacing the brown sugar with palm sugar gives a deeper caramel flavour and a darker colour, popular in the older recipes.

## Variations
- **Kokada pretu:** Made with dark muscovado for a near-black truffle.
- **Kokada di chukulati:** Roll the finished truffles in cocoa powder for a chocolate-coated version.
- **Spiced kokada:** Add ¼ tsp grated nutmeg and a pinch of ground clove.
- **Rum kokada:** Stir 1 tbsp dark rum into the mixture at the end (for adults only).
- **Kokada di lechi:** Replace the water with condensed milk for a denser fudge-style truffle.

## Serving
With strong black coffee · at the end of a heavy meal · packed in paper twists for children · alongside a glass of cold milk · with a slice of bolo pretu on the same plate (the rich-on-rich Sunday pairing).

## Storage
- Keeps in an airtight tin at room temperature for 2 weeks
- Refrigerates 1 month, but bring to room temperature before eating (the texture firms up cold)
- Do not freeze (the texture goes mealy on thawing)
