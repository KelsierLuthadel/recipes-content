# Bolo di Banana

![Bolo di Banana](resources/curacao-bolo-di-banana.jpg)

*Curaçao's banana sponge cake: very ripe bananas mashed into a buttery brown-sugar batter spiced with cinnamon and nutmeg, baked in a loaf tin, glazed with a thin sugar syrup while warm.*

**Serves:** 10

**Prep Time:** 20 minutes

**Cook Time:** 55 minutes

## Overview
Bolo di banana is the everyday banana cake of Curaçao, the loaf that turns up at family afternoons, school fundraisers, and on the counter at the harbour-side bakeries. Black-spotted overripe bananas (the kind too soft for the fruit bowl) are mashed into a rich batter of butter, dark brown sugar, eggs, flour, and the Curaçao baking spice trio of cinnamon, nutmeg and a whisper of clove. A glug of rum or vanilla goes in at the end; some cooks add a handful of crushed walnuts or raisins. The cake is baked in a loaf tin until the top cracks and the inside stays moist, then brushed while still warm with a thin sugar syrup that soaks into the surface. It's lighter than the dense fruit-stuffed bolo pretu, an everyday cake rather than a ceremonial one.

## Ingredients

### For the cake
- 4 very ripe bananas (around 450 g unpeeled; skins should be heavily blackened)
- 180 g unsalted butter, softened
- 200 g soft dark brown sugar
- 3 large eggs
- 1 tsp vanilla extract
- 1 tbsp dark rum (optional but traditional)
- 280 g plain flour
- 1 tsp baking powder
- ½ tsp baking soda
- 1 tsp ground cinnamon
- ½ tsp grated nutmeg
- ¼ tsp ground clove
- ½ tsp salt
- 80 ml whole milk
- 60 g chopped walnuts (optional)

### For the glaze
- 60 g caster sugar
- 60 ml water
- 1 tbsp dark rum or 1 tsp vanilla

## Method

### Stage 1 - Prepare
1. Heat oven to 170°C.
2. Butter a 1.2 kg loaf tin and line the base and long sides with greaseproof paper.
3. Peel and mash the bananas in a bowl with a fork; the puree should be lumpy not smooth.

### Stage 2 - Make the batter
1. Beat the butter and brown sugar with electric beaters for 5 minutes until pale and fluffy.
2. Add the eggs one at a time, beating well after each.
3. Beat in the vanilla, rum and mashed banana; the mixture may look curdled, which is fine.
4. Sift flour, baking powder, baking soda, cinnamon, nutmeg, clove and salt.
5. Fold half the dry ingredients into the wet, then the milk, then the rest of the dry, just until combined.
6. Fold in the walnuts if using.

### Stage 3 - Bake
1. Pour the batter into the prepared tin; smooth the top.
2. Bake 50-55 minutes, until risen, deeply golden, with a clean skewer from the centre. Cover loosely with foil at 40 minutes if the top darkens too quickly.

### Stage 4 - Glaze
1. While the cake bakes, dissolve the caster sugar in the water in a small pan; bring to a low boil for 2 minutes. Off the heat, stir in the rum or vanilla.
2. Lift the cake out of the oven; while still in the tin and hot, prick the top all over with a skewer.
3. Spoon the warm syrup slowly over the surface; let it soak in.
4. Cool 20 minutes in the tin, then turn out onto a wire rack to cool fully.

## Notes
- **The bananas must be overripe:** Yellow bananas with no spots make a bland cake. Black-skinned bananas are at peak sweetness and aroma; this is the whole flavour of the loaf.
- **Don't overmix the batter:** Fold the flour in just until you can't see white streaks. Overmixing develops gluten and gives a tough crumb.
- **The glaze step is the difference:** Skipping the syrup gives a perfectly nice banana cake but not bolo di banana. The soak is what gives the loaf its sticky moist surface.
- **Prick deep:** Use a thin skewer right down to the bottom so the syrup gets all the way through.
- **The rum is optional:** A teaspoon of vanilla works for children's birthdays.

## Variations
- **Bolo di banana ku chukulati:** Fold 100 g chopped dark chocolate into the batter.
- **Coconut version:** Add 50 g desiccated coconut to the dry ingredients.
- **Spiced citrus:** Add the zest of an orange and a pinch of ground cardamom.
- **Bolo di banana ku raisin:** Stir 80 g raisins (soaked in rum) into the batter.
- **Loaf to bundt:** Bake in a 24 cm bundt tin for 45 minutes; halve the glaze, brush only the outside.

## Serving
Sliced thick for afternoon coffee · warm with a scoop of vanilla ice cream · toasted in a dry pan for breakfast · packed in paper for road trips · a thin slice alongside black coffee.

## Storage
- Wrapped in greaseproof paper inside a tin, keeps 5 days at room temperature
- Refrigerates 1 week; the cake firms up cold, bring to room temperature before eating
- Freezes 2 months wrapped tightly; thaw at room temperature for 3 hours
