# Keshi Yena

![Keshi Yena](resources/keshi-yena.jpg)

*The Curaçaoan classic: a whole hollowed Edam cheese stuffed with spiced chicken stew of olives, capers, raisins and tomato, baked until the rind blisters and the inside turns molten, then sliced open at the table.*

**Serves:** 6

**Prep Time:** 30 minutes

**Cook Time:** 1 hour 30 minutes

## Overview
Keshi yena (literally "stuffed cheese") was born from Dutch colonial waste: enslaved cooks salvaged the empty Edam wax shells the Dutch left after carving up the cheese for themselves, and stuffed them with whatever spiced meat stew was to hand. The result is the dish that defines Curaçao. A whole young Edam, hollowed out to a centimetre thickness, lined with the carved-out cheese, packed with a tomato-rich chicken stew studded with green olives, capers, raisins and pickled onions, sealed shut and baked until the cheese melts into the filling and the rind crisps. At the table it is sliced open like a pumpkin pie, the cheese stringing as the spoon goes in. Modern home cooks use small ramekins lined with Edam slices, but the whole-cheese version is the proper one for a Sunday.

## Ingredients

### For the cheese shell
- 1 whole young Edam, around 2 kg (in its wax)
- 12 slices Gouda or Edam (200 g) to line the dish if using ramekins instead

### For the chicken stew filling
- 800 g chicken thighs, boneless and skinless, diced 2 cm
- 2 tbsp vegetable oil
- 1 large onion, finely chopped
- 1 green pepper, finely chopped
- 4 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 tomato, diced
- 2 tbsp tomato puree
- 100 ml chicken stock
- 80 g green olives, sliced
- 30 g capers, drained
- 50 g raisins
- 50 g pickled cocktail onions, drained
- 1 tbsp Worcestershire sauce
- 1 tsp sweet paprika
- 1 tsp ground cumin
- ½ tsp ground allspice
- 2 bay leaves
- 1 tbsp fresh thyme leaves
- Salt and black pepper

### To finish
- 2 eggs, lightly beaten
- 30 g fine dried breadcrumbs

## Method

### Stage 1 - Hollow the cheese
1. Peel away the red wax from the Edam.
2. Slice off the top quarter as a lid; reserve.
3. With a sturdy spoon, scoop out the interior, leaving a wall about 1 cm thick. Reserve all the carved-out cheese; grate roughly.
4. Soak the hollow shell and the lid in cold water for 30 minutes; this softens the rind and stops it from cracking. Pat dry.

### Stage 2 - Make the stew
1. Heat the oil in a wide pan over medium-high heat. Brown the diced chicken in batches; lift out to a plate.
2. In the same pan add the onion and green pepper; cook 8 minutes until soft.
3. Add garlic, paprika, cumin and allspice; stir 30 seconds.
4. Return chicken with tomato, tomato puree, stock, bay and thyme. Simmer 25 minutes, partly covered, until the chicken is tender and the sauce thick.
5. Stir in olives, capers, raisins, pickled onions and Worcestershire. Taste for salt and pepper. Cool slightly.
6. Beat in the eggs and 100 g of the grated reserved cheese; the egg binds the filling so it slices cleanly.

### Stage 3 - Stuff and bake
1. Heat oven to 175°C.
2. Set the hollow Edam in a deep oven dish lined with baking paper (to catch any cheese drips).
3. Spoon the filling into the cavity, packing firmly. Sprinkle breadcrumbs over the top, scatter on a layer of the reserved grated cheese.
4. Place the cheese lid back on (or leave open for the top to brown).
5. Bake 45-50 minutes until the cheese walls soften and the top is bubbling and gold.
6. Rest 10 minutes; bring to the table whole; slice down through the rind with a serrated knife so each portion gets cheese wall and filling.

## Notes
- **Edam, not gouda:** Young Edam has the right balance of melt and structure. Mature gouda goes greasy; old Edam is too firm to hollow.
- **Don't skip the soaking:** Dry rind cracks in the oven. The cold water step keeps it elastic.
- **The egg binder:** Without it, the filling slumps when you slice the cheese open. Beat the eggs in once the stew has cooled enough not to scramble them.
- **Ramekin version:** If a whole cheese is unavailable, line 6 buttered ramekins with overlapping slices of Edam or young gouda, fill with stew, cover with more cheese slices, bake 25 minutes at 180°C.
- **The pickled onions:** A non-negotiable. They cut the richness of the cheese and bring a sharp note that ties the filling together.

## Variations
- **Keshi yena di galiña kachó:** Made with stewed hen rather than chicken thigh; longer cook, deeper flavour.
- **Keshi yena di piska:** Salted cod or fresh white fish filling, common at Lent.
- **Keshi yena di karni:** Beef shin or shoulder, diced and braised 2 hours before stuffing; a Sunday family version.
- **Spiced shrimp filling:** Prawns folded into the last 5 minutes of cooking.

## Serving
Serve straight from the dish · funchi or white rice on the side · a green salad or pickled cucumber to cut the richness · a glass of cold beer or Blue Curaçao on the rocks.

## Storage
- Leftovers refrigerate 3 days; the filling keeps better than the cheese wall, which can weep oil
- Reheat covered in a 160°C oven for 15-20 minutes until warmed through
- The chicken stew filling alone freezes 2 months; thaw, restuff and bake fresh
