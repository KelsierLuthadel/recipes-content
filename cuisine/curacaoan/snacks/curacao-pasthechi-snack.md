# Pasthechi (Snack Size)

![Pasthechi (Snack Size)](resources/curacao-pasthechi-snack.jpg)

*The kiosk-sized version of Curaçao's famous turnover: smaller hand-held pasthechi sealed around gouda or spiced beef, deep-fried to order, eaten standing at the counter with a paper napkin.*

**Serves:** Makes around 24 snack-size turnovers

**Prep Time:** 45 minutes (plus 1 hour resting)

**Cook Time:** 30 minutes

## Overview
A pasthechi from a kiosk (a snèk, the Curaçao word for the corner shop / fast-food window) is smaller than the home-baked version, the size of a palm rather than a full hand. They're stacked behind glass at every street stall on the island, kept warm under a heat lamp, sold by the half-dozen in waxed paper. The dough is the same faintly sweet pastry as the bigger version, the filling is the same gouda or beef, but the proportions tilt: a thinner shell, less filling, made for hand-held eating between buses. They're what schoolchildren take for lunch, what office workers grab at 10am, what passes for an afternoon snack with cold soda. The shape is the half-moon, fork-sealed, no different from the larger version other than scale.

## Ingredients

### For the dough
- 400 g plain flour
- 75 g caster sugar
- 1 tsp salt
- ½ tsp baking powder
- 100 g cold butter, cubed
- 1 egg yolk
- 150 ml cold water (more if needed)
- 1 tsp white vinegar

### For the cheese filling
- 300 g young gouda, grated
- 1 tbsp plain flour
- 1 tsp dijon mustard
- ¼ tsp grated nutmeg
- 80 ml whole milk
- Black pepper

### To fry
- 1 litre vegetable oil

## Method

### Stage 1 - Make the dough
1. Whisk flour, sugar, salt and baking powder.
2. Rub in the butter to coarse crumbs.
3. Mix yolk, water and vinegar; bring the dough together.
4. Knead briefly until smooth; wrap, rest in the fridge 1 hour.

### Stage 2 - Make the cheese filling
1. Combine grated cheese with flour, mustard, nutmeg, plenty of pepper.
2. Warm the milk in a small pan; tip in the cheese mix; stir over very low heat until thick and ropey, around 2 minutes.
3. Cool fully; chill until firm enough to scoop.

### Stage 3 - Shape
1. Roll the dough out 2 mm thick (thinner than the full-size version for snack-shells).
2. Cut 8 cm circles (a tumbler or small jar lid works as a template).
3. Place a teaspoon of filling on one half; brush the edge with water; fold; seal with a fork.
4. Repeat for all the rounds; you should get around 24.

### Stage 4 - Fry
1. Heat the oil to 175°C in a deep heavy pan.
2. Fry the pasthechi in batches of four, 3-4 minutes, turning, until deep gold and puffed.
3. Lift onto kitchen paper. Eat hot.

## Notes
- **Smaller than the dinner version:** This is the key thing; an 8 cm round, a teaspoon of filling, two bites and gone. Don't be tempted to overfill.
- **Roll thinner:** A snack pasthechi has a delicate ratio of shell to filling; 2 mm dough is right, 3 mm makes them too bready.
- **Sealing matters more on the small ones:** Less filling, more edge proportionally, so a single weak spot leaks. Press the fork firmly.
- **Eat hot:** A pasthechi straight from the fryer is the whole point; cooled, the dough goes leathery.
- **Quick-fry, hot oil:** Small turnovers cook fast at 175°C. Don't drop the heat below this or they soak up oil.

## Variations
- **Mini beef pasthechi:** Use the spiced minced beef filling from the full pasthechi recipe; halve the quantity per turnover.
- **Spicy:** Add finely diced madame jeanette chilli to the cheese filling.
- **Pasthechi di hot dog:** Wrap a small piece of sausage in the dough as a snack-stall novelty.
- **Curaçao-style breakfast:** Add scrambled egg to the cheese filling.
- **Baked snack pasthechi:** Brush with egg wash, bake at 200°C for 16 minutes for a lighter version.

## Serving
Eat hot from the paper · with a spoon of pika sauce or ketchup · a glass of cold awa di lamunchi · a paper twist of fries on the side · standing at a kiosk counter (correct method).

## Storage
- Best within 1 hour of frying
- Refrigerate up to 2 days; re-crisp in a 200°C oven for 5 minutes
- Freeze unfried (on a tray, then bagged); fry from frozen for 5-6 minutes at 175°C
