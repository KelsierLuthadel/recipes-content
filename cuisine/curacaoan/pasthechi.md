# Pasthechi

![Pasthechi](resources/pasthechi.jpg)

*Curaçao's national turnover: a sweet-savoury yellow pastry folded around a filling of spiced gouda or stewed minced beef, sealed at the edge with a fork, then deep-fried until the dough puffs and turns gold.*

**Serves:** 12 turnovers

**Prep Time:** 40 minutes (plus 1 hour resting)

**Cook Time:** 30 minutes

## Overview
The pasthechi is what every Curaçaoan eats for breakfast, what they grab from a roadside kiosk at lunch, and what travels home from the bakery in a paper bag at the end of the school day. The dough is unusual: it carries a little sugar, a yolk, sometimes a splash of milk, giving the finished crust a faint sweetness against the savoury filling. The classic two versions are pasthechi di keshi (gouda cheese sharpened with a little nutmeg and mustard) and pasthechi di karni (slow-stewed minced beef with raisins and olives). The shape is a half-moon, fork-sealed, the size of a hand. Deep-fried gives the proper crackle and gold; baking works but you lose the kiosk character. They're often eaten while standing on a kerb, hot enough to burn the roof of your mouth.

## Ingredients

### For the dough
- 400 g plain flour
- 75 g caster sugar
- 1 tsp salt
- ½ tsp baking powder
- 100 g cold butter, cubed
- 1 egg yolk
- 150 ml cold water (more as needed)
- 1 tsp white vinegar

### For the cheese filling
- 250 g young gouda, grated
- 1 tbsp plain flour
- 1 tsp dijon mustard
- ¼ tsp grated nutmeg
- 60 ml whole milk
- Black pepper

### For the beef filling
- 300 g minced beef
- 1 onion, finely chopped
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 small tomato, diced
- 1 tbsp tomato puree
- 1 tbsp Worcestershire sauce
- 30 g raisins
- 30 g green olives, sliced
- 1 tsp ground cumin
- ½ tsp ground allspice
- 1 tsp sweet paprika
- 100 ml beef stock
- 1 tbsp vegetable oil
- Salt and pepper

### To finish
- 1 litre vegetable oil for deep-frying
- 1 egg, beaten (if baking instead)

## Method

### Stage 1 - Make the dough
1. Whisk flour, sugar, salt and baking powder in a wide bowl.
2. Rub in the cold butter until the mixture looks like coarse sand.
3. Mix the yolk, water and vinegar; pour in and bring together with a fork.
4. Tip onto a clean surface; knead for 2 minutes until smooth. Wrap; rest in the fridge for 1 hour.

### Stage 2 - Make the cheese filling
1. Stir the grated cheese, flour, mustard, nutmeg and a generous grind of pepper together.
2. Heat the milk in a small pan; tip in the cheese mixture; stir over very low heat for 2 minutes until thick and ropey.
3. Cool fully; refrigerate until firm enough to scoop.

### Stage 3 - Make the beef filling
1. Heat the oil in a frying pan. Cook the onion 5 minutes; add garlic, cumin, allspice and paprika; 30 seconds.
2. Add the beef, breaking it up; brown for 5 minutes.
3. Stir in tomato, tomato puree, Worcestershire and stock. Simmer 12 minutes until almost dry.
4. Stir in raisins and olives; cook 2 more minutes. Season; cool fully.

### Stage 4 - Shape
1. Roll the dough on a lightly floured surface to 3 mm thick.
2. Stamp out 12 cm circles (a small saucer works as a template).
3. Place a heaped tablespoon of filling on one half of each circle, leaving a 1 cm border.
4. Brush the border with water. Fold the dough over to enclose; press the edge with a fork to seal.

### Stage 5 - Fry
1. Heat the oil to 175°C.
2. Slide the pasthechi in two or three at a time; fry 4-5 minutes, turning once, until deep gold.
3. Lift onto kitchen paper. Eat hot, but careful, the filling is molten.

## Notes
- **The sweet dough:** Don't skip the sugar; it's what makes pasthechi recognisable. Without it you've made an empanada, not a pasthechi.
- **Fork-seal firmly:** A weak seal opens in the fryer and the filling leaks. Press hard with the fork along the full edge.
- **Don't overfill:** A tablespoon is plenty. Overstuffed pasthechi split open and the filling burns in the oil.
- **Bake option:** Brush with beaten egg and bake at 200°C for 20 minutes for a less rich, less crisp version, popular at home.
- **Hot oil temperature:** Below 170°C and the pastry soaks oil and goes greasy; above 180°C and the outside burns before the dough cooks through.

## Variations
- **Pasthechi di piska:** Salted-cod filling, soaked overnight, flaked into a mild béchamel.
- **Pasthechi di galiña:** Shredded stewed chicken with capers.
- **Pasthechi di kabaron:** Spiced prawn filling, popular at fish stalls.
- **Pasthechi di banana:** A sweet version with caramelised banana and brown sugar, eaten as a snack.
- **Tropical chilli pasthechi:** Add finely diced madame jeanette chilli to the beef filling for the modern hotter style.

## Serving
Eat hot, straight from the paper · a spoon of pika sauce or ketchup on the side · cold drink, awa di lamunchi or a sweet milkshake · a thin paper napkin (they leak).

## Storage
- Best eaten within 2 hours of frying; the pastry softens as it cools
- Refrigerate up to 2 days; reheat in a 200°C oven for 8 minutes to re-crisp
- Freeze unfried turnovers on a tray, transfer to a bag once solid; deep-fry from frozen for 6-7 minutes
