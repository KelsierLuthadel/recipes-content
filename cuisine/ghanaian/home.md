# Ghanaian Cuisine

![Ghanaian Cuisine](resources/hero.jpg)

A cuisine of starch-stew-pepper triplets: a rice (jollof, waakye), a swallow (banku, fufu, kenkey) or a pounded plantain, paired with a stew and a fierce hot sauce. The pan-West-African jollof rivalry runs hot with Nigeria; Ghanaian jollof is drier, more rice-forward, finished with parboiled jasmine and a slick of tomato. Palm oil and dried fish run deep, plantains underpin the snack economy (kelewele, tatale, kose, red-red) and the regional reach is wide: Akan groundnut soup, Ga kenkey and shito, Ewe fufu varieties. Street-food layers (bofrot, meat pie, kelewele) sit alongside the formal stews.
