# Groundnut Soup

![Groundnut Soup](resources/groundnut-soup.jpg)

*The Akan stew of pure peanut paste cooked with tomato, ginger and chilli until it goes brick-red and silky, with bone-in goat or chicken simmered through, served with fufu or rice balls.*

**Serves:** 4

**Prep Time:** 15 minutes

**Cook Time:** 1 hour 30 minutes

## Overview
Groundnut soup (nkatenkwan in Twi) is the rich nutty Akan stew that lines every Sunday-lunch table. The base is pure groundnut paste (smooth natural peanut butter, no sugar), thinned with stock and cooked long with tomato, onion, ginger, garlic and a whole scotch bonnet until the fat separates and rises in a brick-red slick. Bone-in chicken or goat is the classic protein; the bones give the broth depth. The texture should be silky, neither watery nor claggy, and the colour should glow. Serve with omotuo (rice balls) or fufu, never bread, and ladle generously. Skim the oil if you want, but most cooks leave it.

## Ingredients

- 1 kg bone-in chicken pieces or goat shoulder, cut into chunks
- 200 g natural smooth peanut butter (unsweetened)
- 4 ripe tomatoes
- 1 large onion, halved
- 1 red bell pepper
- 2 scotch bonnets (one whole, one chopped)
- 4 cm ginger
- 4 garlic cloves
- 2 tbsp tomato paste
- 1.5 litres stock or water
- 2 bay leaves
- 1 tsp ground crayfish (optional)
- 1 stock cube
- 2 tsp salt

## Method

### Stage 1 - Brown the meat
1. Season the chicken or goat with 1 tsp salt and the stock cube.
2. Place in a heavy pot with half the onion (chopped), 2 cm ginger (sliced), 2 garlic cloves (minced) and the chopped scotch bonnet.
3. Cover and steam over medium heat for 15 minutes, letting the meat release its juices.

### Stage 2 - Blend the base
1. Blend the tomatoes, the other onion half, the red bell pepper, the remaining ginger and garlic and the tomato paste with 200 ml water until smooth.

### Stage 3 - Loosen the peanut butter
1. In a bowl, whisk the peanut butter with 400 ml of warm stock or water until smooth and pourable.

### Stage 4 - Build the soup
1. Pour the blended tomato mixture into the pot with the meat; cook 10 minutes.
2. Stir in the loosened peanut butter, the remaining stock, bay leaves and the whole scotch bonnet.
3. Bring to a steady simmer; reduce heat to low.
4. Cook uncovered for 45 minutes (60 for goat), stirring every 5 minutes so the bottom does not catch. The colour will deepen and the oil will rise.

### Stage 5 - Finish
1. Skim off most of the surface oil if you want a lighter soup (or leave it for richness).
2. Stir in the ground crayfish; check the salt.
3. Rest 5 minutes before serving.

## Notes
- **The peanut butter quality matters:** Use natural unsweetened peanut butter with only peanuts and salt. Sweetened or hydrogenated will not give the right flavour or texture.
- **The oil separation is the sign of doneness:** When a clear red oil rims the pot, the soup is ready. Stir before serving so it doesn't look greasy.
- **The whole scotch bonnet:** Floats in the soup releasing aroma; remove before serving or break for more heat.

## Variations
- **With kontomire:** Add 100 g chopped cocoyam leaves or spinach in the last 10 minutes.
- **With smoked fish:** Add 200 g flaked smoked fish for a deeper-flavoured version.
- **Vegan:** Skip the meat; add 400 g mushrooms and 200 g sweet potato chunks; use vegetable stock.
- **Spicier:** Pierce the scotch bonnet before adding.

## Serving
- Eat hot with omotuo (rice balls) or fufu · ladle the soup over · a piece of meat on top · a glass of cold water alongside.

## Storage
- Keeps 4 days refrigerated and freezes 2 months
- Reheat slowly; the peanut butter can separate if rushed
- Stir hard while reheating to bring back together
