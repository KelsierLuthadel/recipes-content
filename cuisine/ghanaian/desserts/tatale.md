# Tatale

![Tatale](resources/tatale.jpg)

*Sweet over-ripe-plantain pancakes mashed with grated onion, ginger and chilli, fried in palm or vegetable oil until the edges caramelise and the centre stays moist.*

**Serves:** 4 (about 10 pancakes)

**Prep Time:** 10 minutes

**Cook Time:** 20 minutes

## Overview
Tatale is the home rescue for very-over-ripe plantains, the ones with black skin that have gone too soft to fry as slabs. They get mashed into a thick batter with grated onion, ginger, a small amount of flour to bind, a pinch of chilli and salt and sugar to taste, then fried as small pancakes in shallow oil. The result is sweet and faintly savoury at once, with a slightly crisp underside and a soft, fragrant centre. The grated onion is the surprise, it cuts the sweetness and gives the pancakes a depth most fried-fruit cakes lack. Eat as a snack, breakfast or dessert, often with a cup of milky tea. Children's food and grown-up nostalgia food at once.

## Ingredients

- 4 very over-ripe plantains (black-skinned, soft)
- 1 small onion, finely grated
- 2 cm ginger, grated
- 1/2 scotch bonnet, deseeded and finely chopped (optional)
- 60 g plain flour or millet flour
- 1 tsp ground anise or aniseed (optional)
- 1/2 tsp ground nutmeg
- 1/2 tsp salt
- 1-2 tbsp sugar (to taste; over-ripe plantain may be sweet enough)
- Vegetable or red palm oil for shallow-frying

## Method

### Stage 1 - Mash the plantain
1. Peel the plantains; mash in a bowl with a fork until smooth (a few lumps are fine).

### Stage 2 - Mix the batter
1. Stir in the grated onion, ginger, scotch bonnet if using, anise, nutmeg, salt and sugar.
2. Sift in the flour; stir until just combined. The batter should be thick but spoonable, like a stiff porridge.
3. Rest 5 minutes.

### Stage 3 - Fry
1. Heat 1 cm of oil in a wide pan over medium heat.
2. Drop heaped tablespoons of batter into the pan, flattening lightly into rough rounds.
3. Fry 2-3 minutes per side until deep gold and caramelised at the edges.
4. Lift onto kitchen paper.

### Stage 4 - Serve
1. Pile on a plate; eat warm.

## Notes
- **Plantain blackness:** The plantain skin must be mostly black; under-ripe plantain will not mash properly or carry the flavour.
- **Grated onion, not chopped:** Grating gives a smoother batter and disperses the onion flavour evenly.
- **Oil choice:** Vegetable oil is everyday; a small amount of palm oil mixed in (1 tbsp) gives a deeper colour and traditional aroma.

## Variations
- **Tatale with gari:** Add 2 tbsp gari to the batter for a faint nutty crunch.
- **Coconut tatale:** Add 30 g desiccated coconut to the batter.
- **Without flour:** Use mashed black-eyed peas for binding instead.
- **Sweeter:** Drizzle warm tatale with honey or a spoon of evaporated milk.

## Serving
Eat warm as a snack · with a cup of milky tea or Milo · for breakfast with a fried egg · with a spoon of yoghurt if you want a softer pair.

## Storage
- Best eaten fresh
- Keeps 1 day at room temperature in a covered tin; reheat in a hot pan
- Do not freeze; the texture goes wet
