# Meat Pie

![Meat Pie](resources/meat-pie.jpg)

*A half-moon shortcrust pasty with a peppery minced-beef-and-onion filling, brushed with egg and baked until deep gold, the road-side and bus-park snack of Ghana and West Africa.*

**Serves:** Makes 10-12 pies

**Prep Time:** 30 minutes (plus 30 minutes pastry chill)

**Cook Time:** 30 minutes

## Overview
The Ghanaian meat pie is a half-moon shortcrust pasty, the kind sold in glass cabinets at petrol stations, bus stations and supermarket counters across the country. The pastry is firm and slightly buttery, the filling is minced beef with onion, carrot, curry powder, thyme and a careful pinch of scotch bonnet, bound with a roux to keep the filling moist without making the pastry soggy. The shape is the half-moon, sealed with a fork crimp and brushed with beaten egg for the gold finish. Eat warm or at room temperature, often with a cold Coke or a Malta. Closer in feel to a Cornish pasty than a Nigerian meat pie (smaller, slightly less doughy), and a staple lunchbox item.

## Ingredients

For the pastry:
- 400 g plain flour
- 200 g cold butter, cubed
- 1 tsp salt
- 1 tsp baking powder
- 1 large egg
- 80-100 ml cold water

For the filling:
- 1 tbsp vegetable oil
- 1 small onion, finely chopped
- 1 carrot, peeled and finely diced
- 2 garlic cloves, minced
- 2 cm ginger, grated
- 1/2 scotch bonnet, deseeded and finely chopped
- 400 g minced beef
- 1 tsp curry powder
- 1 tsp dried thyme
- 1/2 tsp ground white pepper
- 1 stock cube
- 1 tsp salt
- 200 ml beef stock or water
- 2 tbsp plain flour
- 2 medium potatoes, peeled and diced 5 mm (about 250 g)

For the glaze:
- 1 egg yolk
- 1 tbsp milk

## Method

### Stage 1 - Make the pastry
1. In a large bowl, rub the cold butter into the flour, salt and baking powder until the texture is like breadcrumbs.
2. Whisk the egg with 80 ml cold water; pour into the flour.
3. Bring together with a knife, then briefly with your hands, into a firm dough. Add a splash more water if needed. Do not over-work.
4. Wrap; chill 30 minutes.

### Stage 2 - Cook the filling
1. Heat the oil in a wide pan over medium heat.
2. Add the onion; cook 5 minutes until soft.
3. Stir in the carrot, garlic, ginger and scotch bonnet; cook 3 minutes.
4. Push to one side; add the mince and brown for 5 minutes, breaking it up.
5. Add the diced potato, curry powder, thyme, white pepper, stock cube and salt.
6. Pour in 200 ml stock; cover; simmer 12 minutes until the potato is tender.
7. Sprinkle in the 2 tbsp flour; stir for 2 minutes until the liquid thickens to a coating sauce. The filling should be moist, not wet.
8. Cool fully before assembling.

### Stage 3 - Assemble
1. Heat the oven to 190 C (fan).
2. Roll the pastry on a lightly floured surface to 3 mm thick.
3. Cut rounds with a 13 cm bowl or cutter (about 10-12 rounds; re-roll trimmings).
4. Spoon 2 tbsp of filling onto one half of each round.
5. Brush the edges with water; fold over into a half-moon; press the edges together and crimp with a fork.
6. Place on a lined baking tray.

### Stage 4 - Glaze and bake
1. Whisk the egg yolk with the milk; brush over each pie.
2. Bake 25-30 minutes until deep gold.
3. Rest 5 minutes; eat warm.

## Notes
- **The filling must cool fully:** Warm filling melts the pastry edges and stops them sealing. Cool flat on a tray to speed it up.
- **The roux thickener:** The 2 tbsp flour stirred in at the end is what binds the filling so it does not leak through the pastry.
- **Crimp tight:** A loose seal leaks. Fork-crimp firmly or use a fluted pastry wheel.

## Variations
- **Chicken meat pie:** Swap the mince for finely diced chicken thigh.
- **Sardine pie:** Use 2 tins of sardines (mashed) with the onion-pepper base; cheaper and lighter.
- **With egg:** Tuck a slice of hard-boiled egg into each pie before sealing.
- **Vegan:** Use lentils and mushrooms in place of the meat; replace the egg wash with milk-and-oil glaze.

## Serving
- Eat warm with a cold Coke or Malta · with a small bottle of shito · packed in a lunchbox · alongside a cup of strong tea.

## Storage
- Keeps 3 days refrigerated
- Reheat in a 180 C oven for 8 minutes; do not microwave (the pastry softens)
- Freezes 2 months baked; reheat from frozen in a 180 C oven for 20 minutes
