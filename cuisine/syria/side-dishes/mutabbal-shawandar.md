# Mutabbal Shawandar

![Mutabbal Shawandar](resources/mutabbal-shawandar.jpg)

*Syria's beetroot mutabbal: roasted beetroot blended with tahini, lemon, garlic and yogurt into a vivid magenta dip. Earthy and bright.*

**Serves:** 4 as a mezze

**Prep Time:** 10 minutes

**Cook Time:** 45 minutes (roasting beets)

## Overview
Mutabbal shawandar is the brilliant magenta beetroot dip of Syrian mezze tables, roasted beetroots blended with tahini, lemon, garlic and yogurt into something earthy and bright and unmistakable on a plate. The colour does half the work; place a bowl of mutabbal next to plain hummus and a green parsley salad on a long mezze table and you've got the visual triangle that defines the spread. Roasting concentrates the sweetness in a way boiling never can (boiling waterlogs the beets and dilutes the flavour), so don't be tempted by the shortcut. Wash three medium beetroots, wrap each in foil with a splash of water and a pinch of salt, and roast at 200°C for 45 to 60 minutes till a knife slides through easily. Cool ten minutes, then slip off the skins with paper towels (the warm skin slides off easily; raw beetroot stains, so wear an apron). Roughly chop the peeled beets into the food processor with tahini, Greek yogurt, lemon juice, garlic, ground cumin, salt and olive oil, blitz to a smooth thick dip and taste; adjust lemon and salt. Tip onto a wide plate and spread with the back of a spoon, making ridges across the surface, then swirl extra tahini over the top, drizzle with olive oil, sprinkle with sumac, scatter chopped parsley and pomegranate seeds. Serve at room temperature with warm pita or fresh vegetable batons as part of a wider mezze.

## Ingredients

- 500 g medium beetroots (about 3)
- 4 tablespoons tahini
- 2 tablespoons Greek yogurt
- 1 lemon (juice)
- 1 garlic clove
- ½ teaspoon ground cumin
- ½ teaspoon salt
- 1 tablespoon olive oil

### To finish
- 1 tablespoon tahini (to swirl)
- 1 tablespoon olive oil (to drizzle)
- 1 teaspoon sumac
- 2 tablespoons fresh parsley (chopped)
- 2 tablespoons pomegranate seeds (optional)

## Method

### Stage 1 - Roast the beetroot
1. Heat oven to 200°C (180°C fan).
1. Wash the beets (don't peel); wrap each in foil with a splash of water and a pinch of salt.
1. Roast 45-60 minutes until a knife slides through easily.
1. Open the foil; cool 10 minutes.
1. Slip off the skins with paper towels (the warm skin slides off easily).

### Stage 2 - Blend
1. Roughly chop the peeled beets.
1. Place in a food processor with tahini, yogurt, lemon juice, garlic, cumin, salt and olive oil.
1. Blitz to a smooth thick dip.
1. Taste; adjust lemon and salt.

### Stage 3 - Plate
1. Tip onto a wide plate; spread with the back of a spoon, creating ridges.
1. Swirl extra tahini over the top.
1. Drizzle with olive oil.
1. Sprinkle with sumac; scatter parsley and pomegranate seeds.

### Stage 4 - Serve
1. Eat with warm pita or fresh vegetable batons.

## Notes
- **Roast not boil:** Boiling waterlogs the beets and dilutes the flavour. Roasting concentrates the sweetness; foil-wrap protects from drying.
- **Tahini quality:** Use a Middle Eastern tahini (Lebanese, Syrian or Palestinian brands). Supermarket-aisle versions are often bitter; look for a light, runny tahini that pours.
- **Vivid colour:** Beetroot stains. Wear an apron and protect the worktop.

## Storage
- Refrigerate 3 days. The colour deepens slightly.
- Doesn't freeze well.
