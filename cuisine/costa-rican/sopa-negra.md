# Sopa Negra

![Sopa Negra](resources/sopa-negra.jpg)

*The Tica black-bean soup: long-simmered black beans blended with their broth, ladled hot over a soft-poached egg, finished with onion, coriander and a splash of Salsa Lizano.*

**Serves:** 4

**Prep Time:** 15 minutes (plus overnight soak)

**Cook Time:** 2 hours

## Overview
Sopa negra is the Costa Rican answer to a head-cold, a long lunch and a hangover, all in one bowl. The base is black beans simmered low with onion, sweet pepper, garlic, coriander and cumin for two hours, then half the beans are blended to a smooth liquid and stirred back in for body. The signature is the poached egg: cracked into a teacup, slid into the simmering soup just before serving, the white turning silken and the yolk staying soft. It comes to the table with chopped raw onion, fresh coriander and a wedge of lime, with a stack of corn tortillas alongside. Tica grandmothers say it is the cure for everything. They are probably right.

## Ingredients

- 400 g dried black beans (or 800 g cooked)
- 2 litres water (or bean cooking liquid)
- 1 large white onion, halved
- 1 red sweet pepper, halved
- 4 garlic cloves, smashed
- 1 large bunch coriander stalks (reserve leaves)
- 2 bay leaves
- 1 tsp cumin seeds
- 2 tsp salt
- 2 tbsp vegetable oil
- 1 small white onion, finely diced
- 2 garlic cloves, finely chopped
- 2 tbsp Salsa Lizano
- 4 fresh eggs (room temperature)
- Fresh coriander leaves, finely chopped white onion, lime wedges, to serve

## Method

### Stage 1 - Cook the beans
1. Soak the dried beans overnight in cold water (skip if using cooked beans).
2. Drain, place in a heavy pot with the 2 litres water, halved onion, halved pepper, smashed garlic, coriander stalks, bay leaves and cumin seeds.
3. Bring to a boil, drop to a low simmer, cover loosely and cook for 1.5 hours, until the beans are soft.
4. Lift out the spent aromatics; add the salt and cook 10 minutes more.

### Stage 2 - Build the body
1. Ladle half the beans with some broth into a blender; blend until smooth.
2. Pour the puree back into the pot with the whole beans; stir to combine. The texture should be silky with whole beans in it.

### Stage 3 - The finishing sofrito
1. Heat the oil in a small pan over medium heat.
2. Soften the finely diced onion for 4 minutes; add the chopped garlic and cook 1 minute.
3. Tip the sofrito into the soup with the Salsa Lizano; simmer for 5 minutes for the flavours to come together.

### Stage 4 - Poach the eggs in the bowl
1. Bring the soup to a gentle simmer (not a hard boil; the bubbles will tear the eggs).
2. Crack one egg into a small cup; slide it gently into the simmering soup. Repeat with the other three, spacing them apart.
3. Cover and cook for 3 minutes for a runny yolk, 4 for a set yolk.
4. Ladle into wide bowls, lifting one egg per bowl carefully with a slotted spoon.
5. Scatter raw chopped onion and coriander leaves on top; serve with lime wedges.

## Notes
- **The egg is the point:** A bowl of sopa negra without the egg is just bean soup. The yolk breaks open and enriches the broth as you eat.
- **Simmer not boil:** A hard boil will shred the egg whites. Keep the surface just trembling.
- **Blend half:** Blending half the beans is the standard Tica trick for body. Blending all of it gives you a refried-bean puree; blending none gives you a thin pot.
- **Onion on top, not in the pot at the end:** A raw onion garnish is what sets the soup apart. The crunch and bite cut through the dense bean broth.

## Variations
- **Sopa negra con chicharrón:** Top with a few crisp chicharrones in place of (or alongside) the egg.
- **Vegan sopa negra:** Skip the egg; top with diced avocado and a drizzle of olive oil.
- **Sopa negra con maíz tostado:** Scatter toasted corn kernels on top for crunch.
- **Sopa negra picante:** Slice a small piece of scotch bonnet into the sofrito for the Caribbean-coast heat level.
- **Cuban-style:** Add a splash of sherry vinegar at the end and serve with white rice on the side.

## Serving
- Serve hot in wide bowls with the poached egg whole in the centre · raw chopped onion and coriander scattered over · lime wedge on the rim · a stack of warm corn tortillas alongside

## Storage
- The bean soup base keeps 4 days refrigerated and improves overnight
- Poach the eggs fresh each time; do not store with eggs in
- Freezes 2 months (without the egg)
- Reheat gently on the stove; add water if too thick
