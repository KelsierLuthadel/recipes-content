# Arroz con Leche

![Arroz con Leche](resources/arroz-con-leche.jpg)

*Costa Rican rice pudding: long-grain rice simmered low in milk with a cinnamon stick and a strip of lemon peel, sweetened with condensed milk and finished with a dusting of ground cinnamon.*

**Serves:** 6

**Prep Time:** 5 minutes

**Cook Time:** 45 minutes

## Overview
Arroz con leche is the dessert a Tica abuela makes when there is a cup of leftover rice and a tin of condensed milk in the cupboard. The Costa Rican version is creamier and sweeter than the Spanish original: long-grain rice is simmered low in whole milk with a cinnamon stick and a strip of citrus peel for an hour, then sweetened with both sugar and a tin of condensed milk at the end so the texture stays loose and the flavour stays rich. A grating of nutmeg is sometimes added at the end, and a slug of dark rum or a splash of vanilla extract goes in at family parties. It is served cold in small bowls with a heavy dusting of ground cinnamon on top, and is one of the country's most-loved everyday puddings.

## Ingredients

- 200 g long-grain white rice (or pudding rice)
- 1 litre whole milk
- 1 cinnamon stick
- 1 strip lemon peel (no white pith)
- 1 strip orange peel (no white pith)
- 100 g caster sugar
- 1/2 tin (200 ml) sweetened condensed milk
- 1 tsp vanilla extract
- 1/2 tsp ground nutmeg
- 2 tsp ground cinnamon, for dusting
- 100 ml double cream, optional, for stirring in at the end
- 50 g raisins, optional

## Method

### Stage 1 - Soften the rice
1. Rinse the rice in cold water until it runs clear.
2. In a small pan, cover with 250 ml water and bring to a boil; simmer 5 minutes until the rice is starting to soften but is not yet cooked through.
3. Drain.

### Stage 2 - Simmer in milk
1. In a heavy pot, combine the parcooked rice, whole milk, cinnamon stick, lemon peel and orange peel.
2. Bring to a gentle simmer over medium-low heat.
3. Cook uncovered for 35 minutes, stirring every 4 minutes to stop the rice catching on the bottom, until the rice is plump and the milk has reduced and thickened to a loose-pudding consistency.

### Stage 3 - Sweeten and finish
1. Lift out the cinnamon stick and citrus peels.
2. Stir in the caster sugar; cook 2 minutes for it to dissolve.
3. Stir in the condensed milk, vanilla and nutmeg; cook 5 minutes more, stirring, until the pudding is glossy and ribbons off the spoon.
4. Off the heat, stir in the double cream (if using) and the raisins (if using).

### Stage 4 - Chill and serve
1. Pour into 6 small bowls; the pudding thickens further as it cools.
2. Chill for at least 2 hours.
3. Dust the surface with ground cinnamon just before serving.

## Notes
- **Parcook the rice in water first:** Cooking rice straight in milk takes forever and is prone to scorching. The 5-minute water parcook gives a head-start.
- **Stir often:** Milk-based puddings catch and burn on the bottom of the pan in minutes. Stir every 4 minutes with a flat-edged wooden spoon.
- **Loose now, set later:** The pudding should still be loose when you take it off the heat. It thickens significantly as it cools.
- **Citrus peel pith out:** Strip only the coloured outer layer of citrus peel. The white pith goes bitter as it cooks.

## Variations
- **Arroz con leche con ron:** Stir in 3 tbsp dark rum at the end for the family-party version.
- **Arroz con leche con coco:** Replace 250 ml of the milk with coconut milk and finish with toasted coconut flakes on top.
- **Arroz con leche con cajeta:** Drizzle dulce de leche on top before the cinnamon dusting.
- **Arroz con leche al horno:** Tip the pudding into a baking dish, dust with cinnamon-sugar and bake at 180 C for 20 minutes for a caramelised top.
- **Arroz con leche con pasas:** Soak the raisins in warm rum for 30 minutes before stirring in.

## Serving
- Serve cold in small bowls with a heavy dusting of ground cinnamon on top · a thimble of rum on the side · or warm with a curl of butter for a winter version

## Storage
- Arroz con leche keeps 4 days refrigerated, covered
- Thickens further on chilling; loosen with a splash of milk if too set
- Freezes 1 month but the texture becomes grainy on thaw
