# Frijoles Molidos

![Frijoles Molidos](resources/frijoles-molidos.jpg)

*Smooth-blended Tica black beans, stewed with onion, sweet pepper and coriander, then puréed into a glossy, spreadable paste that goes on tortillas at breakfast or sits next to the rice on a casado plate.*

**Serves:** 6

**Prep Time:** 10 minutes (plus overnight soak)

**Cook Time:** 2 hours

## Overview
Frijoles molidos translates as "ground beans", the Costa Rican name for black beans cooked low and slow with a sofrito, then blended to a glossy, spreadable paste. They differ from refried beans (which are dry-fried in fat after cooking): these stay loose, smooth and almost saucy, like a thick black-bean soup reduced down. The mix is spread on warm corn tortillas for breakfast, ladled alongside white rice on the casado plate, used as a base for gallo pinto, or spooned onto patacones as a topping. Every Tica kitchen makes them in big batches and keeps a container in the fridge: this is the foundation black-bean preparation of the country.

## Ingredients

- 500 g dried black beans, soaked overnight
- 2.5 litres water
- 1 large white onion, halved
- 1 red sweet pepper, halved and seeded
- 4 garlic cloves, smashed
- 1 large bunch coriander stalks
- 2 bay leaves
- 1 tsp cumin seeds
- 1 tbsp salt
- 3 tbsp vegetable oil
- 1 small white onion, finely diced
- 3 garlic cloves, finely chopped
- 1 tsp ground cumin
- 2 tbsp Salsa Lizano

## Method

### Stage 1 - Cook the beans
1. Drain the soaked beans; place in a heavy pot with the water, halved onion, halved sweet pepper, smashed garlic, coriander stalks, bay leaves and cumin seeds.
2. Bring to a boil, drop to a low simmer, cover loosely and cook for 1.5 hours until the beans are very soft (they should crush easily between two fingers).
3. Add the salt; cook for 10 minutes more.
4. Lift out the spent aromatics and discard.

### Stage 2 - Blend smooth
1. Working in batches, ladle the beans with their cooking liquid into a blender (cover the lid with a tea towel and start on low).
2. Blend until completely smooth; the texture should pour like a thick soup.
3. If you prefer a chunkier mash, blend two-thirds and leave one-third whole.

### Stage 3 - Stew the puree
1. Heat the oil in a wide pan over medium heat.
2. Soften the diced onion for 5 minutes; add the chopped garlic and ground cumin; cook 1 minute.
3. Add the Salsa Lizano and stir.
4. Pour in the blended beans; simmer for 15 minutes, stirring every 3 minutes, until thickened to a glossy spreadable consistency. Season.

## Notes
- **Cook the beans soft:** Underdone beans blend grainy. Cook until they crush easily between two fingers.
- **Salt at the end of bean cooking:** Adding salt at the start can toughen the bean skins. Add after 1.5 hours.
- **Blend with the liquid:** Use the bean cooking liquid in the blender, not water; that is where the flavour is.
- **Texture preference:** Smooth is the Tica restaurant standard. A semi-coarse mash with some whole beans is the home-cooking variant.

## Variations
- **Frijoles refritos:** Fry the smooth puree in 3 tbsp lard or oil until it darkens and pulls away from the pan, for the Mexican-style refried version.
- **Frijoles con chicharrón:** Stir in 100 g crisp crumbled pork chicharrón at the end for a richer paste.
- **Frijoles dulces de coco:** Cook the beans in coconut milk in place of water, for the Limón-coast Caribbean version.
- **Frijoles picantes:** Add a slice of scotch bonnet to the sofrito for a slow heat.
- **Frijoles con queso:** Top with crumbled fresh white cheese and a few coriander leaves for the table.

## Serving
- Serve warm on a casado plate next to white rice · spread on warm corn tortillas for breakfast · topped with crumbled fresh white cheese · as a base under a fried egg and gallo pinto · spooned onto patacones as a topping

## Storage
- Frijoles molidos keep 5 days refrigerated and improve overnight
- Reheat with a splash of water (they thicken in the fridge)
- Freezes 3 months in portion-size containers
