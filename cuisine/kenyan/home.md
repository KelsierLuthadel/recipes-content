# Kenyan Cuisine

![Kenyan Cuisine](resources/hero.jpg)

A food culture built on two strong axes. The Kikuyu highlands of central Kenya turn maize, beans, potato, pumpkin leaves and collards into the staple plates: ugali (a firm maize-meal mush), sukuma wiki (collards stretched through the week), githeri (the maize-and-bean pot) and mukimo (the green-flecked mash of Mount Kenya). The Swahili coast adds an Indo-Arab-African layer, cardamom-and-clove pilau, the fried doughnuts mandazi and mahamri, and coconut milk through everything. Nyama choma, the long Saturday tradition of charcoal-grilled goat, lamb or beef eaten outdoors with kachumbari and ugali, is the national social ritual. A century of Indian indenture and railway labour left chapati, samosa and bhajia on every roadside, fully naturalised. And the tea-and-chai economy in the central highlands (Kenya is among the world's largest tea exporters) means a kettle of spiced milk-tea is usually already on the boil.
