# Mbeju

![Mbeju](resources/mbeju.jpg)

*A pan-fried cassava-and-cheese pancake, eaten with breakfast mate cocido across the Paraguayan countryside. Crisp at the edges, dense and chewy at the centre, with a deep cheese flavour pulled straight from the pan.*

**Serves:** 4

**Prep Time:** 10 minutes

**Cook Time:** 20 minutes

## Overview
Mbeju (pronounced "buh-jew", a Guaraní word for cake) is the morning breakfast of Paraguay, especially in the rural departments of Cordillera and Guairá. The "batter" is not really a batter at all: cassava starch is rubbed together with lard, crumbled fresh queso paraguay and a splash of milk into a loose crumb that looks more like wet sand than dough. The crumb is pressed into a hot heavy pan with a little fat, cooked until the underside seals into a golden crust, then carefully flipped and cooked on the second side. The finished mbeju comes out crisp at the edges and chewy in the centre, with strands of melted cheese running through. It is eaten straight from the pan with a guampa of hot mate cocido, broken into pieces with the hands. Traditional cooks make it on a heavy cast-iron disc set over wood embers; the modern frying pan does the same job.

## Ingredients

- 400 g sweet cassava starch (almidón dulce; "polvilho doce" in Brazilian stores; or tapioca flour)
- 200 g queso paraguay or young feta, finely crumbled
- 80 g lard, soft (or 80 g butter)
- 1 tsp salt
- 80-100 ml whole milk, warm (as needed)
- Extra lard or butter for the pan

## Method

### Stage 1 - Make the crumb
1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the cassava starch and salt.
2. Add the soft lard and the crumbled feta.
3. Rub everything together with your fingertips, as if making pastry, until the mixture looks like coarse wet sand and the cheese is broken down through the starch.
4. Sprinkle in the warm milk a tablespoon at a time, mixing as you go. Stop when the crumb just holds together if you squeeze a fistful in your palm; it should not become a dough.

### Stage 2 - Cook the first side
1. Heat a heavy non-stick or cast-iron pan over medium heat. Smear a thin film of lard across the surface.
2. Tip half the crumb into the pan in a loose mound. Spread it evenly to a thickness of 1 cm and press lightly with the back of a spoon to compact it.
3. Lower the heat slightly. Cook 5-6 minutes without moving until the underside is set into a golden crust and lifts away cleanly when you slide a spatula under.

### Stage 3 - Flip and finish
1. Place a flat plate over the pan and turn pan and plate together so the mbeju lands on the plate cooked side up.
2. Smear another film of lard in the pan and slide the mbeju back in, raw side down.
3. Cook another 4-5 minutes until the second side is golden.
4. Slide onto a board, cut into wedges and eat hot.
5. Repeat with the remaining crumb to make a second mbeju.

## Notes
- **Crumb, not dough:** if it forms a smooth dough, you have added too much milk and the mbeju will be heavy. Stop at the wet-sand stage.
- **Heavy pan only:** a thin pan will burn the crust before the centre cooks through.
- **The flip:** practice the plate-flip on the first one; it is the only awkward step.
- **Eat hot:** mbeju goes leathery as it cools. Reheat in a dry pan, not the microwave.

## Variations
- **Mbeju de coco:** half cassava starch, half desiccated coconut; sweeter and a little nuttier.
- **Mbeju de almidón con anís:** half a teaspoon of crushed anise seed in the crumb.
- **Mbeju dulce (sweet):** add 2 tbsp sugar and skip the salt; for a dessert version.
- **Mini mbeju:** make 6-8 small mbeju instead of 2 large ones; cook each 3 minutes per side.

## Serving
Breakfast with mate cocido · afternoon snack with tereré · with butter and honey for a sweet version · broken into pieces with the hands · alongside chipa for a Paraguayan country breakfast spread.

## Storage
- Best within the hour of cooking; the texture suffers after that
- Day-old mbeju revives in a dry hot pan for 2 minutes per side
- Does not freeze well: the texture turns rubbery
- The dry crumb can be made an hour ahead and pan-cooked to order
