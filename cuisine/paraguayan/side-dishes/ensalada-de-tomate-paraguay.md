# Ensalada de Tomate y Cebolla

![Ensalada de Tomate y Cebolla](resources/ensalada-de-tomate-paraguay.jpg)

*The standing Paraguayan asado salad: ripe tomato, raw white onion, a fistful of parsley, oil and vinegar. Served in a wide shallow dish to be spooned over grilled beef and slabs of chipa guazú.*

**Serves:** 6

**Prep Time:** 15 minutes

**Cook Time:** None

## Overview
The Paraguayan tomato-and-onion salad is so ubiquitous at the asado table that it functions less as a salad than as a condiment. Sliced ripe tomatoes and thinly sliced white onion are tossed with a generous handful of chopped parsley and a sharp dressing of sunflower oil and white wine vinegar; the resulting juices pool at the bottom of the dish and become as important as the salad itself. Diners spoon the salad and its dressing over their grilled beef, mandioca and chipa guazú, where it cuts the richness of the meat. There is nothing complicated about it, but the proportions matter: more tomato than onion, plenty of acid, plenty of parsley, and the onion has to be sliced thin enough to be translucent. It is made an hour ahead so the onion softens slightly in the dressing.

## Ingredients

- 6 ripe medium tomatoes
- 1 large white onion (or a sweet red onion)
- 1 large handful flat-leaf parsley, finely chopped
- 4 tbsp sunflower oil (or a light olive oil)
- 2 tbsp white wine vinegar
- 1 tsp salt
- Black pepper
- Optional: 1 clove garlic, finely minced
- Optional: 1 tsp dried oregano

## Method

### Stage 1 - Slice the vegetables
1. Cut the tomatoes in half from top to bottom, then slice each half into 6 mm wedges. Discard any hard core.
2. Peel the onion and halve it. Slice each half as thinly as you can manage (under 2 mm; use a mandoline if you have one).
3. If the onion is sharp, soak the slices in cold water for 10 minutes, then drain and pat dry.

### Stage 2 - Dress
1. In a wide shallow serving dish, combine the tomato wedges and onion slices.
2. Sprinkle over the salt, pepper, garlic (if using) and oregano (if using).
3. Drizzle the oil and vinegar over the top.
4. Scatter the chopped parsley over everything.
5. Toss gently with two spoons just to coat. Do not crush the tomatoes.

### Stage 3 - Rest and serve
1. Let the salad stand at room temperature for at least 30 minutes (up to 2 hours) so the tomato releases its juice and the onion softens.
2. Toss again just before serving.
3. Bring to the table in the same dish; the pooled juices are for spooning.

## Notes
- **Tomato quality is everything:** ripe deep-red tomatoes in season are what make this salad work. Out of season, switch to a tin of good Italian tomatoes drained and diced.
- **Onion thinness:** thick onion slices ruin the salad. Use a mandoline if your knife skills are not razor-thin.
- **Rest time matters:** at least 30 minutes for the dressing to draw out the tomato juice.
- **Sunflower oil over olive:** Paraguayans use sunflower because it is local; light olive oil works but heavy extra-virgin overpowers the salad.

## Variations
- **Ensalada mixta:** add half a thinly sliced cucumber and a sliced green bell pepper.
- **Ensalada con palta:** add a peeled cubed avocado just before serving.
- **Ensalada con queso:** scatter 100 g crumbled feta over the dressed salad.
- **Ensalada de tomate verde:** made with green unripe tomatoes for a sharper, more acidic version.
- **With ají:** add a finely chopped fresh ají (Paraguayan chilli) for a country variant.

## Serving
- Always at the asado table · spooned over grilled beef, ribs and sausages · alongside sopa paraguaya and chipa guazú · with mandioca · with vori vori or bori bori as a fresh contrast to the soup.

## Storage
- Best fresh; eats well for 4 hours at room temperature
- Refrigerates 1 day but the tomato loses texture
- Drain off excess juice if storing overnight
- Do not freeze
