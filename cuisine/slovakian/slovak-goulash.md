# Slovak Goulash

![Slovak Goulash](resources/slovak-goulash.jpg)

*Slovak gulaš: chunks of beef shin braised slow with sweet paprika, double their weight in soft onions, caraway and marjoram, served with small flour-and-egg dumplings.*

**Serves:** 4

**Prep Time:** 20 minutes

**Cook Time:** 2 hours

## Overview
Slovak gulaš sits between Hungarian gulyás (a soup) and Austrian goulash (a stew); the Slovak version is the stew side, thick enough to hold its shape on a spoon, finished with a generous pour of paprika oil glistening at the surface. The technique is in the onion stage: chopped onions equal in weight to the meat, cooked low and slow in lard until they collapse and turn the colour of dark honey. That, plus sweet Hungarian paprika bloomed off the heat, is the dish. Caraway and marjoram tie it together, and small flour dumplings (haluštičky) or knedlíky bread dumplings finish the plate. Served with sour cream or a slice of dark bread.

## Ingredients

- 800 g beef shin or chuck, cut into 3 cm cubes
- 800 g onions, finely chopped
- 4 tbsp lard or vegetable oil
- 4 garlic cloves, finely chopped
- 3 tbsp sweet Hungarian paprika
- 1 tsp smoked paprika
- 2 tsp caraway seeds, lightly crushed
- 2 tsp dried marjoram
- 1 tbsp tomato paste
- 1 bay leaf
- 1 strip of lemon zest
- 700 ml beef stock or water
- 1 tbsp plain flour (optional, for thickening)
- Salt and black pepper

### Haluštičky (small dumplings)
- 200 g plain flour
- 1 egg
- 100 ml water
- 1 tsp salt

## Method

### Stage 1 - The onions
1. Melt the lard in a heavy pot over medium-low heat.
2. Add the chopped onions and a good pinch of salt; cover loosely and cook 25-30 minutes, stirring now and then, until soft, collapsed and golden.
3. Uncover and cook 10 more minutes to deepen the colour.

### Stage 2 - Build the stew
1. Add the garlic, crushed caraway and marjoram; cook 1 minute.
2. Take the pot off the heat (this matters); stir in both paprikas with the tomato paste so they bloom without scorching.
3. Return to heat; add the beef and stir to coat in the paprika onions.
4. Pour over the stock, drop in the bay leaf and lemon zest; bring to a simmer.

### Stage 3 - Slow braise
1. Cover the pot and braise on low heat 1 hour 30 minutes (or 2 hours for shin), until the beef is fork-tender and the sauce thick.
2. Stir every 20 minutes; add a splash of water if it runs dry.
3. For a thicker finish, whisk the flour with 3 tbsp cold water, stir in, and simmer uncovered 5 more minutes.
4. Lift out the bay and lemon zest; taste and adjust salt.

### Stage 4 - The dumplings
1. Whisk flour, egg, water and salt in a bowl to a thick batter.
2. Bring a wide pan of salted water to a rolling boil.
3. Scrape small pieces of batter (1 cm) off a wet spoon into the water; cook 3 minutes until they float and look set.
4. Drain and toss with a knob of butter.

### Stage 5 - Serve
1. Spoon the dumplings into warm bowls; ladle the gulaš over the top.
2. Eat hot with a spoon of sour cream if liked.

## Notes
- **Onions equal meat.** Slovak gulaš is heavy on onions; do not skimp. They are the sauce.
- **Bloom the paprika off heat.** Paprika scorches at high heat and goes bitter; take the pot off the stove before stirring it in.
- **Use real Hungarian paprika.** Generic ground paprika is dusty and weak; sweet Hungarian (édes) is the right pour.
- **Shin or chuck.** Shin gives the silkiest braise; chuck is a faster, easier alternative.

## Variations
- **Segedínsky guláš.** Add 400 g drained sauerkraut and 200 ml sour cream in the last 20 minutes for the Slovak-Hungarian crossover version.
- **With knedlíky.** Serve with sliced bread-dumpling (steamed yeasted bread roll, sliced) in place of haluštičky.
- **With potato.** Add 300 g cubed potato in the last 30 minutes for a one-pot stew.
- **Hunter's style.** Add 200 g sliced wild mushrooms with the onions.
- **Bryndza finish.** A small spoon of bryndza melted in the bowl, an east-Slovak touch.

## Serving
Serve in deep bowls · dumplings underneath, stew over · sour cream on top · slice of dark rye and a cold pilsner alongside.

## Storage
- Refrigerate up to 4 days; flavour improves overnight.
- Freezes 3 months without the dumplings; freeze dumplings separately.
- Reheat gently with a splash of water on the stove.
