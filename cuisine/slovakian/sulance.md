# Šúľance

![Šúľance](resources/sulance.jpg)

*Soft thumb-rolled potato-dough noodles tossed in browned butter and showered with ground poppy seed and icing sugar: the sweet kitchen-supper of Slovak family Sundays.*

**Serves:** 4

**Prep Time:** 30 minutes

**Cook Time:** 10 minutes

## Overview
Šúľance are short potato-dough fingers, the same dough family as Italian gnocchi or Czech bramborové šišky, rolled by hand into little finger shapes and boiled in salted water. The poppy seed (mak) version is the most traditional Slovak treatment: hot butter foamed in a pan, the noodles tossed through, then a heavy dusting of finely ground blue poppy seed sweetened with icing sugar. The contrast is the dish: soft yielding noodle, nutty butter, the gentle bitter-sweet crunch of poppy. Served as a dessert in some households and as a full meal in others (Slovaks accept that a sweet kitchen-supper is a meal in its own right).

## Ingredients

### Dough
- 500 g floury potatoes (Maris Piper or russet), peeled
- 250 g plain flour, plus extra for dusting
- 1 egg yolk
- 1 tsp salt

### Topping
- 100 g ground blue poppy seed (mak)
- 80 g icing sugar
- 80 g unsalted butter
- 1 tbsp warm milk (optional, for a softer poppy mix)

## Method

### Stage 1 - The dough
1. Boil the peeled potatoes in salted water 20 minutes until tender. Drain and steam-dry 5 minutes.
2. Pass through a ricer or mash smooth; spread on a tray to cool slightly (warm not hot).
3. Tip onto a floured board; add the flour, egg yolk and salt.
4. Knead lightly into a soft dough; do not overwork or the noodles go gummy.

### Stage 2 - Shape the noodles
1. Cut the dough in 6 pieces; roll each into a long rope 1.5 cm thick on a floured surface.
2. Cut the ropes into 3 cm pieces; roll each piece between your palms or under your fingers on the board into little fingers tapered at the ends.
3. Set on a floured tray; do not let them touch.

### Stage 3 - Boil
1. Bring a wide pan of salted water to a rolling boil.
2. Drop the šúľance in batches; they will sink, then rise to the surface in 2-3 minutes.
3. Lift out with a slotted spoon into a warm bowl; keep a few spoons of cooking water.

### Stage 4 - Finish
1. Melt the butter in a wide pan over medium heat; cook until foamy and just nut-brown.
2. Tip the hot drained šúľance into the butter; toss gently to coat.
3. Mix the ground poppy seed with the icing sugar (and a spoon of warm milk for a softer paste, if liked).
4. Divide noodles between bowls; shower generously with the poppy-sugar mix.
5. Eat hot.

## Notes
- **Blue poppy seed, finely ground.** Whole poppy seeds will not give the right cushy texture; buy ground (mletý mak) or grind whole seeds in a spice grinder until fine and damp.
- **Don't overknead.** Potato dough goes tough fast. Bring it together with the lightest touch.
- **Roll on a floured board.** A dusting of flour on the work surface stops sticking; do not flour the dough itself heavily, it dries it out.
- **Test a single noodle.** Drop one in first; if it falls apart, add a little more flour to the dough.

## Variations
- **Tvaroh version.** Substitute the poppy with 200 g dry curd cheese (tvaroh) mixed with sugar and melted butter.
- **Walnut version.** Substitute the poppy with finely ground walnuts and icing sugar.
- **Breadcrumb version.** Toss the noodles in butter with toasted breadcrumbs and sugar (the Czech-leaning take).
- **With plum jam.** Spoon a generous swirl of lekvár (plum jam) over the buttered noodles.
- **Savoury cousin.** Toss with crispy bacon and cabbage for a savoury supper plate.

## Serving
Serve hot in shallow bowls · extra icing sugar at the table · a spoon of plum jam if liked · a small glass of milk or a cup of strong tea alongside.

## Storage
- Eat fresh; the noodles stiffen as they cool.
- Cooked plain šúľance keep 2 days refrigerated; reheat in a buttery pan, do not microwave.
- The raw shaped dough freezes 1 month on a tray; cook from frozen, adding 1 minute to the boil.
