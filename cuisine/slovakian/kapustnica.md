# Kapustnica

![Kapustnica](resources/kapustnica.jpg)

*The thick, smoky Christmas-Eve sauerkraut soup of Slovakia: sour cabbage simmered with smoked sausage, dried mushrooms, prunes and paprika, served as the first course of the Štedrý deň feast.*

**Serves:** 6

**Prep Time:** 20 minutes

**Cook Time:** 1 hour 30 minutes

## Overview
Kapustnica is the most important soup of the Slovak year. Every household makes a pot for Christmas Eve (Štedrý deň), the centrepiece of the Slovak holy supper, and every family argues their version is the proper one. The base is sauerkraut, smoked meat, dried wild mushrooms and a generous spoon of sweet Hungarian paprika; prunes give a quiet sweetness against the sour cabbage, and a finish of sour cream lifts the colour to rust-pink. The soup builds depth as it cooks. It is thick, hearty, smoky and tart at once, and one bowl with good bread is a meal. Make it the day before; it tastes better on day two.

## Ingredients

- 800 g sauerkraut, drained but not rinsed (reserve 100 ml of the brine)
- 300 g smoked Slovak sausage (klobása) or Polish kabanos, sliced into 1 cm rounds
- 200 g smoked pork ribs or smoked pork belly, cut into chunks
- 1 large onion, finely chopped
- 3 garlic cloves, finely chopped
- 2 tbsp lard or vegetable oil
- 30 g dried wild mushrooms (porcini or mixed forest)
- 100 g pitted prunes
- 2 tbsp sweet Hungarian paprika
- 2 bay leaves
- 1 tsp caraway seeds
- 5 whole allspice berries
- 1.5 litres water or unsalted stock
- 1 tbsp plain flour (optional, for thickening)
- 200 g sour cream, to finish
- Salt and black pepper

## Method

### Stage 1 - Soak and prep
1. Cover the dried mushrooms with 250 ml warm water; soak 30 minutes. Lift out, slice, and reserve the soaking liquid (strain it).
2. If the sauerkraut is very sour, give it one rinse; otherwise drain it and keep 100 ml of the brine.

### Stage 2 - Build the base
1. Melt the lard in a large heavy pot over medium heat.
2. Add the onion; cook 6-8 minutes until soft and pale gold.
3. Add the garlic and caraway; cook 1 minute.
4. Take the pot off the heat; stir in the paprika so it blooms without scorching.

### Stage 3 - Simmer
1. Return the pot to the heat; add the smoked ribs (or belly), the sausage, sauerkraut, mushrooms with their strained soaking liquid, prunes, bay leaves and allspice.
2. Pour over the water or stock; bring to a simmer.
3. Cook gently uncovered for 1 hour, stirring now and then. The soup should be thick and the sauerkraut tender.
4. Taste; add reserved sauerkraut brine for more sourness, or a pinch of sugar if too sharp.

### Stage 4 - Finish
1. For a slightly thicker soup, whisk the flour with 4 tbsp cold water, stir in, and simmer 5 more minutes.
2. Lift out the bay leaves and allspice; shred any large meat pieces back into the pot.
3. Ladle into deep bowls; top each with a generous spoon of sour cream and let it melt in.

## Notes
- **Smoked is essential.** Plain (unsmoked) sausage will not give the right depth; use Slovak klobása, Polish kabanos, kielbasa or smoked German sausage.
- **Day two is better.** Cool the soup, refrigerate overnight, reheat gently. The flavours marry and the sourness softens.
- **Dried mushrooms not optional.** Forest mushrooms (porcini, cep) carry the earthy backbone; do not substitute fresh button mushrooms.
- **The prunes.** A small handful gives a quiet sweetness against the sauerkraut; this is traditional, not a quirk.

## Variations
- **Lean version.** Skip the smoked meat entirely; double the mushrooms and add 100 g dried beans, soaked overnight. Eat in observance of the Christmas Eve fast.
- **Bryndza top.** Crumble a spoon of bryndza into each bowl in place of sour cream, an east-Slovak style.
- **With dried plums and red wine.** Add 100 ml dry red wine with the stock for a richer take.
- **Hungarian leanings.** Add a small pinch of smoked paprika alongside the sweet for a borderlands version.
- **With dumplings.** Drop small flour dumplings (haluštičky) into the simmering soup in the last 10 minutes.

## Serving
Serve very hot in deep bowls · sour cream stirred in at the table · slice of dense rye bread or chleba alongside · a small glass of slivovica before, or a cold beer with.

## Storage
- Refrigerate up to 4 days; flavour improves on day two and three.
- Freezes 2 months; sausage texture softens slightly on thaw.
- Reheat gently on the stove; do not boil hard once sour cream is in.
