# Bryndzové halušky

![Bryndzové halušky](resources/bryndzove-halusky.jpg)

*Slovakia's national dish: small potato dumplings tossed through soft salty sheep's-milk bryndza cheese and crowned with crisp smoked bacon and its hot rendered fat.*

**Serves:** 4

**Prep Time:** 20 minutes

**Cook Time:** 25 minutes

## Overview
Bryndzové halušky is Slovakia in a bowl: small ragged potato dumplings (halušky) boiled like gnocchi, drained, then folded through soft bryndza cheese from the Liptov highlands so the cheese melts into a creamy coat. The whole thing is finished with smoked bacon (slanina) fried until crisp, and the hot fat poured over the top at the table. Bryndza is the load-bearing ingredient: a soft, white, slightly tangy sheep's-milk cheese with the texture of cream cheese and the salt of a soft feta. Mixed with sour cream it goes spoonable. The bacon and its fat are not optional; they carry the dish.

## Ingredients

### Halušky dough
- 600 g floury potatoes (Maris Piper or similar), peeled
- 200 g plain flour, plus extra for adjusting
- 1 large egg
- 1 tsp salt
- Cold water as needed (a few tbsp)

### To finish
- 250 g bryndza cheese (Slovak sheep's-milk cheese; soft feta mashed with sour cream is the substitute)
- 100 g sour cream
- 200 g smoked streaky bacon (slanina), cut into 1 cm cubes
- 1 tbsp chopped chives, optional

## Method

### Stage 1 - Make the dough
1. Grate the raw peeled potatoes finely into a bowl; do not squeeze out the liquid.
2. Add the flour, egg and salt; mix with a wooden spoon to a thick sticky batter, the texture of porridge that just holds a shape.
3. If too wet, add a spoon more flour; if too stiff, a splash of water.

### Stage 2 - Cook the halušky
1. Bring a large pan of salted water to a rolling boil.
2. Hold a halušky board (or a wide-holed grater) over the pan; spread a scoop of batter on top, then scrape pieces of dough off the edge into the water with a knife or a small palette. Aim for small ragged pieces 1-2 cm across.
3. Work in 2 or 3 batches; the dumplings rise to the surface in 2-3 minutes.
4. Scoop the cooked halušky out with a slotted spoon into a warm bowl.

### Stage 3 - Bacon and assembly
1. While the halušky cook, fry the bacon cubes in a dry pan over medium heat until crisp and the fat runs golden. Keep the fat in the pan.
2. Mash the bryndza with the sour cream in a large warm bowl until creamy and spreadable.
3. Tip the hot drained halušky into the bryndza; fold gently so the cheese coats every piece.
4. Divide into 4 bowls; spoon the hot bacon and its fat over the top; scatter chives if using.
5. Eat at once.

## Notes
- **The bryndza substitute.** Real Slovak bryndza is hard to find outside Central Europe; the closest substitute is soft feta (200 g) mashed with full-fat sour cream (50 g) to a creamy paste.
- **The halušky board.** A wooden halušky board with holes is traditional; a wide-holed box grater held over the pan does the same job. Push the dough across the holes with the back of a knife.
- **Bacon fat is the seasoning.** Do not drain the bacon; the hot rendered fat poured over each bowl is what the dish is built on.
- **Texture.** The dumplings should be tender and slightly chewy. Test one from the first batch; if rubbery, add a spoon more water to the batter.

## Variations
- **Strapačky.** Substitute the bryndza with sauerkraut (drained and warmed) for a tangy version eaten in the Czech-bordering west.
- **Halušky s kapustou.** Toss the halušky with fried cabbage, smoked sausage and caraway in place of bryndza.
- **Vegetarian.** Skip the bacon; fry a small chopped onion in butter and pour the butter-onion over the bowl instead.
- **With žinčica.** Serve a small glass of fermented sheep-milk whey (žinčica) alongside, the High Tatras shepherd tradition.

## Serving
Serve hot in warm bowls · top with extra sour cream and chopped chives · a cold pilsner alongside · or a small glass of borovička before.

## Storage
- Eat fresh; the halušky go gluey once cold.
- Leftover bryndza-sour-cream mix keeps 3 days refrigerated.
- The raw dough does not keep; cook the lot in one go.
