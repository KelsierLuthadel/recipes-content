# Vyprážaný syr

![Vyprážaný syr](resources/vyprazany-syr.jpg)

*The Slovak deep-fried cheese plate: a thick slab of Edam-style cheese breaded and fried until the crust is crisp and the centre runs molten, served with tartare sauce and chips.*

**Serves:** 2

**Prep Time:** 10 minutes (plus 1 hour chilling)

**Cook Time:** 8 minutes

## Overview
Vyprážaný syr is one of the most beloved snack-bar plates in Slovakia, sold at every roadside hostinec, ski-lodge canteen and student cafeteria across the country. A 1.5 cm slab of Edam, Eidam or a similar semi-hard melting cheese is breaded twice for a thick crust, then deep-fried until the outside is deep gold and the inside has just turned molten. The plate is finished with tartare sauce and chips, with a lemon wedge to cut through the richness. It is sometimes called the Slovak vegetarian schnitzel; in truth, it is the national late-night student dinner. The double-breading is what stops the cheese from bursting out.

## Ingredients

- 4 thick slices of Edam, Gouda or similar semi-hard melting cheese, each 1.5 cm thick (about 100 g each)
- 100 g plain flour
- 2 large eggs, beaten
- 200 g dry breadcrumbs (panko or fine white)
- 1 tsp paprika
- 1/2 tsp white pepper
- Sunflower oil for deep-frying (about 1 litre)
- Lemon wedges, to serve

## Method

### Stage 1 - Bread the cheese
1. Pat the cheese slabs dry; this stops the breadcrumbs going soggy.
2. Mix the paprika and pepper into the breadcrumbs.
3. Dredge each slab first in flour (tap off the excess), then in egg, then in breadcrumbs, pressing to coat.
4. Repeat the egg and breadcrumb steps: this double coating is essential, no shortcut.
5. Set on a tray and chill 1 hour in the fridge; this lets the crust firm up and stops the cheese leaking out.

### Stage 2 - Fry
1. Heat sunflower oil in a deep heavy pan to 180 C; the oil should be at least 4 cm deep.
2. Lower 2 breaded slabs into the oil; fry 2-3 minutes per side until deep gold and the breading is hollow-sounding when tapped.
3. Lift out with a slotted spoon onto kitchen paper; salt lightly.
4. Repeat with the rest. Eat at once: the cheese sets quickly once off the heat.

## Notes
- **Double-bread, no shortcut.** A single coat will tear; the double coat gives a wall thick enough to hold the cheese.
- **Chill before frying.** A firm cold breaded slab gives the crust time to set in the oil before the cheese melts and pushes through.
- **Use a semi-hard melting cheese.** Edam, Eidam, Gouda or a young provolone all work. Mozzarella melts too soft (it runs out); halloumi will not melt at all.
- **Oil temperature matters.** Too cold and the crust soaks oil; too hot and the breading scorches before the cheese melts. 180 C is the target.

## Variations
- **Smažený sýr.** The Czech version, identical recipe; eaten with potato salad in Czechia and chips in Slovakia.
- **Stuffed.** Tuck a slice of smoked ham inside two thinner pieces of cheese before breading for a cordon-bleu-style snack.
- **With garlic.** Rub the cheese with a crushed garlic clove before breading for a sharper note.
- **Spicy crust.** Add a teaspoon of cayenne or smoked paprika to the breadcrumb mix.
- **Air-fried.** Spray with oil and air-fry 8 minutes at 200 C for a lighter take; the texture is different but it works.

## Serving
Serve hot with tartare sauce and a lemon wedge · alongside chips and a pickled cucumber · in a soft white roll as a hand-held snack · with a cold pilsner.

## Storage
- Eat at once; the breading goes soggy fast and the cheese seizes as it cools.
- Pre-breaded raw slabs keep 24 hours in the fridge before frying; fry from cold.
- The fried version does not store; do not reheat (the breading turns leather and the cheese turns rubber).
