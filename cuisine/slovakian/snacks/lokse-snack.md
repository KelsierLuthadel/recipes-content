# Lokše snack-size

![Lokše snack-size](resources/lokse-snack.jpg)

*Small thin potato lokše brushed with hot goose fat and topped with sweet fried onion: the hand-held country snack from the autumn goose dinners of western Slovakia.*

**Serves:** 4 (makes 12 small lokše)

**Prep Time:** 25 minutes

**Cook Time:** 25 minutes

## Overview
The full-sized lokše are a flatbread side; the small ones are a snack. Cut to the palm of your hand, brushed with hot rendered goose fat and topped with sweetly caramelised onion, they are the standard country snack at the autumn goose feasts (Husacie hody) of the Slovácko and Záhorie regions, sold from market stalls and country pubs from late September through Martinmas in November. Stacked under a tea towel; eat warm with one hand. The goose fat is essential to the flavour and the tradition: this is a snack built around the rendered fat from the dripping pan of a roasting goose.

## Ingredients

### Dough
- 500 g floury potatoes (Maris Piper), peeled
- 200 g plain flour, plus extra for rolling
- 1 tsp salt

### Topping
- 4 tbsp goose fat (or duck fat as substitute)
- 2 large onions, halved and thinly sliced
- 1 tsp salt
- 1 tsp caster sugar
- A pinch of caraway seeds, lightly crushed
- A grind of black pepper

## Method

### Stage 1 - The dough
1. Boil the peeled potatoes in salted water 20 minutes until tender. Drain and steam-dry 5 minutes.
2. Mash smooth or pass through a ricer; let cool to warm.
3. Tip onto a floured board with the flour and salt.
4. Bring together with your hands into a soft dough; knead briefly. Do not overwork.

### Stage 2 - Fry the onion
1. Melt 2 tablespoons of the goose fat in a wide heavy pan over medium-low heat.
2. Add the sliced onions, salt, sugar and caraway; cook 15 minutes, stirring now and then, until soft, sweet and pale gold.
3. Add a grind of pepper; keep warm.

### Stage 3 - Roll and cook
1. Divide the dough into 12 small balls.
2. On a floured board, roll each ball into a thin round 12 cm across and 2 mm thick.
3. Heat a heavy iron pan or griddle over medium-high heat (no oil).
4. Lay a round on the dry pan; cook 60 seconds until the underside is freckled brown.
5. Flip; cook another 60 seconds. Stack the cooked lokše under a clean tea towel to keep soft.

### Stage 4 - Finish
1. Melt the remaining 2 tablespoons of goose fat in a small pan.
2. Brush each warm lokše with hot goose fat.
3. Spoon a generous heap of soft fried onion onto each; fold in half or roll.
4. Eat warm with one hand.

## Notes
- **Goose fat is the flavour.** Duck fat is the close substitute; butter and oil are a long way off the right note. Save the dripping from any roast goose or duck.
- **Cook the onion slow.** A fast brown gives crispy onion; a slow soft cook gives the sweet, jammy onion this snack needs.
- **Eat warm.** Once cold, lokše go leathery and the goose fat sets. Cook and serve in batches if you have a crowd.
- **Use day-old potato.** Boiled and cooled potato gives a less sticky dough than hot potato; you can boil the potatoes a day ahead and chill them.

## Variations
- **Sweet snack.** Smear with plum jam (lekvár) and a dust of icing sugar in place of the onion.
- **With pork crackling.** Top with shredded crispy pork crackling (oškvarky) instead of fried onion.
- **With bryndza.** Smear with bryndza and top with bacon for the sheep-cheese version.
- **With sauerkraut.** Soft warmed sauerkraut and a spoon of caraway for the sour version.
- **With goose liver.** A spoon of pâté and onion is the festival-stall luxury version.

## Serving
Serve warm folded in half · with a glass of young white wine (Burčiak in autumn) · a small slivovica before · or as the snack at the goose feast.

## Storage
- Eat the day cooked; the texture stiffens fast.
- Cooked lokše keep 1 day stacked under cling film; reheat on a dry pan 30 seconds per side.
- The fried onion keeps 4 days refrigerated; reheat gently in goose fat.
