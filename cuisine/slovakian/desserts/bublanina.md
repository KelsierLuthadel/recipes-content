# Bublanina

![Bublanina](resources/bublanina.jpg)

*The Slovak sour-cherry sponge cake of summer: a light buttery batter scattered thick with pitted cherries that sink as it bakes, leaving the surface dotted like bubbles (bublina = bubble).*

**Serves:** 8

**Prep Time:** 15 minutes

**Cook Time:** 40 minutes

## Overview
Bublanina is the Slovak (and Czech) summer cake, baked in every household when the sour cherries (višne) come into season in late June. The name comes from bublina (bubble): as the cake bakes, the cherries sink into the batter, leaving a dimpled surface that looks like a sheet of bubbles. The batter is a basic creamed-butter sponge enriched with milk, kept soft and pourable so the cherries sit on top before sinking. Vanilla and lemon zest scent the crumb; a dust of icing sugar finishes it. Sour cherries are essential to the flavour balance (the cake is sweet, the cherries are sharp); sweet cherries work but the cake reads as flat without them. Best slightly warm.

## Ingredients

- 150 g unsalted butter, soft
- 150 g caster sugar
- 3 large eggs
- 200 g plain flour
- 1.5 tsp baking powder
- A pinch of salt
- 1 tsp vanilla extract
- Zest of 1 lemon
- 100 ml whole milk
- 500 g sour cherries (fresh or frozen-and-thawed), pitted
- Icing sugar, to dust

## Method

### Stage 1 - Prep
1. Heat the oven to 180 C (160 fan).
2. Butter a 24 cm square cake tin (or a 28 by 20 cm rectangle) and line the base with parchment.
3. Pit the cherries; pat dry on kitchen paper if very wet.

### Stage 2 - The batter
1. Cream the butter and sugar in a stand mixer (or by hand) for 4-5 minutes until pale and fluffy.
2. Beat in the eggs one at a time, then the vanilla and lemon zest.
3. Whisk the flour, baking powder and salt together in a bowl.
4. Fold the flour into the butter mix in three additions, alternating with the milk, until smooth and pourable. Do not overmix.

### Stage 3 - Bake
1. Spread the batter evenly into the lined tin.
2. Scatter the cherries on top in a single thick layer; do not press them down. They will sink as the cake bakes.
3. Bake 35-40 minutes until risen, deep gold on top, and a skewer comes out clean from the cake (it will be sticky from cherry juice if it touches a cherry).

### Stage 4 - Finish
1. Cool 10 minutes in the tin, then lift onto a rack.
2. Dust generously with icing sugar.
3. Cut into squares; serve slightly warm or at room temperature.

## Notes
- **Sour cherries are the point.** Visnja or morello cherries give the right tang against the sweet sponge. Sweet eating cherries leave the cake tasting cloying.
- **Frozen cherries work.** Thaw and drain well; pat dry before scattering.
- **Do not stir cherries in.** They go on top of the batter and sink on their own; stirring them through makes the crumb soggy.
- **Lemon zest carries the flavour.** A heavy hand on the zest balances the sweetness.

## Variations
- **Plum bublanina.** Substitute pitted halved damson plums in late summer.
- **Apricot version.** Fresh apricot halves in July, cut-side up.
- **Berry mix.** Raspberries and blueberries together, the modern home version.
- **Apple-cinnamon.** Sliced eating apples and a heavy dust of cinnamon-sugar before baking; an autumn take.
- **With ground walnuts.** Stir 50 g ground walnuts into the flour for a nutty crumb.

## Serving
Serve warm or at room temperature · dusted with icing sugar · alongside a strong coffee or a glass of cold milk · whipped cream or vanilla ice cream alongside for a Sunday lunch.

## Storage
- Keeps 3 days in an airtight tin at room temperature.
- Reheat slices briefly in a low oven (140 C) for 5 minutes to refresh.
- Freezes 1 month wrapped; thaw at room temperature, then re-dust with icing sugar.
