# Žemlovka

![Žemlovka](resources/zemlovka.jpg)

*Slovak bread-and-apple pudding: stale white-bread slices soaked in cinnamon milk, layered with sweetened grated apple and raisins, baked golden, the thrift-kitchen dessert of family Sundays.*

**Serves:** 6

**Prep Time:** 20 minutes

**Cook Time:** 40 minutes

## Overview
Žemlovka is the Slovak (and Czech) way of using up stale white bread rolls (žemle, the small white buns that go hard within a day). Slices are soaked in cinnamon-vanilla milk, layered with grated tart apples, raisins and sugar, then baked until the top crisps and the inside sets to a soft sweet pudding somewhere between bread pudding and a clafoutis. It eats hot from the oven with a dust of icing sugar, or cold the next morning with coffee. Cooks call it the dessert that uses what's in the kitchen: stale bread, the last of the apples, raisins from the pantry, eggs and milk. The everyday Slovak baked sweet.

## Ingredients

- 300 g stale white bread (Slovak žemle rolls or any white loaf), sliced 1 cm thick
- 500 ml whole milk
- 3 large eggs
- 80 g caster sugar
- 1 tsp ground cinnamon
- 1 tsp vanilla extract
- 50 g unsalted butter, melted, plus more for the dish
- 800 g tart eating apples (Bramley or Granny Smith), peeled and coarsely grated
- 80 g caster sugar (for the apple)
- 80 g raisins, soaked 10 minutes in hot water (or 2 tbsp rum), drained
- 1 tsp ground cinnamon (for the apple)
- Zest of 1 lemon
- Icing sugar, to dust

## Method

### Stage 1 - Prep
1. Heat the oven to 180 C (160 fan).
2. Butter a 24 by 20 cm baking dish.
3. In a wide bowl, whisk the milk, eggs, sugar, cinnamon and vanilla.
4. Lay the bread slices in the milk mixture; let soak 5 minutes per side, turning gently.

### Stage 2 - The apple layer
1. Toss the grated apple in a bowl with the second 80 g sugar, the second 1 tsp cinnamon, the raisins and the lemon zest.

### Stage 3 - Layer
1. Lay a tight layer of soaked bread slices on the bottom of the buttered dish, breaking pieces to fit.
2. Spread half the apple mix evenly over the bread.
3. Add a second layer of soaked bread.
4. Top with the remaining apple mix.
5. Finish with a final layer of soaked bread slices, overlapping like roof tiles.
6. Pour any leftover milk mix over the top; brush with the melted butter.

### Stage 4 - Bake
1. Bake 35-40 minutes until the top is deep gold and crisp, and the centre is set when you press it.
2. Cool 10 minutes; dust generously with icing sugar.
3. Cut into squares; serve warm.

## Notes
- **Stale bread is the right bread.** Fresh bread goes mushy; day-old bread soaks up the milk and holds its shape.
- **Grate the apples, do not slice.** Grated apple releases its juice into the pudding; sliced apple stays crunchy and pulls away from the bread.
- **Soak the raisins.** Dry raisins burn at the surface; a 10-minute soak (in water or rum) plumps them and stops the scorch.
- **Eat warm.** Žemlovka is best 20 minutes out of the oven; cold the next day is a fine breakfast.

## Variations
- **With curd cheese.** Add a layer of tvaroh (curd cheese) sweetened with sugar and an egg yolk between bread and apple.
- **Pear version.** Substitute half the apple with grated firm pear in autumn.
- **Plum version.** Use halved pitted plums in place of apples in late summer.
- **With rum.** Soak the raisins in rum (not water); a more grown-up version.
- **Croissant version.** Use stale buttery croissants in place of bread for a richer pudding.

## Serving
Serve warm · dusted with icing sugar · with a spoon of sour cream, whipped cream or vanilla ice cream alongside · or cold the next morning with coffee.

## Storage
- Keeps 3 days refrigerated, covered.
- Reheat squares in a hot oven (180 C) for 8 minutes to re-crisp the top.
- Freezes 1 month; thaw overnight in the fridge before reheating.
