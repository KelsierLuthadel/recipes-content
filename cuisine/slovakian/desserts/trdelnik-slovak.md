# Trdelník

![Trdelník](resources/trdelnik-slovak.jpg)

*The Slovak spit-rolled cinnamon-sugar cake: a coiled rope of yeasted dough wrapped on a wooden trdlo, roasted over coals, then rolled in cinnamon sugar and crushed walnuts.*

**Serves:** 6 (makes 6 trdelníks)

**Prep Time:** 30 minutes (plus 1 hour proving)

**Cook Time:** 15 minutes

## Overview
Trdelník comes from the Hungarian-Slovak borderlands around the Skalica region of western Slovakia, where it has been protected since 2007 as Skalický trdelník (a Slovak EU geographical indication). The dough is a soft yeasted brioche-style, rolled into a long rope, wound around a thick wooden dowel (trdlo) and roasted slowly over hot embers (or in a hot oven) until golden. Once baked, the hollow cylinder is rolled in cinnamon sugar mixed with finely chopped walnuts, sliding off the dowel as a crisp-edged ring you tear apart by hand. Eat hot from the spit; the modern Bratislava street version is sometimes filled with ice cream or chocolate, but the traditional one is just dough, sugar and walnut.

## Ingredients

### Dough
- 500 g strong white flour
- 80 g caster sugar
- 7 g instant yeast
- 1 tsp salt
- 200 ml whole milk, warm
- 80 g unsalted butter, melted
- 2 egg yolks
- Zest of 1 lemon

### Coating
- 150 g caster sugar
- 2 tbsp ground cinnamon
- 100 g walnuts, finely chopped

### To brush
- 50 g unsalted butter, melted

## Method

### Stage 1 - Mix and prove
1. Whisk the flour, sugar, yeast and salt in a large bowl.
2. Add the warm milk, melted butter, egg yolks and lemon zest; mix to a soft sticky dough.
3. Knead 8-10 minutes (by hand or in a mixer) until smooth and elastic.
4. Cover and prove 1 hour until doubled.

### Stage 2 - Shape
1. Heat the oven to 200 C (180 fan); set a rack high.
2. Wrap 6 wooden dowels (about 25 cm long, 5 cm wide) in baking parchment; brush with melted butter.
3. Divide the dough into 6 pieces; roll each into a long rope 1 cm thick and about 70 cm long.
4. Wind each rope tightly around a dowel in a spiral, butting the coils together so they touch but do not overlap.
5. Press the seams gently so the spiral fuses as it bakes.

### Stage 3 - Bake
1. Brush each spiral with melted butter; place dowels horizontally across a roasting tin (so the trdelníks hang above the bottom).
2. Bake 12-15 minutes, turning the dowels a quarter turn every 4 minutes, until deep gold and crisp on the outside.

### Stage 4 - Coat
1. Mix the caster sugar, cinnamon and chopped walnuts on a long tray.
2. While each trdelník is still hot, brush again with melted butter, then roll across the cinnamon-sugar-walnut tray, pressing to coat.
3. Slide the trdelník off the dowel; let it cool 2 minutes on a rack so the shell crisps.

## Notes
- **The wooden dowel.** Traditional bakers use a turned wooden trdlo; a clean rolling pin or thick wooden spoon handle (wrapped in parchment) substitutes at home.
- **Wind it tight.** The coils need to touch so they fuse into a hollow tube; gaps will leave you with a spiral that falls apart.
- **Bake hot and fast.** A low oven gives you bread, not trdelník; the outside should be deep gold and the inside still soft.
- **Eat warm.** Trdelník goes stale within hours; this is a same-day pastry.

## Variations
- **Skalický trdelník.** The protected version uses walnuts in the dough as well as the coating; this is the proper Slovak original.
- **Almond version.** Substitute walnuts with finely chopped almonds.
- **Ice cream filled.** The modern Bratislava street version is filled with soft-serve vanilla (eat fast).
- **Chocolate spiral.** Brush the inside of the cooled cylinder with melted chocolate before serving.
- **Sweet-stuffed.** Smear with apricot jam and whipped cream and eat with a fork.

## Serving
Eat hot from the spit · tear into rings by hand · with strong coffee, hot mulled wine or a small glass of slivovica · at Christmas markets or Easter celebrations.

## Storage
- Eat the day baked; the outside softens overnight.
- Keep in an airtight tin 1 day; refresh briefly in a hot oven (200 C) for 3 minutes.
- Freeze tightly wrapped for 1 month; refresh from frozen at 200 C for 8 minutes.
