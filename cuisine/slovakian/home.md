# Slovakian Cuisine

![Slovakian Cuisine](resources/hero.jpg)

Slovakia is the sheep-cheese heartland of the Tatras, where bryndza (the soft, salty sheep's-milk cheese of the Liptov highlands) lands on the national dish, bryndzové halušky, alongside small potato dumplings and crisp bacon. The kitchen sat for centuries at the crossroads of Hungary, Czechia, Poland and Austria, and the cooking shows it: paprika braises from the south, dumplings and cabbage from the north, schnitzels from Vienna, and the Christmas-Eve cabbage soup (kapustnica) that ties them all together. Goose fat and lard are the cooking fats of the country tradition; potatoes, cabbage, sauerkraut, mushrooms and smoked sausage do the daily work.
