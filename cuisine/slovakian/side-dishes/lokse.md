# Lokše

![Lokše](resources/lokse.jpg)

*Thin Slovak potato flatbread cooked dry on a hot griddle, the soft warm flatbread of the country kitchens of Záhorie and the slopes of the Tatras.*

**Serves:** 4 (makes 8 flatbreads)

**Prep Time:** 20 minutes

**Cook Time:** 25 minutes

## Overview
Lokše are the potato flatbread of western Slovakia, traditional to the Záhorie region but eaten across the country, especially at family goose dinners (lokše and roast goose are the autumn pairing). Mashed potato is bound with flour, a touch of salt, and (sometimes) a knob of duck or goose fat, then rolled out thin and cooked dry on a hot iron griddle until blistered. The result is soft, flexible, vaguely chewy and pale gold, with brown freckles where it touched the heat. Eat fresh and warm: brushed with goose fat for the savoury treatment, or smeared with plum jam or poppy-sugar for the sweet. The simplest, most everyday Slovak bread.

## Ingredients

- 500 g floury potatoes (Maris Piper), peeled
- 200 g plain flour, plus extra for rolling
- 1 tsp salt
- 1 tbsp goose fat, duck fat or unsalted butter, melted (optional)

## Method

### Stage 1 - The dough
1. Boil the peeled potatoes in salted water for 20 minutes until tender. Drain and steam-dry 5 minutes.
2. Mash smooth or pass through a ricer; let cool to warm.
3. Tip onto a floured board with the flour, salt and melted fat if using.
4. Bring together with your hands into a soft dough; knead briefly until smooth. Do not overwork.

### Stage 2 - Roll
1. Divide the dough into 8 balls.
2. On a floured surface, roll each ball into a thin round 18-20 cm across and about 2 mm thick. Re-flour the board between rounds.
3. Set rolled rounds on a lightly floured tray, separated by a sheet of parchment.

### Stage 3 - Cook
1. Heat a heavy iron pan or griddle over medium-high heat (no oil).
2. Lay a round on the dry pan; cook 60-90 seconds until the underside is freckled brown and the top is dry.
3. Flip; cook another 60 seconds.
4. Stack the cooked lokše under a clean tea towel to keep soft and warm.

### Stage 4 - Finish
1. Brush each warm lokše with melted goose fat, duck fat or butter to serve savoury.
2. For sweet, dust with icing sugar or smear with plum jam.

## Notes
- **Cook dry.** Lokše are not fried; the pan should be hot and bare. Any oil and they go greasy.
- **Roll thin.** Too thick and they go biscuit-like. 2 mm is the target.
- **Keep them under a cloth.** Lokše go leathery in open air; stack under a tea towel as you cook.
- **Use them fresh.** Lokše are at their best within the hour. Reheat briefly on a dry pan if you must.

## Variations
- **With goose fat and onion.** Brush warm with goose fat and a scatter of fried onion, the autumn-goose-dinner treatment.
- **Sweet with poppy.** Dust with ground poppy seed and icing sugar (see šúľance topping).
- **Sweet with plum jam.** Spread with lekvár, fold in half, eat warm.
- **With bryndza.** Smear with bryndza and crisp bacon, the sheep-cheese take.
- **As wraps.** Wrap around roast goose or pork at a Slovak feast in place of bread.

## Serving
Brush warm with goose fat or butter · alongside roast goose, duck or pork · with bryndza or plum jam · as a hand-held supper with smoked sausage.

## Storage
- Eat warm; the texture stiffens within hours.
- Stack with parchment between; refrigerate 2 days, reheat on a dry pan 30 seconds per side.
- Freeze cooked lokše between parchment sheets for 1 month; reheat from frozen on a hot dry pan.
