# Zemiakové placky

![Zemiakové placky](resources/zemiakove-placky.jpg)

*Crisp Slovak potato pancakes seasoned with garlic, marjoram and caraway, fried golden in lard, the side-plate (and roadside snack) of the country kitchens.*

**Serves:** 4 (makes 12 pancakes)

**Prep Time:** 15 minutes

**Cook Time:** 20 minutes

## Overview
Zemiakové placky are the Slovak version of the Central European grated-potato pancake (the Polish placki, the Czech bramboráky), shallow-fried golden and crisp at the edges. The Slovak signature is the seasoning: a heavy spoon of garlic crushed into the batter with a generous pinch of dried marjoram and caraway, the flavours that mark Slovak kitchen cooking. They are eaten alongside a stew or goulash, served with sour cream and chives as a light supper, or sold from roadside hostincas as a hand-held snack. The trick is fast frying in hot fat: the outside crisps, the inside stays tender.

## Ingredients

- 1 kg floury potatoes (Maris Piper)
- 1 small onion, finely grated
- 4 garlic cloves, crushed to a paste
- 2 eggs
- 60 g plain flour
- 1 tsp salt
- 1/2 tsp black pepper
- 1 tbsp dried marjoram
- 1 tsp caraway seeds, lightly crushed
- 100 g lard or 100 ml sunflower oil, for frying

## Method

### Stage 1 - Grate and squeeze
1. Peel the potatoes and grate them on the coarse side of a box grater into a large bowl.
2. Working in handfuls, squeeze the grated potato hard in a clean tea towel over a bowl to remove the liquid. Reserve the potato; let the liquid stand 5 minutes so the starch sinks.
3. Pour off the clear liquid and scrape the white starch from the bottom of the bowl back into the potato.

### Stage 2 - Mix the batter
1. Add the grated onion, garlic, eggs, flour, salt, pepper, marjoram and caraway to the potato.
2. Mix with a spoon to a thick, sticky batter. Make it just before frying; the batter discolours fast.

### Stage 3 - Fry
1. Heat lard in a wide heavy pan over medium-high heat until shimmering (1 cm deep).
2. Drop heaped tablespoons of batter into the pan; press flat with the back of a spoon to 1 cm thick rounds about 8 cm across.
3. Fry 2-3 minutes per side until deep gold and crisp at the edges.
4. Lift onto kitchen paper to drain; salt lightly.
5. Repeat with the remaining batter; keep warm in a low oven if needed.

## Notes
- **Squeeze hard.** Wet potato gives a soggy pancake. The dry-then-add-starch-back trick is what gives the crisp shell.
- **Heavy garlic.** A full spoonful of crushed garlic is the Slovak signature; do not cut back.
- **Marjoram is essential.** This is the herb that flavours half of Slovak kitchen cooking; do not substitute oregano (the flavour is too sharp).
- **Eat fresh.** Placky soften within minutes; serve straight from the pan.

## Variations
- **With bryndza.** Smear with bryndza and top with fried bacon, a sit-down plate.
- **Carpathian style.** Top with goulash or a stew for a hearty supper plate (the Polish/Slovak style).
- **Sweet version.** Skip the garlic and savoury herbs; serve with stewed apples and cinnamon-sugar.
- **With sour cream and chives.** The classic Slovak finish for a light meal.
- **With smoked salmon.** A modern restaurant pairing in Bratislava.

## Serving
Serve hot · sour cream and chopped chives on top · alongside slovak goulash · with a vinegar-and-dill cucumber salad · or as a hand-held snack with a smear of garlic mayo.

## Storage
- Eat fresh; the texture goes leathery once cold.
- Reheat in a hot oven (200 C) for 5 minutes to re-crisp; do not microwave.
- The raw batter does not keep; cook all in one go.
