# Roti Parcel

![Roti Parcel](resources/roti-parcel.jpg)

*A soft Indo-Fijian flour roti wrapped tightly around a centre of spiced curried potato, folded into a square envelope and eaten on the move. The roadside lunch of every Fijian working day.*

**Serves:** 4

**Prep Time:** 30 minutes (plus 30 minutes dough rest)

**Cook Time:** 25 minutes

## Overview
The roti parcel is the Indo-Fijian working lunch: a soft flour roti, freshly cooked on a tawa, wrapped tightly around a spoonful of curried potato or pumpkin and folded into a square envelope held shut with the heat of the roti itself. Sold wrapped in a sheet of greaseproof paper at every Fijian bus stand, market and roadside stall; eaten with one hand while the other holds the cup of tea or the bottle of soft drink. The skill is in the roti: pliable enough to fold without splitting, thin enough to give the filling room, sturdy enough to survive the half-hour bus ride to wherever lunch ends up being eaten. The filling is a quick-cooked spiced potato bound with onion and chilli; one parcel is a full lunch.

## Ingredients

### For the roti
- 400 g plain flour (or 200 g plain + 200 g atta flour for the more traditional mix)
- 1 tsp salt
- 2 tbsp vegetable oil
- 240 ml warm water

### For the filling
- 600 g potatoes, peeled and cut into 1 cm dice
- 3 tbsp vegetable oil
- 1 tsp cumin seeds
- 1 small onion, finely chopped
- 2 cloves garlic, finely minced
- 1 thumb of ginger, finely grated
- 2 green chillies, finely chopped
- 1 tbsp Fijian or Madras curry powder
- 1/2 tsp turmeric
- 1/2 tsp ground coriander
- 1 tsp salt
- A handful of fresh coriander, chopped
- Juice of half a lemon

### For cooking the roti
- A little oil or ghee for brushing

## Method

### Stage 1 - Make the dough
1. Combine flour and salt in a bowl.
2. Rub in the oil with your fingertips.
3. Add the warm water gradually; knead 8 minutes to a soft pliable dough.
4. Cover; rest 30 minutes.

### Stage 2 - Cook the filling
1. Boil the diced potatoes 8 minutes until just tender; drain.
2. Heat the oil in a wide pan over medium heat; add cumin seeds; sizzle 20 seconds.
3. Add onion; cook 5 minutes until soft.
4. Add garlic, ginger and green chillies; cook 1 minute.
5. Stir in the curry powder, turmeric and ground coriander; 30 seconds.
6. Add the potatoes; mash lightly with the back of a spoon, leaving plenty of texture.
7. Salt to taste; finish with lemon juice and fresh coriander.
8. Cool slightly before filling.

### Stage 3 - Roll and cook the roti
1. Divide the rested dough into 4 balls.
2. Roll each on a lightly floured surface into a thin round about 24 cm across.
3. Heat a dry tawa or heavy flat pan over medium-high heat.
4. Lay a roti on the hot pan; cook 1 minute until the surface bubbles slightly.
5. Flip; brush the cooked side with oil; cook 1 minute more.
6. Flip again; brush; cook 30 seconds.
7. The roti should be flexible and lightly speckled brown, not crisp.

### Stage 4 - Fill and fold
1. Lay a hot roti on a board.
2. Spoon a quarter of the filling into the centre.
3. Fold the bottom of the roti up over the filling.
4. Fold both sides in.
5. Fold the top down to seal; the heat of the roti holds it closed.
6. Wrap in greaseproof paper to hold.
7. Repeat with the rest.

## Notes
- **Roll thin but not transparent:** about the thickness of a five-cent coin. Thicker and the parcel will not fold cleanly; thinner and the filling pushes through.
- **Fold while the roti is hot:** the residual heat sets the fold. A cooled roti folds stiffly and cracks at the edges.
- **Filling should be just warm, not hot:** boiling filling steams the parcel and softens the roti too quickly.

## Variations
- **Pumpkin filling:** swap potato for the same weight of butternut pumpkin diced and cooked the same way.
- **Egg roti parcel:** spread a thin omelette on the cooked roti before adding the potato filling; the village teenager favourite.
- **Chicken parcel:** replace the potato with shredded leftover curry chicken.
- **Coconut chutney inside:** smear a tablespoon of green coconut-coriander chutney across the roti before filling.
- **Spicy version:** double the green chillies and add a teaspoon of crushed chilli flakes to the filling.

## Serving
Serve wrapped in greaseproof paper · with a small pot of green chutney on the side · with sweet milky tea or a cold soft drink · at a Fijian bus stand · as a packed lunch · for a working day on the move.

## Storage
- Eat within 4 hours of making; the roti softens as the filling steams.
- The filling refrigerates 3 days; make fresh roti each time.
- Do not freeze the assembled parcel; the texture suffers.
