# Belgian Cuisine

![Belgian Cuisine](resources/hero.jpg)

Belgium's kitchen is a quiet world heavyweight: carbonnade flamande (beef braised slowly in dark Trappist ale, with a slice of mustard-spread bread melted into the sauce), moules-frites (a stockpot of mussels in white-wine-and-celery broth, served with twice-fried Belgian frites and mayonnaise), waterzooi (the Ghent chicken-and-cream stew thickened with egg yolks), and vol-au-vent à la Belge (puff-pastry shells holding chicken and mushroom in a velouté sauce). Liège waffles with sugar pearls, speculoos biscuits, and Trappist beer from Westvleteren, Chimay and Westmalle define the sweet-and-drinks side. Belgian frites (not "French" fries) are double-fried in beef tallow and served from a paper cone with a choice of twenty sauces; the country eats more chocolate per head than any other.
