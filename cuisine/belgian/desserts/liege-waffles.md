# Liège Waffles

![Liège Waffles](resources/liege-waffles.jpg)

*Belgium's denser, sweeter waffle: brioche-style yeasted dough studded with pearl sugar that caramelises into hard sticky pockets in the iron. Eat hot from a street cart.*

**Serves:** 8 waffles

**Prep Time:** 25 minutes (plus 90 minutes for the dough to rise)

**Cook Time:** 4 minutes per waffle

## Overview
Liège waffles are the dense, sweet, chewy waffles that Belgians eat from street carts. Entirely different from the Brussels waffle (which is light, rectangular, eaten on a plate with cream and strawberries). The dough is brioche-style and yeasted, enriched with butter and eggs, soft and slightly sticky, risen twice. The pearl sugar is the traditional Liège technique and the texture-defining ingredient; coarse white sugar pearls about 4 mm across kneaded in at the very end so they survive the bake intact. As the waffle cooks, the pearls partially melt at the edges, caramelise and form sticky crunchy nuggets throughout the waffle, which is the entire point. Granulated sugar dissolves into the dough and gives a sweet but textureless waffle that misses the dish completely. The iron has deep oval pockets (not square Brussels-style), and the traditional cook is brief and hot enough that the outer caramel goes hard and amber. Eat hot and plain from the cart, no cream or syrup.

## Ingredients

### The dough
- 500 g strong white bread flour (12-14% protein)
- 60 g caster sugar
- 1 teaspoon salt
- 7 g instant dry yeast (1 sachet)
- 3 large eggs (about 180 g), at room temperature
- 100 ml whole milk, lukewarm
- 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
- 200 g unsalted butter, soft (room temperature, but not melted), in 2 cm cubes

### The crucial ingredient
- 200 g pearl sugar (Lars or Belgian sucre perlé, 4 mm pearls)

### For cooking
- 1 tablespoon neutral oil or melted butter (to brush the iron once before the first waffle)

### To serve
- Hot, plain. The Liège purist eats them as is.
- Optional Brussels-style additions: a dusting of icing sugar, a dollop of whipped cream, sliced strawberries, hot Belgian chocolate sauce.

## Method

### Stage 1 - Mix the dough
1. In the bowl of a stand mixer (with dough hook), combine the flour, caster sugar, salt and yeast.
2. Whisk the eggs, lukewarm milk and vanilla together in a jug.
3. Pour the wet into the dry; mix on low speed 3 minutes till a rough dough forms.

### Stage 2 - Add the butter slowly
1. With the mixer running on medium-low, add the soft butter one cube at a time.
2. Wait for each cube to incorporate before adding the next.
3. Once all the butter is in, mix on medium 8-10 minutes till the dough is smooth, glossy and pulls away from the sides of the bowl.
4. The dough will be soft and slightly sticky, that's correct.

### Stage 3 - First rise
1. Scrape the dough into a lightly oiled bowl.
2. Cover with cling film.
3. Let rise at warm room temperature 60-75 minutes till doubled.

### Stage 4 - Knead in the pearl sugar
1. Turn the risen dough onto a lightly floured surface.
2. Press it flat into a rough rectangle.
3. Scatter half the pearl sugar over the top.
4. Fold the dough over itself and press flat again.
5. Scatter the other half of the pearl sugar; fold again.
6. Knead briefly (1 minute) till the pearl sugar is evenly distributed but the pearls are intact.

### Stage 5 - Portion and second rest
1. Divide the dough into 8 equal portions (about 130 g each).
2. Shape each into a ball.
3. Place on a tray lined with parchment, leaving space between.
4. Cover loosely with cling film or a damp tea towel.
5. Let rest 25-30 minutes, this is a short rise, not a full proof.

### Stage 6 - Cook the waffles
1. Heat a Belgian waffle iron to its highest setting (around 200°C).
2. Brush the iron lightly with oil or melted butter for the first waffle only.
3. Place one ball of dough in the centre of the iron; close gently.
4. Cook 3-4 minutes till the iron stops steaming and the outside is deep golden.
5. The pearl sugar will have partially melted and caramelised into amber pockets, some may run out onto the iron; that's fine.
6. Use wooden tongs or a fork to lift the waffle out. Don't use metal directly on the caramel, it sticks.

### Stage 7 - Serve immediately
1. Let the waffle rest on a wire rack 1 minute, the caramel sets hard and the inside finishes steaming.
2. Eat hot, in the hand, like street food.
3. If serving multiple, hold in a low oven (90°C) for up to 30 minutes; they soften but stay edible.

## Notes
- **Pearl sugar is everything:** if you can't get pearl sugar, you cannot make a Liège waffle. Don't try with granulated; the texture is the entire point.
- **Don't crowd the iron:** the dough expands; one ball per pocket.
- **Caramel will leak:** some of the pearl sugar will melt and run out onto the iron. Wipe the iron with a damp cloth between waffles or it builds up.
- **Hot iron, short cook:** 3-4 minutes is enough at 200°C. Longer and the outside caramel burns dark and bitter.
- **No syrup, no whipped cream:** the Liège purist eats them plain. The sugar IS the topping.
- **Don't refrigerate the dough overnight:** the butter firms and the pearls can shed sugar; better to cook the same day.

## Variations
**Liège waffles with chocolate chips:** add 100 g of small dark chocolate chips when kneading in the pearl sugar, modern Belgian street-cart variant.
**Brown sugar Liège waffles:** swap half the caster sugar in the dough for soft dark brown sugar, deeper, more molasses-like.
**Liège waffles with cinnamon:** add 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon to the dough, winter variant.
**Brussels-style waffle (the other Belgian waffle):** a yeasted batter (not a dough), cooked thin and crisp in a rectangular iron, served with cream and strawberries, completely different recipe, see Brussels waffle in a separate file.
**Stroopwafel-Belgian crossover:** roll the cooked Liège waffle while still warm into a cone shape filled with whipped cream, the Brussels carnival variant.
**Vegan Liège waffles:** swap eggs for 2 tablespoons of flax-and-water, butter for vegan block butter, milk for oat milk, texture slightly different but still good.

## Serving
At a Belgian street cart in Liège, Brussels or Antwerp (the traditional setting) · at a Belgian Christmas market · at a Belgian carnival or fair · as the Belgian street-food snack between meals · at home for a Saturday-morning brunch · paired with a Belgian hot chocolate.

## Storage
- Best eaten within 2 hours of cooking. After that the caramel softens and the dough goes slightly stale.
- Cooled waffles can be refrigerated 2 days in an airtight container; refresh in a 180°C oven for 4 minutes.
- Freezes 2 months individually wrapped; reheat from frozen in a 200°C oven for 6 minutes.
- The dough (before the pearl sugar is added) refrigerates 24 hours; knead in the sugar just before cooking.
- Don't microwave the waffles, the caramel sugar melts and goes sticky-soft.
