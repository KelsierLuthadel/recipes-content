# Three Sisters Stew

![Three Sisters Stew](resources/three-sisters-stew.jpg)

*The Haudenosaunee winter one-pot built on corn, beans and squash: butternut, hominy and pinto beans simmered slow with sage and a touch of maple.*

**Serves:** 4

**Prep Time:** 20 minutes

**Cook Time:** 50 minutes

## Overview
The Haudenosaunee winter one-pot built on the indigenous trinity of corn, beans and squash, planted together in the field where each helps the others grow (corn for the bean to climb, beans to fix nitrogen for the squash and corn, squash leaves to shade the soil for moisture) and combined on the plate to form a complete protein. Butternut, hominy and pinto beans simmered slow with sage and a touch of maple, finished with toasted pumpkin seeds. You soften diced onion in sunflower oil over medium for eight minutes, add garlic for a minute. Push the onion to the side, add more oil and brown cubed butternut squash for five minutes over medium-high stirring to colour the edges. Bloom dried sage, ground sumac (or hold the lemon for the end), smoked paprika and pepper for 30 seconds, tip in chopped tomatoes and vegetable stock. Bring to a boil, drop to a simmer partial lid for 30 minutes till the squash is tender and starting to break down. Stir in drained pinto beans and tinned hominy (the nixtamalised kind, not sweetcorn; the fragrance is part of the dish), cook 12 to 15 minutes uncovered to thicken and integrate. Off the heat stir in maple syrup, taste and balance with salt (and lemon if you skipped the sumac). Ladle into bowls, scatter toasted pumpkin seeds and fresh sage, drizzle olive oil across. Cornbread or frybread is the natural side.

## Ingredients

- 3 tablespoons sunflower oil
- 1 onion (large, diced)
- 4 garlic cloves (sliced)
- 1 butternut squash (medium, peeled, seeded, cut into 2 ½ cm cubes - about 800 g prepared)
- 1 teaspoon dried sage
- 1 teaspoon ground sumac (or substitute 2 teaspoons lemon juice at the end)
- 1 teaspoon smoked paprika (modern; substitutes for hardwood-cooking smokiness)
- ½ teaspoon black pepper
- 1 (400 g) tin chopped tomatoes
- 800 ml vegetable stock
- 1 (400 g) tin pinto beans (drained and rinsed)
- 1 (400 g) tin hominy (drained, or substitute sweetcorn)
- 1 tablespoon maple syrup
- 1 ½ teaspoons salt (to taste)

### Garnish
- 3 tablespoons toasted pumpkin seeds (pepitas)
- 2 tablespoons fresh sage (chopped)
- 1 tablespoon olive oil (drizzle)

## Method

### Stage 1 - Soften the onion
1. Heat 2 tablespoons of oil in a wide heavy pot over medium heat.
1. Add onion; cook 8 minutes until soft and gold.
1. Add garlic; cook 1 minute.

### Stage 2 - Squash
1. Push the onion to the side; add the remaining oil.
1. Add the squash; brown over medium-high heat 5 minutes, stirring occasionally, to colour the edges.

### Stage 3 - Spice
1. Stir in dried sage, sumac, smoked paprika and pepper; cook 30 seconds.

### Stage 4 - Simmer
1. Tip in the chopped tomatoes and stock.
1. Bring to a boil; reduce to a simmer; partial lid.
1. Cook 30 minutes until the squash is tender and starting to break down.

### Stage 5 - Add the beans and corn
1. Stir in the pinto beans and hominy.
1. Cook 12-15 minutes uncovered to thicken slightly and integrate.

### Stage 6 - Finish
1. Off heat; stir in the maple syrup.
1. Taste; balance with salt.
1. If you skipped the sumac, add a squeeze of lemon now.

### Stage 7 - Serve
1. Ladle into bowls; scatter toasted pumpkin seeds and fresh sage; drizzle with olive oil.
1. Cornbread or frybread is the natural side.

## Notes
- **Three sisters companion planting:** Corn provides the stalk for beans to climb; beans fix nitrogen for the corn and squash; squash's broad leaves shade the soil to keep moisture and discourage weeds. The three together feed each other in the field - and on the plate they form a complete protein.
- **Hominy vs sweetcorn:** Hominy (nixtamalised maize) is the authentic choice and has a fragrance sweetcorn lacks. Tinned hominy is widely available in international shops or online.
- **Sage and squash:** Sage is the herb of this dish. Don't substitute thyme or rosemary; the flavour shifts entirely.

## Storage
- Refrigerate 4 days; reheats beautifully - the stew thickens overnight and the flavour deepens.
- Freezes 2 months.
