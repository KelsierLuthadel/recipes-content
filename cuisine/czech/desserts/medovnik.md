# Medovník (Czech Honey Cake)

![Medovník](resources/medovnik.jpg)

*Czech honey layer cake: thin honey-and-spice biscuits stacked with a sour cream-condensed milk filling, refrigerated for a day so the layers soften into a single rich slab. Cuts into elegant wedges; tastes of honey and warm spice.*

**Serves:** 12

**Prep Time:** 1 hour (plus 24 hours setting)

**Cook Time:** 30 minutes (across 6 thin layers)

## Overview
Medovník (medovník is masculine; the same cake is called medovik in Russian and Slovak - the recipe is shared across the post-Soviet space and has Czech and Slovak versions too) is the honey layer cake that turns up at every Czech bakery and most Sunday tables. The construction is patient: six thin honey-and-spice biscuit layers, each baked individually, then stacked with a tangy filling made from sour cream beaten with condensed milk (sometimes plus butter and walnuts). After 24 hours in the fridge, the dry biscuit layers absorb moisture from the filling and the whole cake softens into a tender slab. The flavour is gentle - honey-sweet, slightly spiced (cinnamon, ginger), with the tangy filling cutting the richness. Topped with crushed cake offcuts and a dust of icing sugar.

## Ingredients

### Honey biscuit layers (makes 6)
- 200 g unsalted butter
- 250 g caster sugar
- 200 g clear honey
- 3 large eggs
- 1 tbsp bicarbonate of soda
- 1 tbsp white vinegar
- 600-700 g plain flour (start with less; the dough should be soft but not sticky)
- 2 tsp ground cinnamon
- 1 tsp ground ginger
- 0.5 tsp ground cloves
- A pinch of salt

### Filling
- 600 ml sour cream (full-fat)
- 1 tin (397 g) sweetened condensed milk
- 200 g unsalted butter, softened
- 1 tsp vanilla extract
- 100 g walnuts, finely chopped (optional)

### To finish
- Icing sugar for dusting
- Reserved cake crumbs (from trimming)
- Optional: 2 tbsp ground walnuts

## Method

### Stage 1 - Dough base
1. In a heavy saucepan over low heat, combine the butter, sugar and honey.
2. Stir until melted and uniform; remove from heat.
3. Cool to lukewarm (about 5 minutes).

### Stage 2 - Add eggs and the bicarbonate
1. Whisk in the eggs one at a time.
2. In a small bowl, mix the bicarbonate of soda with the vinegar; stir into the mixture (the foam adds lightness).

### Stage 3 - Combine the dry
1. Sift the flour with the cinnamon, ginger, cloves and salt.
2. Add to the wet mixture gradually, mixing into a soft pliable dough (start with 600g flour; add more if too sticky to roll).
3. Don't overwork; just bring it together.
4. Cover; rest at room temperature 30 minutes.

### Stage 4 - Divide and roll
1. Preheat the oven to 180°C.
2. Divide the dough into 6 equal pieces.
3. Roll each piece between two sheets of greaseproof paper into a thin round about 23 cm across and 3 mm thick.
4. Trim each round to a neat 22 cm circle (saving the trimmings for the topping).

### Stage 5 - Bake the layers
1. Bake each layer on its lower greaseproof sheet on a baking tray 6-8 minutes until deeply golden.
2. The layers should be firm and dry, not soft.
3. Slide carefully onto a wire rack to cool fully.
4. Bake all 6 layers - if you have multiple trays you can bake 2 at a time, rotating; if one oven, one at a time.
5. Bake the offcut trimmings on a separate tray 8 minutes; cool; crush into crumbs for the topping.

### Stage 6 - Make the filling
1. In a large bowl, beat the softened butter with an electric mixer until pale and fluffy.
2. Slowly add the condensed milk, beating until smooth.
3. Beat in the sour cream and vanilla.
4. Fold in the chopped walnuts (if using).
5. The filling should be thick but spreadable.

### Stage 7 - Assemble
1. Place the first cooled biscuit layer on a serving plate or cake board.
2. Spread a generous 5 mm layer of filling over.
3. Top with the next layer; press lightly; spread filling.
4. Continue until all 6 layers are stacked.
5. The top layer gets a generous coat of filling, plus filling spread around the sides.

### Stage 8 - Finish
1. Press the reserved cake crumbs over the top and sides, sticking them into the filling.
2. Dust with icing sugar.
3. Refrigerate at least 24 hours (and up to 3 days).
4. The biscuit layers absorb the filling moisture and soften from crisp to tender.

### Stage 9 - Serve
1. Cut into thin wedges (the cake is rich).
2. Serve cold from the fridge with strong black coffee.

## Notes
- **Don't skip the 24 hours:** Eating medovník fresh from assembly is a different (worse) cake - the layers are still crisp, the filling is still loose. After a day in the fridge the cake transforms.
- **Sour cream + condensed milk:** The tang-sweet combination is what makes the filling Czech rather than generic buttercream. Don't substitute.
- **Roll thin:** Each layer should be 3 mm thick. Thick layers don't soften in the fridge time and the cake stays hard.

## Serving
A small wedge of medovník with a cup of strong black coffee is the Czech afternoon tradition. Cut thin slices - this is a rich cake.

## Storage
- Refrigerates 5 days, covered.
- Doesn't freeze well (the filling separates on thawing).
- Improves between days 1-3; starts to soften too far around day 4.
