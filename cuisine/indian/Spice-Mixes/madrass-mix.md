# Madras Spice Mix

![Madras Spice Mix](resources/madrass-mix.jpg)

*The Madras spice mix: a darker, hotter Tamil-inspired blend of mustard seed, fenugreek, curry leaves and dried chilli.*

**Prep Time:** 5 minutes

**Makes:** about 25 g

## Overview
The British curry-house madras blend: base curry powder bolstered with extra chilli powder, paprika and additional cumin and coriander to push the spice profile darker and hotter. The mix supports the madras curry, named for the South Indian city (now Chennai) but a thoroughly Anglo-Indian invention developed in mid-20th-century curry-house kitchens. The dish sits a step below vindaloo on the heat ladder, with chilli and pepper doing the work rather than vinegar. The base curry powder gives the broad foundational profile; the added chilli, paprika and warm spices push the blend toward the South-Indian-flavoured darker direction. Toasting the dried spices briefly in a dry pan before blending lifts the aromatics; the mix keeps two or three months in a sealed jar and is worth refreshing every season.

## Ingredients
- 2 tsp [Base Curry Powder](base-curry-powder.md)
- 1 tsp chilli powder
- 1 tsp paprika
- ½ tsp cumin
- ½ tsp coriander

## Method
1. Mix all spices thoroughly.
2. Store airtight.

## Notes
- Medium heat, well-rounded flavour.
- Adjust chilli powder to increase or decrease heat level.

## Serving
Use 2-3 tsp per Madras curry.

## Storage
- Store in an airtight container in a cool, dry place for up to 6 months
- Keep away from direct sunlight and moisture
