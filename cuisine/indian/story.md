# Indian

*A subcontinent in a spice tin: the world's deepest cuisine, layered over four thousand years and twenty-eight states. Start here.*

## A short history

Indian cooking didn't begin with curry. The Harappan kitchens of 2500 BCE already used cumin, coriander, ginger and turmeric — four of the five spices that still anchor the cuisine today. What followed was layer after layer of incoming influence: Persian rice technique that became biryani, Portuguese chillies that arrived through Goa and went on to define Vindaloo, Mughal almond-and-cream cookery that produced korma and pasanda, British-era army kitchens that codified the curry powders we now associate with restaurant menus.

What stayed constant across millennia is the principle of *masala*: a spice mix bloomed in hot fat at the start of cooking, used as the structural flavour-bed every dish is built on. Get the masala right and the rest of the recipe is just sequencing.

## Regional variations

India isn't one cuisine — it's at least six.

- **North Indian** — the cooking most non-Indians meet first. Wheat breads (naan, paratha, roti), dairy-rich gravies (butter chicken, paneer dishes), tandoor-grilled meats, garam masala as the closing flourish. Punjabi and Mughlai traditions sit here.
- **South Indian** — rice replaces wheat. Coconut, curry leaves, mustard seed, tamarind define the palate. Dosa, idli, sambar, rasam. Vegetarian by default in many homes. Quicker, lighter, brighter than the north.
- **Bengali** — eastern India and Bangladesh. Fish and mustard oil at the centre. Sweets reach a virtuoso peak: rasgulla, sandesh, mishti doi. Panch phoran (five-spice) is the signature temper.
- **Gujarati** — north-western, predominantly vegetarian. Slightly-sweet edge to most savoury dishes. Thalis are the everyday format. Dhokla, undhiyu, kadhi.
- **Goan** — Portuguese-influenced. Vinegar plays where elsewhere it doesn't. Vindaloo, sorpotel, fish caldine.
- **Hyderabadi / Andhra** — the Deccan plateau. Famously fiery; biryani is the signature. The mutton biryani of Hyderabad is one cuisine's defining dish.

A dish that means one thing in Delhi can mean another in Chennai. Treat each regional tradition as its own cuisine and the variety stops feeling overwhelming.

## Signature ingredients

The kitchen runs on a small, repeating set of building blocks:

- **[Garam Masala](../../base-ingredients/curry-powder/garam-masala.md)** — the closing-stage warm-spice blend
- **[Panch Poran (Indian Five Spice)](../../base-ingredients/spices/panch-poran.md)** — Bengali tempering mix
- **[Madras Mix](../../base-ingredients/curry-powder/madras-mix.md)**, **[Vindaloo Mix](../../base-ingredients/curry-powder/vindaloo-mix.md)**, **[Korma Mix](../../base-ingredients/curry-powder/korma-mix.md)** — restaurant-style heat tiers
- **Cumin, coriander, turmeric, mustard seed** — the daily four
- **Ginger-garlic paste** — the aromatic base every curry starts with
- **Ghee** — clarified butter; the canonical cooking fat for richer dishes
- **Coconut milk** — south Indian gravies
- **Tamarind, kokum, dried mango (amchur)** — the sourness toolkit
- **Curry leaves** — fragrance; fried in oil at the start of southern dishes

Stock these and most of the catalogue's recipes open up.

## Learn it in seven recipes

If you want to build the muscle for Indian cooking without trying to absorb the whole thing at once, work through these in order. Each one teaches one technique that compounds into the next.

1. [Tarka Dal](dal-makhani.md) — a lentil stew with a tempered-spice finish. Teaches the *tarka* (spice-blooming) move every other recipe leans on.
2. [Garam Masala](../../base-ingredients/curry-powder/garam-masala.md) — make the mix once and you stop reaching for a jar; reading the catalogue gets easier.
3. [Butter Chicken](butter-chicken.md) — the famous one. Teaches yoghurt marination, the two-pan-method (sauce + protein cooked separately, combined late), and how a tomato-cream gravy is built.
4. [Lamb Karahi](lamb-karahi.md) — a fast braise in a wok-style pan. Teaches gravy reduction over high heat, and what happens when you actually let the oil split out.
5. [Saag Aloo](side-dishes/saag-aloo.md) — a spinach-and-potato side. Teaches the southern-style mustard-seed-and-curry-leaf temper, plus how to wilt greens without making them grey.
6. [Bhindi Masala](side-dishes/bhindi-masala.md) — okra braised in onion-tomato gravy. Teaches the slime-management technique okra needs (high heat, dry, no stirring until set).
7. [Hyderabadi Mutton Biryani](hyderabadi-mutton-biryani.md) — the boss level. Layered rice, marinated mutton, dum cooking. Once you've made this you can make any rice dish.

After those seven, the rest of the catalogue is variations on themes you already know.
