# Antiguan Saltfish Cakes

![Antiguan Saltfish Cakes](resources/antigua-saltfish-cakes.jpg)

*Crisp golden fritters of flaked salt cod beaten through a thyme-scented batter and dropped into hot oil, the after-school snack and rum-shop chaser of every Antiguan village.*

**Serves:** 24 fritters

**Prep Time:** 15 minutes (plus soak)

**Cook Time:** 20 minutes

## Overview
Saltfish cakes (called accras or fritters across the wider Caribbean, but always saltfish cakes in Antigua) are the small fried bites that turn up at every island moment, packed lunches, beach picnics, Sunday cricket matches, weddings and rum-shop counters. The batter is loose: flour, baking powder, water, scallion, thyme, a fierce mince of Scotch bonnet, and folded through it the flaked desalted cod. Dropped from a spoon into hot oil, the fritters puff up round, brown deeply outside and stay fluffy inside. The trick is the heat of the oil and the looseness of the batter; a stiffer mix gives a denser doughier cake. Eaten hot with a pinch of pepper sauce.

## Ingredients

- 200 g boneless salt cod, soaked overnight
- 250 g plain flour
- 2 tsp baking powder
- 1/2 tsp salt
- 4 scallions, finely chopped
- 1 tbsp fresh thyme leaves
- 1 small Scotch bonnet, deseeded and finely minced
- 1 small onion, finely chopped
- 250 ml cold water
- Black pepper
- Vegetable oil for deep-frying

## Method

### Stage 1 - Desalt and flake
1. Drain the soaked cod. Cover with fresh water in a pan and bring to the boil. Simmer 8 minutes, drain, taste; repeat if still very salty.
2. Cool the cod, then flake fine with your fingers, discarding any bones.

### Stage 2 - Mix the batter
1. Combine the flour, baking powder, salt and a few grinds of black pepper in a large bowl.
2. Stir in the scallion, thyme, Scotch bonnet and onion.
3. Add the flaked cod and toss to coat in the flour.
4. Pour in the cold water gradually, mixing to a thick batter that drops slowly from a spoon. Adjust with a splash more water if too stiff.
5. Rest 10 minutes.

### Stage 3 - Fry
1. Heat 5 cm of oil in a heavy pot to 175 C.
2. Drop heaped teaspoons of batter into the oil, 5 or 6 at a time.
3. Fry 3-4 minutes, turning once, until deep golden and puffed.
4. Lift onto kitchen paper. Salt lightly if needed.
5. Repeat with the rest of the batter.

## Notes
- **The soak:** Overnight in cold water with one change brings the cod down to the right saltiness. The boil after is a safety net for a salty batch.
- **The Scotch bonnet:** Be careful with the seeds; deseed and wear a glove if you are sensitive. Half a small pepper is the local heat.
- **The oil:** 175 C is the right temperature. Drop a small bit of batter in to test, it should sizzle and float within 3 seconds.

## Variations
- **Pumpkin saltfish cakes:** Mash in 100 g cooked pumpkin for a sweeter, softer cake.
- **Vegetable fritters:** Replace the saltfish with 200 g grated callaloo or okra for a vegetarian version.
- **Conch fritters:** Substitute minced conch (pre-tenderised) for the saltfish.
- **Cornmeal fritters:** Replace 80 g of the flour with fine yellow cornmeal for a crunchier crust.

## Serving
Serve hot with pepper sauce · with a wedge of lime · alongside cold beer or rum punch · packed into lunchboxes with avocado.

## Storage
- Best eaten fresh
- Refrigerate up to 2 days, reheat in a hot oven or air fryer to re-crisp
- Freezes 2 months cooked, reheat from frozen in a hot oven
