# Antiguan

*The kitchen of two small Caribbean islands. Fungie (cornmeal mash) as the universal starch, the slow-stewed pepperpot as the national dish, and the breadfruit-and-saltfish breakfast that's the same on Antigua as it is on Barbuda.*

## A short history

Antiguan and Barbudan cooking is the cuisine of a small island nation in the Eastern Caribbean. The British colonised in 1632 and established sugar plantations; the enslaved West African population brought the cooking that survives — okra, callaloo, breadfruit, the saltfish tradition (Atlantic salt cod traded into the Caribbean as cheap protein for plantation labour), the slow-cooked one-pot.

The 1981 independence brought codification of national dishes — fungie and pepperpot was declared the national dish; the breadfruit, brought from Tahiti in 1793 to feed enslaved workers (Captain Bligh's mission, the Bounty's second voyage), became the universal Antiguan starch alongside cornmeal.

The 20th-century tourism economy (Antigua claims to have 365 beaches — "one for every day") brought hotel-restaurant fusion food, but the home kitchen continues unchanged: pepperpot at Sunday lunch, saltfish for breakfast, doucouna (sweet cornmeal dumplings) for tea.

## Regional variations

The two-island nation is small and the cuisine is broadly homogenous:

- **Antigua (the main island)**: codified mainstream. Pepperpot, fungie, conch.
- **Barbuda (the smaller island)**: more lobster and conch, less developed restaurant scene.

## Signature ingredients

- **Cornmeal**: in fungie, in doucouna.
- **Saltfish (salt cod)**: with breadfruit, in chop-up.
- **Breadfruit**: roasted whole or boiled, the universal starch.
- **Pumpkin (squash)**: in pepperpot, in soups.
- **Okra**: in pepperpot, in fungie.
- **Scotch bonnet pepper**: the heat.
- **Bay leaf, thyme, scallion**: the herb base.

## Learn it in five recipes

1. **[Fungie and Pepperpot](/recipes/cuisine/antiguan/fungie-and-pepperpot)**: cornmeal-and-okra mash with slow-stewed beef, salt meat and pumpkin in a thick brown gravy. The national dish.
2. **[Saltfish and Chop-Up](/recipes/cuisine/antiguan/saltfish-and-chop-up)**: salt cod sautéed with tomato, onion and pepper, served with chop-up (mashed eggplant, okra and spinach). The Antiguan breakfast.
3. **[Goat Water](/recipes/cuisine/antiguan/goat-water)**: spicy goat stew with potato and dumpling, the carnival weekend dish.
4. **[Conch and Dumplings](/recipes/cuisine/antiguan/conch-and-dumplings)**: pressure-cooked conch in a brown gravy with flour dumplings.
5. **[Doucouna](/recipes/cuisine/antiguan/doucouna)**: sweet cornmeal dumplings with raisins, coconut and cinnamon, wrapped in banana leaves and boiled. The traditional Antiguan dessert.

## Where to go next

Pair everything with Wadadli beer or with English Harbour rum (Antigua's premium rum brand). For Carnival (late July - early August), goat water is the universal late-night dish; eat after the j'ouvert dawn parade with hot pepper sauce, a beer, and bare feet.
