# Estonian

*The kitchen of the northernmost Baltic state. Black bread, salt-cured herring, the barley-and-potato porridge (mulgipuder) of the southern uplands, blood sausage at Christmas, and the cold-soup tradition of the Russian east.*

## A short history

Estonian cooking sits between Finnish (across the Gulf), Latvian (south), Russian (east) and German-Lutheran (the Baltic German nobility) traditions. The basic shelf — rye and barley bread, potato (introduced in the 18th century and now central), pork, herring, Baltic sprats, cabbage, dairy, forest mushrooms and berries — has been the working kitchen for centuries.

The 700-year German-influenced upper class kept Estonian food rural-peasant in character for most of its history. Independence (1918), the Soviet annexation (1940–1991), and the post-1991 recovery have each shaped the modern kitchen in different ways. The Tallinn restaurant scene has emerged as one of the most exciting in Northern Europe in the 2010s-2020s, drawing on the country's foraging tradition.

## Regional variations

- **Tallinn and the north**: codified mainstream. Verivorst, rosolje.
- **Southern (Tartu, Mulgi)**: the mulgipuder country. Barley-and-potato porridge tradition.
- **Western islands (Saaremaa, Hiiumaa)**: fish-heavy. Smoked sprats, herring traditions.
- **Eastern (Setomaa)**: closer to Russian. More fermented cabbage, more cold soup.

## Signature ingredients

- **Black bread (rukkileib)**: dense, sour rye, the cultural icon.
- **Herring**: pickled, salt-cured, smoked.
- **Potato**: in nearly every dish.
- **Pork**: cured, smoked, fresh. Sült (head cheese) and verivorst (blood sausage) at Christmas.
- **Buckwheat (tatar)**: porridge, in dumplings.
- **Sour cream (hapukoor)**: dressing everything.
- **Dill, caraway**: the herb pair.

## Learn it in five recipes

1. **[Verivorst](/recipes/cuisine/estonian/verivorst)**: blood sausage with barley, marjoram and onion, baked till the casing crisps. The Christmas Eve standard, served with lingonberry jam and sauerkraut.
2. **[Mulgipuder](/recipes/cuisine/estonian/mulgipuder)**: pearl-barley-and-mashed-potato porridge with crispy bacon and onion. The southern Estonian (Mulgi region) signature.
3. **[Rosolje](/recipes/cuisine/estonian/rosolje)**: pink potato-and-beetroot salad with herring, gherkin, apple, hard-boiled egg and sour cream. The Christmas table classic.
4. **[Sült](/recipes/cuisine/estonian/sult)**: jellied pork head cheese with bay leaf, peppercorns and vinegar.
5. **[Hapukapsasupp](/recipes/cuisine/estonian/hapukapsasupp)**: sour-cabbage soup with pork ribs and barley. The winter weeknight bowl.

## Where to go next

Pair everything with Saku or A. Le Coq beer (Estonia's two oldest breweries) or with Vana Tallinn (a sweet rum-based liqueur with vanilla and citrus). End with kama (mixed grain flour with kefir or yoghurt and berries — the traditional Estonian breakfast/dessert).
