# Kama Balls

![Kama Balls](resources/kama-balls.jpg)

*The Estonian protein-bar of the pre-protein-bar era: roasted-grain flour mixed with kefir and honey, rolled into balls, dusted with extra kama.*

**Serves:** Makes 12 balls

**Prep Time:** 15 minutes

**Chilling Time:** 30 minutes

## Overview
Kama is the most Estonian ingredient there is: a finely milled flour made from a pre-roasted blend of rye, barley, oats and peas, with a deeply nutty, almost coffee-like flavour. It is the breakfast of every Estonian summer cottage (stirred into kefir or buttermilk with a drizzle of honey and a handful of berries), and it is the base for a small no-bake snack ball that has become a children's lunchbox standard. Kama is combined with thick kefir, soft butter, honey and vanilla, rolled into balls, and dusted with a little extra dry kama. The result is dense, slightly sour, naturally sweet and full of slow-release energy. No oven, no flour, no fuss.

## Ingredients

- 150 g kama flour (roasted four-grain mix), plus 2 tbsp for rolling
- 100 ml thick kefir (or buttermilk or thick yoghurt)
- 50 g unsalted butter, very soft (or 50 ml neutral oil)
- 60 g honey
- 1 tsp vanilla extract
- A pinch of salt
- Optional: 50 g raisins, chopped dried cranberries, or chopped nuts

## Method

### Stage 1 - Combine
1. In a wide bowl beat the soft butter, honey, vanilla and salt with a wooden spoon until smooth.
2. Add the kefir; stir to combine (it will look slightly curdled, this is fine).
3. Add the kama 50 g at a time, stirring after each addition, until a stiff, slightly sticky paste forms.
4. Fold in raisins, dried cranberries or chopped nuts if using.

### Stage 2 - Rest
1. Cover and chill 20-30 minutes. The kama drinks up the moisture and the paste firms into a pliable, dough-like consistency.

### Stage 3 - Roll
1. Place 2 tbsp dry kama in a small dish.
2. Scoop the dough by the heaped tablespoon (about 30 g each) and roll between dry palms into smooth balls.
3. Roll each ball through the dry kama to dust the outside.

### Stage 4 - Chill and serve
1. Place on a plate and chill another 20 minutes if you want a firmer bite; serve straight away if you want soft.

## Notes
- **Real kama:** Estonian kama is sold ready-mixed (the classic blend is roasted rye, barley, oats and dried peas, milled fine). It is available from Baltic delis and online; once you have it, you keep it for months in a dry cupboard.
- **Substitute:** No kama? A mix of fine roasted barley flour, oatmeal toasted in a dry pan, and a touch of buckwheat flour gets close in flavour. Pure oats or pure barley work but are less complex.
- **Honey balance:** Adjust to taste. Estonian kama is meant to taste of the grain first and the sweetener second.
- **Variations:** Cocoa powder (1 tbsp) and a splash more honey turn these into a chocolate-kama ball; lemon zest brightens them for summer.

## Serving
Serve cold straight from the fridge as a snack, breakfast on the go, or with a cup of black coffee. Pack into lunchboxes wrapped in baking paper.

## Storage
- Keeps 5 days refrigerated in a sealed container
- Freezes 1 month; thaw 30 minutes at room temperature
- The texture firms over the first day in the fridge as the kama continues to absorb moisture
