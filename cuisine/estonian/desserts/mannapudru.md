# Mannapudru

![Mannapudru](resources/mannapudru.jpg)

*The Estonian nursery dessert: silky semolina pudding cooked in milk with vanilla, served warm under a tart spoonful of rhubarb compote.*

**Serves:** 4

**Prep Time:** 10 minutes

**Cook Time:** 25 minutes

## Overview
Mannapudru is Estonian semolina pudding, the warm milky dessert that every Estonian child grew up eating after Sunday lunch and that every Estonian adult still craves on a cold Tuesday in February. Fine semolina is whisked into hot milk with sugar and vanilla and cooked for ten minutes until thick and silky, then poured into bowls. The classic finish is a small puddle of stewed fruit on top: rhubarb in spring, strawberry or raspberry in summer, lingonberry or cranberry in winter. The cold-sharp fruit cuts through the warm cream of the semolina and lifts what could be a heavy dessert into something light and clean.

## Ingredients

### For the semolina
- 1 litre whole milk
- 100 g fine semolina
- 60 g caster sugar
- 1/2 tsp salt
- 1 tsp vanilla extract (or seeds from 1/2 pod)
- 30 g unsalted butter

### For the rhubarb compote
- 400 g rhubarb, trimmed and cut into 2 cm lengths
- 80 g caster sugar
- 1 tbsp water
- 1/2 tsp vanilla extract
- A small strip of lemon zest

### To finish
- A little cold milk or single cream
- A spoon of butter

## Method

### Stage 1 - Make the rhubarb compote
1. Combine the rhubarb, sugar, water and lemon zest in a small pan.
2. Bring to a gentle simmer; cook 6-8 minutes until the rhubarb has collapsed but still holds some shape.
3. Stir in the vanilla; remove from the heat and set aside (warm, not hot).

### Stage 2 - Heat the milk
1. Pour the milk into a wide heavy pan; add the sugar, salt and vanilla.
2. Heat over medium heat, stirring, until the milk is steaming and just shy of a boil.

### Stage 3 - Whisk in the semolina
1. With one hand whisking the milk in a steady swirl, pour the semolina in slowly with the other. Whisk constantly to prevent lumps.
2. Drop the heat to low.
3. Cook, stirring with a wooden spoon, for 8-10 minutes. The pudding will thicken steadily; finished, it coats the back of the spoon like custard and leaves a clean line when you draw a finger across.
4. Stir in the butter off the heat.

### Stage 4 - Serve
1. Ladle into warm bowls.
2. Spoon rhubarb compote into the centre of each.
3. Drizzle a little cold milk or cream around the edge; a small knob of butter on top is the Estonian flourish.

## Notes
- **Fine semolina:** Manna is the very fine wheat semolina sold for milk puddings. Coarse semolina (couscous-fine) gives a grittier pudding.
- **Pour slow, whisk fast:** Lumps form when semolina goes in all at once. A thin stream and a constant whisk give a perfectly smooth pudding.
- **Texture:** Mannapudru should be thick but pourable. It firms up further as it cools; thin with hot milk if needed.
- **Fruit variations:** Frozen mixed berries simmered with a little sugar work year-round; lingonberry or cranberry jam loosened with hot water also works as a quick topping.

## Serving
Serve warm in shallow bowls with a generous spoon of rhubarb compote in the centre and a splash of cold milk around the edge. A cup of strong black tea on the side.

## Storage
- The semolina keeps 2 days refrigerated; reheat in a pan with milk to loosen
- The rhubarb compote keeps 1 week refrigerated and freezes 3 months
- Cold mannapudru sets to a firm pudding that is also good sliced and pan-fried in butter the next day
