# Afghan

*The kitchen at the crossroads of Central Asia, Persia and the subcontinent. Long-grain rice with caramelised carrot and raisin, lamb cooked slow, the kebab tradition, and a love of yoghurt that runs through nearly every plate.*

## A short history

Afghan cooking sits where the Silk Road crossed itself. From Persia came the rice tradition (palaw), the saffron-and-dried-fruit profile, and dolma. From the Indian subcontinent came the spice rack (cumin, coriander, cardamom) and the use of yoghurt. From Central Asia came the dumpling tradition (mantu) and the lamb-on-the-fire kebab.

The signature dish — Kabuli pulao (long-grain rice with lamb, caramelised carrots and raisins) — was apparently codified in the 19th-century Kabul royal kitchens. It's now Afghanistan's unofficial national dish and the gateway to the entire cuisine.

Decades of war and emigration have created a robust diaspora kitchen (Iran, Pakistan, Germany, the US, the UK) that's preserved the cuisine while it became harder to access internally. The Afghan restaurant tradition in California (the Helmand brothers' chain), Hamburg and London has carried the cooking worldwide.

## Regional variations

- **Kabul and the centre**: the codified mainstream. Kabuli pulao, mantu, the urban refinement.
- **Pashtun belt (south and east)**: closer to Pakistani Pashtun. Chapli kebab, lamb karahi, more chilli.
- **Hazara (central highlands)**: distinct dumpling tradition (mantu, ashak), more turnip and squash.
- **North (Mazar-e-Sharif)**: closer to Central Asian. Plov-style rice, more lamb.

## Signature ingredients

- **Long-grain basmati**: the rice of choice. Aged, fragrant.
- **Lamb**: the meat.
- **Yoghurt (mast)**: dressing for many dishes; the base of chaka (strained yoghurt sauce).
- **Coriander and dill**: the herb pair.
- **Cumin and cardamom**: the warm spices.
- **Dried fruit (raisins, apricots)**: in pulao, in stews.
- **Pomegranate molasses**: souring agent in some stews.

## Learn it in four recipes

1. **[Kabuli Pulao](/recipes/cuisine/afghanistan/kabuli-pulao)**: long-grain rice with chunks of slow-cooked lamb, topped with caramelised carrots, raisins and pistachio slivers. The national dish.
2. **[Mantu](/recipes/cuisine/afghanistan/mantu)**: small steamed dumplings of spiced minced lamb and onion, served with tomato-lentil sauce and garlic yoghurt.
3. **[Chapli Kebab](/recipes/cuisine/afghanistan/chapli-kebab)**: flat patties of spiced minced lamb with pomegranate seeds, shallow-fried.
4. **[Lamb Karahi (Afghan)](/recipes/cuisine/afghanistan/lamb-karahi-afghani)**: lamb wok-fried with tomato, ginger, green chilli and coriander. The Afghan answer to the Pakistani karahi.

## Where to go next

Pair everything with chai (black tea, sometimes cardamom-spiced) and naan-e-Afghani (long oval flatbread). Build an Afghan table around the pulao centrepiece with mantu or chapli on the side, a yoghurt sauce, and a fresh herb-and-radish platter.
