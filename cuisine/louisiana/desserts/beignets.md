# Beignets

![Beignets](resources/beignets.jpg)

*New Orleans' iconic powdered doughnut: a yeasted dough cut into squares, deep-fried till the squares puff and turn golden, then absolutely smothered in powdered sugar. The Café du Monde classic; the dish you eat with a chicory café au lait in the French Quarter.*

**Serves:** Makes 24 beignets

**Prep Time:** 30 minutes (plus 2 hours dough rise)

**Cook Time:** 15 minutes

## Overview
Beignets are New Orleans' iconic Creole doughnut and the canonical breakfast/snack at the world-famous Café du Monde in the French Quarter (open 24 hours since 1862): a soft yeasted dough enriched with milk, butter, sugar and egg, rolled flat, cut into squares (not rings; the square is the signature), then deep-fried till they puff golden brown. Absolutely smothered in icing sugar (the proper Café du Monde method is to drop them in a paper bag with a cup of icing sugar and shake hard). Served with a steaming chicory café au lait.

## Ingredients

### Dough
- 1 sachet (7 g) instant yeast
- 250 ml warm milk
- 80 g caster sugar
- 1 large egg (beaten)
- 60 g melted butter
- 1 teaspoon fine sea salt
- 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
- 500 g plain flour

### Frying
- Vegetable oil for deep-frying (about 1.5 litres)

### Coating
- 300 g icing sugar (you'll want a lot)

### To serve
- Café au lait with chicory (the Café du Monde signature)

## Method

### Stage 1 - Activate yeast
1. In bowl, combine warm milk, sugar, yeast.
2. Rest 10 min till foamy.

### Stage 2 - Make dough
1. Add beaten egg, melted butter, salt, vanilla.
2. Add flour gradually; mix to a soft dough.
3. Knead 8 min till smooth.

### Stage 3 - First rise
1. Cover; rise 1.5 hours till doubled.

### Stage 4 - Roll and cut
1. Punch down; roll out on floured surface to 1cm thick.
2. Cut into 6cm squares with sharp knife.

### Stage 5 - Heat oil
1. Heat oil to 175°C (350°F) in deep pan.

### Stage 6 - Fry
1. Fry beignets 2-3 at a time, 60-90 sec per side, till puffed and golden.
2. They should puff dramatically.
3. Drain briefly.

### Stage 7 - Smother in sugar
1. Place icing sugar in a large paper bag (or wide bowl).
2. Drop in hot beignets while still warm.
3. Shake or toss vigorously.
4. Pile on plates with extra sugar on top.

### Stage 8 - Serve immediately
1. With café au lait.

## Notes
- **Yeasted dough:** essential.
- **Cut squares:** not rings.
- **Absolutely smother in sugar:** more is more.
- **Eat immediately while warm:** soften quickly.

## Variations
**Savoury (less canonical):** skip sugar; dust with grated cheese.
**With apple filling:** brush with apple compote before dusting.
**Smaller bite-size:** cut into 3cm squares.

## Serving
With chicory café au lait. Sunday breakfast; late-night French Quarter snack.

## Storage
- Best immediately.
- Dough keeps refrigerated 24 hours.
- Cooked beignets go stale within hours; don't store.
