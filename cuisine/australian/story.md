# Australian

*The kitchen of the BBQ, the Sunday lamb roast, the meat pie, and the cosmopolitan multicultural food capital of the Asia-Pacific. British colonial roots crossed with Asian and Mediterranean immigration, plus a growing Indigenous (bush food) revival.*

## A short history

Australian cooking sits on three roots. The Indigenous (Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander) tradition is the oldest — 60,000+ years of bush foods (kangaroo, wallaby, fish from rivers and coasts, wattleseed, lemon myrtle, finger lime, native fruits and tubers). The British colonisation from 1788 layered on lamb (the export industry), beef (the world's biggest beef-exporting country), the Sunday roast tradition, tea, and meat pies as the everyday lunch.

The post-WWII immigration waves transformed Australian food. Italian (Sydney's Leichhardt and Melbourne's Carlton), Greek (Melbourne has the largest Greek population outside Greece — second largest Greek city in the world), Lebanese, Vietnamese, Chinese, Thai, Japanese, Korean, Indian, Sri Lankan, Sudanese, Ethiopian — every wave added its kitchen. The result is one of the most multicultural food scenes in the world, with the Sydney-Melbourne fine-dining axis among the world's most exciting.

The Bushfood/Native Australian movement (since the 1980s, accelerating in the 2010s) has brought Indigenous ingredients onto the mainstream menu. Restaurants like Attica (Melbourne) and Saint Peter (Sydney) lead the charge.

The Australian BBQ (the "barbie") is the social institution — sausages, lamb chops, prawns over a portable gas or charcoal grill, cold beer, the everyday weekend ritual.

## Regional variations

- **Sydney (NSW)**: the cosmopolitan kitchen. Asian-fusion strongest, the seafood tradition, the harbour-side dining.
- **Melbourne (Victoria)**: Italian-Greek-Lebanese-Vietnamese. Coffee capital (the world's best espresso, by many measures). The brunch institution.
- **Tasmania**: cool-climate produce. Salmon, oysters, apples, the world's best dairy.
- **South Australia (Adelaide, Barossa)**: wine country. The German-Lutheran heritage (Adelaide Hills), Mediterranean influences.
- **Queensland**: tropical fruit, prawns, the Pacific seafood, the "Sunshine State" outdoor lifestyle.
- **Western Australia (Perth)**: Indian Ocean seafood, the most isolated big city, distinctive Asian fusion.
- **Northern Territory**: Indigenous bush food strongest, crocodile, barramundi, the outback cooking.

## Signature ingredients

- **Lamb**: Australian lamb is the export benchmark.
- **Beef**: from the Northern cattle stations and the South.
- **Barramundi**: the iconic native fish.
- **Vegemite**: yeast-extract spread on buttered toast. The cultural icon (love it or hate it).
- **Pavlova**: meringue with cream and fresh fruit (claimed by Australia and New Zealand; the rivalry is permanent).
- **Macadamia nuts**: native to Australia.
- **Native ingredients**: wattleseed, lemon myrtle, finger lime, bush tomato, mountain pepper, kakadu plum.

## Learn it in six recipes

1. **[Aussie Burger](/recipes/cuisine/australian/aussie-burger)**: beef patty with the works — bacon, fried egg, beetroot (the Australian touch), pineapple, lettuce, tomato, cheese.
2. **[Beef Meat Pie](/recipes/cuisine/australian/beef-meat-pie)**: a beef-and-gravy pie in flaky pastry. The everyday Australian lunch, sold at the bakery on every corner, eaten with tomato sauce (ketchup).
3. **[Roast Lamb with Rosemary](/recipes/cuisine/australian/roast-lamb-rosemary)**: Sunday lamb roast with rosemary and garlic, served with mint sauce and roast vegetables.
4. **[Pan-Fried Barramundi](/recipes/cuisine/australian/pan-fried-barramundi)**: Australia's native fish pan-fried with lemon, butter and herbs.
5. **[Charcoal Chicken Skewers](/recipes/cuisine/australian/charcoal-chicken-skewers)**: marinated chicken on skewers — the BBQ staple, with Lebanese-Australian seasonings.
6. **[Lamb Mint Chilli Burger](/recipes/cuisine/australian/lamb-mint-chilli-burger)**: lamb burger with mint, chilli and yoghurt — the Aussie-Mediterranean fusion.

## Where to go next

Pair everything with Australian wine (Penfolds Shiraz, Margaret River Cabernet, Yarra Valley Pinot Noir) or with VB, XXXX or Coopers beer. End with a pavlova or with lamingtons (sponge cubes dipped in chocolate icing and rolled in desiccated coconut).
