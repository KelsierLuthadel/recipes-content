# Peruvian Cuisine

![Peruvian Cuisine](resources/hero.jpg)

Peru's plate is a three-region geography in one country: the Pacific coast (cebicherias serving ceviche, the national dish - raw white fish "cooked" in leche de tigre, the bracing citrus-and-chilli marinade), the Andean highlands (lomo saltado bringing Chinese-Peruvian Chifa fusion to the everyday plate; aji de gallina creamy chicken; causa rellena layered cold yellow-potato cakes; anticuchos beef-heart skewers from Lima street carts), and the Amazon jungle (juanes rice-and-chicken bundles in bijao leaves; paiche the giant Amazon fish). Yellow aji amarillo paste and red rocoto chillies drive the seasoning; cilantro and lime brighten everything; the Andean potato culture (over 4,000 native potato varieties) underpins it all. Pisco sour is the national cocktail; chicha morada (purple-corn drink with cinnamon and pineapple) the traditional non-alcoholic table drink; emoliente the street-corner herbal brew sold from carts at dawn.
