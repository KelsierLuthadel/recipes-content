# Lutenica (Pepper-Tomato Relish)

![Lutenica](resources/lutenica.jpg)

*North Macedonia's other pepper relish: a thick blended relish of roasted red peppers, tomatoes, garlic and chilli - distinct from ajvar by including tomato. Spread on bread; spooned alongside grilled meat; the slightly sharper sibling of ajvar.*

**Serves:** Makes 600 g

**Prep Time:** 20 minutes

**Cook Time:** 1 hour

## Overview
Lutenica (the name suggests "spicy" from "luto" - hot in Bulgarian/Macedonian) is the slightly sharper, slightly more tomato-forward sister of ajvar. The construction: roasted red peppers (same as ajvar) combined with cooked tomatoes, garlic, sugar, vinegar, salt and a generous pinch of chilli; reduced till thick. Where ajvar is smoky and aubergine-creamy, lutenica is brighter, more acidic, more tomato-driven. Both are canonical Macedonian condiments; many households make both. Three details: ROASTED PEPPERS (the depth), COOKED TOMATOES (the brightness), and SHARPER ACID BALANCE (more vinegar than ajvar).

## Ingredients
- 1 kg red bell peppers (or capia)
- 800 g ripe tomatoes (or 2 tins chopped tomatoes)
- 6 garlic cloves (chopped)
- 100 ml sunflower oil
- 3 tablespoons white wine vinegar
- 2 tablespoons caster sugar
- 1 teaspoon hot paprika
- 1 teaspoon fine sea salt
- ½ teaspoon black pepper

## Method
1. Roast peppers at 220°C for 30 minutes till skins blacken. Peel; deseed.
2. Cook tomatoes in a pan 10 minutes till broken down.
3. Pulse peppers and tomatoes in a food processor to a coarse purée.
4. Heat oil in a heavy pan; cook garlic 1 minute.
5. Add pepper-tomato mix; reduce heat to LOW.
6. Cook 60 minutes till thick and the oil rises.
7. Stir in vinegar, sugar, salt, paprika, black pepper.
8. Pack into sterilised jars.

## Notes
- **More acidic than ajvar:** bigger vinegar hit.
- **Smoother than ajvar:** the tomatoes break down completely.
- **Eat with bread:** the canonical Balkan way.

## Variations
**Hot lutenica:** double the chilli.
**With aubergine:** ajvar-lutenica hybrid.
**With carrot:** add 1 grated carrot for sweetness.

## Serving
On bread · alongside grilled meat · with cheese · in sandwiches · at a Macedonian breakfast.

## Storage
Sealed jars 6 months; refrigerate after opening, 1 month.
