# Stegt Flæsk med Persillesovs (Danish Pork Belly with Parsley Sauce)

![Stegt Flæsk](resources/stegt-flaesk.jpg)

*Denmark's national dish (voted so by the Danes in 2014): thick slices of pork belly fried hard till the fat renders and the edges crisp dark gold, served with boiled new potatoes and a generous ladle of creamy white persillesovs (parsley sauce). The ultimate Danish comfort food; Friday-night dinner in every Danish home.*

**Serves:** 4

**Prep Time:** 15 minutes

**Cook Time:** 30 minutes

## Overview
Stegt flæsk med persillesovs (literally "fried pork belly with parsley sauce") is officially Denmark's national dish - voted so in a 2014 nationwide poll by the Danish Ministry of Environment and Food (beating frikadeller, smørrebrød, and roast pork to the top spot). The construction is satisfyingly simple: thick slices of pork belly (about 1cm thick, skin-on or off depending on regional preference) are seasoned with salt and pepper and fried hard in a dry pan till the fat renders out and the edges crisp deeply dark golden. Served with a heap of small boiled new potatoes (cooked in their skins, peeled at the table) and a generous ladle of creamy white persillesovs - a Danish parsley sauce made from a butter-flour roux thickened with milk, finished with masses of chopped fresh parsley and a touch of nutmeg. The persillesovs is the heart of the dish - a properly thick, glossy, parsley-flecked cream sauce that floods the plate. Often served with pickled beetroot on the side. Three details: thick pork belly slices (1cm - not bacon-thin), fried hard till the edges crisp (not just warmed through), persillesovs is the heart of the dish (not optional).

## Ingredients

### Pork belly
- 800 g pork belly (skin-on or off; 1cm thick slices - ask your butcher to slice it, or buy ready-sliced)
- 2 teaspoons fine sea salt
- 1 teaspoon ground black pepper

### Persillesovs (parsley sauce)
- 60 g butter
- 4 tablespoons plain flour
- 600 ml whole milk (warmed)
- 1 large bunch fresh flat-leaf parsley (about 60g; leaves and tender stems chopped fine)
- 1 teaspoon fine sea salt
- ½ teaspoon ground white pepper
- ¼ teaspoon ground nutmeg
- 1 tablespoon Dijon mustard (optional; for depth)

### To serve
- 1.2 kg small new potatoes (skin-on)
- 1 small bunch fresh dill (chopped, for the potatoes)
- 50 g butter (for the potatoes)
- Pickled beetroot (sliced; from a jar)
- A glass of cold pilsner (Carlsberg, Tuborg, Royal)
- Optional: a small glass of cold akvavit

## Method

### Stage 1 - Boil the potatoes
1. Scrub the small new potatoes; leave the skin on.
2. Bring a large pan of well-salted water to the boil.
3. Add the potatoes; simmer 15-20 minutes till tender when pierced with a fork.
4. Drain; return to the hot pot off the heat to dry out briefly.
5. Toss with butter and chopped dill.
6. Keep warm.

### Stage 2 - Make the persillesovs
1. In a saucepan, melt the butter over medium heat.
2. Whisk in the flour; cook 2 minutes, stirring, till lightly golden.
3. Gradually whisk in the warmed milk in a slow steady stream.
4. Bring to a gentle simmer; cook 5-6 minutes, whisking, till thickened to a glossy heavy custard.
5. Stir in chopped parsley (reserve a small handful for finishing), salt, white pepper, nutmeg, and Dijon mustard (if using).
6. Cook 1 minute more; the parsley should turn the sauce vivid green.
7. Taste; adjust salt.
8. Keep warm over the lowest heat (cover the surface with cling film if standing more than 5 minutes to prevent a skin).

### Stage 3 - Fry the pork belly
1. Heat a large dry cast-iron pan over medium-high heat - no oil or butter needed; the pork renders its own fat.
2. Season the pork belly slices with salt and pepper on both sides.
3. Lay the slices in the dry pan in a single layer (cook in batches if needed; don't crowd).
4. Cook 4-5 minutes on the first side, undisturbed, till the fat has rendered and the bottom is deeply golden.
5. Flip; cook 3-4 minutes on the other side till crispy at the edges.
6. The skin (if on) should crackle slightly; the fat should be translucent and rendered.
7. Drain on paper towels briefly.

### Stage 4 - Plate
1. Mound buttered potatoes on each plate.
2. Arrange 3-4 slices of fried pork belly alongside.
3. Ladle the warm persillesovs generously over the potatoes (some Danes drown the potatoes; the sauce IS the dish).
4. A scatter of the reserved fresh parsley.
5. Sliced pickled beetroot on the side.

### Stage 5 - Serve immediately
1. Cold pilsner.
2. A small shot of akvavit if going proper Danish.

## Notes
- **Thick pork belly slices (1cm):** bacon-thin doesn't work. Ask your butcher.
- **Dry pan, no added fat:** the pork belly's own fat is the fry medium.
- **Persillesovs is the heart:** the sauce makes the dish. Don't skimp on parsley or thickness.
- **Pickled beetroot on the side:** the canonical Danish cut against the richness.
- **National dish status:** if Denmark could only eat one dish, this is it. Voted 2014.

## Variations
**Stegt flæsk i ovn (oven version):** roast the pork belly slices on a rack at 220°C for 15-20 minutes instead of pan-frying. Less interactive, more even.
**With apple-and-bacon (æbleflæsk):** swap the persillesovs for apples cooked with bacon and onion. A different but equally Danish preparation.
**With fennel parsley sauce:** add chopped fennel fronds to the persillesovs for a brighter variant.
**Vegetarian-ish:** swap the pork belly for thick slices of grilled portobello mushrooms; keep the persillesovs (works beautifully).
**With grilled chops:** use thick pork chops instead of pork belly.

## Serving
At a Danish family Friday-night dinner · at a Danish countryside kro (country inn) · at a Christmas lunch (julefrokost) · at a Danish Embassy reception abroad · at home with a cold beer and a small akvavit.

## Storage
- Cooked pork belly refrigerates 3 days; re-crisp in a hot pan briefly.
- Persillesovs refrigerates 3 days; reheats with a splash of milk to loosen. Doesn't freeze well.
- Boiled potatoes refrigerate 3 days; reheat in butter.
