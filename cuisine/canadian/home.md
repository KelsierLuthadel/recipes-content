# Canadian Cuisine

![Canadian Cuisine](resources/hero.jpg)

Canada's coast-to-coast plate is a country-wide patchwork of regional dishes: poutine (Quebec's national plate of fries + cheese curds + brown gravy), tourtière (the Quebec spiced-pork-and-beef pie eaten on Christmas Eve), the Montreal smoked-meat sandwich (rye, mustard, hand-cut brisket from Schwartz's), and Pacific salmon plank-grilled on cedar over an open fire (the Coast Salish technique that now defines BC outdoor cooking). Sweet things lean to Ontario butter tarts, BC Nanaimo bars, and Quebec maple-syrup pie. Indigenous fry bread (bannock), the Toronto peameal-bacon sandwich, Saskatoon-berry compote from the prairies, and beavertails pastries from the Ottawa skating canals fill the snack-and-side categories. The national love of maple runs through everything sweet; the Caesar (clamato + vodka + spices) is the national cocktail, the London Fog (Earl Grey + steamed milk + vanilla) is the Vancouver-invented winter warmer.
