# Potato Latkes

![Potato Latkes](resources/potato-latkes.jpg)

*New York's Jewish potato pancakes: grated potato and onion bound with egg and matzo meal, formed into thin pancakes, pan-fried in oil till deeply golden and crispy. The Hanukkah miracle-of-oil tradition; the traditional Jewish-American comfort food served with sour cream and applesauce.*

**Serves:** Makes 16 latkes (serves 4-6)

**Prep Time:** 25 minutes

**Cook Time:** 25 minutes

## Overview
Potato latkes (also called levivot or just "latkes") are one of the most iconic Jewish-American foods and the traditional Hanukkah dish (eaten on the eight-night festival commemorating the miracle of the oil that lasted eight days at the Second Temple; hence latkes being fried in lots of oil): grated raw potato and onion squeezed of all moisture (essential for crispness), bound with beaten egg, matzo meal (or flour or breadcrumbs), salt and pepper, formed into thin pancakes, and pan-fried in hot oil till deeply golden and crispy on both sides. Served with sour cream (creamy contrast) and applesauce (sweet contrast); the traditional two accompaniments, both essential.

## Ingredients

- 1 kg starchy potatoes (Russet or Maris Piper; peeled)
- 1 large onion
- 2 large eggs (beaten)
- 5 tablespoons matzo meal (or plain flour or panko)
- 2 teaspoons fine sea salt
- 1 teaspoon ground black pepper
- 1 teaspoon baking powder (optional; for lightness)
- 250 ml vegetable oil for frying (more as needed)

### To serve
- Sour cream
- Applesauce (homemade or jarred)
- Smoked salmon (lox; optional fancier topping)
- Chopped fresh chives or dill

## Method

### Stage 1 - Grate
1. Coarsely grate the potatoes and onion (use box grater or food processor with grating attachment).
2. Place in a clean kitchen towel.

### Stage 2 - Squeeze dry
1. Twist the towel into a bundle.
2. Squeeze out ALL the liquid over the sink.
3. Critical step; wet potato gives soggy latkes.
4. The starchy white liquid will mostly drain; let it settle in a bowl 5 min; pour off liquid but reserve the white starch at the bottom (add it back to the latkes).

### Stage 3 - Mix
1. Transfer squeezed grated potato and onion to a bowl.
2. Add reserved starch (from the bottom of the liquid).
3. Stir in eggs, matzo meal, salt, pepper, baking powder.
4. Mix thoroughly.

### Stage 4 - Heat oil
1. Heat 1 cm of oil in a wide pan to 175°C (350°F).

### Stage 5 - Form and fry
1. Take heaped tablespoons of mixture.
2. Press into thin oval pancakes (about 8 cm wide, 1 cm thick).
3. Lower into hot oil.
4. Fry 3-4 min per side till deep golden.
5. Don't overcrowd.
6. Drain on paper towels.

### Stage 6 - Keep warm
1. Place cooked latkes on a rack in a warm oven (100°C) while you fry the rest.

### Stage 7 - Salt and serve
1. Sprinkle with extra salt while hot.
2. Pile on platter.
3. Sour cream and applesauce in bowls.
4. Optional: smoked salmon and chopped dill on top.

## Notes
- **Squeeze potato VERY dry:** essential.
- **Add starch back:** for binding.
- **Hot oil 175°C:** for crispness.
- **Sour cream AND applesauce:** both essential.

## Variations
**With sweet potato:** half potato, half sweet potato.
**With apple:** grate in a tart apple.
**Larger latkes:** for a meal (entrée size).
**With smoked salmon and crème fraîche:** fancier brunch version.

## Serving
At Hanukkah; year-round at Jewish delis; with sour cream and applesauce.

## Storage
- Best immediately.
- Cooked refrigerate 2 days; reheat in oven at 180°C 8 min (never microwave; goes soggy).
- Freeze 1 month; reheat from frozen.
