# New York Cuisine

![New York Cuisine](resources/hero.jpg)

The melting-pot cooking of New York City and the wider state: the foldable thin-crust slice of NY pizza, the deli pastrami on rye at Katz's, the everything bagel with lox at the Lower East Side appetising shops, the diner eggs benedict, the spaghetti-and-meatballs from Italian-American Brooklyn, the Manhattan clam chowder (red, not white), the New York strip steak from Peter Luger, the Reuben sandwich, the soft pretzel from the street cart. Cheesecake at Junior's, black-and-white cookies from the corner bakery, cannoli from Little Italy. A city cuisine built on Italian, Jewish, Polish, Puerto Rican and Chinese immigrant kitchens layered over four centuries of Dutch and English foundations.
