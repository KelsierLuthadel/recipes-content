# Zimbabwean

*The kitchen of southern Africa's plateau. Sadza maize porridge as the universal starch (eaten with the right hand, used to scoop stew), peanut butter ground into nearly every sauce, and slow-cooked oxtail or beef as the celebration plate.*

## A short history

Zimbabwean cooking sits on the southern African farming-and-herding tradition. The Shona and Ndebele peoples grew maize (the now-universal staple, introduced from the Americas via the Portuguese in the 16th century and absorbed into a millet-eating diet), beans, sweet potato, peanuts (ground into dovi — the universal peanut butter sauce that flavours nearly everything), pumpkin and pumpkin leaves.

The British colonial period (Southern Rhodesia, 1923–1980) layered on tea, beef ranching, the formal Sunday roast tradition and the dairy industry. The post-independence (1980) period kept the everyday food traditional; the home plate is sadza-and-relish at most meals.

The recent decades of political-economic turbulence have not changed the everyday food. Sadza (the white-maize stiff porridge), peanut butter relishes, slow-cooked stews and pumpkin leaves cooked with peanut butter remain the universal plate.

## Regional variations

- **Harare and Mashonaland**: codified mainstream. Sadza with nyama (meat) and muriwo (greens).
- **Bulawayo and Matabeleland**: Ndebele-influenced. More cattle dishes.
- **Manicaland (east)**: more cassava, closer to Mozambican coast.

## Signature ingredients

- **Maize meal**: in sadza. Coarse-ground white maize.
- **Peanut butter (dovi)**: in nearly every relish.
- **Pumpkin leaves (muboora)**: cooked with peanut butter as a side.
- **Beef and goat**: the celebration meats.
- **Kapenta**: small dried fish from Lake Kariba; salty, intense.
- **Mopane worms**: dried caterpillars, high protein. The traditional bush food.
- **Tomato, onion, dried chillies**: the relish base.

## Learn it in four recipes

1. **[Dovi](/recipes/cuisine/zimbabwe/dovi)**: chicken (or beef) slow-cooked in a peanut-butter-and-tomato sauce. The signature Zimbabwean dish.
2. **[Huku ne Dovi](/recipes/cuisine/zimbabwe/huku-ne-dovi)**: chicken in peanut sauce — the named version of the above, with pumpkin leaves stirred through at the end.
3. **[Nyama Stew](/recipes/cuisine/zimbabwe/nyama-stew)**: beef slow-cooked with tomato, onion and garlic. Served over sadza.
4. **[Oxtail Stew](/recipes/cuisine/zimbabwe/oxtail-stew)**: oxtail slow-braised till the meat falls off the bone. The Sunday family plate.

## Where to go next

Pair everything with Mazoe Orange Crush (the national cordial) or with Zambezi or Bohlinger's lager. End with mukute fruit (chocolate berry) if in season, or with the simple rice-and-milk pudding (manhanga ne mukaka).
