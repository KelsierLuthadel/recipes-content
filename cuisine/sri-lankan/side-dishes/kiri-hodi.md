# Kiri Hodi (Coconut Milk Gravy)

![Kiri Hodi](resources/kiri-hodi.jpg)

*The mildest Sri Lankan curry on the rice & curry plate: thin coconut milk simmered with onion, garlic, turmeric, curry leaves and a squeeze of lime, served as the gentle counterpoint to everything else on the plate.*

**Serves:** 4

**Prep Time:** 5 minutes

**Cook Time:** 12 minutes

## Overview
Kiri hodi ("milk gravy" in Sinhala) is the mildest curry in the Sri Lankan plate, a thin saffron-coloured coconut-milk gravy that gives the rest of the meal something to dip into. The build is simple: thin coconut milk, sliced onion, garlic, turmeric, mustard seeds, curry leaves, pandan, a slit green chilli (more for show than heat), salt and a squeeze of lime at the end. Pours like a soup, sits between yellow and pale-green in colour, takes 12 minutes to make. Common with hoppers, string hoppers, and as the gentle middle of a busy rice & curry plate.

## Ingredients

- 1 tablespoon coconut oil
- 1 small onion (sliced thin)
- 2 garlic cloves (smashed)
- 1 green chilli (slit lengthways)
- 1 sprig fresh curry leaves
- 1 pandan leaf (5 cm)
- 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
- ¼ teaspoon fenugreek seeds
- ½ teaspoon ground turmeric
- 400 ml thin coconut milk (the second pressing, or thick coconut milk + 200 ml water)
- 1 ½ teaspoons fine salt
- 1 tablespoon fresh lime juice

## Method

1. Heat the coconut oil in a saucepan over medium heat. Add the mustard seeds, fenugreek, curry leaves and pandan; fry 30 seconds until the mustard pops.
1. Add the onion, garlic and green chilli; cook 2 minutes, the onion should soften but not brown.
1. Stir in the turmeric and salt; cook 15 seconds.
1. Pour in the coconut milk; bring to a gentle simmer over low heat. Don't let it boil hard or the milk splits.
1. Simmer uncovered 6 to 8 minutes. The gravy should reduce slightly and turn pale yellow.
1. Off the heat, stir in the lime juice. Taste and adjust salt.

## Notes
- **Low heat all the way.** Coconut milk splits if boiled hard, and split kiri hodi looks broken even when it tastes fine. Gentle simmer only.
- **It IS supposed to be thin.** Resist the urge to reduce it down to a thicker sauce. Kiri hodi is meant to pour, not cling.
- **Maldive fish (umbalakada) is optional.** Some recipes add a teaspoon for umami; the strict vegetarian version skips it.

## Storage
- Refrigerate up to 2 days; rewarm very gently over low heat. Don't freeze.
