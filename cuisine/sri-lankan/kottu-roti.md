# Kottu Roti

![Kottu Roti](resources/kottu-roti.jpg)

*Shredded godhamba roti chopped with two metal cleavers on a hot griddle alongside vegetables, egg, curry and chilli: Sri Lanka's chaotic late-night street meal, signature soundtrack a rhythmic metallic clatter.*

**Serves:** 2 to 3

**Prep Time:** 15 minutes (assuming pre-made roti)

**Cook Time:** 15 minutes

## Overview
Kottu roti (sometimes "kothu" roti) is Sri Lanka's most distinctive street food, originally invented in Batticaloa as a way to use leftover godhamba roti and curry. The whole dish is cooked on a flat steel griddle while two cleavers chop everything together at speed, the rhythmic "clack-clack-clack" of the cleavers is so loud and constant that kottu shops can't operate in residential neighbourhoods after dark. Shredded godhamba roti goes onto the griddle with chopped onions, leek, carrot, green chilli, garlic, egg, a generous spoonful of curry powder, and whatever leftover curry the cook has, chicken curry is traditional, vegetable for a vegetarian version, cheese kottu for the gluttonous variant. The whole thing comes together in about 5 minutes of frantic chopping. At home, two flat-bladed spatulas or wide knives stand in for the cleavers.

## Ingredients

### The roti shred
- 4 godhamba roti (or paratha; chopped into ribbons about 1 cm wide and 5 cm long; if homemade, follow standard godhamba prep)

### The vegetable-egg stir-fry
- 3 tablespoons coconut oil (or vegetable oil)
- 1 medium onion (finely sliced)
- 1 leek (white and pale green parts, sliced thin)
- 1 carrot (julienned)
- 2 garlic cloves (chopped fine)
- 2 cm fresh ginger (grated)
- 2 green chillies (deseeded and sliced; more for hot)
- 1 sprig fresh curry leaves
- 3 eggs (beaten lightly with a pinch of salt)

### The seasoning
- 1 tablespoon Sri Lankan curry powder (roasted)
- 1 teaspoon Kashmiri chilli powder
- 2 tablespoons soy sauce
- 1 teaspoon fine salt
- 1 teaspoon caster sugar

### The leftover curry (optional but classic)
- 200 g pre-cooked chicken curry, including some of its sauce (see [Sri Lankan Chicken Curry](sri-lankan-chicken-curry.md)); OR substitute 200 g cooked diced chicken + 4 tablespoons curry sauce

### To serve
- Fresh lime wedges
- Pol sambol on the side (see [Pol Sambol](pol-sambol.md))
- A small bowl of extra curry sauce poured over

## Method

### Stage 1 - Mise en place
1. Have everything chopped and within arm's reach of the pan; once the cook starts it goes fast.
1. Heat a wide flat-bottomed pan or griddle over high heat until shimmering.

### Stage 2 - Stir-fry the vegetables
1. Pour in the oil; add the curry leaves, onion, leek and carrot. Stir-fry hard for 2 minutes; the vegetables should retain some bite.
1. Add the garlic, ginger and green chillies; cook 30 seconds.
1. Push the vegetables to one side; pour the beaten eggs into the cleared space.
1. Scramble the eggs roughly with the spatula for 30 to 45 seconds until just set; then mix into the vegetables.

### Stage 3 - Add the meat and seasoning
1. Stir in the chicken curry (or substitute) along with its sauce.
1. Sprinkle over the curry powder, chilli, soy, salt and sugar.
1. Stir-fry 1 minute until everything is hot and well combined.

### Stage 4 - The chop
1. Add the shredded roti.
1. Using two wide spatulas (or two flat-bladed knives, or two metal turners), chop and stir energetically for 3 to 4 minutes. The goal is to break the roti down further AND distribute every flavour through every shred. Chop hard, scrape from the bottom, fold.

### Stage 5 - Serve
1. Tip out onto plates; squeeze fresh lime over the top.
1. Serve with extra curry sauce in a small bowl on the side, pol sambol, and more lime.
1. Eat immediately while hot.

## Notes
- **Godhamba roti is the right bread.** It's a stretchy, flaky Sri Lankan flatbread with layers; tearing it into kottu ribbons gives the right chew. Paratha is the best supermarket substitute.
- **The chop is the dish.** Don't just stir, actively break the roti down further as you go. The texture should be a mix of strands and small fragments.
- **High heat throughout.** Kottu wants a hot griddle; a moderate pan ends up steamed and soggy.
- **Egg variations.** Some kottu cooks scramble the egg first and stir through; others crack the egg into the centre of the pan and let it crisp on the bottom for a more textural element. Both correct.

## Variations
- **Cheese kottu.** Sprinkle 100 g grated mozzarella over the kottu in the last 30 seconds; stir until melted. A late-night Colombo favourite.
- **Vegetable kottu.** Skip the chicken and add another 200 g of mixed vegetables; replace the curry sauce with 4 tablespoons of vegetable curry or coconut milk.
- **Dolphin kottu (chicken + cheese + sausage).** All three meats plus cheese; a name that nobody can quite explain. Serves 4 instead of 2 to 3.

## Storage
- Best within an hour. Doesn't store well, the roti goes soft.
