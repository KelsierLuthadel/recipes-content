# Lakror

![Lakror](resources/lakror.jpg)

*The thin-crust spinach-and-leek pastry of Korçë: two sheets of hand-rolled dough, a filling of wilted spinach, leek and crumbled cheese, baked in a wide round pan till the top blisters dark. Cut into snack-size wedges; eat warm.*

**Serves:** 6-8 wedges

**Prep Time:** 40 minutes

**Cook Time:** 40 minutes

## Overview
Lakror is the Korçë-region pastry that sits halfway between a pie and a flatbread: two sheets of thin hand-rolled dough sandwich a filling of wilted greens, with neither butter nor filo in sight. The Korçë version uses spinach and leek bound with crumbled white cheese and a beaten egg; other versions use pumpkin, leek and rice, or tomato and onion in summer. The dough is rolled out as thin as the cook can manage (the goal is the top blistering and crisping while the bottom catches a dark gold), and the pie is baked in a wide round tin under a hot oven till the top is almost burnt. Cut into wedges, lakror is the standing pastry of the Korçë afternoon: sold from the bakery window by the slice, packed for picnic lunches in the mountains, set down on the table for guests with a glass of raki. Eat warm, with a tomato salad on the side.

## Ingredients

### For the dough
- 400 g plain flour
- 1 tsp salt
- 250 ml warm water
- 3 tbsp olive oil, plus extra for brushing

### For the filling
- 500 g spinach, washed
- 3 leeks (about 400 g), trimmed and thinly sliced
- 3 tbsp olive oil
- 200 g feta cheese, crumbled
- 100 g gjizë or ricotta
- 2 large eggs, beaten
- 1 tsp dried oregano
- 1/2 tsp ground black pepper
- 1 tsp salt

## Method

### Stage 1 - Make the dough
1. Mix the flour and salt in a large bowl.
2. Pour in the warm water and the olive oil.
3. Bring together with a wooden spoon, then knead on a floured surface for 8 minutes until smooth and elastic.
4. Divide into 2 equal balls; rub each with a little oil.
5. Cover; rest for 30 minutes.

### Stage 2 - Make the filling
1. Heat the olive oil in a wide pan over medium heat.
2. Add the sliced leeks; cook for 8 minutes until soft and pale gold (do not brown).
3. Tip in the spinach in handfuls, stirring till wilted.
4. Take the pan off the heat; tip the greens into a colander and press out the excess liquid (important, or the lakror goes soggy).
5. Transfer to a bowl; let cool for 10 minutes.
6. Stir in the feta, gjizë, beaten eggs, oregano, pepper and salt.

### Stage 3 - Roll and assemble
1. Heat the oven to 220°C (fan 200°C); brush a 30 cm round baking tin generously with olive oil.
2. Roll the first dough ball out as thin as you can on a floured surface (aim for about 35 cm across).
3. Lay it into the tin; let the edges hang over the side.
4. Brush the dough with olive oil.
5. Spread the filling evenly over the dough.
6. Roll the second dough ball just as thin; lay over the top.
7. Trim, then crimp the two doughs together at the edge.
8. Brush the top with olive oil; prick with a fork all over.

### Stage 4 - Bake
1. Bake for 35-40 minutes until the top is dark gold and crisp; the bottom should sound hollow when tapped.
2. The very best lakror has a few small dark blisters across the top.

### Stage 5 - Serve
1. Cool in the tin for 10 minutes.
2. Cut into wedges; eat warm.

## Notes
- **The dough thickness:** As thin as you can roll it. Thick dough turns the lakror into a bread, not a pastry.
- **Drain the greens:** Press hard in the colander. Wet greens give a soggy base.
- **The bake:** A high hot oven (220°C) gives the dark blistered top that marks a good lakror.

## Variations
- **Pumpkin lakror (lakror me kungull):** Substitute 600 g grated pumpkin (squeezed dry) for the spinach; add 3 tablespoons cooked rice.
- **Tomato lakror (lakror me domate):** Use 500 g chopped tomatoes (drained) and 2 chopped onions for a summer version.
- **With cornmeal crust:** Add 100 g fine cornmeal to the dough for a coarser texture (a Korçë grandmother trick).
- **Meat lakror:** Add 200 g cooked, well-seasoned minced lamb to the spinach filling.
- **Wild greens version:** Use foraged spring greens (nettle, sorrel, wild leek) in place of the spinach.

## Serving
Cut into wedges with a tomato and cucumber salad · with a glass of cold raki · with plain yoghurt on the side · as picnic food in the Korçë hills · with a small dark Albanian coffee · packed cold in a lunchbox for the next day.

## Storage
- Eat warm the day it is baked.
- Leftovers keep 2 days refrigerated; reheat in a hot oven for 8 minutes to re-crisp.
- Freezes 1 month, wrapped tightly; reheat from frozen at 180°C for 25 minutes.
