# Bitoque

![Bitoque](resources/bitoque.jpg)

*Portugal's pan-fried steak with egg: a thin steak pan-fried in butter and olive oil, served with a fried egg with runny yolk on top, plus chips, white rice and salad on the side. The Portuguese diner classic, the everyday lunch order at every Portuguese tasca and café.*

**Serves:** 2

**Prep Time:** 15 minutes

**Cook Time:** 15 minutes

## Overview
Bitoque is Portugal's everyday pan-fried steak dish and the canonical Portuguese diner-and-tasca lunch: a thin slice of beef (sirloin, rump, or "vazia" steak) pan-fried briefly in a mixture of butter and olive oil, served on a plate with a fried egg (sunny-side-up with runny yolk) on top, plus a heap of chips (papas fritas), a small mound of plain white rice, a fresh salad of lettuce-tomato-onion, and a small bowl of cooking sauce (the pan juices deglazed with white wine, garlic and a touch of mustard). The dish is what every Portuguese worker orders at the corner tasca for lunch; what every Portuguese family makes on a weeknight when they want something quick and substantial. The steak is thin (about 1 cm) and cooked briefly. Butter-and-olive-oil together is the canonical Portuguese fat; pure oil lacks the richness. All four components on the same plate: steak, egg, chips and rice. The combination is what defines the dish.

## Ingredients

### Steak
- 2 thin steaks (sirloin, rump, or thin minute steaks; about 200 g each)
- 1 teaspoon fine sea salt
- 1 teaspoon ground black pepper
- 4 garlic cloves (crushed)
- 1 bay leaf
- 1 teaspoon dried oregano

### Cooking
- 3 tablespoons unsalted butter
- 2 tablespoons olive oil

### Sauce
- 100 ml dry white wine (Portuguese)
- 100 ml beef stock
- 1 teaspoon Dijon mustard
- 1 tablespoon Worcestershire sauce
- 2 tablespoons butter (extra; for the sauce)

### Sides
- 4 cups cooked plain white rice
- 6 medium potatoes (cut into chips; twice-fried)
- 1 small head lettuce (shredded)
- 2 tomatoes (sliced)
- 1 small red onion (sliced)
- 2 tablespoons olive oil + 1 tablespoon white vinegar (for salad)

### Eggs
- 2 large eggs

### To serve
- Crusty bread
- Mustard
- Lemon wedges

## Method

### Stage 1 - Marinate the steak
1. Place steaks on a plate.
2. Sprinkle with salt, pepper, crushed garlic, broken bay leaf and oregano.
3. Rest 10 minutes.

### Stage 2 - Cook the steak
1. Heat 2 tablespoons butter and the olive oil in a wide heavy pan over medium-high heat.
2. When sizzling, add the steaks.
3. Cook 90 seconds per side for thin steaks (medium); longer for thicker steaks.
4. Lift onto a warm plate; cover loosely.

### Stage 3 - Make the sauce
1. In the same pan (don't wash), reduce heat to medium.
2. Add the wine; let bubble 30 seconds.
3. Add the stock, mustard and Worcestershire; bring to a simmer.
4. Reduce 3-4 minutes till slightly thickened.
5. Whisk in the additional 2 tablespoons of butter for shine.

### Stage 4 - Fry the eggs
1. In a separate pan, melt 1 tablespoon butter.
2. Fry the eggs sunny-side up for 3 minutes (whites set, yolks runny).

### Stage 5 - Plate
1. On each plate: a steak, a pile of chips, a mound of rice, a portion of salad (lettuce-tomato-onion dressed with oil and vinegar).
2. Top the steak with a fried egg.
3. Pour the sauce over the steak (or alongside).
4. Crusty bread on the side.

## Notes
- **Thin steaks:** 1 cm thick.
- **Brief cook:** 90 seconds per side.
- **All four components on the plate.**
- **Egg yolk runs into the steak:** the point.

## Variations
**Bitoque de porco:** pork chops instead of beef.
**Bitoque de frango:** chicken breast.
**Bitoque com bacon:** add a strip of bacon over the steak.
**With mushroom sauce:** add sautéed mushrooms to the sauce.

## Serving
On a wide plate with all four sides. Drink: Portuguese red wine or Sagres beer. The everyday Portuguese diner lunch.

## Storage
- Best eaten immediately.
- Leftover steak refrigerated 2 days; reheat briefly.
- Don't refrigerate assembled.
