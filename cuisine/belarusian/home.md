# Belarusian Cuisine

![Belarusian Cuisine](resources/hero.jpg)

A cuisine that orbits the potato so completely that Belarusians are nicknamed "bul'bashy" from "bulba" (potato), grated raw into pancakes, mashed with butter, baked whole under salt pork. The Polish-Lithuanian-Russian crossroads left a layered table: rye bread and salo from one side, kalduny and pierogi-style dumplings from another, cabbage soup and pickled everything from the third. Dairy runs through every course (smetana sour cream, tvorog curd, kefir for cold soups), the dense pine forests give dried boletus mushrooms and wild berries for the long winter, and Orthodox Lent shapes a strong vegetable-and-grain repertoire that runs parallel to the meat-heavy feast days.
