# Smokey Chilli con Carne

![Smokey Chilli con Carne](resources/smokey-chilli-con-carne.jpg)

*An ultra-slow Dutch-oven chilli with four kinds of dried Mexican chilli, smoked paprika, lager and bone broth: the long braise is what builds the depth.*

**Serves:** 6-8

**Prep Time:** 30 minutes

**Cook Time:** 8 hours

## Overview
A weekend chilli con carne that lives in the oven. The flavour is built in three layers: a paste of four dried Mexican chillies (ancho, arbol, guajillo, pasilla) toasted then rehydrated and blended with garlic; a deep base of caramelised onion and celery built on the beef fond; and a finish of smoked paprika, cinnamon and lager that reduces against tinned tomato and bone broth. Eight hours at 130°C breaks the beef down to silk; black beans and bell peppers go in late so they hold their bite.

## Ingredients

### Beef
- 1.5 kg beef (chuck or shin, in 1-inch cubes)
- Salt and crushed black pepper

### Aromatics
- 3 medium (or 2 large) red and yellow/brown onions (finely chopped)
- 3 celery sticks (finely chopped)
- 2 red bell peppers (1-2 cm chunks)
- 2 green bell peppers (1-2 cm chunks)

### Chilli Paste
- 2 ancho dried chillies
- 2 arbol dried chillies
- 6 guajillo (Mexican dried red chilli, mild and sweet-tangy) dried chillies
- 4 pasilla dried chillies
- 5-6 garlic cloves

### Spice Mix
- 2 tbsp ground cumin
- 2 tbsp smoked paprika
- 1 tsp ground cinnamon

### Liquids & Tinned Goods
- 140 g tomato purée
- 500 ml bone broth
- 500 ml lager
- 2 tins (400 g each) chopped tomatoes
- 2 tins (400 g each) black beans (drained and rinsed)
- 2-3 tbsp Worcestershire sauce

### To Finish
- Bunch of fresh coriander (very finely chopped)
- Salt and black pepper to taste

## Method

### Stage 1 - Prep
1. Chop the onions and celery finely; combine in a bowl and set aside.
2. Cut the bell peppers into 1-2 cm chunks; set aside separately (they go in much later).
3. Dice the beef into 1-inch cubes and season generously with salt and crushed black pepper.

### Stage 2 - Toast the Chillies
1. Preheat a dry frying pan over low heat.
2. Chop the tops off the dried chillies, split them open, and shake out the seeds.
3. Raise the heat to medium-high and toast the chillies for around 30 seconds per side, until fragrant; don't let them blacken or they turn bitter.
4. Transfer the toasted chillies to a bowl of just-boiled water and leave for 10 minutes to rehydrate.

### Stage 3 - Brown the Beef
1. Start preheating a Dutch oven over low heat while the chillies soak.
2. Bring the Dutch oven up to medium-high and add a splash of oil.
3. Brown the beef in batches without crowding the pot, turning to colour all sides. Lift each batch out to a plate, leaving the fond behind.
4. While the beef browns, blend the rehydrated chillies, garlic, and a ladle or two of the chilli soaking water to a smooth paste. Set aside.

### Stage 4 - Build the Base
1. With the beef out of the pan, turn off the heat and add the onions and celery. Cover with the lid for 2-3 minutes so the steam helps lift the fond from the bottom.
2. Return to medium heat, add a large pinch of salt, and caramelise the onions and celery for 10-15 minutes, stirring so nothing catches.
3. Preheat the oven to 130°C (fan).
4. Add the cumin, smoked paprika, and cinnamon. Toast for a couple of minutes, stirring constantly, until aromatic.
5. Add the tomato purée and caramelise it against the base, stirring constantly, for 2-3 minutes.
6. Pour in the lager and reduce by half, scraping the fond off the bottom.
7. Stir in the chilli paste and the chopped tomatoes. Bring to a boil, then drop the heat and simmer for 10-15 minutes.

### Stage 5 - First Braise (lid on)
1. Return the browned beef and any resting juices to the pot.
2. Add the bone broth and stir well; the liquid should just about cover the beef.
3. Stir in the black beans and the Worcestershire sauce.
4. Lid on, transfer to the oven and braise for 4 hours.

### Stage 6 - Finish (lid off)
1. Lift the lid off and check seasoning; salt if needed.
2. Fold in the bell peppers.
3. Return to the oven uncovered for another 3-4 hours, stirring occasionally, until the sauce reduces to a thick coat and the beef pulls apart with a fork.
4. Off the heat, stir through the finely chopped coriander. Taste again and adjust salt and pepper.

## Notes
- **Toasting the chillies:** Just until fragrant, around 30 seconds per side. Blackened chillies turn the whole pot bitter; pull them off the heat the moment they start to puff.
- **Caramelising the trinity:** The onion-celery base is the foundation flavour for the entire braise. Don't rush it: 10-15 minutes over medium heat is what builds the sweetness that balances the chilli heat.
- **Why bone broth:** Bone broth carries gelatine that gives the finished sauce body without cornflour or any thickening agent.
- **Lager not water:** The lager deglaze adds a faint bitterness that balances the chilli paste's natural sweetness. Use a light lager: dark beer overwhelms the smoked paprika.
- **Bell peppers in late:** Adding the peppers at the lid-off stage keeps them from collapsing into the sauce; they hold their bite as a textural contrast to the soft beef.
- **Low oven, lid off finish:** 130°C is the sweet spot: high enough that the lid-off stage reduces the sauce, low enough that the beef stays tender instead of going stringy.

## Variations
**Hotter:** Double the arbol chillies from 2 to 4, or add a teaspoon of cayenne with the spice mix.
**Beanless:** Skip the black beans and increase the beef to 2 kg; cook time stays the same.
**Pork swap:** Use 1.5 kg pork shoulder cut into 1-inch cubes; extend the first braise to 5 hours.
**Smoked chipotle layer:** Add 2 tbsp chipotle in adobo with the chilli paste for a deeper smoke and an extra heat note.
**Slow cooker:** After Stage 4, transfer everything to a slow cooker on low for 10-12 hours; finish with the bell peppers stirred in for the last hour on high.

## Serving
Serve with: Steamed rice, cornbread, or a baked potato.
Top with: Sour cream, grated cheddar, sliced spring onion, pickled jalapeños, chopped fresh coriander.
Squeeze of lime wakes it up if it's been sitting overnight.

## Storage
- Keeps 4 days refrigerated; flavour improves overnight as the spices meld
- Freezes well up to 3 months in portion-sized containers
- Reheat gently on the stovetop with a splash of stock or water if the sauce has thickened too much
- Best eaten 24 hours after cooking, when the chilli has rested and the heat has settled
