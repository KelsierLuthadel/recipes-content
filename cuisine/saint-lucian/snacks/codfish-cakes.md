# Saint Lucian Codfish Cakes

![Saint Lucian Codfish Cakes](resources/codfish-cakes.jpg)

*Saint Lucian codfish cakes: salt cod flaked into a soft flour batter with onion, chive, thyme and chilli, then pan-fried into golden disks. The roadside snack you eat hot from a paper bag with hot sauce.*

**Serves:** Makes 12 cakes

**Prep Time:** 20 minutes (plus 12 hr salt cod soak)

**Cook Time:** 20 minutes

## Overview
Codfish cakes are the Caribbean's answer to the fritter, sold from every Lucian roadside stand at lunchtime and made in every Lucian kitchen on Saturdays. Salt cod is soaked overnight to draw out the salt, then boiled briefly, flaked fine and folded into a soft batter of flour, baking powder, water, onion, chive and thyme, with a finely diced scotch bonnet for the heat. Spoonfuls go into hot oil and puff into golden disks the size of a small saucer. Eat hot, dipped in pepper sauce or tamarind sauce, washed down with a cold sorrel or mauby.

## Ingredients

### The cod
- 300 g salt cod (saltfish), one piece
- Water for soaking

### The batter
- 300 g plain flour
- 1 tsp baking powder
- 1 tsp salt
- 1/2 tsp black pepper
- 1 tsp granulated sugar
- 1 small onion, very finely chopped
- 2 spring onions, finely sliced
- 2 cloves garlic, grated
- 1 tbsp fresh thyme leaves
- 1 tbsp fresh chive or parsley, chopped
- 1/2 scotch bonnet, finely chopped (or to taste)
- About 250 ml cold water (enough to make a thick drop batter)
- 500 ml vegetable oil for frying

## Method

### Stage 1 - Soak and cook the cod
1. Rinse the salt cod under cold water; place in a bowl, cover with cold water, soak 12 hours (overnight), changing the water 2-3 times. The cod plumps and the salt level drops.
2. Drain; place in a small pan with fresh water; bring to a simmer; cook 10 minutes.
3. Drain; cool. Peel off any skin; pull out any bones.
4. Flake very finely with your fingers or two forks. Taste a flake; it should be pleasantly salty, not aggressive. If still too salty, simmer in fresh water 5 more minutes.

### Stage 2 - Mix the batter
1. In a wide bowl, whisk flour, baking powder, salt, pepper and sugar.
2. Stir in the onion, spring onions, garlic, thyme, chive and scotch bonnet.
3. Stir in the flaked cod.
4. Pour in the water gradually, stirring, until you have a thick drop batter (heavy but it falls off the spoon).
5. Rest 10 minutes.

### Stage 3 - Fry
1. Heat the oil in a wide deep pan to 175 C.
2. Drop heaped tablespoons of batter into the oil, 4-5 at a time, flattening slightly with the back of the spoon. They should puff and float.
3. Fry 3-4 minutes per side until deep golden and crisp on the outside, cooked through inside.
4. Lift onto kitchen paper.

### Stage 4 - Serve
1. Eat hot, with pepper sauce or tamarind sauce alongside.

## Notes
- **The salt soak:** Non-negotiable. Without the soak the cakes are inedibly salty. 12 hours is the Lucian standard; 8 in a hurry.
- **Drop batter, not stiff dough:** The batter should fall off the spoon in a heavy lump. Too stiff and the cakes are dense; too loose and they spread thin.
- **Flake fine:** Coarse chunks of cod stay stringy in the cake. Flake until almost shredded.

## Variations
- **Smoked haddock:** A cold-water substitute when salt cod is hard to get; reduce the soak and skip the boil.
- **Sweet pepper:** Add 1 finely chopped red bell pepper for sweetness and colour.
- **Lime zest:** A teaspoon of grated lime zest in the batter lifts the cod.
- **Tinned mackerel cakes:** Same batter, tinned mackerel in place of the cod; ready in 20 minutes total.
- **Coconut version:** Replace 50 ml of the water with coconut milk for a richer cake.

## Serving
Serve very hot · pepper sauce or tamarind dipping sauce · slice of lime · cold mauby or sorrel · also good split into a bake for breakfast

## Storage
- Best straight from the pan
- Refrigerate cooked cakes 2 days; refresh in a 180 C oven 5 minutes (avoid the microwave, it makes them rubbery)
- The mixed batter holds 2 hours refrigerated; the soaked-and-flaked cod holds 24 hours
