# Caribbean Cuisine

![Caribbean Cuisine](resources/hero.jpg)

A long warm chain of islands sharing a common pantry but a dozen distinct kitchens. Scotch bonnet and allspice run through nearly everything; coconut, lime, ginger and thyme do the heavy lifting; rice and peas, salt cod, jerked meats, fried plantain and slow-simmered one-pot stews repeat from island to island with their own local twist. The Spanish-Caribbean side leans on sofrito, pork, and roasted root vegetables (Cuba, Puerto Rico, the Dominican Republic); the British-Caribbean side leans on curry, rotis, and pelau (Jamaica, Trinidad, Barbados, Antigua, Grenada, Saint Lucia); the French-Caribbean side is Haiti's epis-driven home cooking; and the Dutch-Caribbean side (Aruba) holds keshi yena, stoba and pasthechi. Tie it all together with rum and a bowl of soaked sorrel at Christmas. Browse by island below.
