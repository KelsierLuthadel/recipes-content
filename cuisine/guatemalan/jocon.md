# Jocón

![Jocón](resources/jocon.jpg)

*Chicken in a vivid green sauce of tomatillo, coriander, spring onion and toasted pumpkin seed. A Kaqchikel Maya dish from the Sololá highlands, the green twin of pepián.*

**Serves:** 6

**Prep Time:** 25 minutes

**Cook Time:** 1 hour 15 minutes

## Overview
Jocón is the green Mayan stew of the Sololá and Chimaltenango highlands, the verdant twin of pepián. The sauce is built on tomatillos, a generous bunch of coriander and the green ends of spring onions, blended with toasted pumpkin and sesame seeds, garlic and a single green chilli for warmth. Chicken simmers in the sauce until tender; the colour stays bright green because the herbs go in late and the sauce never boils hard. The toasted seeds give body without thickening to a paste. Eaten over white rice with tamalitos blancos and a wedge of lime, the everyday Sunday lunch of the central highlands.

## Ingredients

### For the chicken
- 1.5 kg chicken pieces (thighs and drumsticks), bone in skin on
- 1.5 litres water
- 1 onion, halved
- 4 garlic cloves
- 2 bay leaves
- 1 tbsp salt

### For the green sauce
- 600 g tomatillos (about 12 medium), husked
- 1 large bunch coriander (about 80 g), stalks and leaves
- 8 spring onions, white and green parts
- 1 green chilli (jalapeño or serrano), seeded for milder, seeds in for heat
- 4 garlic cloves
- 80 g pumpkin seeds
- 30 g sesame seeds
- 2 stale corn tortillas, torn
- 1 tsp ground cumin
- 1 tsp salt

### To finish
- 2 tbsp vegetable oil or lard
- 1 small bunch fresh coriander (extra, for the finish)
- Salt to taste

## Method

### Stage 1 - Poach the chicken
1. Combine the chicken, water, onion, garlic, bay and salt in a heavy pot.
2. Bring to a simmer, skim, drop to low and cook gently for 35 minutes until tender. Lift the chicken out. Strain the broth and reserve.

### Stage 2 - Toast the seeds
1. Heat a dry comal or heavy pan over medium heat.
2. Toast the pumpkin seeds, stirring constantly, 3 to 4 minutes until they pop and turn pale gold.
3. Toast the sesame seeds the same way, 2 minutes.
4. Tip into a bowl to stop the cooking.

### Stage 3 - Cook the tomatillos
1. Drop the tomatillos into a pot of simmering water for 4 to 5 minutes until they turn olive-green and soften (do not let them burst).
2. Drain, reserving 250 ml of the cooking liquid.

### Stage 4 - Blend the sauce
1. Combine the tomatillos, coriander, spring onions, green chilli, raw garlic, toasted seeds, torn tortillas, cumin, salt and 250 ml of the reserved chicken broth in a blender.
2. Blend on high for 60 to 90 seconds until smooth and bright green.
3. Do not over-blend; the colour starts to dull after 2 minutes.

### Stage 5 - Bring it together
1. Heat the oil in a heavy pot over medium heat.
2. Pour in the green sauce; it spits and turns a duller green at first.
3. Simmer gently for 12 to 15 minutes, stirring often. The sauce brightens again as it cooks; if it gets too thick, thin with chicken broth.
4. Slide the chicken pieces back in, baste with the sauce, and simmer 10 more minutes for the flavours to come together.
5. Stir through the extra chopped coriander at the end. Taste and salt. Serve hot over white rice.

## Notes
- **Blanch the tomatillos.** This step sweetens them and dulls the harsh raw edge. Skip it and the sauce tastes sharp.
- **Don't boil hard once the sauce is built.** A hard boil turns the green sauce olive-brown and bitter; keep it at a gentle simmer.
- **The toasted seeds are the body.** They give the velvet texture; do not skip toasting them or the sauce stays watery.
- **Coriander stalks count.** The stalks carry more flavour than the leaves; use both.
- **Adjust the chilli.** A serrano with seeds gives a brisk heat; a jalapeño without seeds is mild and family-friendly.

## Variations
- **Jocón verde verde:** add a handful of fresh mint with the coriander for a brighter perfume.
- **Pork jocón:** swap the chicken for pork shoulder, simmered 1 hour 30 minutes.
- **With samat:** if you can source samat (Verapaz wild coriander), use it alongside the regular coriander.
- **With potato and güisquil:** add chunks of waxy potato and chayote with the chicken at the end.
- **Jocón de pavo:** the wedding version, with turkey pieces.

## Serving
- With white rice · tamalitos blancos · sliced avocado · lime wedges · warm corn tortillas

## Storage
- Keeps 3 days refrigerated (the green dulls after that)
- Freezes 2 months but the colour fades; the flavour holds
- Reheat gently over low heat to protect the green; do not boil
