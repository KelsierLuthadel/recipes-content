# Latvian

*The Baltic kitchen of dark rye bread, smoked fish, the pearl-barley-and-mushroom one-pot, and the fermented sour-cabbage soup that runs through every Latvian winter.*

## A short history

Latvian cooking sits at the centre of the three Baltic states, with strong Estonian and Lithuanian overlap and significant German-Lutheran influence (the Baltic German nobility ran the region for centuries before independence). The basic shelf — rye bread (the dark Latvian rye is the country's most-loved cultural export), pork, potatoes, cabbage, beetroot, smoked fish from the Baltic and the lakes, dairy and forest mushrooms — has been the working kitchen for generations.

The Soviet period (1940–1991) flattened the cuisine into bulk-feeding staples; the post-1991 recovery has restored regional dishes alongside the modern Riga restaurant scene. The diaspora kitchens in the US (Boston, Cleveland) and the UK preserve the codified tradition.

## Regional variations

- **Riga and central**: codified mainstream. Karbonāde, pelēkie zirņi.
- **Kurzeme (west, coastal)**: more fish. Smoked sprats are the regional speciality.
- **Vidzeme (central north)**: forest cooking. Mushrooms, berries, game.
- **Latgale (east)**: closer to Russian and Belarusian. More dumpling traditions.

## Signature ingredients

- **Rye bread (rupjmaize)**: dense, dark, sour. The cultural icon.
- **Grey peas (pelēkie zirņi)**: a large dried pea, soaked and cooked with bacon. The Christmas Eve dish.
- **Smoked sprats**: tiny Baltic fish, smoked and tinned, exported worldwide.
- **Pork**: cured, smoked, fresh.
- **Sour cabbage**: in soup, with pork.
- **Sour cream**: dressing soups and stews.
- **Caraway**: in bread, in cheese (Jāņu siers, the summer-solstice cheese).

## Learn it in five recipes

1. **[Pelēkie Zirņi ar Speķi](/recipes/cuisine/latvian/pelekie-zirni-ar-speki)**: grey peas with bacon and onion. The Christmas Eve and Lutheran tradition.
2. **[Karbonāde](/recipes/cuisine/latvian/karbonade)**: pork chop pounded thin, breaded, pan-fried. The Sunday-lunch standard.
3. **[Skābu Kāpostu Zupa](/recipes/cuisine/latvian/skabu-kapostu-zupa)**: sour cabbage soup with pork and barley. The winter weeknight bowl.
4. **[Rasols](/recipes/cuisine/latvian/rasols)**: potato salad with diced ham, peas, gherkin and mayonnaise. The Soviet-era inheritance, now ubiquitous at every celebration.
5. **[Sklandrausis](/recipes/cuisine/latvian/sklandrausis)**: round rye-pastry tart filled with mashed carrot and potato. The Kurzeme regional speciality, the only Latvian dish with EU protected designation.

## Where to go next

Pair everything with Riga Black Balsam (the country's signature bitter liqueur — herbs, roots, oak, 45% ABV) or with kvass (fermented rye drink). The summer solstice festival of Jāņi (June 23-24) features caraway cheese, beer, and grilled meats around bonfires.
