# Bietes ar pelēko

![Bietes ar pelēko](resources/bietes-ar-peleko.jpg)

*Latvian beetroot and grey pea salad: roasted beetroot diced small, cooked grey peas, finely chopped red onion and dill, dressed with sour cream, mustard and a splash of cider vinegar. Magenta, earthy, sweet-sour. The autumn-and-winter side.*

**Serves:** 4 to 6

**Prep Time:** 20 minutes

**Cook Time:** 1 hour 15 minutes (mostly roasting)

## Overview
A pairing the Latvian table has served for centuries: the sweet earth of roasted beetroot lifted by the buttery starch of cooked grey peas, brought together with a sharp sour-cream dressing. Roasting (not boiling) the beetroot keeps the sweetness in the root rather than washing it into the water; the peas need overnight soak and a slow simmer until tender but holding shape. Fold them together while the peas are still warm so they pick up a little of the beetroot juice, dress with sour cream sharpened with mustard and cider vinegar, finish with dill and red onion. Eat cool, not fridge-cold, with rupjmaize and cold meats. The dish leans particularly Latvian in autumn when the beetroots are fresh-dug and the dried peas of the year are ready to cook.

## Ingredients

### Roasted beetroot
- 600 g fresh beetroot (4 medium; tops trimmed)
- 1 tablespoon sunflower oil

### Grey peas
- 150 g dried grey peas (or marrowfat peas), soaked overnight in cold water
- 1 bay leaf
- 1 teaspoon salt (added late)

### Dressing and finish
- 150 g thick sour cream
- 1 tablespoon Dijon mustard
- 2 teaspoons cider vinegar
- 1 teaspoon caster sugar
- 1 small red onion, finely chopped
- 2 tablespoons finely chopped dill
- 1 tablespoon finely chopped flat-leaf parsley
- Salt and black pepper

## Method

### Stage 1 - Cook the peas
1. Drain the soaked peas; rinse.
2. Cover with fresh cold water by 3 cm in a pot; add the bay leaf.
3. Bring to a gentle simmer; cook 75 to 90 minutes until tender but holding their shape.
4. Stir in salt for the last 10 minutes. Drain; discard the bay.

### Stage 2 - Roast the beetroot
1. Heat the oven to 200°C (180°C fan).
2. Wash the beetroots, leave skins on, rub with the oil.
3. Wrap each loosely in foil; place on a tray.
4. Roast 50 to 70 minutes (size-dependent) until a knife meets no resistance.
5. Rest 10 minutes; slip off the skins with kitchen paper. Wear gloves; beetroot stains.

### Stage 3 - Dice
1. Cut the warm peeled beetroot into 8 to 10 mm cubes.
2. Tip the beetroot cubes and the still-warm cooked peas into a wide bowl.

### Stage 4 - Dress
1. Whisk the sour cream, mustard, cider vinegar, sugar, salt and pepper.
2. Pour over the warm beetroot and peas; fold gently.
3. Add the red onion and most of the dill and parsley; fold once more.

### Stage 5 - Rest and serve
1. Cover, rest at room temperature 30 minutes or chill 1 hour.
2. Scatter the reserved herbs on top.
3. Serve cool, with rupjmaize.

## Notes
- **Roast, do not boil, the beetroot.** Boiled beetroot loses its sweet earth into the water. Foil-wrapped roast holds the sugar.
- **Fold warm peas with cool beetroot.** The peas absorb a touch of the beetroot colour and juice, which is the point.
- **Cider vinegar over white wine vinegar.** Cider sits softer alongside the beetroot; white wine vinegar reads too sharp.

## Variations
- **With apple and walnut:** Add one diced tart apple and a handful of toasted walnuts at the end; turns the salad into a winter starter.
- **With smoked herring:** Top with strips of cold smoked herring for a salad-as-supper.
- **Without sour cream:** Replace with 4 tablespoons of olive oil and an extra teaspoon of mustard for a vegan version.

## Serving
Serve as a side with karbonāde, roast pork, cold cuts, or smoked fish. A spoon on the Christmas Eve table next to pelēkie zirņi.

## Storage
- Keeps 3 days refrigerated; the colour intensifies and the flavour deepens.
- Do not freeze.
