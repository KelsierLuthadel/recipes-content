# Latvian rye bread chips

![Latvian rye bread chips](resources/rye-bread-chips.jpg)

*Latvian beer snack: thin batons cut from stale dark rye, tossed with oil, salt, garlic and caraway, then baked or pan-fried until crisp. The bar snack that sits next to cold lager and salted herring all across Riga.*

**Serves:** 4 (as a snack)

**Prep Time:** 10 minutes

**Cook Time:** 18 minutes

## Overview
Walk into any Riga bar and the small bowl set down with your beer is rupjmaizes grauzdiņi (rye bread chips, often called Latvian beer snacks abroad). Stale Latvian rye is cut into thin batons or small cubes, tossed with sunflower oil, plenty of salt, finely chopped garlic and crushed caraway, then baked on a tray until crisp. The texture goes from chewy in the centre to fully crisp depending on how long you leave them; bars usually serve them mid-crisp so the rye chew remains. Some versions go further with a final dunk in warmed garlic sour cream as a dip; others use a melted-cheese-and-garlic crust grilled to a dark mahogany top. The plain salt-garlic version is the foundation, and the right one to start with. Eat with cold beer, kvass, or a glass of Riga black balsam mixed with blackcurrant juice.

## Ingredients

### Rye bread chips
- 400 g stale rupjmaize (dark Latvian rye), at least 3 days old
- 4 tablespoons sunflower oil
- 4 garlic cloves, very finely chopped (or crushed)
- 1 teaspoon caraway seeds, lightly crushed
- 1 teaspoon flaky sea salt
- Freshly ground black pepper

### Optional garlic-cream dip
- 200 g thick sour cream
- 2 garlic cloves, crushed
- 1 tablespoon finely chopped dill
- Pinch of salt
- Squeeze of lemon

## Method

### Stage 1 - Cut the bread
1. Trim the crusts from the rupjmaize if very hard (optional; many like them).
2. Cut the bread into thin batons about 6 cm long, 1 cm wide and 1 cm deep. Even sizing matters for even crisping.
3. Or cut into 1.5 cm cubes for a different shape.

### Stage 2 - Toss with oil and aromatics
1. Heat the oil gently in a small pan with the chopped garlic; warm 1 minute until fragrant, do not brown.
2. Tip into a wide bowl with the bread batons.
3. Add the crushed caraway, salt and pepper; toss with hands so every baton picks up oil and garlic.

### Stage 3 - Bake
1. Heat the oven to 180°C (160°C fan).
2. Spread the batons in a single layer on a parchment-lined tray.
3. Bake 15 to 18 minutes, shaking the tray every 5 minutes, until the batons are deep dark gold and crisp on the outside (the centres may still have a faint chew, which is right).
4. Watch closely after 15 minutes; the line between crisp and burnt is short.

### Stage 4 - Make the dip
1. Whisk together sour cream, crushed garlic, dill, salt and lemon juice.
2. Spoon into a small bowl.

### Stage 5 - Serve
1. Tip the chips into a serving bowl while still warm.
2. Set the dip alongside.
3. Eat with cold beer.

## Notes
- **Stale bread is essential.** Fresh rye holds too much moisture and the chips bake soggy. Three to four days old is right; older still is fine.
- **Even sizing.** Mixed sizes give a mix of burnt and underdone in the same batch. Cut to similar dimensions.
- **The garlic must not burn.** Warm the oil with the garlic gently; if the garlic browns in the pan, the chips will taste bitter. Soft and fragrant only.

## Variations
- **Pan-fried version:** Toast the batons in 80 g butter and 2 tablespoons sunflower oil in a wide pan, 8 minutes turning, until crisp; richer, less neat than baking.
- **Cheese-crusted (siera grauzdiņi):** Top the baked batons with grated cheese (Latvian Rūsiņš or Edam), grill 2 minutes until melted and bronzed.
- **Sweet version (kanela grauzdiņi):** Replace garlic and caraway with cinnamon and brown sugar; toss with butter, bake the same way; eat with whipped cream as a dessert.

## Serving
Serve in a wooden bowl with cold lager or kvass. They sit on the bar table with salted sprats, pickled cucumbers and a glass of Riga black balsam.

## Storage
- Keeps 5 days in an airtight tin; if they soften, refresh 4 minutes at 160°C.
- Freeze 1 month; refresh in the oven.
- The garlic dip keeps 3 days refrigerated; do not freeze.
