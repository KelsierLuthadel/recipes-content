# Nepalese

*The kitchen of the Himalayas. Dal bhat — lentils and rice — eaten twice a day by most Nepalis. The momo (steamed dumplings), the gundruk (fermented leafy greens), and a quietly distinct cuisine standing between Indian, Tibetan and Chinese traditions.*

## A short history

Nepalese cooking sits at the meeting of South Asian and Central Asian-Tibetan traditions. The basic shelf — rice (in the lower elevations), millet and buckwheat (in the mid-hills), barley and yak in the high Himalaya, dal (lentils) in dozens of forms, fermented leafy greens (gundruk and sinki — the Nepalese equivalent of sauerkraut), and a small set of spices used much more sparingly than in Indian cooking — has been the working kitchen for centuries.

Dal bhat — lentils-and-rice with a vegetable side and a small pickle — is the universal meal. "Dal bhat power, 24 hour" is a trekking-circuit slogan because the meal is what powers the porters who carry trekkers' loads. Most Nepalis eat dal bhat twice a day, every day.

Momos (steamed dumplings) are the second pillar. Introduced from Tibet (originally via Newar traders), they've become a Nepali national snack — eaten with tomato-and-sesame chutney (achar) in every roadside teashop.

## Regional variations

- **Kathmandu Valley (Newar cuisine)**: the distinct, refined urban tradition. Yomari (steamed sweet dumplings), bara (lentil patties), chatamari (rice-flour crepes — the Newar "pizza").
- **Terai (southern plains)**: closer to Indian (Bihari) cooking. More rice, more curry-style dishes.
- **Hills (central, where most Nepalis live)**: dal bhat country.
- **Himalayan north (Sherpa, Thakali, Tamang)**: closer to Tibetan. Thukpa, sha-bha-lay, tsampa.

## Signature ingredients

- **Rice (bhat)**: the everyday staple.
- **Lentils (dal — red, yellow, black, green)**: in dal bhat.
- **Mustard oil**: the cooking fat in the hills.
- **Fenugreek seeds**: tempered into dal.
- **Cumin and coriander**: the spice pair.
- **Gundruk**: fermented dried leafy mustard greens. Tart and pungent.
- **Sesame seeds**: ground into achar (chutney).
- **Timur (Sichuan peppercorn)**: the local pepper.

## Learn it in three recipes

1. **[Dal Bhat Tarkari](/recipes/cuisine/nepalese/dal-bhat-tarkari)**: lentils, rice, vegetable curry, achar (pickle). The universal Nepali meal — eaten twice daily by most of the country. Refills are free; eat till you're full.
2. **[Chicken Momos](/recipes/cuisine/nepalese/chicken-momos)**: steamed dumplings filled with spiced minced chicken, served with achar (tomato-sesame chutney). The national snack.
3. **[Aloo Tama](/recipes/cuisine/nepalese/aloo-tama)**: potato-and-bamboo-shoot curry with black-eyed peas. The signature Newar dish.

## Where to go next

Pair everything with chiya (milky-sweet spiced black tea) or with raksi (the local rice or millet spirit). For a proper Nepali table: dal bhat at the centre, momos as a snack between meals, gundruk on the side, achar with everything. Eat with the right hand from the metal thali plate.
