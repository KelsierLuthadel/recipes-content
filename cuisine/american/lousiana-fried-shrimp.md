# Louisiana Fried Shrimp

![Louisiana Fried Shrimp](resources/lousiana-fried-shrimp.jpg)

*Gulf Coast fried shrimp: large peeled shrimp marinated in evaporated milk and Louisiana hot sauce, dredged in flour and baking powder, flash-fried golden and shatter-crisp. Cocktail sauce or remoulade on the side.*

**Serves:** 4

**Prep Time:** 15 minutes (plus 1 hour marinade)

**Cook Time:** 10 minutes

## Overview
The Louisiana fried shrimp you order on the Gulf Coast comes by way of an evaporated-milk-and-hot-sauce marinade that's worth knowing about: the milk solids cling to the shrimp and help the flour coating grab; the vinegar in the hot sauce tenderises the protein; the egg in the marinade gives the breading a tiny bit of binder so it doesn't slip off in the fryer. An hour in the marinade is enough. The dredge is just flour and baking powder - the baking powder is the secret to a properly puffed, crackly crust. You fry at a hot 195°C in peanut oil so each shrimp cooks through in 90 seconds, gold and crisp without any greasy droop. Eat them with cocktail sauce, remoulade or a squeeze of lemon, on a paper-lined basket with cold beer.

## Ingredients

### Marinade
- 2 large eggs
- 150 ml evaporated milk
- 2 teaspoons distilled white vinegar
- 1 teaspoon Louisiana hot sauce (Crystal brand)
- 450 g large shrimp (peeled and deveined)

### Frying
- Kosher salt
- Freshly ground black pepper (or Cajun seasoning)
- Peanut oil or coconut oil, for frying
- 120 g plain flour
- 1 tablespoon baking powder

### To serve
- Cocktail sauce, remoulade or your favourite dipping sauce
- Lemon wedges

## Method

### Stage 1 - Marinate
1. In a bowl, whisk the eggs, evaporated milk, vinegar and hot sauce together until well blended.
2. Add the peeled shrimp; stir gently to coat.
3. Cover and refrigerate 1 hour.

### Stage 2 - Drain and season
1. Lift the shrimp out with a slotted spoon onto a plate; discard the marinade.
2. Season the shrimp lightly with salt and pepper (or a dusting of Cajun seasoning).

### Stage 3 - Heat the oil
1. Fill a large heavy saucepan with 5 cm of oil, no more than half full.
2. Heat over high heat to 195°C on a deep-frying thermometer.

### Stage 4 - Dredge
1. In a shallow bowl, whisk the flour and baking powder together.
2. One at a time, dredge each shrimp in the flour mixture, pressing lightly so the coating clings; shake off excess.

### Stage 5 - Fry in batches
1. Slip the dredged shrimp into the hot oil a few at a time (don't crowd the pan).
2. Fry 1-2 minutes, turning once or twice with tongs, until golden brown and curled.
3. Lift onto paper towels to drain.
4. Repeat with the remaining shrimp, letting the oil come back to 195°C between batches.

### Stage 6 - Season and serve
1. Season the just-drained shrimp with a little more salt if needed.
2. Pile on a paper-lined basket or plate.
3. Serve immediately with the dipping sauce and lemon wedges.

## Notes
- **Evaporated milk is the trick:** Don't substitute regular milk; the concentrated solids in evaporated milk are what give the coating its grip and its character.
- **Baking powder in the dredge:** This is what makes the coating puff and shatter rather than going hard. Don't skip it.
- **Right oil temperature:** 195°C is hotter than most fried foods. The high heat is what cooks the shrimp through in 90 seconds; lower and you end up with overcooked tough shrimp inside a properly browned crust.

## Storage
- Best fresh from the fryer.
- Leftovers refrigerate 1 day; reheat in a hot oven 5 minutes to re-crisp (the microwave makes them rubbery).
