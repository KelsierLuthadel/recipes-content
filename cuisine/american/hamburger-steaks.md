# Hamburger Steaks with Onion Gravy

![Hamburger Steaks with Onion Gravy](resources/hamburger-steaks.jpg)

*Southern comfort food: seasoned ground beef patties browned hard, then simmered in a rich beef-and-onion gravy built from beef stock, dried onion and a few pantry seasonings. Pairs with mashed potatoes and peas; a workday "Salisbury steak" that doesn't apologise for being simple.*

**Serves:** 6

**Prep Time:** 15 minutes (plus 1 hour resting)

**Cook Time:** 25 minutes

## Overview
American Southern diner food and a close cousin of Salisbury steak: browned ground beef patties simmered in a thick brown gravy of slow-cooked onions, beef stock and dried onion that gives the gravy its diner-counter character. American kitchens reach for a packet of Lipton Beefy Onion soup mix for this; UK kitchens build the same effect from a beef stock cube, dried onion flakes, onion granules and a little cornflour, which carries the same glutamate depth and dried-onion concentrate without the import shelf. The patties are seasoned bigger than a regular burger: Cajun seasoning, a warm cumin-coriander-paprika rub in place of US Sazon, garlic paste, Worcestershire and a touch of maple syrup or honey, so the meat itself carries flavour even before the gravy hits it. The result is salty-savoury-rich, with sweet caramelised onion in the gravy and a meaty depth that just goes on. The only thing that ruins it is overworking the beef mince, which makes the patties tough. Plated with mashed potatoes and peas is the Southern Sunday-lunch convention.

## Ingredients

### Patties
- 900 g (2 lbs) ground beef (85/15)
- ¼ cup chopped scallions (plus more to garnish)
- 1 tablespoon Cajun seasoning
- ½ teaspoon ground cumin
- ½ teaspoon ground coriander
- ½ teaspoon paprika (sweet or smoked)
- ½ teaspoon garlic granules
- ¼ teaspoon dried oregano
- 1 tablespoon garlic paste
- 1 tablespoon Worcestershire sauce
- 1 tablespoon maple syrup (or honey)
- 1 teaspoon dried minced onion

### Onion gravy
- 2 tablespoons olive oil (or avocado oil)
- 1 yellow onion (medium, roughly chopped)
- 1 beef stock cube, crumbled
- 2 teaspoons dried onion flakes
- 1 teaspoon onion granules
- 1 teaspoon cornflour
- pinch of caster sugar
- 2 tablespoons plain flour
- 480 ml beef stock (made up from a second cube, or fresh)

## Method

### Stage 1 - Mix and rest
1. Combine the ground beef with the scallions, Cajun seasoning, cumin, coriander, paprika, garlic granules, oregano, garlic paste, Worcestershire, maple syrup and dried minced onion.
1. Mix gently with the hands until just combined, don't overwork.
1. Refrigerate 1-24 hours.

### Stage 2 - Form and brown
1. Shape the mix into 12-13 patties.
1. Heat the oil in a large skillet over medium heat.
1. Brown the patties in batches, 3-4 minutes per side, until deeply seared.
1. Transfer to a plate.

### Stage 3 - Gravy
1. Reduce heat to medium-low.
1. Sauté the chopped onion in the same pan 5-6 minutes until golden, scraping up the fond.
1. Sprinkle the crumbled stock cube, onion flakes, onion granules, cornflour, sugar and the flour over; stir 1 minute to toast.
1. Slowly pour in the beef stock, whisking constantly.
1. Bring to a gentle boil; the gravy thickens.

### Stage 4 - Finish
1. Return the patties to the gravy.
1. Simmer 5-7 minutes, basting the patties.
1. Taste; adjust salt.
1. Garnish with fresh scallions.
1. Serve with mashed potatoes and peas.

## Notes
- **The onion gravy mix:** the stock cube, onion flakes, onion granules, cornflour and sugar listed above stand in for an American packet of Lipton Beefy Onion soup mix. If you can find a Knorr or Schwartz onion soup sachet that's a one-line swap. In a US kitchen, use one packet of Lipton Beefy Onion soup mix in place of these five lines.
- **The seasoning rub:** the cumin, coriander, paprika, garlic granules and oregano listed in the patties stand in for an American packet of Goya Sazon. Sazon's signature orange colour comes from annatto; add a small pinch of ground turmeric or annatto if you want the colour.
- **Don't overwork the beef:** a tough patty is the most common failure. Mix just to combine; don't knead.
- **Maple syrup / honey:** the subtle sweetness in the seasoning balances the savoury Worcestershire and warm spices.

## Storage
- Keeps 3 days refrigerated; reheats well in a covered pan with a splash of broth.
- Freezes 2 months.
