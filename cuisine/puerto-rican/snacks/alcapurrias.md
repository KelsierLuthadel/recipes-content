# Alcapurrias

![Alcapurrias](resources/alcapurrias.jpg)

*Puerto Rico's stuffed root-vegetable fritters: a thick masa of grated green banana and yautía (taro) wrapped around a spiced beef-and-olive filling, formed into long oblong shapes and deep-fried till the outside is deep golden and crispy and the inside stays soft. The Boricua street-cart classic, the traditional fritter of every beach stand on the island.*

**Serves:** 4-6 (about 12 alcapurrias)

**Prep Time:** 1 hour

**Cook Time:** 25 minutes

## Overview
Alcapurrias is one of Puerto Rico's most distinctive street snacks and a beloved beach-stand classic from Piñones to Luquillo. A thick savoury masa made from grated green banana and yautía (taro root), seasoned with achiote oil, wraps around a fragrant beef-and-olive picadillo filling. Formed into long oblong shapes about 12 cm long and deep-fried at 175 °C till the outside is deep golden and crispy while the inside stays soft and pillowy. The roots are non-negotiable: properly green unripe bananas and yautía give the right starchy slightly sweet base that fries beautifully (white sweet potato or yuca substitute for yautía if you can't find it; ripe bananas are wrong). Achiote oil gives the masa its characteristic orange-yellow colour, and the proper deep-fry temperature is the difference between crispy and oily-soggy. Eat immediately with a squeeze of lime and a cold Medalla beer or tropical fruit drink. The traditional Boricua beach-day snack.

## Ingredients

### Masa
- 4 large green bananas (about 600 g flesh)
- 600 g yautía (taro root; or white sweet potato; or yuca as substitute)
- 80 ml achiote oil (or 80 ml olive oil + 1 teaspoon turmeric)
- 1 ½ teaspoons fine sea salt
- 1 tablespoon sazón

### Filling (picadillo-style)
- 400 g minced beef (or pork; or 50/50)
- 2 tablespoons olive oil
- 3 tablespoons sofrito
- 1 medium onion (finely chopped)
- 4 garlic cloves (crushed)
- 1 small green bell pepper (finely chopped)
- 2 tablespoons tomato paste
- 200 ml tomato sauce
- 1 tablespoon sazón
- 1 teaspoon ground cumin
- 1 teaspoon dried oregano
- 1 teaspoon fine sea salt
- 1 teaspoon ground black pepper
- 50 g raisins
- 60 g pitted green olives (sliced)
- 1 tablespoon capers (drained)
- 2 tablespoons fresh coriander (chopped)

### Frying
- Vegetable oil for deep-frying (about 1.5 litres; for 7 cm depth)

### To serve
- Lime wedges
- Pique
- Salt
- Cold Medalla beer

## Method

### Stage 1 - Make the filling
1. Heat the olive oil in a wide pan over medium heat.
2. Add the sofrito, chopped onion and bell pepper; cook 6 minutes till soft.
3. Add the crushed garlic; cook 30 seconds.
4. Add the minced beef; brown for 6-7 minutes, breaking up with a wooden spoon.
5. Add the tomato paste and tomato sauce; cook 3 minutes.
6. Add the sazón, cumin, oregano, salt and pepper.
7. Add the raisins, olives and capers.
8. Simmer 10 minutes till the filling is thick (not wet) and the flavours have melded.
9. Stir in the chopped coriander.
10. Cool to room temperature.

### Stage 2 - Prepare the masa vegetables
1. Peel the green bananas (use a knife; the skin is tough). Cut into chunks.
2. Peel the yautía with a sharp knife (the skin is fibrous). Cut into chunks.
3. Submerge the chunks in cold water (prevents browning) till ready to grate.

### Stage 3 - Grate the masa
1. Drain the bananas and yautía.
2. Grate on the fine side of a box grater (or pulse in batches in a food processor till finely grated; not pureed).
3. Place in a wide bowl.
4. Add the achiote oil, salt and sazón.
5. Mix thoroughly with a wooden spoon till uniform; the masa should be a thick orange-yellow paste.

### Stage 4 - Shape the alcapurrias
1. Tear a 25 cm × 20 cm piece of parchment paper or a banana leaf rectangle.
2. Wet your hands with cold water (prevents sticking).
3. Place 3 tablespoons of masa on the paper/leaf; flatten into a 12 cm × 7 cm rectangle, about 8 mm thick.
4. Place 1.5 tablespoons of filling down the centre of the masa, leaving a 1.5 cm border at each side.
5. Lift the long edges of the paper to fold the masa over the filling, sealing the filling inside.
6. Press the edges to seal.
7. Smooth into a long oblong shape (about 12 cm long, 4 cm wide); seam-side down.
8. Transfer to a tray.
9. Repeat with the remaining masa and filling; you should have 12 alcapurrias.

### Stage 5 - Heat the oil
1. Pour vegetable oil into a deep heavy pot or fryer to a depth of 7 cm.
2. Heat to 175°C (350°F).

### Stage 6 - Deep-fry
1. Lower 2-3 alcapurrias into the hot oil with a slotted spoon.
2. Fry 5-6 minutes, turning occasionally, till the outside is deeply golden-brown and crispy.
3. Lift out; drain on kitchen paper.
4. Continue with the remaining alcapurrias in batches.

### Stage 7 - Serve immediately
1. Pile the alcapurrias on a plate.
2. Lime wedges, pique and an extra sprinkle of salt on the side.
3. Eat hot with a cold beer.

## Notes
- **Green bananas + yautía:** the traditional PR masa. Substitute with white sweet potato or yuca if yautía is unavailable.
- **Fine grate:** the proper texture is finely grated, not pureed. The masa should still have some bite.
- **Cool the filling:** hot filling can melt the masa during assembly. Properly cooled filling is essential.
- **175°C oil:** higher burns the outside; lower gives oily fritters.
- **Don't overcrowd the fryer:** 2-3 at a time. Overcrowded fritters drop the oil temperature.

## Variations
- **Crab alcapurrias (alcapurrias de jueyes):** swap the beef filling for cooked crab meat with sofrito; a coastal Puerto Rican specialty.
- **Chicken alcapurrias:** swap the beef for shredded cooked chicken; lighter version.
- **Vegetarian alcapurrias:** fill with a mixture of mashed sweet potato, cheese, sautéed mushrooms and olives.
- **Mini alcapurrias:** make smaller (5 cm long); 24 mini fritters for party trays.

## Serving
- Hot from the fryer with lime wedges and pique on the side. Cold beer or fresh limeade. At beach stands, weekend parties, or as a starter before a Boricua family meal.

## Storage
- Best eaten immediately; the texture suffers as they cool.
- Refrigerated 2 days; reheat in a hot oven (200°C / 400°F) for 6-8 minutes to re-crisp.
- Uncooked alcapurrias freeze 2 months on a tray, then transferred to a bag; cook from frozen at 165°C for 7-8 minutes.
- Don't microwave; they go limp.
