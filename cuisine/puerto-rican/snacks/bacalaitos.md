# Bacalaítos

![Bacalaitos](resources/bacalaitos.jpg)

*Puerto Rico's salt-cod fritters: thin lacy pancakes of flour-and-water batter studded with shredded desalted cod and fresh cilantro, fried in shallow oil till the edges crisp golden and the centre stays tender. The Boricua beach-stall classic, eaten standing up with a squeeze of lime and a beer.*

**Serves:** 4 (about 12 fritters)

**Prep Time:** 25 minutes (plus overnight cod desalting)

**Cook Time:** 25 minutes

## Overview
Bacalaítos (literally "little salt-cods") is one of Puerto Rico's most beloved street snacks and a fixture of every beach stand and fonda across the island. Thin lacy pancakes made from a flour-and-water batter studded with shredded desalted salt cod (bacalao), finely chopped onion, fresh cilantro and culantro, crushed garlic, sazón and a touch of baking powder. The batter drops by the ladleful into shallow hot oil and spreads into 12 cm rounds, frying till the edges go golden-brown and crispy and the centre stays slightly tender. Desalting the cod properly is essential: salt cod is heavily cured and needs twelve to twenty-four hours of soaking in cold water with several changes, or the fritters come out wildly oversalted. The batter wants to be thin enough to spread into a round in the pan, since thick batter gives heavy doughy fritters. The lacy crispy edges are the goal, not uniform thickness. Eat immediately with a squeeze of lime and an ice-cold Medalla beer.

## Ingredients

### Salt cod
- 400 g salt cod (bacalao); desalted (see method)
- 2 litres cold water (for desalting)

### Batter
- 250 g plain flour
- 1 ½ teaspoons baking powder
- 1 teaspoon fine sea salt (taste first; the cod is salty)
- 1 tablespoon [Sazón](../../../base-ingredients/spices/sazon.md)
- 1 teaspoon dried oregano
- 1 teaspoon ground cumin
- 1 teaspoon ground black pepper
- 1 teaspoon ground turmeric (optional, for colour)
- 350-400 ml cold water (start with 350, add more if needed)

### Aromatics
- 1 small onion (very finely chopped)
- 4 garlic cloves (crushed)
- 1 small green bell pepper (very finely chopped)
- 1 large bunch fresh cilantro (about 30 g; finely chopped)
- 1 small bunch fresh culantro/recao (about 15 g; finely chopped): or double the cilantro
- 1 small fresh chilli (deseeded, finely chopped, optional)

### Frying
- Vegetable oil for shallow frying (1 cm depth in a wide pan)

### To serve
- Lime wedges
- Pique (Puerto Rican vinegar hot sauce)
- Mayo-ketchup dipping sauce
- Ice-cold Medalla beer

## Method

### Stage 1 - Desalt the cod (24 hours ahead)
1. Place the salt cod in a wide bowl.
2. Cover with cold water by 5 cm.
3. Refrigerate; change the water every 4-6 hours.
4. Total soaking time: 18-24 hours.
5. Test by tasting a small piece raw: should be pleasantly salty but not strongly so.
6. Drain and pat dry.

### Stage 2 - Cook and shred the cod
1. Place the desalted cod in a saucepan; cover with cold water; bring to a simmer.
2. Cook 8-10 minutes till the cod flakes easily.
3. Drain; cool slightly.
4. Remove any skin and bones (if present in your cod).
5. Shred into thin flakes with two forks or your fingers.

### Stage 3 - Make the batter
1. In a wide bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, salt, sazón, oregano, cumin, pepper and turmeric.
2. Add 350 ml of cold water; whisk to a smooth batter.
3. The batter should be the consistency of a thin pancake batter (about like single cream).
4. Add more water 1 tablespoon at a time if needed to reach the right thinness.

### Stage 4 - Add the cod and aromatics
1. Stir in the shredded cod, finely chopped onion, crushed garlic, finely chopped green pepper, chopped cilantro, chopped culantro and chopped chilli.
2. Mix gently; the batter should now be a thin pancake batter studded with the cod and herbs.
3. Taste; the batter should taste pleasantly salty (don't add more salt; the cod adds plenty).

### Stage 5 - Heat the oil
1. Pour vegetable oil into a wide heavy frying pan to a depth of 1 cm.
2. Heat over medium-high heat till 180°C (350°F).
3. Test with a small spoonful of batter; it should sizzle aggressively and brown in 60 seconds.

### Stage 6 - Fry the bacalaítos
1. Pour a ladleful (about 80 ml) of batter into the hot oil.
2. Use the bottom of the ladle to spread the batter into a thin 12 cm round.
3. Fry 2-3 minutes per side till deeply golden-brown and crispy at the edges.
4. The edges should be lacy and dark; the centre slightly tender.
5. Lift out with a slotted spoon; drain briefly on kitchen paper.
6. Continue with the remaining batter; you should get 12-15 fritters total.

### Stage 7 - Serve immediately
1. Pile the bacalaítos on a serving plate.
2. Provide lime wedges, pique and mayo-ketchup for dipping.
3. Pour cold beer.
4. Eat hot; bacalaítos go off-texture as they cool.

## Notes
- **Desalt the cod properly:** the 18-24 hour cold-water soak with water changes is essential. Skipping gives wildly oversalted fritters.
- **Thin batter:** the traditional bacalaítos has lacy crispy edges. Thick batter gives doughy fritters that aren't right.
- **Don't add salt to the batter:** the cod brings plenty. Taste before adding any.
- **Cilantro and culantro:** both for the proper PR flavour. If you can find culantro/recao at a Caribbean market, use it.
- **Spread thin in the pan:** use the ladle to spread the batter into a 12 cm round before it sets. Aim for thin and lacy.

## Variations
- **Spicier bacalaítos:** add 1 chopped habanero pepper to the batter; properly Caribbean fierce version.
- **With crab instead of cod:** swap the salt cod for 200 g of cooked crab meat (no desalting needed); gives a more luxurious variation.
- **Vegetarian fritters (bacalaítos sin bacalao):** skip the cod; double the herbs; add 100 g of grated cheese (queso fresco or feta) for protein/flavour; less traditional but works.
- **Mini bacalaítos:** use teaspoons of batter instead of ladles; makes 30+ tiny fritters perfect for party canapés.

## Serving
- On a serving plate, hot from the pan. Lime wedges and pique for dipping. Mayo-ketchup sauce for a more substantial dip. Cold Medalla beer or fresh limeade. At beach stands, fondas, or Boricua family parties.

## Storage
- Best eaten immediately; they go off-texture as they cool.
- Keep refrigerated 2 days; reheat in a hot oven (200°C / 400°F) for 4 minutes or in an air fryer.
- Don't microwave; they go limp.
- The batter is best fresh; if you must keep it, refrigerate up to 4 hours.
- Don't freeze fried bacalaítos.
