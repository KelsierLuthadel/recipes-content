# Rösti

![Rösti](resources/rosti.jpg)

*Swiss-German fried potato cake: coarsely grated potatoes pressed into a pan and fried until golden and crisp on both sides. The Bern breakfast that became a national dish.*

**Serves:** 4

**Prep Time:** 10 minutes (plus parboiled potatoes from the day before)

**Cook Time:** 25 minutes

## Overview
Rösti is the Swiss-German answer to fried potato: coarsely grated waxy potatoes packed into a pan, fried in butter until the bottom forms a golden, lacy crust, then flipped (with a plate) and fried on the other side. The version that became the national dish comes from Bern, where farmers ate it for breakfast. The trick is using parboiled potatoes from the day before - the slightly dried, set starches grate into clean strands rather than glueing into mush. Pure rösti is just potato, butter and salt; popular additions are onion, bacon (Berner rösti), grated cheese (käserösti), or a fried egg on top. The Röstigraben - "rösti ditch" - is the cultural-linguistic divide between French- and German-speaking Switzerland, with rösti firmly on the German side.

## Ingredients
- 800 g waxy potatoes (Charlotte, Maris Piper for mash they're not - waxy is the key)
- 1 tsp salt
- 50 g unsalted butter
- 1 tbsp vegetable oil (raises the smoke point of the butter)
- Freshly ground black pepper
- A pinch of grated nutmeg (optional)

### Optional add-ins
- 1 small onion, finely diced (Berner-style)
- 100 g lardons or diced bacon (Berner-style)
- 80 g grated Gruyère (käserösti)
- 4 fried eggs to serve

## Method

### Stage 1 - Parboil the potatoes (day before)
1. Place whole potatoes (skin on) in a large pot of cold salted water.
2. Bring to a boil; simmer 8-10 minutes. The potatoes should be barely tender on the outside, still firm inside (a knife meets resistance at the centre).
3. Drain; cool completely.
4. Refrigerate uncovered overnight - the surfaces dry and the starches set.

### Stage 2 - Grate
1. Peel the cold parboiled potatoes (the skins slip off easily).
2. Grate on the coarse side of a box grater into long shreds.
3. Season the grated potato with salt, pepper and nutmeg; toss gently with your fingers to distribute evenly.

### Stage 3 - Optional add-ins
1. If using bacon: fry the lardons in a dry pan over medium heat 4-5 minutes until crisp; drain on kitchen paper, reserving the fat.
2. If using onion: soften in the bacon fat (or 1 tbsp butter) over low heat 6-8 minutes until translucent; cool slightly.
3. Mix the bacon and/or onion into the grated potato.

### Stage 4 - Fry the first side
1. Heat 30 g butter and the oil in a heavy-based 26 cm non-stick or cast-iron frying pan over medium heat.
2. When the butter foams, add the grated potato and spread evenly across the pan.
3. Press down firmly with a spatula to form a compact cake (about 1.5 cm thick).
4. Reduce heat to medium-low; cook 10-12 minutes without disturbing.
5. The bottom should be deeply golden and crisp - lift an edge with the spatula to check.

### Stage 5 - Flip
1. Place a large plate (the same size as the pan or slightly larger) upside-down over the pan.
2. Holding the plate firmly against the pan with one hand on top and the pan handle in the other, flip in one swift motion. The rösti lands on the plate, crisp side up.
3. Add the remaining 20 g butter to the pan; let it foam.
4. Slide the rösti back into the pan, uncooked side down.
5. Press down again with the spatula.
6. Cook a further 10-12 minutes until the second side is also deeply golden.

### Stage 6 - Optional cheese version
1. For käserösti, scatter grated Gruyère over the top of the rösti during the last 2 minutes of cooking.
2. Cover with a lid briefly to melt.

### Stage 7 - Serve
1. Slide onto a plate or board.
2. Cut into wedges.
3. Top with fried eggs if making the breakfast version.

## Notes
- **Parboil the day before:** The single most important step. Freshly grated raw potato sticks together and turns to mush in the pan. Day-old cold parboiled potato grates into discrete crispy strands.
- **Coarse grater:** Box grater largest holes. A fine grater gives an unfortunate watery hash; a food processor shreds too finely.
- **Don't move it:** Once the potato is pressed into the pan, leave it alone for the full 10-12 minutes. Stirring or lifting prematurely breaks the crust.
- **The plate flip:** A scary moment that becomes routine with practice. The pan should be just hot enough that the rösti releases cleanly; if it sticks, slide a spatula gently around and under first.

## Serving
The Berner rösti with bacon and onion is breakfast or lunch with a fried egg on top, sometimes with a slice of cheese. The plain rösti is the Swiss-German "potato side" served alongside Zürcher Geschnetzeltes (Zurich-style veal in cream sauce), sausages with onion gravy, or roast pork. A small glass of dry white Swiss wine works for adults; apple juice (Apfelschorle) for everyone.

## Storage
- Best fresh and crispy from the pan.
- Leftover rösti refrigerates 2 days; reheat in a dry pan over medium heat (the microwave makes it soggy).
- Freezes 2 months wrapped tightly; reheat from frozen in a 200°C oven 20 minutes.
