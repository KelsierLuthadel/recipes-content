# Käsekuchen (Swiss Cheese Tart)

![Käsekuchen](resources/kaesekuchen.jpg)

*Savoury Swiss cheese tart: shortcrust base filled with a custard of Gruyère, Emmental, eggs and cream, baked until puffed and golden. Slice into slim wedges and eat as a snack, lunch, or with a glass of white wine.*

**Serves:** 8 as a snack, 4 as a light meal

**Prep Time:** 20 minutes (plus 1 hour pastry rest)

**Cook Time:** 35 minutes

## Overview
Käsekuchen in Switzerland means the savoury cheese tart, not the sweet German cheesecake (which is the same word but a different dish). The Swiss version is a deep-dish pastry shell filled with a custard of grated Alpine cheese - Gruyère, Emmental, or a mix - bound with eggs, cream and a little flour, and baked until the top puffs into a deep golden dome. It's a working lunch dish across German-speaking Switzerland, sold in every bakery (Bäckerei) as a hot wedge from a refrigerated counter, picked up with the fingers or a fork. The crust is buttery short pastry; the filling is set just enough to slice cleanly while still wobbling slightly. A side salad turns it into dinner.

## Ingredients

### Shortcrust pastry (or use 350 g bought)
- 250 g plain flour
- 125 g cold unsalted butter, diced
- A pinch of salt
- 1 egg yolk
- 60 ml ice water

### Filling
- 250 g Gruyère, grated
- 150 g Emmental, grated
- 4 medium eggs
- 250 ml double cream
- 150 ml whole milk
- 2 tbsp plain flour
- A pinch of grated nutmeg
- Freshly ground black pepper
- 1 small onion, finely diced and sweated in butter (optional)
- 80 g smoked bacon lardons, fried crisp (optional)

## Method

### Stage 1 - Pastry
1. Combine the flour and salt in a bowl.
2. Rub in the cold butter until the mixture resembles fine breadcrumbs.
3. Stir in the egg yolk; add the ice water a tablespoon at a time until the dough comes together.
4. Form into a disc, wrap in cling film, refrigerate 1 hour.

### Stage 2 - Line the tin
1. Roll the pastry on a lightly floured surface into a 30 cm round.
2. Line a 23 cm deep tart tin (loose-bottomed); press into the corners.
3. Trim the overhang to leave a 1 cm rim above the edge of the tin (the pastry shrinks slightly as it bakes).
4. Prick the base all over with a fork.
5. Refrigerate 15 minutes.

### Stage 3 - Blind bake
1. Preheat the oven to 200°C.
2. Line the pastry with greaseproof paper; fill with baking beans or rice.
3. Bake 15 minutes.
4. Remove the paper and beans; bake a further 5 minutes until the base is dry and just starting to colour.
5. Remove from oven; reduce heat to 180°C.

### Stage 4 - Filling
1. In a large jug, whisk together the eggs, cream, milk and flour until smooth.
2. Add the nutmeg and a generous twist of black pepper. Taste; the cheese is salty so add salt cautiously, if at all.
3. Scatter half the grated cheese over the warm pastry base.
4. If using onion and bacon, scatter them over the cheese.
5. Pour in the egg mixture, filling to within 5 mm of the rim.
6. Scatter the remaining cheese over the top.

### Stage 5 - Bake
1. Carefully transfer to the oven (the filling is liquid - watch the wobble).
2. Bake at 180°C for 30-35 minutes until the top is deeply golden, puffed, and the filling is set but still wobbles slightly in the centre.

### Stage 6 - Rest and serve
1. Cool in the tin 10 minutes (the filling sets fully as it cools).
2. Remove from the tin; slide onto a board.
3. Slice into wedges; serve warm.

## Notes
- **Cheese mix:** The Gruyère gives savoury depth; the Emmental gives stretchy melt. Using one alone works but gives a one-note tart. Comté, Beaufort, or a mature cheddar can substitute.
- **Don't over-bake:** A slight wobble in the centre is the sign of perfectly set custard. Overbaked, the filling weeps water and the texture is rubbery.
- **Pastry must be cold going into the oven:** Otherwise the butter melts before the structure sets and the sides slump. Chill thoroughly between rolling and blind-baking.

## Serving
Serve warm in wedges with a green salad (vinaigrette, no creamy dressing - the tart is rich enough). A glass of dry Chasselas or a light Pinot. Or cold the next day as a packed lunch.

## Storage
- Refrigerates 3 days; eat cold or reheat in a 160°C oven 10 minutes to crisp the pastry.
- Freezes 2 months whole or in wedges; thaw in the fridge before reheating.
- Microwaving makes the pastry soggy; use the oven.
