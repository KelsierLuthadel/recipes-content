# Thai-Style Chilli Garlic Edamame

![Thai-Style Chilli Garlic Edamame](resources/chilli-garlic-edamame.jpg)

*A Thai-style edamame snack: pods tossed in a hot wok with garlic, dried chillies, soy and a splash of sesame oil.*

**Serves:** 4 as a starter

**Prep Time:** 5 minutes

**Cook Time:** 10 minutes

## Overview
Edamame tossed with chilli, garlic and soy is the small bowl that turns up at the start of so many Thai meals, hot pods of young soybeans picked up with the fingers and scraped between the teeth so the beans pop out and the flavoured oil clinging to the pod goes with them. Buy them in the pod (not shelled); the pods aren't eaten but they're the delivery mechanism for all the seasoning. Frozen edamame in their pods are sold at every Asian grocer and in the freezer section of most supermarkets. Bring a saucepan of water to the boil, drop the pods in and simmer over medium heat for three minutes till they're just tender and bright green, then drain and keep warm. In the serving bowl, whisk finely chopped garlic, a splash of vegetable oil and a tablespoon of toasted sesame oil with chilli flakes and light soy sauce, taste and adjust the heat to your liking. Tip the hot drained edamame straight into the bowl and toss thoroughly so every pod is coated in the chilli-garlic oil. Season with flaky sea salt and serve immediately while the pods are still warm; eat by holding the stem end and pulling the pod through your teeth so the beans and flavoured oil come together in the same bite.

## Ingredients
### Vegetables
- 500g (1lb 2oz) edamame beans in the pod

### Aromatics
- 3 garlic cloves, finely chopped

### Fat
- 1 ½ tsp rapeseed (canola) oil
- 1 tbsp sesame oil

### Seasonings
- 1 tbsp red chilli flakes
- 1 tbsp light soy sauce*
- Flaky sea salt, to taste

## Method

### Stage 1 - Cook edamame
1. Bring a saucepan of water to a boil.
1. Add the edamame and simmer over a medium heat for 3 minutes, then drain and keep warm.

### Stage 2 - Make marinade
1. Whisk together the garlic, both oils, chilli flakes and soy sauce in a serving bowl.
1. Adjust the flavour by adding more chilli flakes if desired.

### Stage 3 - Combine and serve
1. Add the hot edamame and stir well to combine.
1. Season with flaky salt to taste and serve immediately.

## Notes
* Many soy sauces contain gluten but gluten-free brands are available.

## Serving
- Serve hot as a starter.

## Storage
- Best served immediately; doesn't store well.
