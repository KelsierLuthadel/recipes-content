# Thai Prawn Toast

![Thai Prawn Toast](resources/prawn-toast.jpg)

*Thailand's prawn toast: prawn paste seasoned with garlic, coriander root and fish sauce.*

**Serves:** 6 or more

**Prep Time:** 15 minutes

**Cook Time:** 5 minutes

## Overview
Thai prawn toast is not the thin-smeared takeaway version, this is a proper prawn mountain piled high on a thick slice of baguette and deep-fried till the bread underneath turns golden and the prawn paste sets into a crisp scattered with sesame seeds. The pile is the dish; skimping on the topping defeats the whole point. Drop peeled and deveined prawns into a food processor with finely chopped coriander stalks, finely chopped kaffir lime leaves, garlic, fish sauce, an egg white and a teaspoon of sugar, then blend to a thick somewhat gooey paste like a soft dough. Slice a French baguette into 2 cm thick slabs and lay them on a clean work surface, then top each with a generous mound of the prawn paste (pile it high, don't be shy) and press the topping firmly down onto the bread so it sticks during frying. Sprinkle sesame seeds across each mound (a light scatter or a full coating, your choice; the seeds toast in the oil and add a nutty crunch). Heat 10 cm of oil in a wide saucepan or wok to 180°C, then carefully lower the slices in prawn-side-down and fry for two minutes till the prawn coating turns light brown. Flip and fry the bread side another minute or so to crisp the underside, lift onto kitchen paper to drain. Serve hot with sweet chilli sauce for dipping.

## Ingredients

### Protein
- 500g (1lb 2oz) peeled and deveined prawns (shrimp)

### Aromatics
- 10 coriander (fresh coriander) stalks, finely chopped (about 2 generous tbsp)
- 6 lime leaves, de-stalked and finely chopped
- 4 garlic cloves, finely chopped

### Seasoning
- 2 tbsp Thai fish sauce
- 1 egg white
- ½ tsp white sugar

### Other
- 1 French baguette, sliced into 2cm (¾in) thick slices
- Sesame seeds (for sprinkling)

### Fat
- Rapeseed (canola) oil, for frying

### Serving
- Sweet chilli sauce

## Method

### Stage 1 - Prepare Paste
1. Place the prawns (shrimp) in a food processor.
2. Add the coriander, lime leaves, garlic, fish sauce, egg white and sugar and blend to a thick paste that is somewhat gooey, like a soft dough.

### Stage 2 - Assemble
1. Lay the bread slices on a clean work surface and top each with a generous amount of the prawn mixture.
2. I like to pile them high like a mound - no skimping allowed!
3. Press the prawn mounds firmly in place on each piece of bread.
4. Sprinkle with sesame seeds. You could just sprinkle a few on or go all out and coat the whole top with them - that’s up to you.

### Stage 3 - Fry
1. Heat about 10cm (4in) of oil in a large saucepan or wok over a medium-high heat.
2. You are aiming for a frying temperature of 180°C (350°F).
3. When your oil is ready, carefully place the bread, prawn-side down, in the oil and fry for 2 minutes until the prawn coating is turning a delicious light brown colour (cook in batches if necessary).
4. Flip the toasts over and fry for another minute or so to colour the other side.
5. Transfer with a slotted spoon to paper towels to soak up any excess oil.
6. Serve hot.

## Notes
- Pile high.

## Serving
Serve hot with sweet chilli sauce.

## Storage
- Best served immediately.
