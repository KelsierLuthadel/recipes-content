# Yellow Curry Paste

![Yellow Curry Paste](resources/thai-yellow-curry-paste.jpg)

*Thailand's yellow curry paste: turmeric, dried chillies, lemongrass, galangal and shrimp paste pounded into a mild golden base.*

**Makes:** Approx. 250 ml (1 cup)

**Prep Time:** 40-60 minutes

**Cook Time:** 5 minutes

## Overview
Yellow curry paste is the mildest of the Thai pastes, the southern-Muslim cousin to red and green with Indian influences showing through in the turmeric, coriander and cardamom that give it a gold colour and a gentler warmth. Fresh turmeric is worth tracking down (Asian grocers sell small fingers in the fresh veg section) because it gives a fresher flavour and the moisture helps the paste come together; ground turmeric works as a substitute but lacks the same depth. Toast coriander seeds, cardamom seeds and an optional pinch of green cardamom in a dry pan till fragrant, then pound to a fine powder with white pepper in a mortar. Add the soaked-and-chopped dried bird's-eye chillies and pound to a paste, then in go smashed garlic, sliced galangal, peeled-and-sliced fresh turmeric, finely chopped kaffir lime leaves, halved shallots, chopped coriander stalks and sliced lemongrass. Pound for 40 to 60 minutes till smooth and buttery, then stir in a teaspoon of shrimp paste and pound till incorporated. Wear gloves; fresh turmeric stains hands yellow for days. Stores 2 weeks refrigerated in an airtight jar; freezes 2 months in small portions. Use in yellow chicken curry with potatoes, or in any milder Thai curry where you want spice warmth without the chilli fire of red or green.

## Ingredients
### Whole spices
- 1 ½ tsp coriander seeds
- 1 ½ tsp cardamom seeds
- ½ tsp green cardamom seeds (optional)
- 1 ½ tsp white pepper

### Chillies and aromatics
- 12 dried red bird’s eye chillies, soaked in water for 30 minutes and cut into small pieces
- 12 garlic cloves
- 1 thumb-sized piece galangal, thinly sliced
- 1 thumb-sized piece fresh turmeric, peeled and thinly sliced, or 1-1 ½ tsp ground turmeric
- 3 lime leaves, stalks removed and finely chopped
- 3 shallots (medium), halved
- 10 thick coriander stalks (about 1 generous tbsp)
- 2 tbsp sliced lemongrass (½ lemongrass stalk)

### Paste
- 1 tsp shrimp paste

## Method

### Stage 1 - Toast and grind spices
1. Heat pan over medium-high heat; toast whole spices until fragrant but not smoking.
1. Transfer to pestle and mortar; pound to fine powder with white pepper.

### Stage 2 - Pound to paste
1. Add dried chillies; pound to paste.
1. Add garlic, galangal, turmeric, lime leaves, shallots, coriander stalks, and lemongrass.
1. Pound 40-60 mins until smooth and buttery.

### Stage 3 - Add shrimp paste
1. Add shrimp paste; pound to incorporate.
1. Check seasoning.

## Notes
- Fresh turmeric stains; use gloves.
- Use mortar and pestle for best flavor.
- Keeps 2 weeks refrigerated; freezes 2 months.

## Serving
- Not served directly; used in yellow curries.

## Storage
- Refrigerate 2 weeks in airtight container.
- Freeze up to 2 months; thaw before use.
