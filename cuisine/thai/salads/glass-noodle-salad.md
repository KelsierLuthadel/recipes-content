# Glass Noodle Salad

![Glass Noodle Salad](resources/glass-noodle-salad.jpg)

*Thailand's yum woon sen: cellophane glass noodles tossed with prawns, pork mince, herbs, lime, fish sauce and chilli.*

**Serves:** 2

**Prep Time:** 15 minutes

**Cook Time:** 15 minutes

## Overview
Yum woon sen is Thailand's glass-noodle salad, slippery transparent cellophane noodles tossed warm with prawns, pork mince, herbs and a sharp lime-and-fish-sauce dressing pounded with garlic and dried shrimp. The noodles are the unusual part; cellophane glass noodles made from mung bean starch (not rice), sold in nests at any Asian grocer, soaked briefly in warm water and then boiled for just two minutes till slippery and transparent. Pound the dressing in a mortar (or a small bowl with a wooden spoon will do): soaked dried shrimp, garlic, coriander stalks, fresh bird's-eye chillies and palm sugar to a coarse paste, then loosen with fish sauce and lime juice and taste for the four-note balance. Boil the noodles, drain, then in the same water cook prawns for two minutes till just pink and the pork mince for another five minutes till cooked through. Tip baby plum tomatoes, sliced shallots and chopped celery with leaves into a salad bowl, layer the cooked prawns and pork over, top with the noodles and pour the dressing across. Toss thoroughly so the noodles separate and every strand gets dressed, then scatter chopped cashews and fresh coriander across the top. Serve immediately while still warm or chilled for half an hour, ideally alongside grilled meats at a Thai barbecue.

## Ingredients
### Noodles
- 100 g (3 ½ oz) raw glass noodles

### Vegetables
- 6 baby plum tomatoes, quartered
- 4 shallots, thinly sliced
- 1 stick celery with leaves, thinly sliced

### Protein
- 10 small (or a few large) raw prawns (shrimp), peeled and deveined
- 125 g (4 ½ oz) minced (ground) pork

### Nuts and herbs
- 10 roasted cashews, roughly chopped
- 3 tbsp roughly chopped coriander (fresh coriander) leaves

### Dressing
- 1 tbsp dried shrimp, soaked in warm water for 10 minutes
- 3 garlic cloves, smashed
- 2 tbsp finely chopped coriander (fresh coriander) stalks
- 3 red bird’s eye chillies, roughly chopped
- 1 tbsp palm sugar, grated
- 3 tbsp Thai fish sauce
- 3 ½ tbsp lime juice

## Method

### Stage 1 - Soak noodles
1. Soak noodles in warm water 10 mins until soft.

### Stage 2 - Make dressing
1. Pound soaked shrimp, garlic, coriander stalks, chillies, and palm sugar in pestle and mortar to paste.
1. Stir in fish sauce and lime juice; taste and adjust.

### Stage 3 - Prepare salad base
1. Place tomatoes, shallots, and celery in salad bowl.

### Stage 4 - Cook noodles and prawns
1. Boil water; cook soaked noodles 2 mins.
1. Drain; set aside.
1. In same pan, keep 125 ml (½ cup) water; cook prawns 2 mins until done.
1. Transfer prawns to rest on noodles.

### Stage 5 - Cook pork
1. Add minced pork to remaining water; cook on high, stirring, until water dissolves and pork is cooked (~5 mins).

### Stage 6 - Assemble salad
1. Add prawns and pork to salad bowl.
1. Top with cooked noodles.
1. Pour dressing over; toss to separate noodles.
1. Add cashews and coriander; stir.
1. Serve immediately or chill 30 mins.

## Notes
- Many Thai fish sauces contain gluten; use gluten-free.
- Adjust chillies for heat.
- Serve hot for best flavor.

## Serving
- Serve as side or main with Thai barbecue.
- Garnish with extra herbs.

## Storage
- Refrigerate 1 day in airtight container.
- Best fresh; dressing may separate.
