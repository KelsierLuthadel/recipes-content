# Bosnian Cuisine

![Bosnian Cuisine](resources/hero.jpg)

Five hundred years of Ottoman rule left the deepest imprint on Bosnian food: ćevapi grilled over coals and tucked into puffed somun, coiled pita stuffed with cheese or spiced meat, syrup-soaked baklava and walnut-stuffed tufahije, the small grainy bosanska kafa served in a fildžan with a sugar cube on the saucer. Habsburg years from the late nineteenth century added a central-European edge, schnitzel-style breaded cuts, sour cream and paprika, strudel pastries that sit comfortably beside the Turkish ones. Slow-pot cooking is the rural backbone, layered meat and vegetables into a clay sač or earthenware lonac and left for hours over wood embers, the lid sealed with dough or weighted with coals. Every meal closes with kafa in a Sarajevo kafana, three or four people around a small copper tray, the conversation slower than the coffee.
