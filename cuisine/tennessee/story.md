# Tennessee

*The state where Southern cooking, BBQ smoke, country-music kitchens and the world's most famous whiskey distillery converge on one table. Memphis dry-rubbed ribs, Nashville hot chicken, and the catfish fry-out — Tennessee is barbecue country with a fried-everything afterthought.*

## A short history

Tennessee cooking is a patchwork of three regional kitchens. East Tennessee (Knoxville, the Smoky Mountains) sits on Appalachian cooking — corn, beans, pork, the moonshine tradition. Middle Tennessee (Nashville, the Cumberland) is the country-music kitchen — fried chicken, hot chicken, biscuits and gravy, the meat-and-three plate lunch. West Tennessee (Memphis, the Delta) is BBQ country — slow-smoked pork shoulder pulled into sandwiches, dry-rubbed ribs that don't need sauce, and the smoky tomato-vinegar dressing that defines Memphis-style.

Memphis BBQ developed its distinctive identity in the early 20th century at family-owned joints like Cozy Corner, Charles Vergos' Rendezvous (the dry-rubbed rib pioneers), and Payne's. The technique — long, slow smoke over hickory; a tomato-vinegar mop sauce; dry rub heavier than wet sauce on ribs — set the Memphis style apart from Texas (beef-led), Kansas City (sweeter sauce), and the Carolinas (mustard or vinegar sauces).

Nashville hot chicken is the more recent regional invention — Prince's Hot Chicken Shack, founded in the 1930s, codified the now-famous extra-spicy fried chicken with cayenne-and-paprika-soaked oil brushed over the crust. The 2010s saw hot chicken explode nationally; Nashville now has it as a tourism magnet alongside the country-music industry.

## Regional variations

- **West Tennessee (Memphis)**: BBQ capital. Dry-rubbed ribs, pulled pork sandwiches with slaw, smoked chicken, the BBQ-spaghetti hybrid.
- **Middle Tennessee (Nashville)**: hot chicken, country ham, meat-and-three plates, biscuits and milk gravy.
- **East Tennessee (Knoxville, Smokies)**: Appalachian. Country fried steak, soup beans, cornbread, moonshine.
- **Tennessee whiskey country (Lynchburg, Tullahoma)**: the Jack Daniel's and George Dickel distilleries. Charcoal-mellowed sour-mash bourbon style.

## Signature ingredients

- **Hickory wood**: the smoke of choice for Memphis BBQ.
- **Cayenne pepper**: the heat in Nashville hot chicken; lots of it.
- **Lard**: the frying fat of choice in country kitchens; pork-rendered, used for biscuits and fried chicken.
- **Buttermilk**: the soak for chicken before frying; the binder for biscuits.
- **Sorghum molasses**: the cane-syrup alternative grown across Tennessee. Sweetener and the glaze on certain hams.
- **Country ham**: dry-cured pork leg, salty and intense. Eaten in thin slices with biscuits and red-eye gravy.
- **Tennessee whiskey**: the cooking spirit and the table drink. Jack Daniel's is a marinade ingredient as much as a beverage.

## Learn it in seven recipes

These seven cover Memphis BBQ, Nashville hot chicken, country ham and the catfish fry. Cook them and the cuisine reveals itself.

1. **[Memphis Pulled Pork](/recipes/cuisine/tennessee/memphis-pulled-pork)**: pork shoulder smoked over hickory for 10+ hours till it pulls apart, served on a brioche bun with slaw and a thin tomato-vinegar sauce. The Memphis standard.
2. **[Memphis Dry-Rub Ribs](/recipes/cuisine/tennessee/memphis-dry-rub-ribs)**: spare ribs coated in a paprika-cayenne-brown-sugar rub, smoked over hickory, served dry (no sauce). The Rendezvous-codified rib style.
3. **[Memphis Brisket](/recipes/cuisine/tennessee/memphis-brisket)**: beef brisket smoked low and slow with a Memphis spice rub. The Memphis answer to the Texas tradition — equally good, less famous.
4. **[Nashville Hot Chicken](/recipes/cuisine/tennessee/nashville-hot-chicken)**: brined chicken pieces, double-coated and fried, finished with a spice paste of cayenne, paprika and the chicken's own frying oil. The Prince's-codified extra-hot fry.
5. **[Tennessee Catfish](/recipes/cuisine/tennessee/tennessee-catfish)**: cornmeal-coated catfish fillets, pan-fried in lard, served with hush puppies and tartar sauce. The Friday-night family meal across the Tennessee River basin.
6. **[Tennessee Whiskey Pork Chops](/recipes/cuisine/tennessee/tennessee-whiskey-pork-chops)**: pork chops marinated in Tennessee whiskey, brown sugar, soy and garlic, grilled. The most Tennessee thing you can cook.
7. **[Tennessee Meatloaf](/recipes/cuisine/tennessee/tennessee-meatloaf)**: ground beef with onion, breadcrumb and a brown-sugar-and-ketchup glaze. The meat-and-three plate's anchor.

Cook the pulled pork, the ribs and the brisket and you have Memphis. The hot chicken is Nashville. The catfish, the whiskey chops and the meatloaf are the everyday Tennessee table.

## Where to go next

Beyond these seven, the catalogue carries the country-fried steak shelf, the biscuits-and-gravy breakfast, more BBQ varieties, and the country-ham canon. Pair everything with a Tennessee whiskey or a cold Yuengling and the cuisine sings.
