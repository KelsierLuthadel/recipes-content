# Nashville Hot Chicken

![Nashville Hot Chicken](resources/nashville-hot-chicken.jpg)

*Nashville's iconic fried chicken: bone-in chicken pieces marinated in buttermilk, fried till crispy, then drenched in a fiery red-orange cayenne paste made from frying oil, cayenne, brown sugar, paprika and Cajun spices. Served on a slice of white bread with dill pickles. The Prince's Hot Chicken Shack original.*

**Serves:** 4

**Prep Time:** 30 minutes (plus 4 hours marinating)

**Cook Time:** 25 minutes

## Overview
Nashville hot chicken is the city's most iconic dish and one of the great American regional fried chicken styles: bone-in chicken pieces (the canonical bird: a quarter; thigh + drumstick or breast + wing) marinated in buttermilk with hot sauce, dredged in seasoned flour, deep-fried till crispy and golden, then drenched (the canonical Nashville verb is "dunked") in a fiery sauce made from rendered frying oil, brown sugar, cayenne, paprika, garlic and onion powder, mixed into a thick orange-red paste. Sauce levels range from "mild" to "shut-up-and-leave-the-restaurant XXX hot". Served on a slice of plain white bread (catches the dripping oil) with dill pickle chips on top. Origin myth: invented in the 1930s by Thornton Prince III's girlfriend, who supposedly tried to punish him for cheating by making fried chicken with extra cayenne; he loved it. Three details: buttermilk marinade, deep-fry first, dunk in hot oil sauce after.

## Ingredients

### Chicken
- 8 bone-in chicken pieces (thighs, drumsticks, breasts, wings)
- 600 ml buttermilk
- 3 tablespoons hot sauce
- 1 tablespoon fine sea salt
- 1 tablespoon paprika

### Dredge
- 400 g plain flour
- 2 tablespoons paprika
- 2 tablespoons garlic powder
- 1 tablespoon onion powder
- 1 tablespoon fine sea salt
- 1 tablespoon ground black pepper
- 1 teaspoon cayenne
- 1 teaspoon baking powder (for crispness)

### Frying
- 1.5 litres vegetable oil for frying

### Nashville hot sauce
- 250 ml of the frying oil (hot, from frying)
- 3-6 tablespoons cayenne (depending on heat level; 3 = mild, 6 = hot)
- 2 tablespoons brown sugar
- 1 tablespoon paprika
- 1 tablespoon garlic powder
- 1 teaspoon mustard powder
- 1 teaspoon fine sea salt
- 1 teaspoon ground black pepper

### To serve
- 8 slices plain white bread
- 1 jar dill pickle chips
- Cold beer
- Sweet tea

## Method

### Stage 1 - Marinate
1. Whisk buttermilk, hot sauce, salt, paprika.
2. Submerge chicken; refrigerate 4 hours (or overnight).

### Stage 2 - Mix dredge
1. Whisk flour, paprika, garlic and onion powder, salt, pepper, cayenne, baking powder.

### Stage 3 - Coat
1. Lift chicken from buttermilk (let excess drip).
2. Press into seasoned flour, coating thoroughly.
3. Rest 10 min on rack (helps crust adhere).

### Stage 4 - Heat oil
1. Heat oil to 175°C (350°F) in heavy deep pot.

### Stage 5 - Fry
1. Fry chicken in batches.
2. Dark meat (thighs, drumsticks) 14-16 min.
3. White meat (breasts, wings) 12-14 min.
4. Internal temp 75°C (165°F).
5. Drain briefly on rack.

### Stage 6 - Make Nashville hot sauce
1. Carefully ladle 250 ml of the hot frying oil into a heat-safe bowl.
2. Whisk in cayenne, brown sugar, paprika, garlic and onion powder, mustard powder, salt, pepper.
3. The sauce should be a thick paste; thin with more oil if needed.

### Stage 7 - Dunk
1. While chicken is still hot, brush or dunk into the spicy oil sauce.
2. Cover thoroughly.
3. The oil seeps into the crust.

### Stage 8 - Serve
1. Place 2 slices white bread on each plate.
2. Top with hot chicken pieces.
3. Add dill pickle chips on top.
4. Cold beer or sweet tea alongside.

## Notes
- **Buttermilk marinade:** essential.
- **Hot frying oil for the sauce:** the Nashville signature.
- **White bread underneath:** catches the drippy oil.
- **Pickles on top:** the canonical pairing.

## Variations
**Mild:** 1-2 tablespoons cayenne in the sauce.
**XXX hot:** 8-10 tablespoons cayenne + 1 tablespoon ghost chilli powder.
**Nashville hot chicken sandwich:** on a brioche bun with mayo, pickles, slaw.
**Boneless:** chicken thighs only, no bone.

## Serving
At Prince's Hot Chicken Shack, Hattie B's, the Nashville canon. With white bread, pickles, beer.

## Storage
- Best immediately.
- Cooked refrigerate 2 days; reheat in oven at 180°C 15 min.
- Hot sauce keeps refrigerated 1 month.
