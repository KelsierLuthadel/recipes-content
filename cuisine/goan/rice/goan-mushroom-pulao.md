# Goan Mushroom Pulao

*Goan vegetarian pulao with mushrooms, peas and the warming kick of cardamom and cinnamon. The everyday rice that fills out a meatless weekday meal.*

**Serves:** 4

**Prep Time:** 10 minutes (plus 30 minutes soak)

**Cook Time:** 30 minutes

## Overview
A whole-spice tempering of cinnamon, cardamom, cloves and bay is bloomed in ghee. Onion is browned with ginger, garlic and a slit green chilli. Sliced chestnut mushrooms are sautéed hard until they shed their water and start to colour, then the soaked basmati is toasted in the spiced fat. Stock is poured in for the steam; peas are scattered on top in the final minutes. Finished with chopped coriander.

## Ingredients
- 300 g aged basmati rice (rinsed, soaked for 30 minutes)
- 250 g chestnut or button mushrooms (cleaned, sliced)
- 80 g peas (frozen)
- 3 tablespoons ghee (or oil + butter)
- 1 small cinnamon stick
- 4 green cardamom pods (lightly crushed)
- 1 black cardamom pod (optional)
- 4 cloves
- 1 bay leaf
- 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
- 1 onion (finely sliced)
- 3 garlic cloves (finely chopped)
- 20 g fresh ginger (finely grated)
- 1 green chilli (slit, optional)
- ½ teaspoon turmeric
- ½ teaspoon ground coriander
- 1 teaspoon salt (to taste)
- 600 ml hot water (or vegetable stock)
- A handful of coriander (chopped)
- Juice of ½ lime

## Method

### Stage 1 - Bloom the spices
1. Heat the ghee in a saucepan with a tight-fitting lid over medium heat.
1. Add the cinnamon, green and black cardamom, cloves, bay and cumin seeds; sizzle for 30 seconds.

### Stage 2 - Brown the base
1. Add the sliced onion and a pinch of salt; cook for 6-8 minutes until golden.
1. Stir in the chopped garlic, grated ginger and green chilli; cook for 1 minute.

### Stage 3 - Sauté the mushrooms
1. Increase the heat to medium-high.
1. Tip in the sliced mushrooms.
1. Cook for 6-7 minutes, stirring, until the mushrooms have shed their water and started to take on colour (the mushrooms should be browned, not steamed).
1. Sprinkle in the turmeric and ground coriander; cook for 30 seconds.

### Stage 4 - Toast the rice
1. Drain the soaked rice well.
1. Tip into the pan with the salt and stir gently for 2 minutes to coat in the spiced fat.

### Stage 5 - Steam
1. Pour in the hot water (or stock).
1. Bring to a boil.
1. Reduce to the lowest heat, cover with a tight-fitting lid.
1. Cook for 12 minutes.
1. Scatter the peas on top.
1. Cover and cook for another 2 minutes.
1. Pull from the heat and rest, still covered, for 10 minutes.

### Stage 6 - Serve
1. Lift the lid and fluff with a fork.
1. Scatter the coriander and squeeze the lime juice over.

## Notes
- **Brown the mushrooms hard:** Pale steamed mushrooms make a watery pulao. The high-heat sauté is what gives the dish savoury depth.
- **Soaked basmati:** A 30-minute soak gives the elongated, separated grains of restaurant pulao. Don't skip.
- **Black cardamom:** Smoky, big, optional but adds a depth that elevates the dish past everyday pulao.

## Storage
- Refrigerate up to 3 days; reheat covered with a splash of water.
- Freezes well in portions for 2 months.
