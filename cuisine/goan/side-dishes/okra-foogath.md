# Okra Foogath

*Goan dry okra with coconut and curry leaves. Pods cooked over high heat to drive off the moisture, then tossed with a temper and fresh coconut. A weekday vegetable side.*

**Serves:** 4

**Prep Time:** 15 minutes

**Cook Time:** 20 minutes

## Overview
Okra is washed and dried thoroughly (the trick that keeps it from going slimy), then sliced and dry-fried over high heat to cook off the surface mucilage. A temper of mustard seeds, curry leaves and dried red chilli is bloomed in coconut oil, onion and garlic are softened, and the okra is folded in with a Goan masala of cumin, turmeric and coriander. Finished with fresh grated coconut and a squeeze of lime.

## Ingredients

### Okra
- 400 g small okra pods (fresh, no soft spots)
- ½ teaspoon turmeric
- 1 teaspoon salt (to taste)

### Temper
- 3 tablespoons coconut oil
- 1 teaspoon black mustard seeds
- 1 teaspoon urad dal
- 2 dried red chillies (broken in half)
- 15 fresh curry leaves
- 1 small onion (finely chopped)
- 3 garlic cloves (finely chopped)

### Masala
- ½ teaspoon cumin seeds
- 1 teaspoon ground coriander
- ½ teaspoon Kashmiri chilli powder

### To finish
- 60 g fresh grated coconut (or 50 g desiccated, rehydrated in 2 tablespoons of warm water)
- Juice of ½ lime

## Method

### Stage 1 - Prep the okra
1. Wash the okra and pat completely dry on a clean cloth or kitchen paper (wet okra is slimy okra; the drying matters more than the recipe).
1. Trim the stem ends.
1. Cut into 1 cm rounds.

### Stage 2 - Dry-fry the okra
1. Heat 2 tablespoons of the coconut oil in a wide pan over medium-high heat.
1. Add the sliced okra in a single layer.
1. Cook for 8-10 minutes, stirring every 1-2 minutes, until tender and dry (any remaining mucilage will have cooked off).
1. Lift the okra out and set aside.

### Stage 3 - Temper
1. Add the remaining tablespoon of coconut oil to the pan.
1. Add the mustard seeds; when they pop, add the urad dal and dried red chilli.
1. Cook for 30 seconds until the dal turns golden.
1. Add the curry leaves; sizzle for 5 seconds.
1. Add the cumin seeds.

### Stage 4 - Soften the base
1. Add the chopped onion and garlic with a pinch of salt.
1. Cook for 4-5 minutes until soft and lightly golden.

### Stage 5 - Combine
1. Stir in the turmeric, ground coriander and Kashmiri chilli; cook for 30 seconds.
1. Return the dry-fried okra to the pan with the salt.
1. Toss gently for 3-4 minutes for the okra to absorb the spice.

### Stage 6 - Coconut and finish
1. Sprinkle the grated coconut over.
1. Toss for 1 minute until the coconut is warm through.
1. Squeeze the lime juice over.
1. Taste and adjust salt.

### Stage 7 - Serve
1. Transfer to a bowl and serve alongside rice and fish curry.

## Notes
- **Dry okra:** Drying every pod is the single technique that defines a good foogath. Wet okra cooks into a rope-textured dish.
- **Cook uncovered:** Lid = steam = slime. Foogath is cooked open-pan throughout.
- **Don't dice too small:** 1 cm rounds keep their shape. Tiny pieces collapse.

## Storage
- Best the day it's made.
- Refrigerate up to 2 days; reheats but softens.
