# Rava-Fried Mackerel

*Goan rava-fried fish: mackerel fillets coated in semolina and pan-fried until shatter-crisp on the outside, the spice paste baked onto the flesh. The Sunday lunch side that often steals the meal.*

**Serves:** 4

**Prep Time:** 15 minutes (plus 30 minutes marinade)

**Cook Time:** 15 minutes

## Overview
Mackerel fillets are scored, rubbed with a thick spice paste of Kashmiri chilli, ginger, garlic, turmeric and palm vinegar, and rested for half an hour. The marinated fillets are pressed into coarse semolina just before frying. The semolina forms a deep golden crust that crackles when broken, while the spice paste melts into the flesh underneath. Pomfret, sardines and king-mackerel work the same way; mackerel is the most-Goan choice.

## Ingredients

### Fish
- 4 mackerel fillets (about 600 g total) (or 8 fillets of sardine; or 4 slices of kingfish)
- 1 teaspoon salt
- Juice of 1 lime

### Masala paste
- 2 teaspoons Kashmiri chilli powder
- 1 teaspoon turmeric
- 1 teaspoon ground coriander
- ½ teaspoon ground cumin
- ½ teaspoon black pepper
- 4 garlic cloves (crushed to paste)
- 20 g fresh ginger (finely grated)
- 1 tablespoon palm vinegar (or cider vinegar)
- ½ teaspoon salt

### Coating
- 80 g coarse semolina (rava; not the fine pasta semolina)
- 1 tablespoon rice flour (for extra crunch)
- 4 tablespoons coconut oil (or vegetable oil), for shallow frying

### To serve
- 1 lime (cut into wedges)
- Sliced onion
- A side of cabbage foogath

## Method

### Stage 1 - Score and salt the fish
1. Make 2-3 shallow diagonal slashes in each fillet.
1. Rub with salt and lime juice.
1. Set aside for 5 minutes.

### Stage 2 - Make the masala paste
1. Combine the Kashmiri chilli powder, turmeric, ground coriander, ground cumin, black pepper, crushed garlic, grated ginger, palm vinegar and salt in a small bowl.
1. Stir into a thick red paste.

### Stage 3 - Marinate
1. Rub the masala paste over each fillet, working it into the slashes.
1. Refrigerate for at least 30 minutes (longer is better; up to 4 hours).

### Stage 4 - Coat
1. Combine the coarse semolina and rice flour on a plate.
1. Lift each fillet out of the marinade, letting the excess drip back.
1. Press both sides of the fillet into the semolina, coating thoroughly.
1. Set aside on a tray (don't stack).

### Stage 5 - Pan-fry
1. Heat the coconut oil in a wide pan over medium-high heat until it shimmers.
1. Lay the fillets in the pan in a single layer.
1. Cook for 3-4 minutes a side until deep golden and the semolina has set into a crust.
1. Lift onto kitchen paper to drain.

### Stage 6 - Serve
1. Serve immediately with lime wedges, sliced onion and a foogath on the side.

## Notes
- **Coarse semolina is the dish:** Fine semolina gives a powdery coating, not a crust. Coarse rava (the kind used for upma) is what shatters when bitten.
- **Press, don't roll:** Pressing the fillets into the semolina drives it into the slashes. Rolling gives a thin, even coating that doesn't grip.
- **Hot oil, dry crust:** Add the fish only when the oil shimmers. Cold oil = soggy crust.

## Storage
- Best within 30 minutes of frying. The crust softens as it sits.
- Not suitable for storage.
