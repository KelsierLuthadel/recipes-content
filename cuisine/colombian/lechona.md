# Lechona Tolimense

![Lechona](resources/lechona.jpg)

*Colombia's whole stuffed roast pig: a deboned pig stuffed with a fragrant rice-pork-pea-spice filling, sewn shut, and slow-roasted for 8 hours till the skin crackles to a crisp deep mahogany and the filling soaks up the pork juices. The Tolima centerpiece of every major Colombian celebration, traditionally cooked in clay ovens. Adapted here for a stuffed pork shoulder home version.*

**Serves:** 8-10

**Prep Time:** 45 minutes (plus overnight marinating)

**Cook Time:** 5 hours

## Overview
Lechona tolimense is one of Colombia's most iconic celebration dishes and the unofficial national dish of the Tolima department in central Colombia. Traditionally a whole deboned pig is stuffed with a fragrant filling of long-grain rice, ground pork, green peas, scallions, garlic and the canonical Colombian spice mix, sewn shut and slow-roasted for eight to twelve hours in a clay oven (the lechona horneada) till the skin goes crackling-crisp deep mahogany and the rice filling has soaked up all the pork juices. At home and outside Colombia the dish adapts to a large bone-in pork shoulder, stuffed the same way, roasted for four or five hours, then finished with a high-heat blast for the cuerito. The skin is non-negotiable; flaccid skin ruins the dish. Served sliced thick at the centre of a platter with the filling alongside, arepas, ají picante and lime wedges. Don't add extra rice on the plate: the filling is the carb, the arepas are the bread.

## Ingredients

### Pork
- 1 bone-in pork shoulder picnic (about 3 kg; skin on; or a boneless pork loin with skin on for easier home version)

### Adobo marinade
- 12 garlic cloves (crushed)
- 3 tablespoons ground cumin
- 3 tablespoons dried oregano
- 2 tablespoons fine sea salt
- 1 tablespoon ground black pepper
- 1 tablespoon paprika
- 1 tablespoon turmeric
- Juice of 4 limes
- 4 tablespoons olive oil

### Filling (the canonical Tolima rice-pork stuffing)
- 400 g long-grain white rice
- 400 g ground pork (or pork shoulder finely diced)
- 4 tablespoons olive oil
- 1 large white onion (finely chopped)
- 8 scallions/spring onions (finely sliced)
- 8 garlic cloves (crushed)
- 200 g frozen green peas
- 1 tablespoon ground cumin
- 1 tablespoon dried oregano
- 2 teaspoons fine sea salt
- 1 teaspoon ground black pepper
- 1 teaspoon achiote/turmeric (for colour)
- 800 ml hot chicken stock (or water)

### For the skin
- 1 tablespoon olive oil
- 1 teaspoon flaky sea salt

### To serve
- Arepas (Colombian; see existing colombian arepas recipe)
- Ají picante (Colombian hot sauce; see recipe in this batch)
- Lime wedges
- Avocado slices
- Fresh salad

## Method

### Stage 1 - Score and marinate (overnight)
1. Pat the pork dry.
2. Score the skin in a diamond pattern, cutting through the skin and into the fat but not into the meat.
3. Combine all marinade ingredients to a paste.
4. Rub thoroughly over the pork; into the scored skin, around the bone, into the meat.
5. Cover and refrigerate 12-24 hours.

### Stage 2 - Make the filling
1. Heat the olive oil in a wide pan over medium heat.
2. Add the chopped onion; cook 6 minutes till soft.
3. Add the ground pork; brown for 6-7 minutes.
4. Add the spring onions, garlic, cumin, oregano, salt, pepper and achiote; cook 1 minute.
5. Add the rice; stir to coat.
6. Add the peas.
7. Pour in the hot chicken stock.
8. Bring to a simmer; cover; reduce to low heat.
9. Cook 18-20 minutes till the rice is just cooked (still slightly firm).
10. Cool to room temperature.

### Stage 3 - Stuff the pork
1. If using bone-in shoulder: make 8-10 deep slits in the meat with a sharp knife (around the bone and through the meat); push the filling deep into each slit.
2. If using boneless pork loin with skin on: butterfly the loin open; spread the filling over; roll up tightly; tie with kitchen twine.
3. Spoon any remaining filling alongside in the roasting tin.

### Stage 4 - Slow-roast
1. Preheat the oven to 160°C (325°F).
2. Place the stuffed pork in a heavy roasting tin, skin-side-up.
3. Cover loosely with foil.
4. Roast 4 hours.

### Stage 5 - Crisp the skin
1. Remove the foil.
2. Brush the skin with olive oil; sprinkle with flaky salt.
3. Turn the oven up to 220°C (425°F).
4. Roast uncovered 30-40 minutes till the skin is deeply crackling-crisp and dark mahogany.

### Stage 6 - Rest
1. Take out; let rest 20 minutes covered loosely with foil.

### Stage 7 - Carve and serve
1. Cut the crispy skin (cuerito) off in pieces; arrange on a separate plate.
2. Slice or shred the pork; serve over a bed of the rice-pork filling.
3. Pass the cuerito; everyone fights over it.
4. Serve with arepas, aji picante and lime.

## Notes
- **Score the skin properly:** essential for crackling-crisp result.
- **The rice filling makes it lechona:** without this, just roast pork.
- **Long marinade:** 12-24 hours.
- **Two-temperature roast:** low and slow, then high blast.
- **Serve with arepas, not rice:** the filling is the carb.

## Variations
**Boneless rolled lechona:** easier home version using boneless pork loin with skin on, butterflied, filled and rolled.
**With chickpeas:** add 1 tin of chickpeas to the filling; gives more body.
**Without ground pork (vegetarian-leaning filling):** double the rice; add 200 g of mushrooms.
**Antioqueño-style:** add chopped chicharrón to the filling; gives a richer fattier version.

## Serving
At the centre of a celebration table, the cuerito on a separate plate. With arepas, aji, lime, salad. Drink: aguardiente (Colombian sugarcane spirit), Club Colombia beer, or fresh limonada.

## Storage
- Keeps refrigerated 5 days; flavour deepens overnight.
- Reheat covered in a 160°C oven for 25 minutes.
- The shredded leftover lechona makes excellent arepa filling.
- Freezes 3 months in portions.
