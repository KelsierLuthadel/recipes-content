# Posta Negra

![Posta Negra](resources/posta-negra.jpg)

*Cartagena's signature beef: a slow-braised beef roast (eye round or chuck) cooked in a sauce of caramelised panela (raw cane sugar), cola, soy sauce, garlic, vinegar and warm spices till the meat goes meltingly tender and the sauce reduces to a glossy mahogany-black coating. The Caribbean-Colombian dish that surprises and delights, served sliced thick with coconut rice and plantains.*

**Serves:** 6-8

**Prep Time:** 25 minutes (plus overnight marinating)

**Cook Time:** 3 hours

## Overview
Posta negra (literally "black roast") is Cartagena's signature beef dish and one of Colombia's most surprising sauces. A whole beef eye round or chuck marinates overnight in panela (Colombian raw cane sugar), garlic, soy sauce, vinegar and warm spices, then slow-braises for two or three hours in a sauce built around the canonical Caribbean-Colombian addition of cola. Yes, cola: the acid tenderises, the sugar caramelises, the colour deepens to mahogany-black. Don't substitute with stock. Panela carries the caramel depth (dark brown sugar substitutes if you can't find a block at the Latin market). The meat goes meltingly tender; the sauce reduces to a glossy syrup-like coating. The result surprises first-timers who think cola sounds odd, and then delights them. Sliced thick at the table, served with arroz con coco (Caribbean-Colombian coconut rice), patacones and a fresh salad. Found on every "típico" menu in Cartagena.

## Ingredients

### Beef
- 1.5 kg beef eye round (or beef chuck; or top sirloin roast)

### Marinade (overnight)
- 12 garlic cloves (crushed)
- 4 tablespoons soy sauce
- 4 tablespoons Worcestershire sauce
- 3 tablespoons vinegar (white or apple cider)
- 2 tablespoons ground cumin
- 2 tablespoons dried oregano
- 2 teaspoons fine sea salt
- 1 teaspoon ground black pepper
- 1 teaspoon ground cloves
- 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon

### Cooking
- 4 tablespoons vegetable oil
- 2 large onions (finely chopped)
- 1 medium green bell pepper (chopped)
- 1 medium red bell pepper (chopped)
- 6 garlic cloves (crushed)
- 4 medium tomatoes (chopped)
- 4 tablespoons tomato paste
- 80 g panela (Colombian raw cane sugar; or 80 g dark brown sugar)

### Cola braise
- 500 ml cola (Coca-Cola, Postobón, or any cola brand)
- 400 ml beef stock
- 100 ml dry red wine (optional)
- 2 bay leaves
- 1 cinnamon stick
- 1 tablespoon ground cumin
- 1 ½ teaspoons fine sea salt
- 1 teaspoon ground black pepper

### To finish
- 1 small bunch fresh coriander (chopped)
- Lime wedges

### To serve
- Arroz con coco (coconut rice)
- Patacones (Colombian twice-fried plantains)
- Fresh salad
- Sliced avocado
- Ají picante

## Method

### Stage 1 - Marinate the beef (overnight)
1. Pat the beef dry.
2. Combine all marinade ingredients to a wet paste.
3. Rub thoroughly over the beef.
4. Cover and refrigerate 12-24 hours.

### Stage 2 - Brown the beef
1. Heat the vegetable oil in a large heavy casserole over medium-high heat.
2. Pat off excess marinade from the beef (reserve the marinade for the braise).
3. Brown the beef on all sides for 8-10 minutes total till deeply golden.
4. Lift out.

### Stage 3 - Caramelise the panela
1. Reduce heat to medium.
2. Add the chopped onions, bell peppers and garlic to the pot; cook 8 minutes till soft.
3. Add the chopped tomatoes and tomato paste; cook 5 minutes till the tomato breaks down.
4. Crumble the panela into the pot; stir till it melts and caramelises slightly (about 2 minutes).

### Stage 4 - Add liquid and beef
1. Pour in the cola and beef stock; let bubble for 30 seconds.
2. Add the red wine (if using) and reserved marinade.
3. Add the bay leaves, cinnamon stick, additional cumin, salt and pepper.
4. Return the browned beef to the pot.
5. The beef should be at least 2/3 submerged; add more stock if needed.

### Stage 5 - Slow-braise
1. Bring to a low simmer.
2. Cover; reduce to lowest heat.
3. Cook 2.5-3 hours, turning the beef every 30-40 minutes, till fork-tender.

### Stage 6 - Reduce the sauce
1. Lift the beef out; cover loosely with foil to keep warm.
2. Strain the sauce through a sieve into a clean pan (press the solids to extract flavour; discard the solids).
3. Skim off excess fat.
4. Bring to a hard boil; reduce 8-10 minutes till the sauce thickens to a glossy mahogany-black syrup.

### Stage 7 - Slice and serve
1. Slice the beef thinly (5 mm) across the grain.
2. Arrange on a serving platter.
3. Pour the reduced sauce generously over.
4. Scatter coriander.
5. Serve with coconut rice, patacones, salad and lime.

## Notes
- **Cola is canonical:** yes, regular Coca-Cola or cola. Don't sub with stock.
- **Panela for caramel depth:** Colombian raw cane sugar; dark brown sugar is the substitute.
- **Slow braise properly:** 2.5-3 hours for fork-tender.
- **Reduce the sauce:** the final reduction makes it glossy. Don't skip.
- **Slice across the grain:** for tender slices.

## Variations
**With prunes:** add 100 g of pitted prunes to the braise; gives a sweeter fruitier version.
**Without cola (purist):** swap cola for 500 ml of stock + 100 g more panela + 50 ml vinegar; less canonical but works.
**Pressure-cooker version:** marinate as in the recipe; brown; pressure-cook 45 minutes; reduce sauce. Saves 2 hours.
**With dark beer instead of cola:** swap cola for dark beer (Costeñita or stout); gives a more bitter savoury version.

## Serving
On a wide platter sliced thick, with the glossy sauce poured over. Arroz con coco, patacones, salad, avocado. Drink: Club Colombia beer, fresh limonada, or aguardiente.

## Storage
- Keeps refrigerated 5 days; flavour deepens.
- Reheat in covered pan with a splash of stock.
- Freezes 3 months.
- Leftover posta negra makes excellent sandwiches on arepas.
