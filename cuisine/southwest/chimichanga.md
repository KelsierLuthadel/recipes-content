# Chimichanga

![Chimichanga](resources/chimichanga.jpg)

*The Southwest's deep-fried burrito: a large flour tortilla wrapped tightly around seasoned shredded beef (or chicken), beans and cheese, then deep-fried in oil till the outside is deeply crispy and golden. Topped with red or green chile sauce, melted cheese, sour cream, guacamole and pico de gallo. The Tucson-Tex-Mex restaurant signature.*

**Serves:** 4

**Prep Time:** 30 minutes (assuming pre-made filling)

**Cook Time:** 15 minutes

## Overview
Chimichangas are the iconic Southwestern Mexican-American restaurant dish, particularly associated with Tucson Arizona (where the dish was supposedly accidentally invented in the 1920s when a cook dropped a burrito into a deep fryer): a large flour tortilla wrapped tightly around seasoned shredded beef (slow-cooked with red chile, garlic and cumin), refried beans, grated cheese (Monterey Jack or cheddar), then folded and deep-fried in oil till the outside crisps deeply golden and the cheese inside melts. Served on a plate covered with red or green chile sauce, melted cheese, sour cream, guacamole, pico de gallo and shredded lettuce.

## Ingredients

### Filling
- 600 g cooked shredded beef (or chicken, or pulled pork)
- 4 tablespoons sofrito (or sautéed onion-garlic)
- 4 tablespoons red chile sauce
- 2 teaspoons cumin
- 2 teaspoons Mexican oregano
- 1 teaspoon fine sea salt
- 400 g refried beans
- 300 g grated Monterey Jack
- 200 g grated cheddar

### Tortillas
- 4 large flour tortillas (25 cm; the bigger the better for wrapping)

### Frying
- Vegetable oil for deep-frying (about 1.5 litres; or enough for 5 cm depth)

### Topping
- 400 ml red enchilada sauce (or green chile sauce)
- 200 g grated cheddar (extra for topping)
- 200 ml sour cream
- 1 ripe avocado (cubed or guacamole)
- 1 cup pico de gallo (traditional)
- Shredded iceberg lettuce
- 1 small bunch fresh coriander

## Method

### Stage 1 - Combine the filling
1. In a pan, combine shredded beef, sofrito, red chile sauce, cumin, oregano and salt. Warm 5 minutes.
2. Set aside.

### Stage 2 - Warm tortillas
1. Wrap tortillas in foil; warm in oven 5 minutes till pliable.

### Stage 3 - Build the chimichanga
1. Take a warm tortilla; place 4 tablespoons refried beans, 2 tablespoons grated cheese, 4 tablespoons shredded beef filling and another 2 tablespoons cheese down the centre.
2. Fold the bottom edge up over the filling; fold the two sides in tightly; roll up tightly.
3. The roll should be tight and sealed.

### Stage 4 - Fry
1. Heat oil to 175°C (350°F).
2. Carefully lower each chimichanga seam-side-down into the oil.
3. Fry 2 minutes; turn; fry 2 minutes on the other side till deeply golden all over.
4. Lift out with a slotted spoon; drain.

### Stage 5 - Top and serve
1. Place each chimichanga on a plate.
2. Ladle warm red (or green) chile sauce over.
3. Top with extra grated cheese (the residual heat melts it).
4. Add dollop of sour cream, cubed avocado, pico de gallo, shredded lettuce, coriander.

## Notes
- **Tight folding essential:** prevents bursting.
- **175°C oil temperature.**
- **Sauce-and-cheese topping covered:** the restaurant style.

## Variations
- **Vegetarian:** swap meat for sautéed mushrooms + extra beans.
- **With green chile:** use green chile sauce instead of red.
- **Baked version (lighter):** brush with oil; bake at 220°C for 25 minutes till crisp.
- **Smaller (mini chimichangas):** use smaller tortillas; appetiser portion.

## Serving
- At the centre of a Southwest plate. Mexican beer, margaritas.

## Storage
- Best eaten immediately.
- Filling alone keeps refrigerated 4 days.
- Don't refrigerate assembled.
