# Fasole Bătută (Romanian Smashed Bean Spread)

![Fasole Bătută](resources/fasole-batuta.jpg)

*Romania's silky white-bean spread, "beaten beans": cooked dried beans pounded with sunflower oil, garlic and onion till smooth, finished with a generous topping of caramelised onion in paprika oil.*

**Serves:** 6-8 as a side

**Prep Time:** 15 minutes (plus overnight soak)

**Cook Time:** 1 hour 30 minutes

## Overview
Fasole bătută (literally "beaten beans") is a Romanian country-kitchen side that turns the pantry's dried white beans into a smooth garlicky spread, smeared onto bread or piled next to mici and sarmale at a Sunday lunch. The construction is in two distinct stages: the beans are slowly simmered with bay, onion and a little garlic till they collapse, then drained and pounded (the modern kitchen uses a hand blender) with sunflower oil, raw garlic, salt and pepper into a thick pale puree. The signature finish is a separate topping of onions caramelised dark with smoked paprika, spooned generously over the pale bean base. The contrast between cool savoury bean spread and warm sweet onion crown is the dish.

## Ingredients

### Bean base
- 400 g dried white beans (cannellini or great northern; soaked overnight in cold water)
- 1 large onion (quartered)
- 4 cloves garlic (peeled, left whole)
- 3 fresh bay leaves
- 1 teaspoon fine sea salt

### Smashed paste
- 4 cloves garlic (raw, peeled)
- 80 ml sunflower oil (or mild olive oil)
- 1 1/2 teaspoons fine sea salt
- 1 teaspoon coarsely cracked black pepper
- Juice of 1/2 lemon (optional)

### Caramelised onion topping (călire de ceapă)
- 2 large onions (finely sliced)
- 60 ml sunflower oil
- 1 teaspoon sweet paprika (boia dulce)
- 1/2 teaspoon hot paprika (boia iute, optional)
- 1 pinch fine sea salt

### To serve
- 1 loaf rustic Romanian country bread (pâine de casă), thickly sliced
- A handful of pickled hot peppers (gogoșari)
- 1 small bunch parsley (chopped)

## Method

### Stage 1 - Cook the beans
1. Drain the soaked beans; rinse.
2. Tip into a large pot; cover with 2 litres cold water.
3. Add the quartered onion, the 4 garlic cloves and the bay leaves.
4. Bring to a boil; skim the scum.
5. Drop the heat; simmer gently 1 hour 15 minutes till the beans are very tender (a bean should crush easily between two fingers).
6. Stir in the salt for the last 10 minutes.
7. Drain, reserving 200 ml of the cooking liquid; discard the onion, bay and cooked garlic.

### Stage 2 - Smash the paste
1. Tip the warm beans into a deep bowl or food processor.
2. Add the 4 raw garlic cloves, the sunflower oil, salt and pepper.
3. Blend (or pound with a wooden spoon) into a smooth thick paste.
4. Add 4-6 tablespoons of the reserved cooking liquid to loosen to a creamy peanut-butter consistency.
5. Squeeze in the lemon juice if using.
6. Check seasoning; the paste should taste assertively garlicky.
7. Scrape into a serving dish; smooth the top with the back of a spoon.

### Stage 3 - Caramelise the onion topping
1. Heat the sunflower oil in a frying pan over medium heat.
2. Add the sliced onions and a pinch of salt.
3. Cook 18-22 minutes, stirring often, till deep mahogany-brown (not burnt; the onions should taste sweet and rich).
4. Off the heat, sprinkle the sweet and hot paprika over.
5. Stir 10 seconds to bloom the paprika (don't return to the heat; paprika scorches).

### Stage 4 - Serve
1. Spoon the warm onion-and-paprika topping over the bean paste, oil and all (the red oil pools on top).
2. Scatter chopped parsley around the edge.
3. Serve with thick slices of country bread and a few pickled peppers.

## Notes
- **Overnight soak:** the long soak is non-negotiable. A quick soak makes the beans tough and the paste gritty.
- **Pound, don't whip:** a food processor turns the beans into glue if over-blended. Pulse to a coarse paste, then loosen with cooking liquid.
- **Raw garlic in the paste:** the punch comes from raw garlic; the cooked cloves go in the pot for flavour but are discarded.
- **Paprika off the heat:** Hungarian and Romanian paprikas scorch in 10 seconds at high heat and turn bitter.
- **Eat warm or room temperature:** never cold from the fridge (the oil seizes).

## Variations
- **With smoked pork:** add 100 g diced smoked pork or kaiser to the onion topping.
- **With chickpeas:** swap half the white beans for cooked chickpeas (the southern Romanian version).
- **Spicy:** double the hot paprika and add a sliced chilli to the onion topping.
- **Vegan-friendly already:** the dish is plant-based by default; no swap needed.
- **Smoother spread version (mezelic):** thin the paste with extra oil and lemon juice; serve as a dip.

## Serving
- At a Romanian Christmas table next to sarmale and mici · as a Lent dish (the fasting tradition since it has no meat) · with a Țuică shot at the start of a Sunday lunch · on a meze board with salată de vinete and zacuscă · spread on rye bread with pickles for a country snack · alongside a bowl of ciorbă for a winter weeknight supper.

## Storage
- Refrigerates 5 days in a sealed container.
- The topping separates from the base when chilled; bring to room temperature 30 minutes before serving.
- Don't freeze (the texture goes grainy).
- The reserved bean cooking liquid keeps 3 days; use to loosen the spread.
