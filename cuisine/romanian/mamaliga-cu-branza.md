# Mămăligă cu brânză

![Mămăligă cu brânză](resources/mamaliga-cu-branza.jpg)

*Romania's everyday carb: stiff yellow cornmeal cooked in salted water until it pulls from the pan, layered with crumbled sheep cheese and a great spoon of sour cream, eaten with a wooden spoon.*

**Serves:** 4

**Prep Time:** 5 minutes

**Cook Time:** 25 minutes

## Overview
Mămăligă is the polenta of Romania, a second bread on every rural table since maize arrived from the New World in the seventeenth century. The basic method is unchanged: boiling salted water, a steady rain of coarse yellow cornmeal, twenty minutes of stirring with a wooden paddle (the mestecău), until the porridge tightens and pulls cleanly from the pot. For mămăligă cu brânză the cooked polenta is layered with crumbled telemea (Romanian sheep cheese) and finished with sour cream (smântână), then either served scooped from the bowl or turned out, sliced with a thread, and grilled. Eat hot, with the cheese still oozing under the spoon.

## Ingredients

- 1 L water
- 1 tsp salt
- 250 g coarse yellow cornmeal (mămăligă or polenta grade)
- 50 g butter
- 250 g telemea or feta cheese, crumbled
- 200 g sour cream (smântână), full fat
- Optional: 4 eggs, soft-fried in butter

## Method

### Stage 1 - Cook the mămăligă
1. Bring the water and salt to a hard boil in a heavy-bottomed pot (cast iron is traditional).
2. Take a handful of cornmeal and tap it into the centre in a steady thin stream, whisking with the other hand.
3. Continue with the rest of the cornmeal, whisking constantly, until all is in.
4. Drop to a low heat; swap the whisk for a wooden spoon.
5. Stir for 20 to 25 minutes, scraping the base, until the polenta is very thick and pulls cleanly from the sides of the pan.
6. Stir in the butter; check the salt.

### Stage 2 - Layer and serve
1. Spoon half the mămăligă into a wide warm serving bowl.
2. Scatter half the crumbled cheese on top.
3. Spoon over half the sour cream.
4. Cover with the remaining mămăligă.
5. Finish with the rest of the cheese and a great pillow of sour cream on top.
6. If using, top each portion with a soft-fried egg.
7. Eat at once with a wooden spoon, scooping through the layers.

## Notes
- **The cornmeal grade:** Coarse stone-ground cornmeal gives the right texture; instant polenta works in a pinch but loses the bite.
- **The wooden spoon:** Metal cuts the gluten-free starch and gives a gluey result. Wood is correct.
- **The cheese:** Telemea is sharp, salty, sheep's-milk; Greek feta is the closest substitute; halloumi works grilled.
- **No lumps trick:** the steady rain of cornmeal into boiling water (not the other way round) is the only way to keep it smooth.
- **Stiff vs soft:** Country mămăligă is stiff enough to turn out and slice; city versions are looser, like Italian polenta.

## Variations
- **Bulz (shepherd's grilled mămăligă):** shape leftover mămăligă around a ball of cheese, roll in flour, grill over embers.
- **Mămăligă în pătură:** with a thick layer of melted butter and aged sheep cheese (urdă) on top.
- **With ciolan (smoked pork hock):** serve the polenta under a slab of slow-cooked smoked pork.
- **Sweet version:** with warm milk and sugar for breakfast, drizzled with honey.
- **Mămăligă cu jumări:** with crisp pork cracklings folded through.

## Serving
- Hot from the pot · with a soft-fried egg on top · alongside sarmale or tochitură · with a glass of cold buttermilk · for breakfast with milk and sugar.

## Storage
- Best fresh; cools fast into a sliceable block.
- Refrigerate slices up to 3 days; pan-fry in butter to revive.
- Do not freeze; the texture goes grainy.
