# Cozonac

![Cozonac](resources/cozonac.jpg)

*Romanian braided Christmas bread: a rich enriched dough rolled around a dark filling of walnut, cocoa, and rum, twisted into a long loaf, and baked until the sides domes burnish and the inside reveals a spiral.*

**Serves:** 2 loaves, 16 slices total

**Prep Time:** 2 hours (plus 3 hours rising)

**Cook Time:** 50 minutes

## Overview
Cozonac is the soul of Romanian Christmas and Easter, the loaf that every household bakes on the days before the feast, the smell that fills the stairwells of Bucharest blocks of flats in late December. The dough is heavily enriched (egg yolks, butter, milk, sugar) and worked long until it sings under the hand, then rolled flat, spread with a deep walnut-cocoa-rum paste, rolled up, and twisted into a loaf tin. The bake is moderate, the crust burnishes the colour of mahogany, and the slice reveals the spiral of dark filling running through the pale crumb. Eat slowly, with strong black coffee, in the long quiet morning after Christmas Eve mass.

## Ingredients

### For the dough
- 500 g strong white flour
- 100 g caster sugar
- 7 g instant yeast (1 sachet)
- 1/2 tsp salt
- 4 egg yolks
- 1 whole egg
- 200 ml warm full-fat milk (38°C)
- 100 g unsalted butter, soft
- 50 ml sunflower oil
- 1 tsp vanilla extract
- Zest of 1 lemon
- Zest of 1 orange

### For the filling
- 300 g shelled walnuts
- 200 ml full-fat milk
- 150 g caster sugar
- 30 g cocoa powder
- 2 tbsp rum
- 2 egg whites (from the yolks above)
- 1 tsp vanilla extract
- 50 g raisins (optional, soaked in rum)

### For the glaze
- 1 egg yolk
- 1 tbsp milk
- 1 tbsp sugar

## Method

### Stage 1 - Make the dough
1. Whisk flour, sugar, yeast, and salt in a large bowl.
2. Beat the 4 yolks, whole egg, warm milk, vanilla, and zests in a jug.
3. Pour into the flour; mix to a shaggy dough.
4. Turn onto an oiled surface; knead 12 minutes (or 8 minutes in a stand mixer with dough hook).
5. Work in the soft butter piece by piece, then the oil slowly.
6. The dough is sticky for a long time; keep kneading until it pulls clean from the bowl and is smooth and elastic (15 to 20 minutes total).

### Stage 2 - First rise
1. Place the dough in a large oiled bowl; cover.
2. Leave in a warm place 1.5 to 2 hours until doubled.

### Stage 3 - Make the filling
1. Grind the walnuts in a food processor to a coarse meal (not paste).
2. Warm the milk, sugar, and cocoa together; stir until smooth; cool slightly.
3. Whip the egg whites to soft peaks.
4. Combine the ground walnuts, cocoa milk, rum, and vanilla.
5. Fold in the egg whites and raisins.
6. The filling should be spreadable, not runny.

### Stage 4 - Shape
1. Grease and line 2 loaf tins (about 25 x 11 cm).
2. Turn out the dough; divide in two.
3. Take one half; divide in two again.
4. Roll each piece on a lightly oiled surface to a thin rectangle (30 x 40 cm).
5. Spread half the filling thin on each rectangle.
6. Roll each up tight from the long edge.
7. Lay the two rolls side by side; twist around each other into a rope.
8. Tuck into the loaf tin, ends down.
9. Repeat with the second half.

### Stage 5 - Second rise
1. Cover loosely with a tea towel.
2. Prove 60 to 90 minutes until the dough rises just above the rim of the tin.

### Stage 6 - Bake
1. Heat the oven to 170°C (fan 150°C).
2. Whisk the glaze; brush over the loaves.
3. Bake 45 to 55 minutes; the loaves should be deep mahogany and sound hollow when tapped on the base.
4. If browning too fast, tent with foil after 30 minutes.

### Stage 7 - Cool
1. Cool 10 minutes in the tin; turn out onto a rack.
2. Cool completely before slicing (slicing warm tears the spiral).

## Notes
- **The knead:** longer than you think. The gluten must develop fully or the loaf collapses.
- **Butter and oil last:** added too early they slow the gluten; work them in stage by stage.
- **Filling firmness:** runny filling leaks; spreadable but dry is correct.
- **The twist:** the two long rolls braided around each other give the spiral when sliced.
- **The bake colour:** a pale cozonac is undercooked; the colour of mahogany is right.

## Variations
- **Turkish delight cozonac:** chopped rahat through the filling.
- **Poppyseed cozonac (cu mac):** poppy seed paste in place of walnut.
- **With candied peel:** mixed peel through the filling for an old Bucharest version.
- **Plain (white) cozonac:** filling without cocoa, walnuts and rum only.
- **Mini cozonăcei:** baked in muffin tins for individual portions.

## Serving
- Sliced thin · with strong black coffee · with a small glass of țuică · at the holiday breakfast table · as a gift bundled in linen.

## Storage
- Wrap tight in linen or foil; keeps 5 days at room temperature.
- Freezes well sliced; defrost wrapped at room temperature.
- Stale slices: dip in egg-milk and pan-fry for a Romanian eggy bread.
