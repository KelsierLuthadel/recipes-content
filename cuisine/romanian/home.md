# Romanian Cuisine

![Romanian Cuisine](resources/hero.jpg)

Romanian food sits at the Balkan, Slavic, and Ottoman crossroads, with the Saxon and Hungarian weight of Transylvania pressed into the north-west. Sarmale (pickled cabbage rolls stuffed with pork and rice, simmered for hours over a bed of smoked bacon) anchor every Christmas and Easter table, served with a great heap of mămăligă (the cornmeal polenta that has been the country's everyday carb for four centuries, a second bread eaten with cheese, sour cream, or stew juice). The soured-soup tradition (ciorbă, sharpened with borș, lemon, or fermented bran water) reaches from the tripe-and-garlic bowls of Bucharest to the country bean ciorbas of Moldavia. Winter pantries lean on murături (mixed pickles), mici (the grilled garlic-and-bicarbonate beef sausages), and slabs of salami and ham. In the apple-and-walnut country of the north-west, Saxon strudels and Hungarian goulash bleed quietly across the maps.
