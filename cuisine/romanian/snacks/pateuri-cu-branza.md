# Pateuri cu brânză

![Pateuri cu brânză](resources/pateuri-cu-branza.jpg)

*Romanian cheese patties: little squares of buttery puff pastry folded around a salty sheep-cheese-and-egg filling, baked until shattering-gold, the bakery staple of every Bucharest corner.*

**Serves:** 12 pateuri

**Prep Time:** 25 minutes

**Cook Time:** 22 minutes

## Overview
Pateuri cu brânză are the everyday Romanian pastry, the warm flaky parcels sold from bakery windows on every street corner, eaten in the hand on the way to work or with a cup of strong coffee mid-morning. The casing is store-bought puff pastry in any modern household (the home laminated version exists but is rarely made now), and the filling is the simplest possible: salty sheep cheese (telemea or feta), egg, sour cream, fresh dill, a pinch of pepper. Cut into squares, dolloped, folded into triangles or envelopes, brushed with egg, baked at high heat until the layers puff and split. The cheese melts into the dough; the dough shatters in the hand.

## Ingredients

### For the pastry
- 500 g all-butter puff pastry, defrosted (one or two blocks)
- Flour for rolling

### For the filling
- 300 g telemea or feta cheese, crumbled
- 100 g cottage cheese or ricotta (well drained)
- 1 egg
- 2 tbsp sour cream
- 3 tbsp finely chopped fresh dill
- 1/2 tsp ground black pepper
- 1/2 tsp sweet paprika (optional)

### For the glaze
- 1 egg, beaten with 1 tbsp milk
- 1 tbsp sesame or nigella seeds (optional)

## Method

### Stage 1 - Make the filling
1. Mash the telemea with a fork in a wide bowl.
2. Add the cottage cheese, egg, sour cream, dill, pepper, and paprika.
3. Mix to a thick spreadable paste.
4. Taste; the cheese is salty; add salt only if needed.

### Stage 2 - Roll the pastry
1. Lightly flour a board.
2. Roll the puff pastry to a rectangle about 3 mm thick (40 x 30 cm).
3. With a sharp knife, cut into 10 cm squares (you should get 12).

### Stage 3 - Fill and fold
1. Place a heaped teaspoon of filling in the centre of each square.
2. Brush two adjacent edges with beaten egg.
3. Fold the opposite corner over the filling onto the egg-brushed edges to make a triangle.
4. Press to seal; crimp the edges with a fork.
5. Alternatively, brush all edges and bring the four corners together over the filling to make an envelope; pinch the centre seams.

### Stage 4 - Glaze
1. Heat the oven to 200°C (fan 180°C); line a tray with parchment.
2. Lay the pateuri on the tray, well spaced (they puff up).
3. Brush all over with the egg glaze.
4. Scatter sesame or nigella seeds on top.

### Stage 5 - Bake
1. Bake 20 to 22 minutes until deep gold and well puffed.
2. Cool 5 minutes on the tray; the filling is volcanic-hot the moment they come out.

## Notes
- **Drain the cottage cheese:** wet cheese gives a soggy bottom; firm cheese gives crisp layers.
- **All-butter pastry:** the layers and the flavour both depend on the butter; margarine puff is a poor second.
- **Seal well:** unsealed edges burst open in the oven and lose the filling.
- **Egg wash:** the gloss and the colour are the egg's job; do not skip.
- **Eat warm, not hot:** the filling needs 5 minutes to settle.

## Variations
- **With spinach (cu spanac):** wilted chopped spinach folded through the cheese.
- **With ham:** 100 g diced cooked ham added to the filling, the Bucharest deli version.
- **Sweet pateuri:** with sweetened curd cheese and raisins in place of the savoury filling.
- **With mushroom:** a chopped mushroom duxelles layered with the cheese.
- **Open-topped (cu brânză deschise):** envelope shape with the corners pinched, leaving a small window of cheese on top.

## Serving
- Warm · in the hand · with a glass of cold milk or a strong coffee · as part of a holiday cold-plate spread · packed for a road trip.

## Storage
- Best the day they are baked; eat warm within 8 hours.
- Refresh at 170°C for 5 minutes to recrisp.
- Freeze baked, wrapped tight: 2 months; reheat from frozen at 180°C for 15 minutes.
