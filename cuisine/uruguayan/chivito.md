# Chivito

![Chivito](resources/chivito.jpg)

*Uruguay's national sandwich: a thin steak griddled fast and stacked with ham, bacon, melted cheese, lettuce, tomato and mayonnaise on a soft roll, served with a heap of golden chips on the side.*

**Serves:** 2 sandwiches

**Prep Time:** 15 minutes

**Cook Time:** 20 minutes

## Overview
The chivito is Uruguay's national sandwich and the proudest line on any Montevideo menu. Born in the coastal town of Punta del Ballena in the 1940s when a roadside cook ran out of goat (chivito means "little goat") and improvised with thin beef steak, it grew into a stacked beef-ham-bacon-cheese-egg-tomato-lettuce-mayonnaise monster on a soft bun, served with chips. The build matters: a thin steak (lomo or palomilla), pounded and griddled in seconds; mozzarella melted over while it rests; ham and crisp bacon layered on; mayonnaise on both sides of a soft roll; lettuce and a thick slice of tomato to balance the richness. Cut in half, served with hot chips piled alongside. Eat with a knife and fork or with both hands and a stack of napkins.

## Ingredients

### For the sandwich
- 2 thin beef steaks (lomo, sirloin or rump), about 120 g each, pounded to 5 mm thick
- 4 slices of cooked ham
- 4 rashers of streaky bacon
- 4 slices of mozzarella (or any good melting cheese)
- 2 large soft sandwich rolls or burger buns
- 4 large lettuce leaves (round lettuce, washed and dried)
- 1 large ripe tomato, sliced
- 4 tbsp good mayonnaise
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- Salt and black pepper
- Optional: 2 fried eggs (chivito al plato style)

### For the chips
- 600 g floury potatoes (Maris Piper or King Edward)
- Sunflower or vegetable oil for frying
- Fine salt

## Method

### Stage 1 - The chips
1. Peel the potatoes and cut into 1 cm thick chips. Rinse in cold water and pat very dry.
2. Heat a deep pan of oil to 140 C. Fry the chips for 6-7 minutes until soft but not coloured. Drain on a rack.
3. Bring the oil up to 180 C. Fry the chips a second time for 3-4 minutes until deep gold and crisp. Drain, salt at once, keep warm.

### Stage 2 - Cook the bacon and ham
1. In a dry frying pan over medium heat, cook the bacon for 4-5 minutes until crisp. Set aside.
2. In the same pan, warm the ham slices for 30 seconds per side. Set aside.

### Stage 3 - The steak
1. Pound the steaks between two sheets of clingfilm to 5 mm with a heavy pan or rolling pin.
2. Season both sides with salt and pepper.
3. Heat 1 tbsp olive oil in a wide pan over high heat until smoking.
4. Lay the steaks in the pan; cook 45 seconds per side for medium rare.
5. Lay 2 slices of mozzarella on top of each steak, cover the pan with a lid, kill the heat. The cheese melts in 60 seconds.

### Stage 4 - Build the sandwich
1. Slice the rolls open and warm them face-down in the pan for 30 seconds.
2. Spread mayonnaise generously on both cut sides.
3. Lay lettuce on the bottom half.
4. Top with tomato slices.
5. Lay the cheese-topped steak on next.
6. Pile on the ham and bacon.
7. Add the fried egg if using.
8. Close the sandwich, press down firmly, cut on the diagonal.
9. Serve immediately with a mountain of hot chips alongside.

## Notes
- **Steak thickness.** Pound thin (5 mm) so it cooks in under a minute and stays tender. A thick steak makes the sandwich impossible to eat.
- **Cheese melt.** The lid-on rest is essential; the residual pan heat melts the cheese cleanly without overcooking the steak.
- **Bread choice.** A soft sandwich roll (think brioche-ish or a Portuguese roll) holds up to the load. Crusty baguette tears the lettuce out and breaks the build.
- **Two hands or knife and fork.** The Uruguayan way is with cutlery; the stack is too tall for one bite.

## Variations
- **Chivito al plato.** Skip the bread; serve the whole stack open on a plate with the chips, ham, bacon, cheese-topped steak, fried egg, lettuce and tomato arranged around. Eat with a knife and fork.
- **Chivito canadiense.** Add a layer of grilled red pepper and a slice of palmito (palm heart) inside.
- **Chivito con huevo.** Always with the fried egg on top; almost the default in many parrilla bars.
- **Chivito de pollo.** A grilled chicken breast in place of the steak, pounded equally thin.

## Serving
- Cut in half on the diagonal · golden chips piled alongside · pickled chillies on the side · a cold lager · plenty of napkins.

## Storage
- Eat fresh; the sandwich does not keep once built.
- Cooked components (bacon, ham, steak) keep 2 days separately, reheat briefly.
- Chips eat best straight from the second fry; do not store.
