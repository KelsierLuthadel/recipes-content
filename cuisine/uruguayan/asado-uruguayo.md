# Asado Uruguayo

![Asado Uruguayo](resources/asado-uruguayo.jpg)

*The Uruguayan Sunday grill: short rib, chorizo, morcilla and kidney cooked slowly over wood embers on the parrilla, served in stages with chimichurri, bread and red wine.*

**Serves:** 6 people

**Prep Time:** 30 minutes (plus 2 hours fire build-up)

**Cook Time:** 2 hours 30 minutes

## Overview
Asado in Uruguay is a Sunday ritual, not a meal. The fire is lit at midday, fed with quebracho or oak until the wood collapses into bright embers, and the meat begins to cook only when the parrilla above the coals is hot enough to hold a hand for three seconds. The order matters: chorizo and morcilla first (the choripán starters with bread and chimichurri to feed the cooks while the rest grills), then offal (kidney, sweetbreads, chinchulines), then the main cut, tira de asado (short rib cut across the bone) cooked bone-down for an hour before flipping. Salt is added only at the start, no marinades, no rubs. The asador never leaves the grill; the rhythm of the cooking sets the pace of the afternoon. Plates land on the table one course at a time, with bread, salad, chimichurri and a bottle of Tannat for company.

## Ingredients

### For the grill (serves 6)
- 1.5 kg tira de asado (beef short rib, cut across the bone into 4-5 cm wide strips)
- 6 chorizos criollos (fresh, not cured)
- 6 morcillas (blood sausages)
- 4 lamb or beef kidneys, halved and trimmed
- Coarse sea salt
- Optional: 500 g sweetbreads or chinchulines

### For the fire
- 4 kg hardwood (quebracho, oak or beech) or 3 kg lump charcoal
- Newspaper and kindling

### To serve
- Crusty bread, sliced
- Chimichurri (see separate recipe)
- A simple tomato-and-onion salad
- A bottle of Tannat or other Uruguayan red

## Method

### Stage 1 - Build the fire
1. Light the wood or charcoal in a deep firebox or chimney starter. Let it burn down 1.5 to 2 hours, until you have a deep bed of grey-white embers with no flame.
2. Rake half the embers under the parrilla grill. Hold the grill 15-20 cm above the coals.
3. Test the heat: you should be able to hold a hand 10 cm above the grill for 3 seconds before it gets too hot.
4. Brush the grill clean with a wire brush. Do not oil.

### Stage 2 - Start the sausages (choripan course)
1. Lay the chorizos on the cooler side of the grill, pricked once or twice with a knife.
2. Cook 12-15 minutes, turning every 4 minutes, until the skin is blistered and brown all over.
3. Add the morcillas in the last 6 minutes; they only need to warm through and crisp the skin.
4. Move to a board; rest 5 minutes. Slice the chorizo onto bread for the cooks, dab with chimichurri.

### Stage 3 - The kidneys
1. Salt the halved kidneys.
2. Grill cut-side down first for 4 minutes, flip, another 3 minutes. They should be deep brown outside, pink inside.
3. Slice and serve as the second small course.

### Stage 4 - The short rib (the main event)
1. Salt the tira de asado generously on both sides with coarse sea salt.
2. Lay bone-side down on the hottest part of the grill.
3. Cook bone-side down for 50-60 minutes. The bones act as a heat barrier; the meat cooks gently from above as the fat renders down. Rake fresh embers under as needed to hold a steady medium heat.
4. Flip onto the meat side. Cook 25-30 minutes for medium, pulling earlier for rarer.
5. The meat is ready when a knife slides easily into the thickest part and the outside is deeply caramelised.

### Stage 5 - Rest and serve
1. Move the short rib to a wooden board, tent loosely with foil. Rest 10 minutes.
2. Cut between the bones into individual ribs.
3. Serve on a hot platter with chimichurri, bread and tomato-onion salad. Pass the wine.

## Notes
- **The fire is the recipe.** Real asado is cooked over wood embers (or hardwood charcoal), never gas. The wood smoke flavours the meat.
- **No marinade.** Uruguayan asado uses only coarse salt at the start. Chimichurri is a finishing sauce, never a marinade.
- **Bone-side down for an hour.** The classic asador move; the bones protect the meat while the fat renders cleanly. Resist flipping early.
- **Pacing.** Asado is a 3-hour table, not a quick meal. Serve in courses (chorizo first, then offal, then the rib), with the bottle of Tannat working through the afternoon.

## Variations
- **Asado de tira con vacio.** Add a strip of vacio (beef flank) cooked alongside the short rib; the flank is the asador's prize cut.
- **With provoleta.** Open with a provoleta (grilled provolone disc with chimichurri) before the chorizo.
- **All-offal asado (achuras).** Skip the short rib; lay on the grill 6 chinchulines, 4 sweetbreads, 4 kidneys and the chorizos. The traditional working-week asado.
- **Costillar al asador.** A whole rib rack lashed to a metal cross and cooked vertical beside (not over) the embers for 4-5 hours. The party-size version.

## Serving
Bring the meat to the table on a wooden board · chimichurri in a small jug · a basket of bread · tomato-onion salad · Tannat in tumblers, not wine glasses.

## Storage
- Leftover grilled meat keeps 3 days refrigerated; eat cold sliced on bread or reheat briefly on a hot grill.
- Chorizo and morcilla also keep 3 days; reheat in a dry pan.
- Cooked offal is best eaten the same day.
