# Ensalada Rusa

![Ensalada Rusa](resources/ensalada-rusa.jpg)

*The Uruguayan asado-table salad: cold boiled potato, carrot and peas tossed in mayonnaise with a touch of mustard, served in a big bowl alongside the grilled meat.*

**Serves:** 6 as a side

**Prep Time:** 20 minutes (plus 1 hour chill)

**Cook Time:** 20 minutes

## Overview
Ensalada rusa (Russian salad) is the cold mayo-bound vegetable salad that travelled from late-tsarist Russia to Spain and then with Spanish immigrants to the River Plate, where it became the obligatory side at every asado, parrilla and Sunday family lunch. The Uruguayan version is honest and unfussy: equal parts of cold-boiled diced potato, diced carrot and green peas, bound with a generous amount of mayonnaise loosened with a spoon of dijon mustard and a squeeze of lemon. Chilled an hour, served in a deep bowl with the meat platter at the centre of the table. The contrast is the whole point: cold creamy starchy sweet salad against hot smoky grilled beef. Some households add a tin of tuna or a chopped hard-boiled egg; the foundational three-vegetable build is what shows up at every asado.

## Ingredients

- 500 g floury potatoes (Maris Piper), peeled
- 300 g carrots, peeled
- 200 g frozen peas (or fresh shelled peas in season)
- 1 tsp salt for the cooking water
- 180 g good mayonnaise
- 1 tbsp dijon mustard
- 1 tbsp lemon juice
- 1/2 tsp salt, plus more to taste
- 1/4 tsp white pepper
- Optional: 2 hard-boiled eggs, finely chopped
- Optional: 1 small tin of tuna in oil, drained and flaked
- Optional: 1 tbsp finely chopped chives or parsley to finish

## Method

### Stage 1 - Boil the vegetables
1. Cut the potatoes into 1 cm cubes. Cut the carrots into 1 cm cubes.
2. Bring a pan of salted water to the boil.
3. Add the potato cubes; cook 6 minutes.
4. Add the carrot cubes; cook 4 minutes.
5. Add the peas; cook 2 minutes more.
6. Test: everything should be tender but still holding shape, not mushy.
7. Drain into a colander. Run under cold water for 2 minutes to stop the cooking.
8. Spread out on a tray; cool to room temperature.

### Stage 2 - Make the dressing
1. In a large bowl, whisk together the mayonnaise, dijon mustard, lemon juice, salt and white pepper. Taste; it should be punchier than you want the final salad, the vegetables will dilute it.

### Stage 3 - Combine
1. Tip the cooled vegetables into the bowl with the dressing.
2. Fold gently with a spoon or spatula until evenly coated; do not crush the potato.
3. Add the chopped hard-boiled eggs or flaked tuna if using.
4. Taste; adjust salt and lemon.

### Stage 4 - Chill and serve
1. Cover, refrigerate at least 1 hour (2 hours is better).
2. Just before serving, give one more gentle fold; check seasoning.
3. Tip into a deep serving bowl; finish with chopped chives or parsley.
4. Serve cold alongside hot grilled meat.

## Notes
- **Cool the vegetables fully before dressing.** Warm vegetables thin the mayonnaise and the salad goes oily.
- **Dice small and even.** 1 cm cubes are the right size; bigger and the salad eats clunky.
- **Dress on the bold side.** Cold blunts seasoning; the dressing should taste slightly too punchy before the chill.
- **Mayonnaise quality matters.** A good shop-bought mayo is fine; supermarket budget mayo gives a thin, vinegary salad.

## Variations
- **Con atún (with tuna).** Add a tin of tuna in oil, drained and flaked, plus a finely chopped shallot. The cantina lunch version.
- **Con huevo (with eggs).** Add 2 chopped hard-boiled eggs; richer, classic for Sunday tables.
- **Con remolacha (with beetroot).** Add 200 g cooked diced beetroot; the salad turns pink, very Russian, very good.
- **With apple.** Add 1 finely diced tart green apple at the end; an Easter and Christmas variant.

## Serving
A deep bowl on the asado table · a serving spoon stuck in the middle · alongside grilled short rib or chivito · with a few black olives on top for the formal version.

## Storage
- Keeps 3 days refrigerated, sealed.
- The salad weeps a little on day 2; drain off any liquid and fold through.
- Do not freeze; the mayonnaise splits.
