# Uruguayan Cuisine

![Uruguayan Cuisine](resources/hero.jpg)

A cattle-country table built around the parrilla (charcoal grill), the asado Sunday ritual, and a deep Italo-Spanish immigration legacy. Beef is the headline; the chivito (the national steak sandwich, loaded with ham, bacon, cheese, lettuce and tomato on a soft roll with chips) sits alongside slow-cooked asado plates of short rib, chorizo and morcilla pulled straight off the embers. The Italian inheritance shows up everywhere: milanesa a la napolitana, pascualina spinach pie, fresh pasta on Sunday, alfajor cookies sandwiched with dulce de leche. The Spanish side delivers puchero stews and empanadas. Mate, drunk hot from a hollow gourd through a metal bombilla, is the constant companion from breakfast to bedtime. Smaller and quieter than Argentina across the river, but with its own clear identity: more meat per head than almost anywhere on earth, a grill-table culture, and a sweet tooth fixed on dulce de leche.
