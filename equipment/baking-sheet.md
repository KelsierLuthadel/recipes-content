# Baking Sheet
*A flat aluminium tray with a 2.5cm rim, also called a half-sheet pan or rimmed baking sheet. The most-used tool in any baker's kitchen, and the workhorse of sheet-pan dinner cooking. Heavy enough not to warp at 220°C, big enough to hold a full chicken dinner.*

## What to buy
**Budget (£10–20)**: an aluminium half-sheet pan (46×33cm, the US "half-sheet" size) from any restaurant supply store or Amazon. Nordic Ware is the benchmark. Heavy-gauge, uncoated, rolled rim for strength. Buy two.

**Good (£30–60)**: Nordic Ware Naturals or USA Pan. Slightly thicker gauge, more durable, lifetime tool. Still aluminium.

**Premium**: rarely worth it. The mid-tier baking sheet is already the right tool. Anything more expensive is either coated (avoid) or weight-without-benefit.

Look for: **aluminium**, **uncoated**, **rolled rim**, **2.5cm rim depth**, **46×33cm (half-sheet)** or **33×23cm (quarter-sheet) for small ovens**. Avoid: dark non-stick coatings (burn cookie bottoms), thin tin sheets (warp at high heat), insulated double-layer sheets (slow cooking, prevent browning).

## How to use
**For sheet-pan dinners**: don't crowd. Vegetables need space to brown — if they're touching, they steam. Use two pans for a meal that serves four; one pan crowds at higher volumes.

**For cookies**: line with parchment paper or a silicone baking mat. The mat reusables a hundred times; the parchment is one-shot but cheaper.

**For fish**: brush with oil, roast at 220°C (425°F) for 10–12 minutes per inch of thickness. The sheet conducts heat efficiently; the fish skin crisps from the underside.

**For roasted vegetables**: toss in oil and salt directly on the sheet (saves a bowl), spread in a single layer, roast at 220°C till browned and tender.

## How to maintain
**Hand-wash** with a soft sponge and dishsoap. Bar Keepers Friend (a non-bleach abrasive cleaner) removes the grease stains and oxidation that build up over time.

**Don't dishwasher** aluminium — the detergent corrodes it and dulls the finish.

**Don't worry about the patina.** A baking sheet that goes brown over time isn't ruined; it's seasoned. The dark patina actually helps browning. Don't try to scrub it back to silver — embrace the colour.

## Common mistakes
- **Crowding**: vegetables steam instead of roasting. Use two pans or do it in batches.
- **Not lining for cookies**: cookies stick, bottoms burn. Parchment paper or a silicone mat is essential.
- **Using a non-stick or coated tray**: dark coatings burn the bottoms of cookies. Aluminium is what you want.
- **One tray when you need two**: a single half-sheet pan can't do a sheet-pan dinner for four. Buy two.

## What it replaces
A pair of half-sheet pans replaces: cookie sheets (use the half-sheet, same thing), specialty fish-cooking trays, single-purpose cake pans (you can make sheet cake on it), roasting pans for small birds (a half-sheet roasts a 3kg chicken perfectly), and the lasagne dish for traybake lasagnes. Two of these run a kitchen for a decade.
