# Cutting Board
*The plank your knife sits on for every meal of the day. Get one big enough to actually use (most boards on sale are too small) and made of the right material — wood or plastic, never glass, stone or ceramic.*

## What to buy
**Budget (£10–25)**: a thick polypropylene board, 35×25cm minimum. Hygienic, dishwasher-safe, replace when it's deeply scarred. The OXO Good Grips or any commercial-grade plastic board.

**Good (£40–80)**: a thick wooden end-grain or edge-grain board in maple, beech, walnut or teak. End-grain (vertical grain — like a butcher block) is the gentlest on knife edges; edge-grain is cheaper and almost as good. 45×30cm or larger. Hand-wash only.

**Premium (£150+)**: a giant end-grain board, 50×40cm or larger, in walnut or maple. The Boos Block is the US benchmark; the Sage by Heston is a UK option. These last 30+ years.

The size matters more than the material. A board smaller than 35×25cm crowds every prep job and slows you down. The "perfect for small kitchens" boards in catalogues are too small to use for real cooking.

## How to use
Set it on a damp tea towel or rubber shelf liner so it doesn't slide. A sliding board is a knife-slip waiting to happen.

Use **separate boards for raw meat and everything else** — colour-coded if you can. The cross-contamination risk is real, but the practical reason is that you can chop garlic on the veg board while the meat board is in the dishwasher.

When the board gets covered in trimmings, scrape them into a bowl with the back (spine) of the knife — never the edge. Edge-scraping rolls the burr and dulls the knife.

## How to maintain
**Plastic boards**: dishwasher-safe. Replace when the cuts get deep enough to harbour bacteria (knife marks more than a millimetre deep). A plastic board is a 2–4 year tool, not a lifetime tool.

**Wooden boards**: hand-wash with hot soapy water, dry immediately on edge. Once a month, oil it with **food-safe mineral oil** (not vegetable oil — vegetable oils go rancid). Soak the board in oil for an hour, wipe off the excess. A well-maintained wooden board lasts decades.

**Never** put a wooden board in the dishwasher; the heat warps it and the wet cycle splits the wood. Never soak it in water either.

## Common mistakes
- **Too small**: cramps every job, slows you down, increases knife injuries because food falls off the edge.
- **Glass, stone or ceramic boards**: destroy knife edges instantly. The board lasts forever and the knife you just spent £150 on lasts a week.
- **Bamboo "wooden" boards**: bamboo is grass, not wood; it's much harder than maple and dulls knives faster. The "eco-friendly" marketing is misleading.
- **Not wiping it dry**: wooden boards split when left wet; plastic boards warp.

## What it replaces
A proper cutting board replaces every "chopping mat" plastic sheet, the kitchen counter (you'd be amazed how many people just chop on the worktop), and the small rectangular boards that come with knife sets. Two boards — one large wooden for veg and prep, one plastic for raw meat and fish — covers a home kitchen for life.
