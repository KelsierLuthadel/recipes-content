# Food Processor

*A motorised bowl with interchangeable blades and discs. Minces onions in 5 seconds, purees hummus in 30, slices a kilo of potatoes in a minute, makes pie pastry in 90 seconds. The single most time-saving appliance for serious home cooking.*

## What to buy

**Budget (£60–120)**: Magimix Compact 3200XL or Cuisinart DLC-8. 600–800W motor, 2.5–3L bowl. Stainless S-blade plus disc set (slicer + shredder). Replace the bowl seal every 5 years; otherwise lifetime tools.

**Good (£200–350)**: Magimix 4200XL, Cuisinart Elite. 950W+, 3.5–4L bowl, three-bowl nesting (large for mixing, medium for everyday, small for spices and herbs). Bigger feed tube (whole tomatoes fit).

**Premium (£500+)**: Robot Coupe R 301 (commercial), KitchenAid 13-cup Pro. Restaurant-grade, near-silent operation, multi-attachment ecosystem.

Look for: **at least 600W motor**, **stainless steel S-blade** (not plastic), **stable base** (rubber feet — these things shake), **disc set included** (slicing + shredding, not bought separately), **dishwasher-safe parts**.

Avoid: mini-choppers sold as "food processors" (too small for real work — useful as a second device for garlic but not as a main), wand blenders sold as "all-in-one" with a "processor attachment" (the attachment is tiny and underpowered).

## How to use

**Pulse, don't run continuously, for mincing.** Onions in a food processor go from "diced" to "puree" in 3 seconds — pulse 5–10 times to keep the texture.

**Process in batches.** A 3L bowl is the right size for most jobs, but processing 2kg of cabbage at once just produces a soggy half-shred at the bottom. Half-fill the bowl per batch.

**The S-blade** is for mincing, pureeing, mixing pastry. The **slicing disc** is for cucumbers, courgettes, potato chips. The **shredding disc** is for cheese, carrots, beetroot.

**For pastry**: cube the cold butter, pulse with flour and salt until pea-sized lumps, add ice water through the feed tube while pulsing. The whole shortcrust takes 90 seconds versus 10 minutes by hand.

## How to maintain

**Hand-wash the bowl and blades** with hot soapy water. The blades are dishwasher-safe but go dull faster in the machine.

**Don't immerse the motor base.** Wipe with a damp cloth only.

**Replace the bowl seal** (the rubber ring at the bottom of the bowl) every 3–5 years. Leaks past the seal mean liquid in the motor housing — instant death for the appliance.

**Keep blades sharp.** They dull eventually; cheaper machines come with cheaper blades. Sharpening services exist for high-end models.

## Common mistakes

- **Over-processing**: hummus goes from chunky to perfect to glue in seconds. Pulse, check, pulse again.
- **Processing dry ingredients without enough oil**: a dry food processor purees become a paste against the sides; need a teaspoon of oil to keep things moving.
- **Using it for tasks that don't need it**: a sharp knife is faster for one onion. The processor pays off when you have 5+ onions, or a kilo of vegetables.
- **Filling above the max line**: liquid sprays out the lid when the motor starts. Half-full is the safe max.

## What it replaces

A food processor replaces: mandolines for slicing (faster, safer — no finger guards needed), the box grater for cheese (when you're shredding 500g+), hand-chopping onions and herbs for big batches, the pastry blender (cuts butter into flour in seconds), and the blender for thick purees (a food processor handles thicker textures better). Doesn't replace the blender for liquids (smoothies and soups); they're complementary.
