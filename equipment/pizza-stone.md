# Pizza Stone / Steel

*A thick ceramic stone or steel plate that sits in the oven and holds enough thermal mass to crisp the bottom of a pizza in 4–6 minutes at 250°C. The difference between "homemade pizza that's okay" and "homemade pizza that's actually really good".*

## What to buy

**Budget — stone (£20–40)**: a 35×35cm cordierite stone, 1.5–2cm thick. Pizzacraft, Vesuvo, or any unbranded cordierite stone. Cheap, effective, slightly slower to heat than steel.

**Good — steel (£60–120)**: a 35×35cm baking steel, 6mm thick. NerdChef Steel Stone, Lakeland Pizza Steel. Faster heat transfer than stone (steel conducts ~20× better than cordierite), produces better crust char, lasts forever. The serious choice.

**Premium (£150+)**: a 9mm or 12mm thick steel, often A36 mild steel cut to size from a steel supplier (cheaper than buying branded). Or a Solidteknics quenched steel.

Look for: **at least 30cm wide** (fits a 12-inch pizza), **6mm thick or more** for steel (thinner ones don't hold enough heat), **1.5cm thick or more** for stone (thinner cracks under thermal shock). For most home ovens, the largest plate that fits is best.

Avoid: glazed pizza stones (the glaze melts at oven temperatures and pizzas stick), "non-stick" pizza stones (the non-stick coating won't survive 250°C+), specialty "individual pizza" small stones (too small to fit a real pizza).

## How to use

**Preheat for at least 45 minutes at maximum oven temperature.** A home oven that says it's at 250°C is usually 220–240°C — the air heats fast but the stone takes time. Use an oven thermometer to confirm; expect 45–60 minutes for the stone to reach equilibrium.

**Slide the pizza on with a peel.** Wooden or aluminium pizza peel, dust with cornmeal or semolina, build the pizza on the peel, slide onto the preheated stone in one smooth motion. Building the pizza directly on the stone is impossible (it bakes onto the surface).

**Bake at full oven temperature** (typically 250°C or as high as the oven goes). Most home pizzas take 4–6 minutes; 7+ minutes and the bottom is starting to burn while the top is still pale.

For an even better result: **use the stone under the broiler/grill setting**. Preheat with the stone in the top position, switch to broiler at the moment of bake. The stone radiates heat up while the broiler radiates heat down — you get a 90-second pizza with a perfectly charred crust.

**Use for bread too.** A baking stone preheated for 30 minutes gives bread a thicker, crustier bottom and improved oven spring (the rapid rise in the first 10 minutes of baking).

## How to maintain

**Don't wash with soap.** Cordierite stones absorb soap; the next pizza tastes like dishwashing liquid. Scrape off cooked-on bits with a metal spatula, brush off with a stiff brush, wipe with a dry cloth. Soak only if absolutely necessary (and let it dry completely — wet stones crack on the next heat-up).

**Steel cleans easier**: hot water, soft brush, dry immediately. Re-season with a thin layer of vegetable oil if it starts to rust (steel will rust if left wet).

**Cool down slowly.** Don't take the stone out of the oven and run cold water over it — thermal shock cracks ceramic and warps steel. Let it cool in the oven.

**Live in the oven.** Both stones and steels are heavy; once installed, leave them. They make the oven more thermally stable (better baking in general), retain heat between cooks, and save you carrying a 5kg slab around.

## Common mistakes

- **Not preheating long enough**: 20 minutes isn't enough. The first pizza on an under-preheated stone has a pale, soggy bottom.
- **Using flour on the peel instead of semolina**: flour burns on the stone and tastes acrid. Semolina or cornmeal slides better and doesn't burn.
- **Washing with soap**: ruins the next bake. Scrape only.
- **Putting a wet stone in a hot oven**: cracks it instantly. Stones must be bone-dry before heating.

## What it replaces

A pizza stone replaces: the baking tray for pizza (which gives a soggy bottom — the tray heats slowly and the dough steams), the home pizza oven (£500+ outdoor unit gives marginally better results), the temptation to give up and order delivery. With a pizza steel, a proper dough recipe and a wood-fired oven mindset, home pizza becomes genuinely competitive with takeaway.
