# Non-Stick Pan

*The 28cm pan that lives on the hob for fried eggs, scrambled eggs, omelettes, pancakes, crepes, fish fillets and any other dish where you'd rather food slide than stick. A consumable — plan to replace it every 2–5 years.*

## What to buy

**Budget (£15–30)**: a Tefal Ingenio or any major-brand 28cm pan with a removable handle. PTFE-coated (the polymer most non-stick pans use). Spend at the budget end — they wear out at roughly the same rate as expensive ones.

**Good (£60–100)**: Tefal Hard Titanium, Anolon Endurance, or De Buyer Choc Resto Induction. Thicker base for even heat, better coating durability. Worth the upgrade if you cook breakfast every day.

**Premium (£150+)**: Demeyere or Le Creuset Toughened Non-Stick. Triple-layer coating, 5-ply construction. Still doesn't last forever, but lasts longest.

**Alternative — ceramic non-stick (£40–80)**: GreenPan, Caraway. Newer technology, sand-based coating. Claims to be PFOA-free and PTFE-free. Performs worse than PTFE when new and dies faster — choose if you have strong concerns about PTFE.

Get **28cm** (11 inch) — big enough for a 4-egg omelette, small enough to fit on the average hob. Smaller (24cm) is fine as a second pan; larger (32cm) is overkill for non-stick (the coating wears out before you need the size).

## How to use

**Medium-low to medium heat only.** Non-stick coatings break down above 260°C (500°F); you reach that on a high flame in 2 minutes. The food doesn't need it anyway — eggs and fish cook better gently.

**Always add oil or butter**, even though the surface is non-stick. The fat carries heat into the food evenly and protects the coating.

**Wood, silicone or plastic utensils only**. Metal utensils scratch the coating; once scratched, that area sticks forever.

The pan is for: eggs, pancakes, crepes, fish, delicate sauces. It's not for: searing steak (the coating can't handle the heat), browning meat (you want fond, not a clean release), deglazing (acid + heat kills the coating).

## How to maintain

**Hand-wash with soft sponge and warm water.** Never the dishwasher (the detergent corrodes the coating, the heat warps the metal). Never abrasive scourers, scouring pads or bicarbonate of soda. Wipe out fat with a paper towel before washing — easier than cleaning a greasy pan.

When the coating starts sticking, it's dying. There's no rescue technique — replace it. Most non-stick pans last 2–5 years of daily home use; if yours is failing at 18 months you're overheating it.

**Don't stack pans on top** of the non-stick — the bottoms scratch the coating. Hang or stack with a felt liner.

## Common mistakes

- **Cranking the heat**: non-stick coatings die above 260°C. Medium is hot enough for eggs.
- **Metal utensils**: an instant kill. Even one scratch turns the pan into a sticky pan with non-stick edges.
- **Dishwashing**: every wash takes a year off the pan's life.
- **Using it for everything**: steak, sear, brown — those want a stainless or cast iron pan that builds fond. Non-stick is for delicate work.

## What it replaces

A non-stick pan replaces specifically: omelette pans, crepe pans, "perfect egg" specialty pans, anything sold for fried eggs. You want **one non-stick** for delicate work plus a **stainless steel pan** (separate guide) for browning and a **cast iron** (separate guide) for searing. Three pans cover almost everything. The set of 12 pans in a "complete cookware set" is mostly unnecessary.
