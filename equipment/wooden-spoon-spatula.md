# Wooden Spoon & Silicone Spatula

*The two non-metal stirring tools every kitchen needs. A solid wooden spoon for stirring hot liquids and scraping fond; a heat-resistant silicone spatula for folding, scraping bowls and dragging sauces out of pans. Buy both, ignore the rest of the utensil block.*

## What to buy

**Wooden spoon, budget (£3–8)**: any beech or maple spoon from a kitchen shop. Make sure it's hardwood, not soft pine. 30cm or longer, sturdy bowl, flat-edge or pointed-tip. Joseph Joseph and Lakeland do good cheap ones.

**Wooden spoon, good (£15–25)**: an olive wood spoon (denser, more attractive, longer life) or a Bérard French-made beech spoon. Lasts decades.

**Silicone spatula, budget (£5–12)**: a one-piece silicone spatula (no separate handle, no seam where food can hide) from any supermarket. Heat-resistant to at least 260°C, dishwasher-safe.

**Silicone spatula, good (£15–30)**: OXO Good Grips, GIR (Get It Right), Le Creuset. One-piece moulded, stiff enough to scrape, flexible enough to fold. The GIR Ultimate Spatula is the cult favourite.

Look for: **silicone-only, one-piece** spatulas (the two-piece kind harbour bacteria in the seam between handle and head); **heat-resistant to 260°C** (silicone is fine; if it's not labelled, assume it's cheap rubber); **wooden spoons** in hardwood (beech, maple, olive — NOT pine or bamboo, which split and stain).

## How to use

**Wooden spoon** for stirring in pans where you'd damage the surface with metal (non-stick) or where you want to scrape fond off the bottom (stainless steel, cast iron). Wood is gentle but firm, won't scratch, doesn't conduct heat back into your hand. Use it for stocks, sauces, stews, anything bubbling on the hob.

**Silicone spatula** for folding (whisked egg whites into batter, whipped cream into ganache), scraping (getting every last drop of batter out of a bowl, dragging sauce out of a saucepan), and stirring delicate things (custards, hollandaise — anywhere you want to coat the spatula and check the consistency).

The wooden spoon is the everyday stirring tool; the spatula is the precision tool.

## How to maintain

**Wooden spoon**: hand-wash with hot soapy water, dry immediately, store standing up (not lying in the cutlery drawer). Every few months, rub with food-safe mineral oil to prevent splitting. NEVER dishwasher (splits the wood, raises the grain), NEVER soak (warps and cracks). Replace when the bowl chips or splinters.

**Silicone spatula**: dishwasher-safe, indestructible at normal kitchen temperatures. Don't leave it touching a hot pan side (it can melt above 260°C). Replace when the silicone tears or the handle separates (one-piece designs prevent this).

## Common mistakes

- **Metal utensils on non-stick**: scratches the coating and ruins the pan. Always non-metal in non-stick.
- **Wooden spoon in the dishwasher**: splits, cracks, ruined. One cycle is enough to destroy a good wooden spoon.
- **Bamboo "wooden" spoons**: bamboo is grass; it splits more easily than hardwood and chars when left near a hot burner.
- **Cheap rubber spatulas (not silicone)**: melt above 180°C; the head separates from the handle.

## What it replaces

A wooden spoon and a silicone spatula replace: the whole 12-piece utensil set sold in a block (slotted spoon, ladle, pasta tongs — sure, useful, but not in a "set"; buy each as needed), the various branded "perfect for X" utensils, and the metal utensils that scratch every non-stick pan you own. These two tools cover 80% of stirring; add a whisk and a pair of tongs and you have the full essential set.
